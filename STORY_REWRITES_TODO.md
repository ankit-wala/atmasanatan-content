## Story Notes (reference for writing)

### navratri-brahmacharini
- Short opening: who is Brahmacharini — "Brahma" = the Absolute, "charini" = she who walks toward; tapa-shakti in motion; Day 2 in the Navadurga arc (after Shailputri has taken earthly form, consciousness must be refined through austerity)
- Keep this to 3-4 lines — no lengthy naam-etymology section
- Main katha: **Prakandasura episode** (popular circulating story, widely used in religious discourse; no single Purana chapter verified)
  - Parvati deep in samadhi; demon Prakandasura attacks the gods
  - Gods cannot fight; they approach Parvati but cannot disturb her tapas
  - Her kamandalu tips over — the water inside becomes a divine flood that sweeps away the demon army
  - Her tapas-fire then rises and destroys Prakandasura himself
  - She never breaks samadhi; the destruction happens through her concentrated tapa-energy alone
- Significance: the philosophy of austerity as power; the kamandalu and japamala as instruments (not status symbols); barefoot = earth-contact is part of tapas
- Update subtitle/summary/reel_hook to match

### navratri-skandamata
- Open with Tarakasura problem (brief — 3-4 lines)
- One line establishing Skanda's birth: "शिव-पार्वती के पुत्र स्कंद का जन्म हुआ"
- Main story: Parvati gives Skanda the Vel (divine spear) — her own Gnana Shakti crystallized as a weapon
  - Skanda had 11 weapons from the 11 Rudras; none were enough
  - Parvati pressed her own power into the Vel and placed it in his 12th hand
  - At Sikkal (Tamil Nadu), this moment is commemorated each Skanda Sashti
- The iconic image: fully armed mother on a lion, holding an infant — the moment just before the battle; two states simultaneously
- Significance: Skandamata is not a passive observer; she is the source of the killing power; the mother who sends her child into battle having given him her own strength
- Add vahaan = lion paragraph here

### navratri-mahagauri
- Vamana Purana version (not the popular Ganga-water story):
  - Shiva calls Parvati "Kali" playfully (or with mild teasing)
  - She takes it seriously — as a sovereign, not a victim — and declares: "I will shed this dark form through tapasya"
  - She does 100-year tapasya standing on one leg
  - Brahma (not Shiva) appears and grants a golden complexion — suvarna rupa
  - The discarded dark form becomes Kaushiki, who goes to the Vindhya mountains → becomes Vindhyavasini (active pilgrimage shrine today)
  - Parvati stands revealed as Mahagauri — always white within; darkness was a garment she wore and shed
- Theological arc: Day 7 (Kalaratri) = dark power that destroys Raktabija; Day 8 (Mahagauri) = same Devi, dark sheath separated → pure luminous white. Two days, one statement.
- Add vahaan = white bull (Nandi purified through tapasya) paragraph
