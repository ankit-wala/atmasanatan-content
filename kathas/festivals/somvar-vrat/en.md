## Katha

Prajapati Daksha had twenty-seven daughters — twenty-seven nakshatras, all given in marriage to Chandra. Chandra was their husband. But his heart rested on one — Rohini. His love for Rohini was so deep that the remaining twenty-six wives began to feel neglected.

Those wives went to their father Daksha. They told him their grief — "Father, Chandra belongs only to Rohini. We live in his house yet are not truly his."

Daksha summoned Chandra. He counselled him — "All of them are my daughters. Treat them all equally."

Chandra listened, nodded — and then returned to Rohini and forgot everything.

Daksha counselled him a second time. Then gave a third warning.

When Chandra still would not relent, Daksha pronounced his curse — *"You shall be consumed by decay. Your brilliance shall wane, your radiance shall depart."*

And the curse took hold.

Chandra began to diminish. Kala by kala, he shrank. The moonlight that had illuminated the night grew dim. The power that sustained medicines, crops, and the ocean on earth began to leave him.

A calamity fell upon the earth. Herbs began to wither. Crops began to dry. The devas and rishis grew troubled.

Together they went to Brahma. Brahma heard them all out. Then he said to Chandra — *"You are suffering the fruit of what you did. Daksha's curse cannot be undone. But Shiva's grace can accomplish everything. Go to Prabhasa Tirtha — where the Sarasvati meets the ocean. There, observe the Monday fast, chant the Mahamrityunjaya mantra, worship Shiva. Not one Monday — a hundred Mondays."*

Chandra reached Prabhasa Tirtha.

There he established a Shivalinga and began his sadhana. Every Monday — fasting, water-abhisheka, belpatra offerings, Mahamrityunjaya japa. With devotion, with humility, without fail.

One by one the Mondays passed. Ten, twenty, fifty — and at last, the hundredth Monday.

On that final Monday, Lord Shiva appeared.

He said — *"Soma, your sadhana is complete. To erase Daksha's curse entirely is not within my power — it is the word of a Prajapati. But I can govern it. You will wax for fifteen days and wane for fifteen days — and this shall forever be your nature. Your radiance shall return."*

Chandra's light came back. Moonlight spread once more over the earth, crops came back to life, and herbs regained their potency.

The place where Chandra found Shiva — that tirtha still exists today. Its name is **Somnath** — the Lord of Soma. The first among the twelve Jyotirlingas.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that whatever devotee observes the Monday fast with true faith — whatever decay exists in their life, whatever is diminishing — through Shiva's grace, it is restored to life.

## Significance

Why Monday is Shiva's day — the answer lies in this very katha.

Soma is the name of Chandra. Shiva is Chandrashekhar — the one who bears Chandra on his brow. When Chandra took refuge with Shiva, Shiva placed him upon his own head. That is why Chandra's day became Shiva's day — Monday is the most sacred day for Shiva's worship.

The glory of the Somnath Jyotirlinga is also bound to this katha. Among the twelve Jyotirlingas, Somnath is the first — at Prabhasa Tirtha in Saurashtra (Gujarat). This is the place where Chandra performed his hundred-Monday sadhana.

The Monday fast is not tied to any particular month. It can be begun on any Monday — it is especially fruitful during Shravana, as Shravana is Shiva's beloved month, but beginning on any Monday of the year is sanctioned by scripture. (The Solah Somvar Vrat — a separate sadhana of sixteen Mondays — is begun from Shravana.)

## Vidhi

- **Sankalpa:** On the first Monday, take a sankalpa stating your intention and how many Mondays you will observe.
- **At dawn:** Rise before sunrise; bathe; go to a Shiva temple or set up a Shivalinga at home.
- **Abhishek:** Pour water and raw milk over the Shivalinga.
- **Offerings:** Belpatra, white flowers (datura, aak, jasmine), and sandalwood; light incense and a lamp.
- **Mantras:** Chant *Oṃ Namaḥ Śivāya* at least 108 times; recite the Mahamrityunjaya Mantra.
- **Fasting:** Complete fast or ekahara (one meal a day); abstaining from grains and taking fruit once is also observed. In some regions, giving up salt is traditional.
- **Evening puja:** Performing Shiva puja during Pradosha Kala (approximately ninety minutes after sunset) is especially fruitful. When Monday and Pradosha coincide, it is called *Som-Pradosha* — the most auspicious Monday of the year.
- **Breaking the fast:** On Tuesday morning after sunrise, with Shiva's name on one's lips.

## Mantras

**Panchakshari Mantra — the essential chant of the Monday fast:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय

oṃ namaḥ śivāya

Meaning: Salutation to Shiva. This five-syllable mantra is a symbol of Shiva's five acts — creation, sustenance, dissolution, concealment, and grace.

---

**Mahamrityunjaya Mantra — the very mantra Chandra chanted:**

> ॐ त्र्यम्बकं यजामहे सुगन्धिं पुष्टिवर्धनम् ।
> उर्वारुकमिव बन्धनान् मृत्योर्मुक्षीय माऽमृतात् ॥

oṃ tryambakaṃ yajāmahe sugandhiṃ puṣṭivardhanam | urvārukamiva bandhanān mṛtyormukṣīya mā'mṛtāt ||

Meaning: O Tryambaka — three-eyed Shiva — we worship you, the fragrant one who nourishes and sustains. As a ripe fruit is freed from its stalk, so release us from the bonds of death and establish us in immortality.
