## Katha

Maharaja Dilipa of the Ikshvaku lineage was a righteous king devoted to dharma and the welfare of his people. His kingdom prospered in every way, yet a deep anguish lay in his heart — he had no child. He and his queen, Maharani Sudakshina, spent their days weighed down by this grief. Despite performing many yajnas and vrats, no fruit came.

At last Maharaja Dilipa and Maharani Sudakshina journeyed to the ashrama of Maharshi Vasishtha. Vasishtha was the primordial guru of all creation and also the kulguru of Dilipa's line. He looked carefully at both of them, and with his divine sight discerned the cause.

Vasishtha said — "O King, once as you were returning from the celestial realm, Nandini, the daughter of Kamadhenu, was standing beneath a tree along the way. You saw her but passed on without offering your salutation. Grief arose in her heart, and it is on account of her curse that you have no son."

Both Dilipa and Sudakshina were filled with sorrow. They asked humbly — "Gurudeva, what must we do?" Vasishtha showed them the path — "Nandini is here in the ashrama. Serve her with complete devotion and sincerity for twenty-one days. Do whatever she asks of you. Your well-being lies in her happiness."

Maharaja Dilipa and Maharani Sudakshina began serving Nandini with humility. Each day they went with her into the forest, brought her fresh grass and water, and cleared every obstacle from her path. Nandini, Kamadhenu's divine daughter — her service was no ordinary task; it was a tapasya.

The twenty-first day arrived. That day was the Purnima of the month of Magha. Suddenly, a fearsome lion appeared in the forest and readied itself to spring upon Nandini. King Dilipa reached for his bow, but his body seemed to turn to stone — he could not move.

Without hesitating for even a moment, Dilipa stepped between Nandini and the lion and said — "O lion, it is my dharma to protect this cow. If you require food, take this body of mine. Do not touch Nandini."

In that very instant the lion vanished. It had been a test sent by Lord Shiva himself — to examine the depth of Dilipa's devotion and surrender.

Nandini was pleased. She said — "O King, you have truly upheld dharma. Ask — what boon do you desire?" Dilipa joined his palms and said — "Mother, grant me a son of radiant spirit and righteous soul."

Nandini's boon was fulfilled. A mighty son was born to Maharani Sudakshina — he was named Raghu. This very Raghu went on to become the resplendent pillar of the Raghuvamsha, and it was in this dynasty that, ages later, Maryada Purushottama Lord Rama took his avatar.

The tradition of the Padma Purana holds that on Magha Purnima — the very tithi on which Dilipa was tested and Nandini granted her boon — bathing in a sacred river yields the same fruit as the entire month of Magha's spiritual practice.

## Significance

Magha Purnima is the holiest day of the Magh Mela. For those who have observed Kalpavasi — spending the entire month of Magha camped at the Prayagraj Sangam — their sadhana finds its completion in the bath on this Purnima.

The scriptures declare — a bath at the Sangam on Magha Purnima destroys the sins of seven lifetimes. This bath is not merely the purification of the body; it is the purification of the soul.

On this day there is a special injunction for the worship of Lord Vishnu. The donation of sesame seeds and grain is considered especially meritorious. Performing tarpan for the ancestors grants them peace.

This tithi is also the founding date of the Raghuvamsha — the root of the dynasty in which Lord Rama came. For this reason the day stands as a symbol of devotion, service, and surrender.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Rise before sunrise during Brahma muhurta — do the bath as early as possible; go to a sacred river (if not possible, bathe at home with water mixed with Gangajal); keep the mind fixed in contemplation of Lord Vishnu while bathing.
- **Fasting:** Optional but meritorious — remain on fruits or eat only once (ekabhukta); a full fast is also acceptable.
- **Vishnu puja:** Worship Lord Vishnu with flowers, tulasi, incense, a lamp, and naivedya; recite the japa of *Oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya*.
- **Dana:** Offer sesame seeds to the poor or a temple; donate grain to feed the hungry; as one is able, also donate cloth and money.
- **Tarpan:** Offer tarpan with water for the ancestors, saying *Oṃ pitṛbhyaḥ namaḥ* while pouring.
- **Katha shravan:** Recite or listen to the Satyanarayan Katha (fourth adhyaya) — it holds special significance on Magha Purnima.
- **Kalpavasi:** Those who have remained at the Prayagraj Sangam throughout the month of Magha complete their Kalpavasa on this very Purnima.
- **Throughout the day:** Speak truthfully, avoid anger, and observe restraint of the senses; in the evening, light a lamp and perform the aarti of Lord Vishnu.
