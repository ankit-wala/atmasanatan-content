## Katha

Once, Dharmaraja Yudhishthira asked Lord Shri Krishna — "Madhava, what is the name of the Ekadashi of the bright fortnight of the month of Magha, and what is its glory?"

Lord Shri Krishna said with a smile — "O King, this Ekadashi is called Jaya Ekadashi. It destroys all sins — it can sever even the most severe curses and bonds of karma. Listen to its wondrous story."

In the Swarga of Devaraj Indra there lived a supremely gifted Gandharva by the name of Malyavan. His voice held such magic that the gods would fall spellbound. In that same celestial assembly there was also a most beautiful Apsara named Pushpavati. The two fell in love with each other — so deeply, so utterly, that nothing else could hold their attention.

One day a grand celebration of music and dance was held in the court of Devaraj Indra. Malyavan began to sing and Pushpavati began to dance — yet both their minds were lost in each other. The notes of the song went off-key, the rhythm fell apart, the steps of the dance faltered. In the assembly of the gods, this was an unforgivable insult.

The face of Devaraj Indra flushed red with fury. He rose and declared — "You two have dishonoured this divine assembly. In your foolishness and infatuation you forgot your duty before the gods. I curse you — fall from Swarga to the earth and wander as pishacha spirits."

Struck by the thunderbolt of the curse, both fell from Swarga and appeared in ghoulish form in the forbidding valleys of the Himalayas. It was terrible — hideous bodies, the torment of hunger and thirst, and on all sides the cruel embrace of snow and cold. They wandered here and there, finding no food, no rest, no companionship.

The month of Magha arrived. It was the night of the Ekadashi of the bright fortnight. Wandering and weary, they came to a stop beneath a great Peepal tree. The cold was so fierce it chilled the bones. They could not sleep, they could not eat — they remained awake through the entire night, shivering without stop. They had no idea what night it was, nor had they made any vow. Yet that night, unknowingly, the fast of Ekadashi was observed by them — abstinence and wakefulness both.

Morning came. With the rising of the sun, a wondrous thing happened. A divine radiance began to pour from both their pishacha bodies. The very forms they had worn when they fell from Swarga — those same beautiful, radiant forms of Gandharva and Apsara — returned. Flowers rained down from the sky.

They flew straight toward Swarga and appeared before Devaraj Indra. Indra was left astonished. He asked — "How did this come to pass?" Malyavan and Pushpavati told him everything — that night in the Himalayas, that Peepal tree, that unknowing fast and wakefulness. Devaraj Indra then bowed in reverence to the glory of Jaya Ekadashi.

In the tradition of the Padma Purana it is said that the person who observes the fast of Jaya Ekadashi with devotion is freed from all bonds of sin and ultimately attains Vishnuloka.

## Significance

Jaya means victory, triumph. This Ekadashi grants victory over downfall. For those who have fallen low in life for any reason — through attachment, through anger, through pride, through circumstance — Jaya Ekadashi is the door to rising again.

The special quality of this story is that Malyavan and Pushpavati did not observe the fast deliberately. Yet even so, the power of the Ekadashi tithi granted them liberation. This establishes that the glory of this date is self-evident and boundless.

Magha Shukla Ekadashi is considered particularly fruitful for those who are struggling with negative states such as fear, anger, lust, and greed. This Ekadashi is a festival of purification of the soul and of resurrection.

## Vidhi

The fast of Jaya Ekadashi begins on the evening of Dashami. On that day eat sattvic food, keep the mind at peace, and stay away from worldly desires.

On the day of Ekadashi, bathe at dawn and meditate upon Lord Vishnu. Offer tulsi leaves, yellow flowers, akshat, incense, and a lamp in worship. Tulsi is especially dear to Lord Vishnu — worship is considered incomplete without tulsi.

Throughout the day keep the mind engaged in bhajan, kirtan, and remembrance of Vishnu. Recitation of the Vishnu Sahasranama or the Ekadashi Mahatmya is especially meritorious.

Night vigil holds special importance on this Ekadashi — in the katha itself, it was the vigil that became the cause of liberation.

## Observance

**Fasting:** On Ekadashi observe a complete nirjala fast or a fruit-based fast. Those who cannot observe a complete fast may sustain themselves on fruit, milk, and water.

**Prohibited:** Rice, urad dal, and tamasic food are forbidden on Ekadashi. Keep the mind away from quarrelling, anger, and falsehood as well.

**Parana:** On Dwadashi, after sunrise, break the fast within the appointed muhurta for parana. Before breaking the fast, offer bhog to the Lord and receive tulsi water.

**Night Vigil:** Night vigil on Jaya Ekadashi is especially fruitful. Continue with bhajan, kirtan, or chanting of Vishnu's name through the night.
