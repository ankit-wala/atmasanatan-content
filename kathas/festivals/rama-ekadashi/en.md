## Katha

Dharmaraja Yudhishthira asked Lord Shri Krishna — "Madhava, what is the name of the Ekadashi of Kartik's dark fortnight? What is its glory?"

The Lord said — "O King, this Ekadashi is called **Rama Ekadashi**. 'Rama' is that name of Lakshmi who spreads light and prosperity through all the worlds — who dwells forever in Vishnu's heart. This Ekadashi, which falls two days before Diwali, is the destroyer of all sins. Now hear its katha."

---

Long, long ago there lived a mighty king named **Mucukunda** — a devoted bhakta of Lord Vishnu, a lover of justice, and ever generous in giving. His subjects lived in happiness; his kingdom ran by dharma.

He had a beautiful and virtuous daughter — **Chandrabhaga**. She was given in marriage to King **Shobhan**. Chandrabhaga was herself a devoted observer of Ekadashi. Every Ekadashi she kept a waterless fast — this had been her vow for many years.

Once, during Kartik Krishna Ekadashi, Shobhan came to visit his father-in-law Mucukunda's kingdom.

Chandrabhaga knew — her husband's body was frail. A waterless fast would be too much for him to bear. She thought carefully within herself — yet her resolve in the dharma of Ekadashi was unshakeable.

She said to Shobhan — "My lord, tomorrow is Rama Ekadashi. In our home it is the practice that no food or water is taken on this day. If you are here, you will need to keep the fast."

Shobhan heard her words. He knew his body was weak — but faced with his wife's faith, he did not refuse. He accepted the vow of Rama Ekadashi.

---

Ekadashi came and went. He remained waterless throughout the day. He meditated on Lord Vishnu. But his body had grown so weak that it could not endure the rigorous fast.

That night — Shobhan passed away.

Chandrabhaga was plunged into grief. Yet within her heart there was also this thought — her husband had kept this Ekadashi with wholehearted devotion. One who leaves the body on the day of Ekadashi is not kept away from Vishnu's grace.

And truly, the merit of the Rama Ekadashi fast was so vast that by its power, Shobhan's soul reached a radiant realm — a golden city near **Mandarachal** mountain. There he received divine pleasures and royal comforts — yet that city was not permanent. What is built by merit alone, and not watered by love, is perishable.

---

Some years passed.

A wandering sage named **Somasharma** came roaming to that divine city. He was struck with wonder at its extraordinary splendour. Just then Shobhan recognised him — a bond from a previous life.

He said — "Venerable sage, this city came to me through the merit of Rama Ekadashi — yet it is impermanent. My Chandrabhaga — my wife — must have been accumulating the merit of her fasts all this while. If she were to offer me the stored merit of her Ekadashi vows, this city would become eternal."

He entreated Somasharma — "Please go and carry my message to her."

---

Somasharma returned. He searched for Chandrabhaga in Mucukunda's kingdom and told her everything.

Chandrabhaga's eyes filled with tears. She said — "Muni-var, I have kept Ekadashi fasts since childhood. I have years of accumulated merit with me. I am ready to offer it all to him."

She set out with Somasharma toward that divine city.

When she reached the gates of the city, she recalled all the years of her Ekadashi merit within her heart and offered it to Shobhan.

At that very moment — the city was transformed. What had been fleeting became eternal. What had been built by merit alone was now also nourished by love.

Shobhan saw Chandrabhaga. The two were reunited.

---

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana it is said — the fast of Rama Ekadashi destroys all sins. One who observes this Ekadashi receives merit equal to the fruit of the Vajapeya yajna. And one who hears this katha — they too become a partaker of the same fruit.

## Significance

**Two days before Diwali:**
The arrival of Rama Ekadashi is the spiritual beginning of Diwali's preparation. Just as the outer home is cleaned before the festival, this Ekadashi cleanses the mind and soul — destroying sins, purifying the heart — so that the Lakshmi puja on Diwali may be offered from a pure and ready heart.

**"Rama" — this name of Lakshmi:**
'Rama' means she who pervades all the worlds — who is the source of joy and prosperity. This Ekadashi is connected to Lakshmi because at this time Vishnu is awakening from his cosmic sleep, and Lakshmi as his beloved is especially worthy of veneration.

**The katha of Chandrabhaga — the power of Ekadashi's merit:**
Shobhan's passing happened on this very Ekadashi — yet he was granted a divine realm. And Chandrabhaga's accumulated merit made that realm eternal. This katha also tells us that the merit of Ekadashi can be offered to beloved ones who have departed.

**The name 'Rambha':**
In some traditions this Ekadashi is also called **Rambha Ekadashi** — 'Rambha' meaning the banana tree or the celestial apsara Rambha — both references connect with the katha of Shobhan and Chandrabhaga. This name is part of the regional diversity of this Ekadashi.

## Vidhi

1. On **Dashami**, eat only once — a sattvic, single meal. Onion, garlic, and meat are to be avoided.
2. On **Ekadashi**, take a bath in the brahma muhurta before dawn and take the vow of the fast.
3. Establish an image of Lord **Vishnu** or a shaligram. Place a picture of **Lakshmi** alongside — this is the combined Ekadashi of Lakshmi and Vishnu.
4. Perform panchamrita abhisheka — milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar.
5. Offer tulsi, lotus or yellow flowers, yellow cloth, a lamp, and incense.
6. Hear or read the katha of Rama Ekadashi.
7. **Night vigil** — remembrance of Vishnu's name, kirtan of "Hari-Hari."
8. On **Dwadashi**, break the fast after sunrise, between the end of Rahu-kalam and the close of Dwadashi.

## Observance

**Fasting:** Waterless or on fruits — until the Dwadashi paran. Remember Shobhan's katha — the true strength of the fast lies in devotion; it is right to choose the form of the fast according to one's own capacity.

**Diwali context:** Diwali comes two days after this Ekadashi — so cleaning the home, lighting lamps, and taking a resolve of Lakshmi's invocation on this day is considered auspicious.

**Offering merit:** In the tradition of Chandrabhaga — the merit of this Ekadashi can be offered in the name of a beloved one who has departed.

**Paran:** On Dwadashi morning, before Rahu-kalam.

## Mantras

**Vishnu Dvadasakshari Mantra — the root mantra of Rama Ekadashi:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya

Meaning: "Salutation to Lord Vasudeva." — This is Vishnu's twelve-syllable mantra; its recitation is auspicious on Rama Ekadashi and all Ekadashis.

**Rama Stuti — invocation of Lakshmiji:**

> या रमा सर्वलोकानां लक्ष्मीर्विष्णुप्रिया सदा ।
> तस्यै नमो नमस्तुभ्यं पुण्यकर्मणि संस्थिते ॥

Yā ramā sarvalokānāṃ lakṣmīrviṣṇupriyā sadā |
tasyai namo namastubhyaṃ puṇyakarmaṇi saṃsthite ||

Meaning: "To her who is Rama (Lakshmi) of all the worlds, who is forever the beloved of Vishnu, who is ever established in acts of merit — salutation again and again."
