## Katha

**The birth of mada:**

After the slaying of Tarakasura, a pride of victory entered the eyes of the gods. The battle had been won, the enemy slain — but the subtle energy that remained on that battlefield was mada. The arrogance of warriors, the conceit of victors, the feeling that "we are supreme."

From that subtle mada-energy, a demon was born — Madasura.

Madasura was no ordinary demon. He had no weapons, no army. His instrument was — his gaze. Whoever looked upon him became infected with mada. The gods who had just emerged victorious became even more intoxicated with pride from his gaze. Their conduct began to deteriorate. Disputes arose among them.

The gods prayed to Ganesha.

---

**The form of Ekadanta:**

Ganesha assumed the form of Ekadanta.

This form had manifested at the time when Parashurama — the supreme devotee of Lord Shiva, filled with the fierce spirit of a kshatriya — once came to Kailasha to meet his father Shiva. Ganesha was standing at the door. Lord Shiva was resting. Ganesha stopped Parashurama — "Father is in rest right now. Please wait a while."

Parashurama's kshatriya pride flared up. He did not stop. In his anger he hurled the axe given to him by Shiva.

Ganesha understood — this axe is a gift from Father. It would not be right to destroy it. He moved one of his tusks forward. The tusk broke. He remained unmoved.

This is Ekadanta — the one who bore the strike of anger upon himself, without being disturbed, without taking revenge.

---

**The end of Madasura:**

Ekadanta Ganesha appeared before Madasura.

Madasura cast his mada-gaze. But upon one who has nothing of his own to prove, mada cannot take hold. Ganesha had no arrogance about his own glory, no pride in his own power. He was calm, steady — complete even with a broken tusk.

Madasura's gaze slipped off Ganesha.

The demon grew alarmed. He assumed large and terrible forms, roared fierce roars — but Ekadanta's serene and steady demeanour did not change.

At last Madasura's mada broke. He fell at Ganesha's feet and said — "Lord, forgive me. I am destructive arrogance — but before you I have no strength."

Ganesha granted him abhaya. Madasura's destructive mada was stilled — it transformed into healthy confidence, not conceit.

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said that the devotee who worships Ekadanta on Ashwin Krishna Chaturthi is freed from the post-victory ego and the arrogance born of competition, and receives the boon of true steadiness.

## Significance

**The meaning of Ekadanta:**
Ekadanta = the one-tusked. That tusk which broke in bearing the strike of Parashurama's anger — and Ganesha did not consider this a deficiency but wore it as a mark. The Ekadanta form is a symbol of ego-free power.

**The connection between Ashwin and mada:**
Ashwin is the month of Navaratri and Dashehra. In this month, the enthusiasm for defeating enemies is at its height. But it is precisely after victory that the greatest danger of mada arises — "We won, therefore we are superior." This is why the Ashwin Sankashti vrata is especially relevant in this month.

**The meaning of Sankashti:**
Sankashti = the one who removes obstacles. The Krishna paksha Chaturthi is Ganesha's tithi. Fasting until moonrise and then breaking the fast after sighting the moon — this sequence makes the sadhaka's practice complete.

## Vidhi

**In the morning:**
1. Bathe before sunrise.
2. Take the sankalpa — "I observe the Sankashti vrata for Ekadanta Ganesha on Ashwin Krishna Chaturthi."
3. Fast throughout the day — ideally without water; those unable may take fruit-based food.

**In the evening — Ganesha puja:**
1. Install the image or picture of Ganesha.
2. Offer modak, durva, and red flowers.
3. Light a lamp.
4. Chant OM Gam Ganapataye Namah.
5. Meditate on Ekadanta Ganesha — that form which is unmoved, serene, and single-pointed.
6. Perform Ganesha aarti.

**At night — after moonrise:**
1. As soon as the moon is visible in the sky, offer naivedya and modak to Ganesha.
2. Offer arghya of water to the moon.
3. Receive prasad and break the fast.

## Observance

**Fast:** From sunrise to moonrise. The fast is not broken without sighting the moon — this is the binding rule of Sankashti.

**Parana:** After sighting the moon, offer bhoga to Ganesha, then receive prasad yourself.

**Bhoga:** Modak, durva, red flowers. Durva is most dear to Ganesha — it is best to gather and offer twenty-one or twenty-eight blades of durva.

**The special spirit of Ashwin:** Amidst the celebrations of Navaratri and Dashehra, this vrata is an occasion to examine the inner ego — the resolve to maintain inner humility alongside outward victory.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.
