## Katha

The month of Bhadrapada — the month of Ganesha Chaturthi. On Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi, millions of devotees install Ganesha, worship him, and celebrate in joy. But the Krishna Chaturthi of that same month — Sankashti — brings with it a deeper, more inward katha.

This is the katha of **Ahamkarasura**.

Ahamkarasura was born in a peculiar moment. Once, Brahma was looking upon creation — upon this vast expanse, this countless diversity. And at that moment, for just an instant, a thought arose within him — *"All this was created by me."*

That thought passed immediately. Brahma is a great jnani.

But a thought that arises even for a single moment does not depart without leaving a trace. From that one instant of ahamkara, a demon took form — **Ahamkarasura**.

He was subtle. He had no sword, no army. His weapon was — entry from within.

He entered the gods. Indra began to feel — *"My throne came to me through my own merit."* Varuna felt — *"I am the lord of water — without me creation cannot function."* Surya felt — *"My light is everything."*

Each deva began to consider himself the greatest. The assemblies filled with arguments. The arrangement by which duties were shared among the gods broke down.

He entered the sages too. Great yogis felt — *"My tapas is the highest."* Competition entered the ashrams, humility departed.

And in human beings Ahamkarasura entered most easily of all — for there he was already somewhat present. He simply inflated what was already there.

The most difficult thing was that no one knew they had been seized by a demon. This is the very nature of ego — it is invisible. The one most afflicted by it understands it least.

When the gods looked at one another, they understood that something was wrong. They went to Brahma.

Brahma said — *"This demon is the most subtle of all. He cannot be defeated by a weapon — for he is without form. The only one who can vanquish him is one who is himself beyond form and definition. Go — take refuge in Dhumravarna Ganesha."*

The gods came to Ganesha.

Ganesha listened. And he assumed a form no one had seen before — **Dhumravarna**. The colour of smoke — neither black, nor white, nor red. Indistinct. One who has no single clear definition.

Ahamkarasura came. He looked upon Dhumravarna.

And could not comprehend him.

Ego requires comparison to survive — *"I am greater than this one, higher than that one."* But when before you stands one whom you cannot measure, cannot define — the mechanism of ego simply stops working.

Dhumravarna Ganesha had neither one colour nor one fixed form. Again and again Ahamkarasura tried to surround him — but how does one surround smoke?

And then Ganesha did one thing — he **touched** him.

That touch was of formlessness — the touch of that state where all is one, where the boundaries of "I" and "you" dissolve.

Ahamkarasura disintegrated at that touch. When the very boundary of "I" ceases to exist, where can ego take hold?

The demon did not die — he dissolved back into that original humility which resides at the centre of every being.

Balance was restored to creation. The gods remembered that they are servants, not masters. The sages remembered that tapas increases humility, not ego.

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said that by hearing this katha of Dhumravarna Ganesha on Bhadrapada Sankashti Chaturthi, and by completing the fast after sighting the moon at moonrise, the tendencies of ego, arrogance, and false superiority are stilled. The devotee who observes this vrata with faith has the trials of life removed.

## Significance

**Sankashti** means — the one who removes obstacles. Sankat (crisis) + harana (removal) = **Sankataharana Chaturthi**. This vrata is observed on the Krishna Chaturthi of every month, and each month a particular form of Ganesha is worshipped.

**Dhumravarna** — smoke-coloured, indeterminate, close to formlessness. This is that form of Ganesha which teaches that Brahman cannot be contained within any definition. What is limitless cannot be grasped by a limited mind.

**The difference between Bhadrapada month and Ganesha Chaturthi:** Two Chaturthis fall in Bhadrapada — both are distinct.
- **Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi** — Ganesha Chaturthi (birth celebration), centred on festivity.
- **Bhadrapada Krishna Chaturthi** — Sankashti Chaturthi (Dhumravarna), centred on vrata.
Both fall in the same month, but the spirit and the tithi of each are different.

**The moonrise-parana rule:** The fast of Sankashti Chaturthi is broken only after sighting the moon. This rule is the distinctive mark of this vrata — parana without sighting the moon is not considered complete.

## Vidhi

- **Fasting:** Fast from sunrise to moonrise; no parana (fast-breaking) before sighting the moon.
- **Diet:** Abstain from grains; fruit-based food (fruits, milk, sabudana) may be taken once during the day.
- **At dawn:** Bathe and install the image or picture of Dhumravarna Ganesha.
- **Morning puja:** Perform panchopachara puja — water, fragrance, flowers, incense, lamp; offer 21 durva grass; offer modak or laddus as naivedya; hear the katha of Dhumravarna Ganesha.
- **Materials:** Modak or laddus, 21 durva grass, red flowers, sindura, ghee lamp.
- **Evening:** Check moonrise time for the tithi on the Atma Sanatan app; await moonrise.
- **Breaking the fast:** Upon sighting the moon, offer arghya with water and Ganesha's name, then take food.
- **Note:** On Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi (Ganesha Chaturthi) sighting the moon is prohibited; on this Sankashti (Krishna Chaturthi) sighting the moon is required — the rules of the two are different.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.

**Vighnashana shloka:**

> वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटिसमप्रभ ।
> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda, vast of form, radiant as a crore of suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.
