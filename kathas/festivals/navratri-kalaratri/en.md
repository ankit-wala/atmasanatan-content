## Katha

It is the seventh day of the Navratri journey — and this is the darkest night.

This is the story of that power — which comes when no other remedy remains.

---

Maa Durga had slain Mahishasura and defeated many demons. But the menace of the asuras had not ended.

Shumbha and Nishumbha — two immensely powerful demons — had seized dominion over all three worlds. Their generals were **Chanda and Munda**, and their most terrifying warrior was — **Raktabija**.

Raktabija carried the boon of Brahma — every time a drop of his blood fell upon the earth, a new Raktabija would be born. The gods hurled weapons and unleashed their arms — but the more fiercely they fought, the more Raktabijas arose. The battlefield became crowded with his copies.

The gods were shaken.

---

Then, from between the brows of Maa Durga — from her very frown — a form emerged.

**Maa Kalaratri.**

All three worlds trembled at the sight of her.

Her complexion was krishna — dark as the deepest night. Her hair wild and unbound. Three eyes blazing like balls of fire. A garland of skulls around her neck. Four arms — one holding a sword, one a barbed iron hook, one raised in abhaya mudra, one extended in varada mudra. She rode upon **a donkey** — the khara, whose slow and steady gait recognises the passage of time itself.

Her name is her definition — **Kala** meaning death and time, **Ratri** meaning that darkness into which all things dissolve.

---

Maa Kalaratri entered the battlefield.

Every time a drop of Raktabija's blood was about to fall — she spread her vast tongue across the earth. Not one drop could touch the ground. Every drop fell upon her tongue — and was swallowed.

No new Raktabijas arose on the battlefield.

Then the Goddess slew Raktabija.

Chanda and Munda also met their end in that same battle. The army of Shumbha and Nishumbha was shattered.

---

The form that appears most terrifying — is the most auspicious of all.

This is why Maa Kalaratri is called **"Shubhankari"** — she who bestows good. She destroys fear; she does not give it. Whoever takes refuge in her, all their fears are annihilated.

This seventh night of Navratri — the darkest night — carries this message: when the darkness is at its most profound, know that the Goddess is about to reveal that form which will swallow that darkness whole.

In the tradition of the Markandeya Purana, it is said that the devotee who worships Maa Kalaratri on Saptami — all their fears, all their enemies, and all their obstacles are destroyed, and the Sahasrara chakra is awakened.

## Significance

**Kala and Ratri — meaning in the name:**
Kalaratri means the night that is like death. Yet this same Kala ends all things and also opens the path to new creation. Maa Kalaratri is not the goddess of destruction — she is the goddess of purification.

**The Shubhankari paradox:**
Among the nine forms of Navratri, Maa Kalaratri's is the most fearsome in appearance — yet the most auspicious in result. This teaches that true compassion sometimes wears a fierce face.

**The Sahasrara chakra:**
Saptami of Navratri is associated with the Sahasrara chakra — the highest energy centre at the crown of the head. The sadhana of this day opens the door to the highest level of consciousness.

**Colour — blue and black:**
The colour of Saptami is **royal blue** or **black** — in some traditions, deep blue. This colour is the symbol of the sky and of infinity.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe and wear blue or black clothing — the colour of Saptami.
- **Puja:** Install an image or idol of Maa Kalaratri; light a lamp of mustard oil; offer **blue or red flowers**.
- **Offerings:** **Black sesame seeds** (kala til) and **jaggery** — the special naivedya of this day.
- **Recitation:** Recite the dhyana shloka of Maa Kalaratri; recite the seventh chapter of the Durga Saptashati.
- **Fasting:** Continue the Navratri fast on Saptami — fruit diet or a single meal. Those who cannot fast all nine days should observe a special fast on this day.
- **Colour:** Saptami colour is **blue** (deep blue / royal blue) — symbol of the infinite sky and divine power; **black** is also accepted in some traditions.
- **At night:** Ratri-jagaran — worship of Kalaratri is especially fruitful after dark; sing bhajans and kirtans and continue recitation of the Durga Saptashati.

## Mantras

**Dhyana Shloka:**

> एकवेणी जपाकर्णपूरा नग्ना खरास्थिता ।
> लम्बोष्ठी कर्णिकाकर्णी तैलाभ्यक्तशरीरिणी ॥
> वामपादोल्लसल्लोहलताकण्टकभूषणा ।
> वर्धनमूर्धध्वजा कृष्णा कालरात्रिर्भयङ्करी ॥

Ekaveṇī japākarṇapūrā nagnā kharāsthitā |
lamboṣṭhī karṇikākarṇī tailābhyaktaśarīriṇī ||
vāmapādollasallohalatākaṇṭakabhūṣaṇā |
vardhanamūrdhvadhvajā kṛṣṇā kālarātrirbhayaṅkarī ||

Meaning: "With her hair in a single braid, japa flowers at her ears, unrobed, mounted upon a donkey, thick-lipped, wearing karnikara flowers at her ears, her body anointed with oil, adorned with a barbed iron ornament at her left foot, a banner raised at her crown, dark in complexion — such is the fearsome Maa Kalaratri."

> नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः ॥

Yā devī sarvabhūteṣu māṃ kālarātri rūpeṇa saṃsthitā |
namastasyai namastasyai namastasyai namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: "To the Goddess who abides in all beings in the form of Maa Kalaratri — salutations, salutations, salutations."
