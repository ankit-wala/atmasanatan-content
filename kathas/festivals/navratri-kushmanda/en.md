## Katha

The fourth day of Navratri's journey — and today the story arrives at the oldest question of all.

How was this universe made?

---

At the very beginning of creation — when there was no sun, no earth, no living being — there was only **the void**. Profound darkness. Not a single ray of light, not a single wave of sound.

Brahma, Vishnu, Mahesh — all three were powerless. They did not yet exist within that void.

Then — in that infinite darkness — a stirring arose.

**Adishakti** awakened.

She was there in that void — before it, beyond it.

And she smiled.

That smile was no ordinary smile. It was **the first act of creation**.

From that smile a light was born — so intense, so blazing, as though millions of suns had ignited at once. From that luminous light an egg-shaped form took shape — **the universe's first egg**.

And from it, all of creation emerged.

This is why her name is **Kushmanda**.

'Ku' meaning little, 'ushma' meaning warmth and energy, 'anda' meaning the cosmic egg. She who gave birth to this vast universe through a mere fraction of her energy — she is Kushmanda.

---

She **resides within the solar sphere** — inside it, at its very centre.

The light and warmth the sun gives to all creation are an extension of her own power. The heat that would reduce anyone else to ash, she bears with ease.

She has **eight arms** — which is why she is also called **Ashtabhuja**. In those eight hands she holds: a water pot, a bow, an arrow, a lotus, an **amrit kalash**, a discus, a mace, and a rosary.

That amrit kalash is especially significant — she showers her devotees with nectar. She destroys disease, grief, and fear.

She rides a **lion** — the mount of radiance and valour.

---

On the fourth day of Navratri we worship that power who is the originator of creation — without whom there would be no sun, no world. Through the first to the third day we journeyed through the Goddess's earthly, lunar, and warrior forms. Today, on the fourth day, a deeper truth is revealed: this very Goddess — the same Shailputri, the same Brahmacharini, the same Chandraghanta — these are all **forms of that one Adishakti**, who smiled and made the universe.

In the tradition of the Devi Bhagavata Purana it is said that devotees who worship Maa Kushmanda with reverence on the fourth day of Navratri are freed from all disease and sorrow, their life, strength, and glory grow, and the **Anahata Chakra** awakens, opening the door of love and compassion.

## Significance

**The meaning of the name — the secret of creation:**
'Ku' = little, 'ushma' = warmth/energy/radiance, 'anda' = cosmic egg. She is the power who brought this entire universe into being through her gentle smile. She is the root cause of creation.

**Dweller of the solar sphere:**
Kushmanda resides within the sun. The sun is the source of light and life — its power comes from her. This is why the colour of Navratri's fourth day is **yellow** — the colour of sunlight, the colour of energy.

**Anahata Chakra:**
On the fourth day of Navratri, meditating on Maa Kushmanda awakens the **Anahata Chakra** (the heart chakra). This is the centre of love, compassion, and joy.

**Ashtabhuja and the amrit kalash:**
Her eight arms are a symbol of the expansion of power in all eight directions. The amrit kalash signifies that she grants her devotees immortality — that is, freedom from the fear of death.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe and wear **yellow garments** — the colour of the fourth day, symbol of the sun's radiance and cheerfulness.
- **Puja:** Install an image or idol of Maa Kushmanda; light a lamp of cow's ghee; recite Maa Kushmanda's dhyana shloka and the Ya Devi stotra.
- **Offerings:** Yellow flowers (marigolds or yellow roses); **pumpkin (kushmanda/kaddu)**; **honey** — a sweet offering especially dear to her.
- **Fasting:** The Navratri fast continues on the fourth day — fruit diet or a single meal; those observing the full nine-day fast keep the vrat on this day as well.
- **Anahata meditation:** During worship, rest attention at the heart centre — visualise the Goddess's smile and the amrit kalash she holds.
- **2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Ashwin Shukla Chaturthi — ~October 14, 2026).

## Mantras

**Dhyana Shloka:**

> सुरासम्पूर्णकलशं रुधिराप्लुतमेव च ।
> दधाना हस्तपद्माभ्यां कूष्माण्डा शुभदास्तु मे ॥

Surāsampūrṇakalaśaṃ rudhirāplutameva ca |
dadhānā hastapadmābhyāṃ kūṣmāṇḍā śubhadāstu me ||

Meaning: "She who holds in her lotus hands a vessel filled with nectar and a vessel brimming with blood — may Goddess Kushmanda grant me auspiciousness."

---

**Ya Devi Stotra:**

> या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ कूष्मांडा रूपेण संस्थिता ।
> नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः ॥

Yā devī sarvabhūteṣu māṃ kūṣmāṇḍā rūpeṇa saṃsthitā |
namastasyai namastasyai namastasyai namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: "To that Goddess who dwells in all beings in the form of Maa Kushmanda — salutations, salutations, salutations."
