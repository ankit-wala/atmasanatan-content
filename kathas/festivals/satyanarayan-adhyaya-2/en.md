## Katha

Kashi — the city of Vishwanath — where the Ganga flows and lamps burn along the ghats.

In that city there lived a poor **woodcutter**.

No one knew his name — yet the people around him knew of his honesty. Every day held the same work: rise at dawn, go to the forest, cut wood, sell it at the market. Whatever he earned, he used to feed his family. He did not think beyond this, nor did he ask for more.

There was poverty — but there was contentment.

---

One day he was returning from the forest with a bundle of wood on his back. Along the way, a puja was taking place in front of a house.

Banana-tree pillars adorned the courtyard. Lamps were burning. A pandit was reciting the katha — *"Satyanarayan Vrat Katha..."* His voice was melodious; a stillness filled the air.

The woodcutter set his bundle down. He folded his hands and stood there for a while.

The katha ended. The prasad was offered to the Lord.

The woodcutter was still there — he had no wish to leave.

The householder noticed him and asked — *"Come, take some prasad."*

The woodcutter said — *"I am an outsider; I was not part of your puja."*

The householder replied — *"In the Lord's prasad, there are no outsiders."*

The woodcutter extended both hands. Prasad was placed in them. He received it with reverence and ate.

---

That very night, Lord Satyanarayan appeared to him in a dream.

Clad in yellow silk, bearing the Panchajanya (his conch), his Sudarshan Chakra (the spinning disc of light), the Kaumodaki (his mace), and a lotus — a radiant form.

He said — *"You received my prasad with devotion. That is enough. Now tell me — what do you desire?"*

The woodcutter bowed his head and said — *"Lord, I do not want great wealth. Only this — let the wood continue to sell, let my family's hunger be met. For me, that is more than enough."*

The Lord smiled.

*"This is true feeling. He who does not ask for more, who lives in contentment — to him I give even more. Go. Your welfare shall come."*

---

The next day arrived.

The woodcutter went to the forest, cut his wood, went to the market.

What he received that day was far more than before. The traders gave him a good price. When he returned home, his wife cooked a good meal.

Days passed, months passed.

One day he was in the same forest where he went every day. As he was cutting wood, his axe struck an old root. When he dug into the earth, an ancient pot emerged — it held gold coins.

The woodcutter was shaken. He covered the pot where it lay and went straight to the royal court. He informed the king.

Seeing his honesty, the king said — *"Half belongs to the treasury, half is yours."*

The woodcutter bowed and said — *"Your Majesty, give me only what is fitting. I have no greed."*

The king gave him a worthy reward — wealth, garments, and honour.

---

The first thing the woodcutter did with his new prosperity was this — he held a Satyanarayan puja in his home. He invited his family, his neighbours, and the people of the village. The katha was performed. Prasad was distributed.

The tradition of the Skanda Purana's Reva Khanda says — those devotees who hear the Satyanarayan Katha with reverence and receive the prasad — Lord Satyanarayan fulfils all their wishes.

## Significance

The story of the second chapter is a lesson in contentment and devotion.

The woodcutter had observed no vrat; he had not organised any puja. He had only received the prasad with reverence — and the Lord saw him.

"Satyanarayan" — the meeting of 'Satya' (truth) and 'Narayan' (the Lord). One who takes refuge in truth, who lives in contentment — upon that person the Lord's gaze falls.

The central message of this katha is this — **he who asks for little is given more by the Lord.** Not greed, but devotion — this is the soul of this vrat.

The katha also teaches that honouring prasad is itself an act of devotion. Whoever comes to a Satyanarayan puja should receive prasad. Let no one leave without it — this is the tradition of this puja.

## Vidhi

- **When:** Any Purnima, Ekadashi, or auspicious occasion.
- **Sankalpa:** Bathe in the morning and make the resolve — "I am observing the vrat and puja of Lord Satyanarayan."
- **Fasting:** Keep the fast before the katha — fruits may be consumed; break the fast only after the katha and prasad.
- **Setup:** Place an image or idol of Lord Satyanarayan (Vishnu) on a pure seat; perform panchamrit abhishek (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar); offer tulsi leaves, flowers, yellow cloth, and a lamp.
- **Katha shravan:** Recite or have recited all five chapters — the family and those present should listen together in a single sitting.
- **Aarti:** Perform the Lord's aarti after the katha.
- **Prasad:** Prepare panjiri or sheera (wheat flour, ghee, sugar, banana); give prasad to every person present — no one should leave without it; leaving or refusing prasad is inauspicious.
- **Paran:** Break the fast after receiving prasad.

## Mantras

**Vasudeva Mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya

Meaning: "Salutation to Lord Vasudeva." — This is the twelve-syllable mantra of Vishnu, chiefly recited during Satyanarayan puja.

**Satyanarayan Dhyana Shloka:**

> सत्यं ज्ञानमनन्तं ब्रह्म सच्चिदानन्दरूपिणे ।
> नित्यानन्दाय शान्ताय सत्यनारायणाय नमः ॥

Satyaṃ jñānamanantaṃ brahma saccidānandarūpiṇe |
nityānandāya śāntāya satyanārāyaṇāya namaḥ ||

Meaning: "To the one who is Truth, Knowledge, and infinite Brahman — of the form of Sat-Chit-Ananda, eternal bliss, and peace — salutation to Satyanarayan."
