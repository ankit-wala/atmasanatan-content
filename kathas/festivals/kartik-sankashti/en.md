## Katha

**Sharada's prayer:**

In a certain town there lived a devotee woman named Sharada.

For years she had observed the Sankashti Chaturthi vrata every month — without a single lapse. Without complaint. Ganesha was the deity of her home — the first salutation of the morning was to him, the last worship of the night was to him.

But there was one thing that gnawed at her heart.

Her household was not in any dramatic crisis. There was no enemy, no grave illness, no terrible calamity. Yet a certain friction-fatigue lived in the house. Small misunderstandings that arose without cause. Financial worries that no sooner were resolved than another appeared. The children's health occasionally faltered. And an unspoken sadness — as though there was light in the house, but it had grown a little dim.

There were no great demons — only the small everyday darknesses.

---

That year Kartik Krishna Chaturthi arrived. The excitement of Deepavali had not yet settled. There was still the fragrance of diyas in the house.

Sharada arranged the puja thali. She placed modak. She placed durva. She lit the lamp.

She sat before Ganesha. And that night — for the first time in years — she asked for nothing.

She simply said —

*"Ganapati, today I am not asking you to remove any demon. I am not seeking any miracle. Just this — come and sit in my home tonight. Stay near."*

---

**The darshana of Saumya Ganesha:**

That night Sharada had a dream.

She saw — in the courtyard of her home, in the cool moonlight, a supremely serene form was seated. Ganesha — but not in any battle-stance. No weapons in the arms, no fury in the brow. He sat — calm, saumya, smiling. As though an old friend had come and settled in the courtyard.

He said nothing.

There was only his presence — and in that presence was a peace Sharada had not experienced in years.

---

When Sharada arose in the morning, something felt changed in the house.

The same house. The same family. But the small misunderstandings that had existed — their solutions began suggesting themselves on their own, as though someone had granted the right perspective. The tangled threads of financial worry found in her a will to unravel them, and a path appeared. The small distance that had grown between her and the children began to melt.

No miracle had occurred.

Only the feeling of Saumya Ganesha's presence had come into the home — and that feeling had changed the quality of the house's atmosphere.

Sharada understood — not every problem contains a demon. Sometimes what is needed is not battle — it is a calm, compassionate presence.

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said that devotees who worship Saumya Ganesha with faith on Kartik Krishna Chaturthi are freed from the friction-fatigue of household life, joy and harmony flow through the home, and simple solutions to the daily small crises present themselves.

## Significance

**The spirit of Saumya Ganesha:**
Saumya = calm, accessible, gentle, favourable. Ganesha has many forms — some martial, some fierce. The Saumya form is the one that reassures the frightened, stills the distressed, and gives rest to the weary. In Kartik month, when the festival of Deepavali ends and life returns to its daily rhythm, the remembrance of Saumya Ganesha is especially relevant.

**The coincidence of Karva Chauth:**
Kartik Krishna Chaturthi is the same tithi as Karva Chauth. On Karva Chauth, married women observe a waterless fast for the longevity of their husbands. Sankashti Chaturthi adds to that same day a dimension of Ganesha worship — the prayer to remove the obstacles of household life. This confluence of two vratas gives household life strength from two directions — the longevity of husband and wife, and the harmony of the family.

## Vidhi

**In the morning:**
1. Bathe before sunrise.
2. Take the sankalpa — "I observe the Sankashti vrata for Saumya Ganesha on Kartik Krishna Chaturthi."
3. Fast throughout the day — ideally without water; those unable may take fruit-based food.

**In the evening — Ganesha puja:**
1. Install the image or picture of Ganesha.
2. Offer modak, durva, and white or yellow flowers — white and yellow colours are appropriate for the Saumya form.
3. Light a lamp.
4. Chant OM Gam Ganapataye Namah.
5. Meditate on Saumya Ganesha — calm, smiling, in the posture of blessing.
6. Perform Ganesha aarti.

**At night — after moonrise:**
1. As soon as the moon is visible in the sky, offer naivedya to Ganesha.
2. Offer arghya of water to the moon.
3. Receive prasad and break the fast.

**Combination with Karva Chauth:** If Karva Chauth also falls on this day, in the evening's puja first perform Ganesha worship, then follow the Karva Chauth vidhi — Ganesha removes the obstacles of household life and makes the Karva Chauth sadhana all the more complete.

**2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang — Kartik Krishna Chaturthi.

## Observance

**Fast:** From sunrise to moonrise. The fast is not broken without sighting the moon — this is the binding rule of Sankashti.

**Parana:** After sighting the moon, offer bhoga to Ganesha, then receive prasad yourself.

**Bhoga:** Modak, durva, and seasonal fruits. Modak is most dear to Ganesha — making it at home is especially auspicious.

**For those observing Karva Chauth as well:** Women who are keeping the waterless fast of Karva Chauth — for them this fast automatically fulfils both vratas together, as both have their parana after sighting the moon.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.

---

**Vighnashana shloka:**

> वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटिसमप्रभ ।
> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda, vast of form, radiant as a crore of suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.
