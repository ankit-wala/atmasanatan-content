## Katha

The month of Vaishakha — the month of new beginnings. The auspiciousness of Akshaya Tritiya, the time to sow new seeds, the first line of hope in the farmer's field. In this same Vaishakha there is a katha of Ekadanta Ganesha that tells us what svadharma is — and what the price of fulfilling it can be.

On that day Lord Shiva was in deep samadhi on Kailasha.

**Ganesha** stood at the door — in the form of a gatekeeper. This was his duty: that no disturbance come to his father's sadhana.

Then **Parashurama** arrived.

Parashurama — the sixth avatar of Vishnu, the mahaguru of the science of weapons, the destroyer of kshatriyas. On his shoulder was that famous axe which Lord Shiva himself had given him. His nature was — swift, fearless, and one who could not tolerate any obstacle.

Parashurama knocked at the door. He said — *"I must meet Shiva."*

Ganesha joined his hands respectfully — *"Respected one, Father is in deep samadhi right now. His sadhana must not be disturbed. Please wait a while."*

Parashurama would not accept waiting. His nature was just such — he had many times freed the earth from kshatriyas; no one could stop him.

He raised the axe.

Ganesha recognised the axe. This was no ordinary weapon — it had been given by his own father. The weapon given by one's father — to block it, to dodge it, to sever it with his own power — this would be a disrespect to his father.

For one moment, the deep understanding that only Ganesha possessed descended within him.

He neither blocked the axe nor stepped back. He moved his **right tusk** forward.

The blade of the axe struck the tusk — and the tusk broke.

On Kailasha, Mata Parvati's anger blazed up. She came toward Parashurama — her form was fearsome. She said — *"You raised a weapon against my son? I will curse you."*

Then **Ganesha** stopped his mother.

He said — *"Mother, be calm. This axe belonged to Father — it was the weapon given by him. I will not stop it with any power whatsoever. My dharma was to protect his sadhana — and I did. The axe was allowed to fulfil its dharma as well."*

Parashurama stopped.

The understanding of what had occurred came to him in an instant. The Ganesha he had thought was an obstruction — was no obstruction at all. He was a son, protecting his father, and doing so in such a way that his father's weapon suffered no dishonour.

Parashurama bowed at his feet.

Ganesha lifted him up and said — *"Parashurama ji, you have lived both the dharma of a brahman and the dharma of a kshatriya — in both there is the power of svadharma. Today both of us have fulfilled our respective dharma."*

The broken tusk — this was no misfortune. It became the symbol of adherence to svadharma. That is why this form came to be called — **Ekadanta**.

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said that by hearing this katha of Ekadanta Ganesha on Vaishakha Sankashti Chaturthi and completing the fast at moonrise, the obstacles arising in new undertakings are removed. The devotee who, remaining firm on the path of dharma, performs this vrata receives the grace of Ekadanta.

## Significance

**Sankashti** means — the one who removes obstacles. This vrata is observed on the Krishna Chaturthi of every month, and each month a particular form of Ganesha is worshipped.

**Ekadanta** — this is that form of Ganesha in which one tusk is whole and the other is broken. This broken tusk is no deficiency — it is the symbol of the truth that in fulfilling svadharma, sometimes something must be given up. What was lost was external; what remained was of the soul.

**The context of Vaishakha:** Vaishakha is the month of new beginnings — Akshaya Tritiya, Buddha Purnima, Parashurama Jayanti. Farmers prepare for the new crop, students enter a new academic year, merchants begin new ventures. In this month the message of Ekadanta is especially relevant — whatever work you begin, do it considering it your dharma, whatever the cost.

**The moonrise-parana rule:** The fast of Sankashti Chaturthi is broken only after sighting the moon. This rule is the distinctive mark of this vrata — parana without sighting the moon is not considered complete.

## Vidhi

- **Fasting:** From sunrise to moonrise; abstain from grains — fruit, milk, and sabudana may be taken once; no parana before sighting the moon.
- **Morning puja:** Bathe and install the image or picture of Ekadanta Ganesha; perform panchopachara puja — water, fragrance, flowers, incense, lamp.
- **Offerings:** Offer 21 (or 3) durva grass; offer red flowers; apply sindoor; offer modak or laddus as bhoga.
- **Katha:** Hear the katha of Ekadanta Ganesha during puja.
- **Evening:** Await moonrise; check the moonrise time for the tithi on the Atma Sanatan app — that is the parana time.
- **Parana:** Upon sighting the moon, offer arghya with water in Ganesha's name; then break the fast with food.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.

> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda, vast of form, radiant as a crore of suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.
