## Katha

In certain years, two mahashaktis are present on the same tithi of Jyeshtha Shukla Ekadashi.

The first is the shakti of Nirjala Ekadashi — that tithi on which the Ekadashi-shakti of Bhagavan Vishnu is at its peak, when Maharshi Vedavyasa told Bhima that this one day of waterless fasting yields the complete merit of all twenty-four Ekadashis of the year. (For the detailed katha, see: *Nirjala Ekadashi — Bhima's Waterless Austerity*.)

The second is the shakti of Gayatri Jayanti — that tithi on which, at Brahma's great yajna, Mother Gayatri Devi first took manifest form in this creation as a radiant brahmin maiden — and from her lips, the divine sound of the Gayatri Mantra resounded for the first time. (For the detailed katha, see: *Gayatri Jayanti — The Manifestation of the Mother of the Vedas*.)

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana, a particular interpretation of this confluence is found.

There was a brahmana named Kaushika — each year he observed Nirjala Ekadashi, yet he could not find stability in mantra-sadhana. When he sat for japa, his mind would wander. On the day of the fast, his body was in one place and his mind in another.

One year, when Nirjala Ekadashi and Gayatri Jayanti fell on the same tithi, Kaushika performed both sadhanas together — the waterless fast and 108 malas of Gayatri japa.

By evening, what he experienced had never happened before. The fast had stilled the body, and in that stilled body the Gayatri Mantra descended as if without obstruction. The mind did not wander; fatigue did not interrupt the japa. He was not doing the japa — the japa was happening.

This is the special quality of this tithi. The waterless fast is the surrender of body and ego — tapas. Gayatri japa is the invocation of divine intelligence — jnana. On the day when both occur together, that day becomes an opportunity for both tapas and mantra-siddhi at once.

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana, it is said that in a year when Nirjala Ekadashi and Gayatri Jayanti fall on the same tithi, a sadhaka who observes the waterless fast with devotion and performs Gayatri japa may receive in a single day the benefit that would otherwise come separately from both sadhanas over many years. This is traditional belief — the fruit is in the hands of God, the faith is in the hands of the sadhaka.

## Significance

This day is the confluence of two streams.

**Nirjala Ekadashi** is the supreme expression of Vishnu-bhakti — the complete surrender of the body. Water is life itself; to renounce water is the most severe renunciation. That Bhima, a man of such immense strength, observed this fast in the fierce heat of Jyeshtha — this was not merely fasting, it was the dissolution of ego.

**Gayatri Jayanti** is the celebration of jnana-shakti — the awakening of the intellect. The Gayatri Mantra is the light at the very source of the Rig Veda, which Maharshi Vishvamitra received at the summit of tapas, which is the first initiation of the Upanayana samskara.

When both fall on the same tithi, the message is this: **tapas and jnana are not separate.** When the body bows in the fast, the intellect opens. To receive Gayatri in complete absorption, the mind must be stilled — and the waterless fast bestows that stillness.

This confluence does not come every year. In the year it comes, that day is an opportunity for special sadhana.

## Vidhi

This day is the confluence of the procedures of two entries. See both complete procedures in their respective entries.

**Condensed confluence procedure:**

- On Dashami, eat sattvic food. After sunset, give up food and water.
- On Ekadashi morning: bathe, wear clean clothes, take the sankalpa — "Today I shall observe Nirjala Ekadashi vrata and Gayatri Jayanti upasana together."
- At sunrise, face east, offer arghya, and begin japa of the Gayatri Mantra.
- Remain without water throughout the day. Use a rudraksha or sphatika mala for japa.
- Minimum 108 malas of Gayatri japa. Do more if possible.
- Worship of Bhagavan Vishnu — tulasi, yellow flowers, the sankalpa-mantra for Nirjala Ekadashi.
- Install a picture or yantra of Mother Gayatri — yellow flowers, yellow sandalwood.
- Night vigil with bhajana and kirtana.
- Break the fast on Dvadashi after sunrise.

## Observance

**Waterless fast:** From sunset on Dashami until the parana on Dvadashi — neither food nor water. This is the essential condition of this day.

**Gayatri japa:** Begin at sunrise. Minimum 108 malas. With a calm, focused mind — not too loud, not in complete silence.

**The sequence of fast and japa:** Because of the fast, the body will remain still. Channel that stillness into japa — this is the secret of this confluence.

**Jal-dan:** One who does not drink water oneself and yet gives cool water to others — their merit is considered greater even than the fast. On this day, donating water at a pyau or giving matkas is considered especially meritorious.

**For those who cannot observe the complete waterless fast:** Do japa of the Gayatri Mantra with fruit and water at minimum. Remembrance of Bhagavan Vishnu and listening to the katha are sufficient for earning merit.

## Mantras

**1. Gayatri Mantra**

> ॐ भूर्भुवः स्वः ।
> तत् सवितुर्वरेण्यं
> भर्गो देवस्य धीमहि ।
> धियो यो नः प्रचोदयात् ॥

oṃ bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ |
tat savitur vareṇyaṃ
bhargo devasya dhīmahi |
dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt ||

Meaning: O Savitri Deva, who pervades all three worlds — Bhu, Bhuvah, and Svah — we meditate upon your supreme divine radiance. May that radiance impel and illuminate our intellects.

---

**2. Nirjala Ekadashi Sankalpa Mantra**

> एकादश्यां निराहारः स्थित्वाहं सुरसत्तम ।
> भोक्ष्यामि पुण्डरीकाक्ष शरणं मे भवाच्युत ॥

ekādaśyāṃ nirāhāraḥ sthitvāhaṃ surasattama |
bhokṣyāmi puṇḍarīkākṣa śaraṇaṃ me bhavācyuta ||

Meaning: O Pundarikaksha, O Achyuta — remaining without food on Ekadashi, I take refuge in you. Protect me.
