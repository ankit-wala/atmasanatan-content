## Katha

Dharmaraja Yudhishthira once asked Lord Shri Krishna — "Madhava, what is the name of the Ekadashi that falls on Ashwin Shukla Paksha? What fruit does one receive by observing it?"

The Lord replied — "O King, this Ekadashi is called **Papankusha Ekadashi**. 'Papa' means sin, and 'ankusha' means a goad — together, the name means that which checks sin at its root, that which holds it back entirely. There is a wondrous story about this Ekadashi in the Brahma Vaivarta Purana. Listen."

---

Long ago, in the forested regions of the Vindhyachal hills, there lived a hunter named **Krodhana**. His life was steeped in cruelty. Each day he slaughtered living creatures, snared innocent animals in his traps, and caused suffering to all who crossed his path. Lying, speaking harshly, doing harm to others — this was the fabric of his daily existence.

He grew old, carrying the weight of a lifetime of sin.

Then came the day of Ashwin Shukla Ekadashi.

That morning, Krodhana went into the forest to hunt. But not a single creature came his way. Midday passed, and then evening — he wandered through the forest, hungry and thirsty, with nothing to show for his efforts. The exhaustion finally overtook him and he collapsed unconscious beneath a tree. He had not even touched water that day — not from devotion, but from helplessness. And so, without knowing it, he had kept a waterless fast on that day.

That night, a wandering sadhu stopped near that same tree. He was chanting the name of Lord Vishnu — *"Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya."* The name fell again and again into the ears of the unconscious Krodhana.

---

A few days later, Krodhana died.

The messengers of Yamaraja came to claim his soul. But at that very moment, the attendants of Lord Vishnu — the **Vishnudutas** — also appeared. Their forms were radiant, clad in yellow silk, bearing the Panchajanya (conch) and Sudarshan Chakra (discus) in their hands.

The messengers of Yama said — "This soul is ours to take. He has sinned throughout his life. These are the orders of Yamaraja."

The Vishnudutas replied — "Wait. This man observed a waterless fast on Ashwin Shukla Ekadashi. He did so under compulsion, not by choice — and yet that fast is a true fast. That night, he heard the name of the Lord. Whoever goes without food on Ekadashi — knowingly or unknowingly — becomes an object of Lord Vishnu's grace."

The messengers of Yama hesitated. The argument grew.

In the end, the reasoning of the Vishnudutas stood unshaken. The compassion of the Lord is boundless — he does not let even a single moment of merit in a sinner go to waste.

Krodhana was not sent to Yamaloka. He was carried instead toward Vaikuntha.

---

The tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana declares — "The fast of Papankusha Ekadashi destroys the sins accumulated across many lifetimes. One who observes this fast with faith receives merit equal to the Ashwamedha Yajna. And even one who goes hungry and thirsty on this day unknowingly — the Lord protects that soul too."

## Significance

**The meaning of 'Papankusha':**
In Sanskrit, 'ankusha' refers to the sharp iron goad used to control an elephant. 'Papankusha' therefore means that which exercises the same control over sin — that which checks it, reins it in, and brings it to a halt. This Ekadashi not only prevents sins from multiplying but also destroys what has already accumulated.

**Known and unknown sin:**
The special message of this Ekadashi is that the compassion of the Lord is greater than any rule. Krodhana did not keep the fast intentionally — and yet he received its fruit. This is a door of hope for all who have sinned out of ignorance or helplessness.

**The importance of giving:**
On this day, feeding the hungry, gifting clothing, and donating grain are considered especially meritorious. Those who satisfy another's hunger — the Lord, in this life and the next, satisfies theirs.

**The dialogue of the Vishnudutas and the Yamadutas:**
This story also teaches that not a single moment of merit is ever destroyed. The messengers of the Lord stand guard over it.

## Vidhi

- **Dashami evening:** Eat only one sattvic meal; nothing after nightfall.
- **At dawn:** Rise during Brahma Muhurta, bathe, and take the vow of the fast.
- **Puja:** Establish an image of Lord Vishnu or a Shaligrama; offer Panchamrita abhisheka, tulsi leaves, yellow flowers, and a lamp; listen to or read the Papankusha Ekadashi katha.
- **Fasting:** The fast runs from the night of Dashami through the parana on Dwadashi. A waterless fast is the highest form; those who cannot manage that should observe a fruit-based fast. Rice is prohibited on Ekadashi.
- **What to avoid:** Anger, falsehood, and harsh speech are to be especially avoided on this day.
- **Evening puja:** Perform Vishnu puja again; offer food or clothing to the poor — feeding the hungry on this day yields exceptional merit.
- **At night:** Keep vigil with Hari kirtan, bhajans, and recitation of Vishnu Sahasranama.
- **Breaking the fast:** On Dwadashi morning, before Rahu Kala — break the fast with tulsi water, fruit, and light sattvic food.

## Mantras

**Vishnu Dwadashakshari Mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya

Meaning: "Salutations to Lord Vasudeva." — The root mantra of the Ekadashi fast.
