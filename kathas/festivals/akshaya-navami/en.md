## Katha

Once, the divine sage Narada visited Vaikuntha.

Lord Vishnu was seated upon Shesha Naga. Lakshmi sat near his feet. Narada offered his salutations and took his place beside the Lord.

After a moment of silence, Narada asked — "Lord, you always reside in Vaikuntha. But does it ever happen that you dwell in a particular place on earth? Is there a moment when you are closest to human beings?"

Lord Vishnu smiled.

He said — "Yes, Narada. There is."

---

"In the month of Kartik — and especially on the Navami of the bright fortnight — I dwell on earth within a tree."

Narada asked in wonder — "Which tree? And why that tree?"

The Lord said — "The amla tree — which is called dhātri."

"In an amla tree?" Narada repeated, astonished. "But Lord, what is so special about this tree?"

---

Lord Vishnu replied —

"Narada, this is no ordinary tree. It arose from the tears of Lord Brahma — shed when he was overwhelmed with emotion after completing the creation of the universe. That is why it is called 'dhātrī' — meaning the very form of the Earth-Mother."

"In its roots, its bark, its leaves, its fruit — there is medicine everywhere. It is close to amrita. It is a symbol of truth. In it there is no rajas or tamas — only sattva. That is why it is exceedingly dear to me."

"And Kartik is the month when all of creation — the moving and the still — comes closest to me. In Kartik, tulasi, amla, and Ganga water — these three are all dear to me. On the Navami of this month, I myself take up residence within the amla tree."

---

Narada folded his hands and asked — "Then what should human beings do on this tithi?"

The Lord said —

"Whoever circumambulates the amla tree on this day, offers water at its roots, and presents flowers and akshat — receives the merit of visiting every pilgrimage: Kashi, Prayag, Dwarka, Mathura — all of them. Because when I am in that tree, the tree itself becomes a tirtha."

"And whoever sits beneath that tree and takes a meal — the merit of that act will never be exhausted."

Narada asked — "Why is it called 'Akshaya' Navami?"

The Lord smiled — "Because every act performed on this tithi — worship, charity, circumambulation, fasting — is akshaya: imperishable. Just as the virtue of the amla never decays, so the merit of this day cannot be diminished. No power can cause it to perish."

---

The Skanda Purana relates that upon hearing this knowledge, Narada descended to earth and declared the glory of this vrat to humankind.

Since then, every year on Kartik Shukla Navami — men and women, householders and renunciants — gather before the amla tree. They circumambulate it. They sit beneath it and share a meal. And they experience the invisible yet living presence of Vishnu dwelling within.

## Significance

Akshaya Navami is the tithi of **the meeting of nature and the Divine**.

**The meaning of "Akshaya"** — that which never perishes. The merit earned on this tithi — any act, any charity, any worship — is akshaya: inexhaustible and imperishable. This is why, like Vaishakh Shukla Tritiya (Akshaya Tritiya), this Navami is also regarded as an "akshaya tithi."

**The amla tree — dhātrī vriksha:** In Ayurveda, the amla (āmalakī) is called "amrita-phala" — the nectar-fruit. It is the richest natural source of vitamin C. The Charaka Samhita describes amla as a rasāyana — a rejuvenator of the body. Medicinally, from root to fruit, this tree is a complete remedy.

**The special significance of Kartik:** The month of Kartik is supremely dear to Vishnu. In this month, Tulasi Vivah (Devuthani Ekadashi), Kartik snan, and amla worship together form a single spiritual chain. Akshaya Navami is an essential link in that chain.

**Women's tradition:** Women in particular observe a fast on this day. Worshipping the amla tree and eating a meal beneath it for the prosperity of the family and the well-being of children is the heart of this festival.

## Vidhi

- **Morning bath and sankalpa:** On Kartik Shukla Navami, rise before sunrise; bathe, put on clean clothes, and take the resolve — "Today, on Akshaya Navami, I worship Lord Vishnu and the dhātrī tree."
- **Worship of the amla tree:** Go to the amla tree; offer water at its roots; apply roli and akshat to the trunk; offer flowers, a lamp, and incense.
- **Three circumambulations:** Circumambulate the amla tree three times, reciting the dhātrī mantra each round; you may also tie a sacred thread around the tree while circling.
- **Remembrance of Vishnu:** Standing before the tree, chant "Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya" — holding in your heart the awareness that Lord Vishnu is present within the tree.
- **Fasting:** Many women observe a fast on this day — ekāhāra (eating once) or falāhāra (fruit only) is appropriate.
- **Meal beneath the tree:** Sit beneath the amla tree and take a meal together as a family — this is the most important observance; include amla (murabba, chutney, or pickle). This meal serves as the pāraṇa for those fasting.
- **Charity:** Offer a meal to a brahmin, clothing or grain to the poor — charity given on this akshaya tithi bears inexhaustible fruit; include amla in the gift.
- **Note:** Kartik Shukla Navami falls two days before Devuthani Ekadashi — the ongoing Kartik snan further multiplies the merit of this festival.

## Mantras

**Dhātri (amla) worship mantra:**

> धात्री त्वमसि संभूता ब्रह्मणस्त्वं महामते ।
> लोकमातरसम्प्राप्ता विष्णुना सम्प्रपूजिता ॥

Dhātrī tvamasi saṃbhūtā brahmaṇastvaṃ mahāmate |
lokamātarasamprāptā viṣṇunā samprapūjitā ||

Meaning: O greatly wise dhātrī, you were born of Lord Brahma. You are the mother of all worlds. You are worshipped by Lord Vishnu — we bow to you.

*(Recite this mantra while circumambulating and while offering water to the tree.)*

---

**Mantra for remembrance of Lord Vishnu:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya

Meaning: Salutations to Lord Vasudeva (Vishnu).

*(Chant this mantra while circumambulating and before the meal.)*
