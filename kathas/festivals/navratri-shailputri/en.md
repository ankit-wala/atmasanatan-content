## Katha

The first day of Navratri's journey — and the first step is **Maa Shailputri**.

This is the story of a woman — one who could give everything for love, and did.

---

In the beginning of creation, there was a daughter of Prajapati Daksha — **Sati**.

From childhood, Sati was a devoted worshipper of Lord Shiva. She performed intense tapas — all to attain Shiva. And Shiva accepted her. That marriage became the talk of all three worlds.

But Daksha never accepted this union. Shiva was not the kind of deity that met his standards — Jatajoota-bearing, dwelling in cremation grounds, lord of spirits and ghosts.

Once, Daksha organised a grand yajna. Invitations were sent to all the gods — but not to Shiva.

Sati resolved to go.

Shiva counselled her — "It is not proper to go somewhere without an invitation."

But Sati said — "He is my father."

She went.

---

The moment Sati arrived at the yajna grounds, she understood.

Daksha humiliated her husband — publicly, grievously.

Sati stood there — but she was shattered within.

She thought — "This body that came from Daksha's house, this body that became witness to the insult of Shiva — this body is not worth keeping."

And she ended her life in the fire of yogic consciousness.

---

Shiva's grief — that sight shook all three worlds.

He wandered, carrying Sati's body. Vishnu divided her body with his Sudarshana Chakra — and those pieces became the 51 Shakti Peethas spread across India.

But Sati had not ended her story.

She was born again — as the daughter of **Himalaya** — **Shailputri**.

Mounted on a bull, trident in her right hand, lotus in her left.

The daughter who awaited Shiva — now born as the mountain's own child — beginning anew.

The pratipada tithi of Navratri is the remembrance of that very rebirth.

In the tradition of Devi Bhagavata Purana, it is said that the devotee who worships Maa Shailputri on the first day of Navratri attains the power of the Muladhara chakra, and stability enters their life.

## Significance

**Nine days — nine powers:**
The nine days of Navratri are a journey through the nine forms of Maa Durga. Shailputri is the beginning of that journey — earth element, stability, Muladhara. Just as a mountain stands unshaken, this shakti gives life its foundation.

**The connection of Sati and Shailputri:**
Shailputri is Sati's rebirth — a reminder that the soul is immortal, love is immortal. Sati's sacrifice was not in vain — it became a new beginning.

**Shakti Peethas:**
Wherever the pieces of Sati's body fell, a Shakti Peetha was formed — 51 peethas, at every corner of India.

## Vidhi

1. Bathe early in the morning and wear red garments — the colour of Pratipada.
2. Establish an image or idol of Maa Shailputri.
3. Offer **red flowers** — hibiscus is especially auspicious.
4. Light a lamp of cow's ghee.
5. Offer tulsi and bel leaves.
6. Recite the dhyana shloka of Maa Shailputri.
7. **Navratri Kalash Sthapana** — the kalash is established on Pratipada (for the nine days).

## Observance

**Navratri Vrat:** The nine-day fast begins from Pratipada — fruit diet or one meal a day.

**Colour:** The colour of Pratipada is **red** — symbol of power, courage, and energy.

**2026 Date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Ashwin Shukla Pratipada — ~October 11, 2026).

## Mantras

**Dhyana Shloka:**

> वन्दे वाञ्छितलाभाय चन्द्रार्धकृतशेखराम् ।
> वृषारूढां शूलधरां शैलपुत्रीं यशस्विनीम् ॥

Vande vāñchitalābhāya candrārdhakṛtaśekharām |
vṛṣārūḍhāṃ śūladharāṃ śailaputrīṃ yaśasvinīm ||

Meaning: "I bow to the illustrious Shailputri — she who grants desired fruits, who wears the crescent moon as her crown, who rides the bull and bears the trident."

---

**Ya Devi Stuti:**

> या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ शैलपुत्री रूपेण संस्थिता ।
> नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः ॥

Yā devī sarvabhūteṣu māṃ śailaputrī rūpeṇa saṃsthitā |
namastasyai namastasyai namastasyai namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: "To the goddess who abides in all beings as Maa Shailputri — salutations, salutations, salutations."
