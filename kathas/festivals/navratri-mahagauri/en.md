## Katha

Ashtami of Navratri — the dawn after the fearsome night of Kalaratri, when the darkness parts and a white light spreads across the sky. On Saptami, the fierce, tamo-guna-charged power of Maa Kalaratri was worshipped; on Ashtami, that same mother appears before us in her supremely peaceful, supremely radiant form — Mahagauri.

Parvati performed years of intense tapasya to win Shiva as her husband. She sat in forests and on mountains, under the open sky, immersed in meditation. The scorching heat of summer, the torrential rains of the monsoon, and the bitter cold of winter — all three seasons tested her body again and again. She never wavered. She had taken one single vow — Shiva, or nothing. The austerities were so severe that years of sun and rain gradually darkened her fair complexion. Limbs that had once shone like moonlight slowly grew dark and dull in appearance.

One day, Bhagavan Shiva himself appeared before her. He saw that the Goddess's body had been darkened by the fire of tapasya. Shiva's heart was filled with compassion. He took the Ganga water from his own matted locks — that same Ganga who dwells upon his own head, who destroys all sins — and bathed Parvati with it.

The moment the stream of Ganga water touched her limbs, a wondrous transformation occurred. The dark complexion fell away as if it were a mere covering. What emerged from beneath was — supremely white, supremely radiant. Whiter than a jasmine flower, more luminous than the full moon. Shiva looked at her and said — "From this day, you are Mahagauri."

Mahagauri — the great and the supremely fair. Her entire form is white. White garments, white flowers, mounted upon a white bull. Of her four hands, two hold a trident and a damaru; the other two are in abhaya mudra and varada mudra — one that removes fear, one that grants boons. Her countenance is supremely serene, like the peace that comes to a seeker's face when the fruits of tapasya are at last received.

This form is not only the Goddess's story — it is every seeker's story. One who walks a difficult path for years sometimes appears dulled in colour, and fatigue comes. But when the fruit of that tapasya arrives — when the stream of grace descends upon them — they too shine forth like Mahagauri.

In the tradition of the Shiva Purana, it is said that those who worship Maa Mahagauri have all the sins of their past lives and the present life washed away. Just as Ganga water dissolved the outward darkness from Parvati, so the Mother's grace dissolves the inner darkness from her devotee. One who worships her on Ashtami is freed from sorrow, poverty, and ignorance, and attains fortune, purity, and liberation.

## Significance

Maa Mahagauri is the presiding deity of the eighth day of Navratri. Among the nine Shaktis of Navratri, this form holds special significance for three reasons.

**The sequence from darkness to light.** The Navratri journey passes through Kalaratri on Saptami and arrives at Mahagauri on Ashtami. This sequence is symbolic — after life's hardest night comes the most radiant dawn. Kalaratri is the symbol of the destruction of fear and ignorance; Mahagauri is the purity and auspiciousness that follows.

**The completion of tapasya.** Mahagauri is the form of Parvati that emerged after years of arduous sadhana. This form establishes the truth that sincere sadhana never goes unrewarded. Maa Mahagauri is the protector of those devotees who are moving toward purity and light after a long struggle.

**Mahashtami — the most sacred day of Navratri.** In many traditions, Ashtami is considered the most exalted tithi of Navratri. This is the day of Durga Ashtami, also called Mahashtami. On this day there is a special tradition of kanya pujan — young girls are honoured as the living form of the Goddess, offered worship, and fed.

**Similarity to Maa Annapurna.** Mahagauri's white, gentle form is also associated with Maa Annapurna — both supremely compassionate, nourishing, and bestowers of peace.

**Colour.** It is auspicious to wear **pink** or **white** clothing for Ashtami. White represents Mahagauri's whiteness; pink represents compassion and affection.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Rise before sunrise; bathe and wear clean white or pink clothing; take the sankalpa: "I perform the worship of Maa Mahagauri on the eighth day — grant me your grace."
- **Kalash and puja:** Establish the kalash; change the water in the nine-day akhand kalash; adorn Maa Mahagauri's image with white flowers; bathe the Goddess with Ganga water; apply white sandalwood paste; light incense and lamp.
- **Offerings:** White flowers (jasmine, mogra, white rose); coconut and white sweets (kheer, coconut laddus) as bhog.
- **Recitation:** Recite one to three chapters of the Durga Saptashati — especially fruitful on Ashtami; if time does not allow, chant one mala of the Devi Kavach, Argalastotra, or the Mahagauri mantra 108 times.
- **Kanya pujan (special to Mahashtami):** Invite nine girls (aged 2–10) as living forms of the Goddess; wash their feet, apply tilak, offer halwa-puri-kala chana, and present a red chunari and dakshina; feed the girls before breaking your own fast.
- **Aarti:** Perform aarti of Maa Mahagauri; seek forgiveness for any errors in the puja; keep the akhand jyot burning.
- **Fasting:** Continue the Navratri fast on Ashtami — fruit diet or nirjala; abstain from grain and salt; the fast may be broken on the evening of Ashtami or the morning of Navami after kanya pujan.
- **Sattvic conduct:** Maintain purity of mind, speech, and action throughout these days — avoid anger, ego, and falsehood; this is the true meaning of upavasa.
- **Preparation:** On the night of Ashtami, gather materials for the Navami havan and parana in preparation for Maa Siddhidatri.

## Mantras

**Mantra 1 — Mahagauri Dhyana Mantra**

> श्वेते वृषे समारूढा श्वेताम्बरधरा शुचिः ।
> महागौरी शुभं दद्यात् महादेवप्रमोददा ॥

Śvete vṛṣe samārūḍhā śvetāmbaradharā śuciḥ | mahāgaurī śubhaṃ dadyāt mahādevapramodadā ||

Meaning: Seated upon a white bull, robed in white, supremely pure, she who brings delight to Mahadeva — may Maa Mahagauri grant us auspiciousness.

---

**Mantra 2 — Sarvabhuta Stuti**

> या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ महागौरी रूपेण संस्थिता ।
> नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः ॥

Yā devī sarvabhūteṣu māṃ mahāgaurī rūpeṇa saṃsthitā | namastasyai namastasyai namastasyai namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: To the Goddess who abides in all beings in the form of Maa Mahagauri — salutations, again and again, salutations.
