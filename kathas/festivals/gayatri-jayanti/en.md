## Katha

In the Satya Yuga, Brahma organized a great yajna. The muhurta — the auspicious moment — had been fixed. The alignment of planets and constellations at that hour was rare, one that would not come again. The devas, the rishis, all had assembled in the yajna-mandapa. The directions were purified. The sacred fire was ready to be kindled.

But there was one unavoidable requirement of the ritual — the wife must be present at the yajna. Brahma's wife, Devi Savitri, had not yet arrived.

They waited. The muhurta slipped further away. Anxiety spread among the assembled devas.

Indra said — "Lord, the muhurta is passing. This delay to the yajna will be inauspicious for all of creation."

Indra and the other devas put their heads together and devised a solution. Nearby, among a group of cowherd people, there was a brahmin girl — radiant, serene, as though light itself had taken form. She was brought forward. The prescribed rites of purification were performed. She was duly wed to Brahma according to the scriptures. And she took her seat at Brahma's left side.

The yajna began.

But this girl was no ordinary human being.

When she sat beside Brahma and the first oblation was poured into the fire — the sound that issued from her lips was no ordinary mantra. It was the voice of that light which precedes even the sun, the shape of that sound in which all four Vedas are contained — the Gayatri Mantra. Oṃ bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ — these three vyahritis are the life-breath of all three worlds. And this luminous girl was the human form of that very mantra — Devi Gayatri.

Just then, Devi Savitri arrived.

Seeing another seated in her place, her heart was stricken. Her anger was natural. She cursed Brahma — "Your worship will be performed only at Pushkar; nowhere else in the world will temples be built to you." This is why, even today, temples to Brahma are very few — Pushkar is the most prominent among them.

But in that same moment, Devi Savitri's gaze fell upon the radiant girl. And she recognized her.

This was no ordinary girl.

This was the voice of the Vedas. This was the sound that existed at the dawn of creation, that illuminates the rishis, that makes the yajna complete. Savitri's anger melted. She embraced Devi Gayatri and said — "You are the living power of the Vedas. Without you, the work of creation is incomplete."

That day was Jyeshtha Shukla Ekadashi.

From that day forward, this tithi has been celebrated as Gayatri Jayanti — in remembrance of the day when the Mother of the Vedas first took embodied form in this creation.

In the tradition of the Devi Bhagavata Purana, it is said that whoever recites the Gayatri Mantra with devotion on this day receives the merit of reciting all the Vedas, and their intellect is purified.

## Significance

Devi Gayatri is not merely a goddess — she is the living form of the mantra that is the root of the Vedas. The tenth mantra of the sixty-second sukta of the third mandala of the Rigveda — this is the mantra that the great rishi Vishwamitra revealed at the pinnacle of his tapasya from Savitr-deva (the solar divine). This is why it is also called Savitri — the one who awakens the divine consciousness of the sun.

**Form of the Veda-Mata:** In tradition, Devi Gayatri is described as five-faced — panchamukhi. These five faces are symbols of the five pranas (prana, apana, vyana, udana, samana). In some traditions it is also said that the five faces represent the four Vedas and their essence — Omkara. In her ten hands she holds various implements that are symbols of power, knowledge, and purification.

**Brahmacharya and Upanayana:** In the Vedic tradition, at the time of the upanayana samskara — when a young boy enters the brahmacharya ashrama — the initiation into the Gayatri Mantra is given first. The Gayatri Mantra is the first light the guru bestows upon the student. The very word "Gayatri" is derived from "gāyantaṃ trāyate" — it protects one who sings it. This mantra is the guardian of the intellect, the mother of knowledge.

**The Confluence of Gayatri Jayanti:** The day of Jyeshtha Shukla Ekadashi is sacred for two reasons — Ekadashi carries its own significance connected to the worship of Lord Vishnu, and on this very tithi lies the tradition of Devi Gayatri's manifestation. Thus this day is a confluence of both Vaishnava devotion and Shakta wisdom.

## Vidhi

- **Preparation (Dashami night):** Take sattvic food; observe brahmacharya; take a sankalpa — "On Jyeshtha Shukla Ekadashi, on the occasion of Gayatri Jayanti, I will worship Maa Gayatri."
- **At dawn:** Rise before sunrise; bathe with Ganga water or clean water; wear yellow or white clothing.
- **Sunrise worship:** Face east; offer arghya from a copper vessel (water, roli, flowers, akshata) to Suryadev while reciting the Gayatri Mantra — this is the foremost act of the day.
- **Puja of Maa Gayatri:** Install an image or yantra of Devi Gayatri; offer yellow flowers, yellow sandalwood paste, and yellow cloth; light a ghee lamp and incense; offer kheer or mishri as bhog.
- **Gayatri Japa:** Recite the Gayatri Mantra at least 108 times (1008 if time allows) using a rudraksha or sphatika mala, mentally or in a soft undertone; those not formally initiated may also recite "Oṃ bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ..." with devotion — Maa's door is open to all.
- **Havan/Yajna:** Offer oblations with til, barley, ghee, and samidha, saying "Oṃ bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ... svāhā" with each oblation; participating in a collective havan at a temple or ashram is equally meritorious.
- **Fasting:** Since this is an Ekadashi, nirjala (waterless) or phalahar (fruit diet) is acceptable; abstaining from grains, lentils, onion, and garlic is essential; avoid harsh speech, anger, and falsehood on this day.
- **Parana:** Break the fast on Dwadashi after sunrise with tulsi water or Ganga water, then take sattvic food.

## Mantras

**1. Gayatri Mantra — The Essence of the Vedas**

> ॐ भूर्भुवः स्वः।
> तत् सवितुर्वरेण्यं
> भर्गो देवस्य धीमहि।
> धियो यो नः प्रचोदयात् ॥

oṃ bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ |
tat savitur vareṇyaṃ
bhargo devasya dhīmahi |
dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt ||

Meaning: "We meditate upon that supreme radiance of Savitr-deva (the divine inspiring power of the sun) which illuminates all three worlds — bhū, bhuvaḥ, and svaḥ. May that light inspire and illuminate our intellect."

This is the mantra of the Rigveda (3.62.10) — the most sacred mantra in the Hindu tradition. It was revealed by the great rishi Vishwamitra at the pinnacle of his tapasya. In the upanayana samskara this is the first mantra of initiation. Reciting it 108 or 1008 times on the day of Gayatri Jayanti yields special merit.

**2. Devi Gayatri Dhyana Shloka — The Five-Faced Form**

> मुक्ता-विद्रुम-हेम-नील-धवलच्छायैर्मुखैस्त्रीक्षणैः
> युक्तामिन्दुकलानिबद्धमुकुटां तत्त्वार्थवर्णात्मिकाम् ।
> गायत्रीं वरदाभयाङ्कुशकशाशूलं कपालं गुणं
> शङ्खं चक्रमथारविन्दयुगलं हस्तैर्वहन्तीं भजे ॥

muktā-vidruma-hema-nīla-dhavalachhāyair mukhais trīkṣaṇaiḥ
yuktām indukalānibaddhamukuṭāṃ tattvārthavarṇātmikām |
gāyatrīṃ varadābhayāṅkuśakaśāśūlaṃ kapālaṃ guṇaṃ
śaṅkhaṃ cakram athāravindayugalaṃ hastair vahantīṃ bhaje ||

Meaning: "I worship Devi Gayatri whose five faces shine respectively with the radiance of pearl, coral, gold, sapphire-blue, and white; each face bearing three eyes; crowned with a diadem adorned with the crescent moon; who is the very form of the letters of tattva and meaning; and whose ten hands hold the varada mudra (gesture of boon-giving), abhaya mudra (gesture of fearlessness), ankusha (goad), kasha (whip), shula (trident), kapala (skull-cup), sphatika mala (crystal rosary), Panchajanya (the divine conch), Sudarshan Chakra (the spinning disc of light), and two lotus flowers."

In the tradition of the Devi Bhagavata Purana, it is said that a sadhaka who visualizes Devi Gayatri's form using this dhyana shloka while performing japa receives swift results — because the mantra and the presiding deity of that mantra are two forms of the same consciousness.
