## Katha

Dharmaraja Yudhishthira once asked Lord Shri Krishna: *"Madhava, what is the significance of Shravan Krishna Ekadashi? What is this Ekadashi called, and what is its fruit?"*

He said: *"Raajan, this is Kamika Ekadashi. Listen — I will tell you its greatness, as Brahma once narrated it to the divine sage Narada."*

Shravan is a month unlike any other. At Devshayani — the Ekadashi of Ashadha — I went to rest on the serpent-bed of Shesha in the ocean of milk, deep in Yoganidra. Shravan is the sacred month when that rest of mine is at its fullest. From the sky comes rain; the earth turns green; the rivers fill. Creation renews itself. And the Ekadashi that falls in this holy month carries even greater weight.

*"He who worships me with tulsi on Kamika Ekadashi — know this: one tulsi leaf gives me more joy than a thousand lotus flowers."*

Tulsi — whose very sight removes sin, whose touch purifies, the watering of whose roots gives the merit of a pilgrimage. Just as the lotus leaf remains in water without being touched by it — so too does the one who worships me with tulsi on Kamika Ekadashi remain untouched by sin even while living amid the world.

He continued: *"He who fasts on this day, keeps vigil through the night, lights a lamp before my image, and offers tulsi — his ancestors are pleased, all his accumulated sins are burnt away. Even the great sin of brahmahatya is destroyed by the merit of this Ekadashi."*

The Dharmaraja asked: *"And one who cannot fully fast?"*

He said: *"Raajan, one who cannot keep a full fast due to bodily weakness may take phalahar — fruits and milk. Even one who simply hears this katha with faith receives the same merit. Hearing this mahatmya alone is equal to the merit of donating a thousand cows."*

When Yudhishthira had heard this account of Kamika Ekadashi, every sorrow in his heart was gone.

## Significance

Kamika Ekadashi means the Ekadashi that fulfills all desires (kāma). This name has come down through tradition.

Kamika Ekadashi is a meeting of two sanctities — the month of Shravan and the Ekadashi fast.

In Shravan, Vishnu is in Yoganidra. This month is the time of his deepest grace. And Ekadashi — the tithi that is most dear to the Lord in any month — becomes even more luminous within this month.

Tulsi is the distinctive gift of this Ekadashi. Tulsi is Vishnu's beloved. Offering tulsi on Kamika Ekadashi carries many times the merit of any ordinary day.

This Ekadashi is especially for those weighed down by heavy sins — the scripture says there is no sin that cannot be cleansed by fasting and tulsi-worship on Kamika Ekadashi.

## Vidhi

- **Dashami eve:** Take sattvic food the night before; pluck tulsi leaves on Dashami (do not pluck on Ekadashi itself).
- **At dawn:** Bathe and take the sankalpa (vow) to observe the fast.
- **Puja:** Offer tulsi leaves, flowers, and a lit ghee diya before an image of Vishnu or a Shalagrama; chant *Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya*.
- **Tulsi service:** Circumambulate the tulsi plant and offer it water — this is considered especially meritorious on Kamika Ekadashi.
- **Special:** Tulsi offering is the central act of this Ekadashi — do not omit it; do not eat rice (general Ekadashi rule).
- **Vigil:** Keep vigil through the night — this is the special requirement of Kamika Ekadashi.
- **Breaking the fast (Parana):** On Dvadashi, break the fast after sunrise within the Dvadashi tithi; begin with tulsi-water.
- **Note:** Shravan brings four Mondays, four Tuesdays — and this Ekadashi; all three together complete the devotional architecture of Shravan.

## Mantras

**Tulsi offering mantra — recite while placing tulsi before Vishnu:**

> तुलसी श्रीसखि शुभे पापहारिणि पुण्यदे ।
> नमस्ते नारदनुते नारायणमनःप्रिये ॥

*Tulasī śrī-sakhi śubhe pāpa-hāriṇi puṇya-de |
Namaste Nārada-nute Nārāyaṇa-manaḥ-priye ||*

Meaning: "O Tulasi — companion of Shri Lakshmi, auspicious, remover of sins, giver of merit — praised by Narada, beloved of Narayana's heart — I bow to you."
