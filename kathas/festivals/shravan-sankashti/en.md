## Katha

Shravan — the heart of the rains. This is the month when all of creation turns green and devotion to Shiva reaches its height. Yet in this very sacred month, something once occurred that no one had anticipated.

**Krodhasura** was born.

He was not the offspring of any ordinary demon. He was born of the accumulated anger — that rage which had gathered over ages upon ages every time a warrior, a demon, or a king had been slain. Where did that anger go? It kept accumulating — and one day it took form.

Krodhasura was pure anger. He needed no weapons. Wherever he went — fire ignited in the mind.

He went to a sage's ashrama — the serene ascetic turned red with rage, curses began to pour from him.

He entered Indra's assembly — the gods began fighting amongst themselves.

He visited a household — discord erupted in the family, quarrels broke out between the parents.

This is the nature of anger — it already exists within every being. Krodhasura merely awakened it. That is why defeating him was so very difficult — his weapon was not outside, it was within.

The gods sought refuge with Brahma. Brahma said — *"Anger is not conquered by anger. The one who can defeat this demon is the one who can hold all experiences within himself — who is not disturbed by whatever comes. Go — take refuge in Lambodara."*

The gods came to Ganesha.

Ganesha listened. And he manifested in the form of **Lambodara** — vast of belly, composed of face, undisturbed in spirit.

Krodhasura came. He unleashed upon Ganesha every strike of anger — bitter words, provocations, insults, proclamations of injustice.

Lambodara — listened to all of it.

And absorbed every word into his vast belly.

Just as the ocean receives all rivers within itself without being disturbed — so too did Lambodara take in every blow of Krodhasura and render it still. When anger finds no response, it cannot sustain itself.

Krodhasura had anger to give. But that anger, upon entering Lambodara, simply disappeared. No echo, no flame.

Krodhasura grew exhausted. Confused. The weapons slipped from his hands.

He stood before Ganesha, drained of power.

Lambodara said — *"There is only one remedy for anger — the capacity to hold it. To be so vast within that anger can be absorbed into you. Your power has not ended — but now let it find direction. Go, and turn that anger against untruth."*

Krodhasura touched Lambodara's feet and departed — transformed.

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said that by hearing this katha of Lambodara on Shravan Sankashti Chaturthi, and by completing the fast after sighting the moon at moonrise, anger, stress, and the restlessness of the mind are calmed. The devotee who observes this vrata with faith in this month has the trials of life removed.

## Significance

**Sankashti** means — the one who removes obstacles. Sankat (crisis) + harana (removal) = **Sankataharana Chaturthi**. This vrata is observed on the Krishna Chaturthi of every month, and each month a particular form of Ganesha is worshipped.

**Lambodara** — this is that form of Ganesha which symbolises capacity and containment. One whose belly is vast can hold everything — joy and sorrow, anger and compassion, delight and grief. This is precisely the quality a sadhaka needs — that whatever life brings, the inner peace not be broken.

**The context of Shravan:** Shravan is the month of Shiva — the most sacred, the most filled with devotion. The Sankashti that falls in this month carries even greater significance. The grace of Shiva and Ganesha — both at once.

**The moonrise-parana rule:** The fast of Sankashti Chaturthi is broken only after sighting the moon. This rule is the distinctive mark of this vrata — parana without sighting the moon is not considered complete.

## Vidhi

**Puja materials:**
- Modak or laddus (Ganesha's beloved naivedya)
- Durva grass (21 durva)
- Red flowers
- Sindura
- Ghee lamp

**Puja procedure:**
1. Bathe in the morning and install the image or picture of Ganesha.
2. Perform panchopachara puja — water, fragrance, flowers, incense, lamp.
3. Offer durva grass to Ganesha — 21 or 3 durva.
4. Offer modak or laddus as bhoga.
5. Hear the katha of Lambodara Ganesha.
6. In the evening, await moonrise.
7. Upon sighting the moon — offer arghya to the moon with water and Ganesha's name.
8. After that, break the fast.

## Observance

**Fast:** From sunrise to moonrise. No parana before sighting the moon.

**Diet:** Abstain from grains on the day of the vrata. Fruit-based food (fruits, milk, sabudana) may be taken once.

**Time of moonrise:** Check the moonrise time for that tithi on DrikPanchang — that is the time for parana.

**Parana:** After sighting the moon, offer arghya with water, then take food.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.

> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda, vast of form, radiant as a crore of suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.
