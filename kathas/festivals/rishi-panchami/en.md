## Katha

In the land of Vidarbha, there lived a learned brahmin named Uttank. His wife was Sushila — devoted, gentle, and firm in her vows.

He had a married daughter. Her husband died young, and she returned to her parents' home as a widow.

One day Uttank saw something that shook him: his daughter's body was covered with countless worms. She writhed in pain.

His heart became deeply troubled. He prayed to God — "Lord, why must my daughter suffer so? Show me a way."

He entered deep meditation.

In that meditation, understanding came to him — a great Maharishi appeared and spoke:

"Uttank, this is the fruit of your daughter's past life. In her previous birth, she was a devout housewife. But on the days of her monthly cycle, she unknowingly entered the kitchen, prepared food, touched vessels, and performed puja — all forbidden by the dharmashastra. She did not know. But the act was done — and its result must be borne."

Uttank asked — "And the remedy?"

The Maharishi said — "Bhadrapada Shukla Panchami — Rishi Panchami. On that day, let your daughter worship the Saptarishis and Arundhati. Let her bathe in a river. Let her use the apamarg branch (achyranthes aspera) for her tooth-cleaning. Let her take only fruit and grain that have grown without ox-ploughing — nature's own gift. Let her observe this vrat with full faith."

The daughter kept the vrat.

She worshipped the Saptarishis — Kashyapa, Atri, Bharadvaja, Vishvamitra, Gautama, Jamadagni, Vashishtha — and together with them, the great Arundhati.

The vrat was complete.

The worms on her body subsided. Her form became pure. Peace came to her face.

In the tradition of the Bhavishyottara Purana, Uttara Parva, it is said that the merit of this vrat equals ten thousand yajnas — and the woman who observes it with devotion finds release from both the conscious and unknowing sins of this life and past lives.

## Significance

**The Saptarishis** — the seven great sages who have been guardians of dharma since the beginning of creation. Their very presence destroys sin. They are the mind-born sons of Brahma, and teachers even to the gods.

**Arundhati** — wife of Maharishi Vashishtha. The supreme embodiment of the devoted wife. At the time of a wedding, the bride and groom are shown the stars of the Saptarishis — and alongside them, the star of Arundhati. This is a reminder of her ideal.

This vrat is observed primarily by women — as expiation for unknowing transgressions during menstruation. **Men too** may observe it for the welfare of their wives.

**The food rule:** On this day, grain harvested from ox-ploughed fields — wheat, rice, lentils — is not consumed. Only fruits, tubers, and vegetables that nature herself has grown are taken.

## Vidhi

1. At dawn, bathe in a river or lake.
2. Clean the teeth with an **apamarg** branch (achyranthes aspera / chirchira) — this is a special observance of this vrat.
3. Wear clean clothes and prepare the worship space.
4. Install an image or depiction of the **seven sages**. Invoke **Arundhati** alongside them.
5. Offer sandalwood, flowers, durva grass, and fruit.
6. Worship with incense, lamp, and naivedya.
7. Hear the Rishi Panchami vrat katha.
8. Take only **permitted food** — fruit, tubers, water chestnut (singhara), sabudana, milk, curd.
9. Perform the evening aarti again.

**2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Bhadrapada Shukla Panchami — ~September 15, 2026).

## Observance

**Bathing:** At dawn in a river or lake.

**Apamarg datan:** The special observance of this vrat.

**Food:** Fruits, tubers, herbs grown without ploughing. Wheat, rice, and other field-cultivated grains are forbidden.

**Night vigil / katha-listening:** Hearing the vrat katha in the evening is an essential part of this observance.

## Mantras

**Saptarishi dhyana mantra:**

> ॐ कश्यपो‌त्रिर्भरद्वाजो विश्वामित्रोऽथ गौतमः ।
> जमदग्निर्वशिष्ठश्च सप्तैते ऋषयः स्मृताः ॥

Oṃ Kaśyapo'trir bharadvājo viśvāmitro'tha gautamaḥ |
Jamadagnir vaśiṣṭhaśca saptaite ṛṣayaḥ smṛtāḥ ||

Meaning: "Kashyapa, Atri, Bharadvaja, Vishvamitra, Gautama, Jamadagni, and Vashishtha — these seven are called the Rishis."

**Arundhati vandana:**

> अरुंधती सती साध्वी वशिष्ठस्य प्रिया सदा ।
> पतिव्रतानां शिरसा नमामि तां पतिव्रताम् ॥

Aruṃdhatī satī sādhvī vaśiṣṭhasya priyā sadā |
Pativratānāṃ śirasā namāmi tāṃ pativratām ||

Meaning: "To Arundhati — chaste, virtuous, ever dear to Vashishtha, foremost among devoted wives — I bow my head in reverence."
