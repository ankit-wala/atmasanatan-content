## Katha

Dharmaraja Yudhishthira asked Lord Sri Krishna — "Madhava, what is the name of the Bhadrapada Shukla Ekadashi? What is special about this day?"

The Lord said — "O King, this Ekadashi is called **Parivartini** — or Parsva Ekadashi. On Ashadha Shukla Ekadashi I enter Yoga Nidra, resting on Shesha in the Kshira Sagara. On this Bhadrapada Ekadashi, at the midpoint of Chaturmas, I change my side — from the left to the right. That is why it is Parsva Ekadashi. Now hear an ancient katha connected to this day."

---

Bali, king of the asuras — grandson of Prahlada.

He was extraordinary. A great ascetic, a great donor. Through his strength and the power of his yajnas, he had established dominion over all three worlds. The gods were expelled from heaven.

Devamata Aditi was grief-stricken. She prayed to Lord Vishnu — "Lord, restore my sons the gods to their place."

Lord Vishnu said to Aditi — "Devi, I will take birth as your son."

And the Lord took the **Vamana** avatar — the form of a small, keen-minded brahmin boy.

---

In those days, Bali was performing the Vishvajit Mahayajna on the banks of the Narmada — a yajna in which he turned no one away empty-handed.

Vamana Bhagavan arrived at the yajna ground. Small in stature, luminous face, with an umbrella and a kamandal in hand.

Bali saw him — "Who is this? Such radiance is not found in an ordinary boy." His guru Shukracharya gave a warning: "Bali, this is no ordinary brahmin. This is Vishnu himself. Give nothing."

But Bali did not heed his guru's words.

He said — "Whether it is Vishnu or anyone — I have made a pledge in this yajna that I will let no one depart empty-handed. Whatever is asked, I shall give."

Vamana smiled and said — "O King, I need only three paces of land."

Bali laughed — "Only that? Three paces? Here — the gift is given."

---

And then that small boy began to grow.

In one step — the entire earth.
In the second step — all of heaven and the sky.

No land remained for the third step.

Bali saw — before him was no longer who had stood there a moment ago. Before him stood **Trivikrama** — the Lord who measured all three worlds at once. His vast form reached from Brahmaloka above to the earth below.

Bali's eyes filled with tears. He bowed his head.

He said — "Lord, place Your third step upon my head."

The Lord saw — in Bali there was no deceit, no remorse. He had given his word — and kept it. This was a great donor.

The Lord was pleased.

He granted Bali a boon — "Bali, you shall be the sovereign of **Sutala loka**. And I myself — Vishnu — shall remain as your doorkeeper. Sutala shall be protected by my hands."

And he said — "On this Bhadrapada Ekadashi, I turn on my side in Sutala — while dwelling close to you."

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana, it is said that the devotee who observes the Parivartini Ekadashi vrat, hears this katha, and meditates on Lord Vamana receives the combined merit of all Ekadashis of Chaturmas.

## Significance

**The meaning of Parivartini / Parsva:**
"Parsva" = side, flank. On this day the Lord turns on his side during Yoga Nidra — this is the midpoint of Chaturmas. Ashadha Ekadashi = he lies down (Shayana). Bhadrapada Ekadashi = he turns (Parivartini). Kartika Ekadashi = he awakens (Prabodhini).

**Vamana and Trivikrama:**
This Ekadashi is also called "Vamana Ekadashi" because the Vamana-Bali katha is specially recited on this day. The actual birthday of Lord Vamana (janmotsava) is celebrated on Bhadrapada Shukla Dwadashi, not on Ekadashi.

**Jal Jhulni tradition:** In the regional tradition of North and Central India, on this day the Lord's image is carried in procession to a river or lake for a water-play celebration. This is also known as "Jal Jhulni Ekadashi."

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe and take the vrat sankalpa on Ekadashi morning.
- **Puja:** Install an image or depiction of Lord Vamana; perform Panchamrit abhisheka with milk, curd, ghee, honey, and sugar; offer yellow flowers, tulasi, and a lamp.
- **Fasting:** Observe the fast without water or with fruit only; completed by the Dwadashi paran.
- **During the day:** Hear the Parivartini Ekadashi katha — the story of Vamana and Bali.
- **At night:** Keep vigil through the night singing kirtan of the Lord's names — night vigil on this Ekadashi is especially meritorious.
- **Breaking the fast:** On Dwadashi morning, before Rahu-kaal.

## Mantras

**Vamana Bhagavan mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वामनाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāmanāya

Meaning: "Salutation to Lord Vamana." — The root mantra of the Vamana avatar.

**Trivikrama stuti:**

> विक्रमस्य त्रयः पादाः त्रयो लोकाः सनातनाः ।
> त्रिविक्रमं नमस्यामि त्रिभुवनेश्वरं हरिम् ॥

Vikramasya trayaḥ pādāḥ trayo lokāḥ sanātanāḥ |
Trivikramaṃ namasyāmi tribhuvaneśvaraṃ harim ||

Meaning: "He whose three strides encompass the three eternal worlds — to that Trivikrama Hari, Lord of the three realms, I offer my salutation."
