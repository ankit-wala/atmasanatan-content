## Katha

Pausha month, Shukla Paksha — even in biting winter cold, the warmth of devotion to Lord Vishnu glows steadily in the hearts of his devotees. The Ekadashi of this fortnight is called **Putrada Ekadashi** — the Putrada, giver of sons, the sacred day that bestows that very blessing.

The Bhavishya Purana tells the glory of this Ekadashi through the story of a king who had everything — a kingdom, abundance, the love of his people — and yet carried a hollowness that no palace wall could fill, no laughter in the royal quarters could ease.

King **Suketumaan** and Queen **Shaibya** of the city of **Bhadravati** were devoted to dharma and to justice. They nurtured their subjects like their own children, gave in charity, performed yajnas — and yet their own home remained without a child. Years passed, decades passed. Without a son, how would the debt to the ancestors be repaid? How would the lineage continue? Who would offer the pinda rites at the end?

The moment these thoughts arose, King Suketumaan's heart would sink into deep grief. One day the anguish grew so sharp that he left the palace and walked alone into the forest — no attendant beside him, no chariot beneath him, only a heavy heart and a faint, flickering hope in the Lord.

Wandering through the forest, he came upon a wondrous place. There were ponds of clear, still water; trees heavy with fruit; and the hermitages of sages that seemed to recall Vaikuntha itself. This was the forest where the supreme devotees of Lord Vishnu dwelt, absorbed in tapasya.

King Suketumaan bowed at the feet of those great sages and laid bare his sorrow. The sages were moved by his pain. They spoke: "O King, the Ekadashi of the Shukla Paksha of Pausha month is called **Putrada Ekadashi**. By the direct grace of Lord Vishnu, this sacred day bestows the blessing of a child. Observe the fast of this Ekadashi with complete faith and proper observance, worship Lord Vasudeva, and spend the night in vigil, repeating his name. On the morning of Dwadashi, break your fast and return home — the Lord will surely fulfil your heart's desire."

A new light kindled in King Suketumaan's heart. Heeding the sages' counsel, he observed the fast on that very Ekadashi — he remained without food throughout the day, performed the worship of Lord Vishnu, offered tulasi leaves, and kept the night vigil singing the Lord's name. At dawn on Dwadashi he broke his fast and returned to Bhadravati.

The Lord's leela is beyond all reckoning. Before long, Queen Shaibya conceived. In due time a son was born — courageous, wise, devoted to Vishnu, and beloved by the people. Tears of joy flowed from the eyes of King Suketumaan and Queen Shaibya. The lineage they had ached to carry forward for so many years now continued. And in the evening of their lives, both attained liberation through devotion to Lord Vishnu.

The tradition of the Bhavishya Purana declares that whatever couple observes the Putrada Ekadashi fast with complete devotion and unbroken faith — Lord Vishnu will surely fulfil their deepest longing, whether it be a child, health, or moksha.

## Significance

**Putrada** means, literally, the giver of sons. Pausha Shukla Ekadashi received this name because fasting on this day, by the grace of Lord Vishnu, grants the blessing of a child to childless couples.

Yet the reach of this Ekadashi is not confined to the longing for a child alone. "Putra" here is a wider symbol — that which carries the lineage forward, carries dharma forward, carries memory forward. In a spiritual sense, the vow of this Ekadashi places the devotee's most earnest desire before the Lord — and Lord Vishnu never leaves a pure longing unanswered.

The scriptures note that Pausha Putrada and Shravana Putrada are two distinct Ekadashis, described in different Puranic traditions with different stories. The Pausha Putrada rests on the authority of the Bhavishya Purana.

## Vidhi

- **Dashami night:** Give up tamasic food and unnecessary speech from the night of Dashami itself.
- **At dawn:** Bathe and wear clean clothes on Ekadashi morning.
- **Puja:** Before the image or picture of Lord Vishnu, perform worship with a lamp, incense, tulasi leaves, yellow flowers, and Panchamrita; recite the Vishnu Sahasranama or listen to and read this katha; perform aarti again in the evening.
- **Fasting:** A complete nirjala (waterless) fast is the highest observance; those who are unable may take fruit — grains, lentils, and rice must be given up entirely. Do not eat rice on Ekadashi; avoid needless argument, anger, and untruth.
- **For whom:** Couples longing for a child should worship together and make their sankalpa before the Lord with sincere hearts; offer tulasi leaves always — tulasi is especially dear to Lord Vishnu.
- **At night:** Keep vigil through the night — sing the Lord's name or read scripture; staying awake until midnight also earns great merit.
- **Breaking the fast:** After sunrise on Dwadashi, before midday — take tulasi water and the Lord's prasad first, then break the fast; offer a meal and donation to a Brahmin or someone in need.
