## Katha

Bhagiratha's tapasya had succeeded.

Brahma had released Ganga — her force as she descended from heaven was tremendous, so tremendous that the earth could not have withstood it. But Lord Shiva had given his word. He stood on Kailash, his locks open. And Ganga plunged straight into those locks.

That day was Jyeshtha Shukla Dashami — which came to be known as "Ganga Dashahara."

But the story of that day was not yet complete.

Ganga was within Shiva's locks — being held, being purified, being tempered. Just as a disciple is refined by residing in the guru's ashram, Ganga was ripening in the depths of those locks. Her velocity was being stilled, her power disciplined.

Bhagiratha waited.

The days passed. The earth was still thirsty — the ashes of sixty thousand ancestors still awaited liberation. Bhagiratha offered prayers to Shiva once more. Then came that sacred day: Vaishakha Shukla Saptami.

Lord Shiva opened his locks.

A stream emerged — first slowly, then with full force. Ganga came out of the locks and for the first time she touched the earth.

This is Ganga Saptami. The moment when Ganga first touched the feet of humanity. That is why this day is also called "Ganga Janma Divasa" — Ganga Dashahara is the day of her descent from heaven, and Ganga Saptami is the day of her manifestation upon the earth.

---

This katha has another dimension — the relationship between Surya and Ganga.

In the tradition of the Brahma Purana, it is said that the Saptami tithi is most dear to Suryadev. Surya is the very power that melts the snow locked on the Himalayan peaks, that keeps the glacier at Gangotri alive, that draws that water down as a river toward the plains. When Ganga descended onto the earth, Suryadev welcomed her. His rays fell upon that water and Ganga shone golden.

This is why there is a tradition on Ganga Saptami of offering arghya to Suryadev alongside the Ganga-snana — the worship of both in the same auspicious hour.

Surya drew Ganga down to the earth. Ganga nourished the earth. And from this union life flourished — fields became lush, thirst was quenched, sin was cleansed.

In the tradition of the Brahma Purana, it is said that one who bathes in the Ganga at dawn on Vaishakha Shukla Saptami and offers tarpana with sesame and flowers — the pitrs of seven generations receive tarpana and attain liberation. This is the same fruit as is obtained on Ganga Dashahara. Those who cannot reach the Ganga's bank may add Gangajal to water at home, bathe in it, and offer arghya to Surya — this too is equally meritorious.

## Significance

Ganga Saptami and Ganga Dashahara — both are connected with Ganga's descent, yet they mark two distinct moments.

**Ganga Dashahara** (Jyeshtha Shukla Dashami): The day Ganga descended from heaven and entered Shiva's locks.

**Ganga Saptami** (Vaishakha Shukla Saptami): The day Ganga was released from Shiva's locks and flowed onto the earth — this is her "bhū-janma divasa," her earth-birth day.

On Dashahara, Ganga descended from the divine realm as a cosmic power. On Saptami she became human — accessible, touchable, the mother of every person.

**The connection between Saptami and Surya:** The Saptami tithi is held in the scriptures as especially auspicious for the worship of Suryadev. Surya Saptami, Ratha Saptami — in all of these the bond between Saptami and Surya is unbreakable. On Ganga Saptami this bond deepens further: the Himalayan streams born of Surya's energy came to the earth's surface today. Therefore on this day the vidhan prescribes the worship of both Ganga and Surya together.

**The fruit of bathing:** According to the Brahma Purana, bathing in the Ganga on this day yields fruit equal to that of Ganga Dashahara. The pitrs of seven generations are liberated. Sin is destroyed.

**Ganga's presence:** The scriptures say that Ganga is not only at her banks — wherever she is remembered with devotion, she is present there. On Ganga Saptami her power is especially awakened.

## Vidhi

The traditional vidhan of Ganga-snana, Surya-arghya, and tarpana on Ganga Saptami:

1. **Morning sankalpa.** Rise at Brahma muhurta. Before bathing, take a sankalpa — "Today, on Vaishakha Shukla Saptami, on Mother Ganga's bhū-janma divasa, I remember her and take Ganga-snana and offer arghya to Suryadev."
2. **Ganga-snana.** If it is possible to go to the Ganga's bank, do so. Otherwise, add Gangajal to water at home and bathe. While bathing, chant "Oṃ namo bhagavatyai Gaṅgāyai namaḥ."
3. **Surya-arghya.** After bathing, face east and take water in a copper vessel. Add red flowers, sesame, and akshata. While offering arghya to Surya, recite "Oṃ Sūryāya namaḥ."
4. **Ganga-pujana.** Before a kalasha filled with Gangajal, offer flowers, akshata, roli, and dhupa-dipa. Recite the "Namami Gange" stotra.
5. **Tarpana.** Taking sesame and flowers, offer tarpana to the pitrs with Gangajal. Offer water with the feeling — "May the pitrs of seven generations attain liberation."
6. **Dipa-dana.** In the evening, light a lamp and float it in the Ganga — or in a vessel of water at home. This is a symbol of illuminating the path of the ancestors.
7. **Dana.** On this day, the gift of sesame, water, fruit, and cloth is meritorious. Feed a Brahmin or give dana according to one's means.

## Observance

**Ganga-snana:** This is the principal rite of the day. Even in the heat of Vaishakha, going to the Ganga bank is beneficial — the water of this day is a remembrance of Ganga's first flow on the earth. Those who cannot reach the bank should add Gangajal to water at home and bathe.

**Surya-arghya:** At sunrise in the morning, offer Suryadev arghya with water mixed with sesame. Surya-upasana is especially fruitful on the Saptami tithi — and on Ganga Saptami the grace of both Ganga and Surya is received at the same moment.

**Tarpana:** Offer tarpana to the pitrs with Gangajal mixed with sesame and flowers. The Brahma Purana states that tarpana on this day liberates pitrs up to seven generations.

**Dipa-dana:** In the evening, light a lamp and offer it to Ganga. Remember the departed members of the family and wish for their sadgati.

**Upavasa:** Fasting on Ganga Saptami is optional. Ekahara (one meal) or phalahara is prescribed. Avoid tamasic food, meat, and alcohol especially on this day.

## Mantras

**Ganga-stuti — Invocation of Mother Ganga**

> नमामि गंगे तव पादपंकजं
> सुरासुरैर्वन्दितदिव्यरूपम् ।
> भुक्तिं च मुक्तिं च ददासि नित्यं
> भावानुसारेण सदा नराणाम् ॥

Namāmi gaṅge tava pādapaṅkajaṃ surāsurairvanditadivyarūpam | bhuktiṃ ca muktiṃ ca dadāsi nityaṃ bhāvānusāreṇa sadā narāṇām ||

Meaning: O Ganga, I bow to your lotus feet — which are venerated by both gods and asuras. You always bestow upon beings both enjoyment (bhukti) and liberation (mukti) according to their sincere feeling.

---

**Surya-arghya verse — Praise of Suryadev**

> ॐ जवाकुसुमसंकाशं काश्यपेयं महाद्युतिम् ।
> तमोऽरिं सर्वपापघ्नं प्रणतोऽस्मि दिवाकरम् ॥

Om javākusumasaṃkāśaṃ kāśyapeyaṃ mahādyutim | tamo'riṃ sarvapāpaghnaṃ praṇato'smi divākaram ||

Meaning: I bow to Divakar Suryadev — radiant as the javakusuma (hibiscus) flower, son of Kashyapa, supremely luminous, enemy of darkness, and destroyer of all sins.

---

**Pitru-tarpana verse — Prayer for the liberation of seven generations**

> गंगे च यमुने चैव गोदावरी सरस्वती ।
> नर्मदे सिंधु कावेरी जलेऽस्मिन् सन्निधिं कुरु ॥

Gaṅge ca yamune caiva godāvarī sarasvatī | narmade sindhu kāverī jale'smin sannidhiṃ kuru ||

Meaning: O Ganga, Yamuna, Godavari, Sarasvati, Narmada, Sindhu, and Kaveri — grant your presence in this water. May the pitrs of our seven generations attain liberation through the tarpana performed with this water.
