## Katha

The month of Jyeshtha — the harshest time of the year. No clouds in the sky, the sun is merciless. Rivers and ponds begin to dry. Going outside in the afternoon becomes difficult.

Near the village of Pali in Maharashtra there was a small boy — **Ballal**.

From childhood Ballal was a devotee of Ganesha. While other children went to the riverbank in the afternoon or played in the lanes, Ballal would make a clay image of Ganesha and worship it. For him this was no game — it was his natural joy.

The village elders did not approve.

They felt that children's work is to play — to go to the ghat, to climb trees, to run with other children. What kind of childhood was this, where a boy sat for hours before a clay image? They complained to Ballal's father.

His father was ashamed.

One day some village elders decided to act. They went to where Ballal sat before his clay image. They picked up the image — and broke it.

They scolded Ballal — *"Stop this now. Play like the other children."*

And they left.

Ballal sat right there.

The shattered pieces of the image were before him. The Jyeshtha sun beat down on his head. He did not get up and go home, did not go to the river. He sat there, and began calling out to Ganesha.

He had no puja thali. No modak. No durva. No water-pot.

There was only — a broken love, which still did not waver. And he was weeping.

The Jyeshtha afternoon passed. Evening came. The moon rose — the moon of that Chaturthi night.

And then **Ganesha appeared**.

He looked upon Ballal — exhausted, sunburned, hungry. Before him the shattered pieces of the clay image.

Ganesha said — *"Ballal, why are you weeping? I was here all along."*

Ballal said — *"Lord, they broke your image. I have nothing now. No materials for puja, no place. How shall I worship you?"*

Ganesha smiled.

He said — *"Ballal, the one who called to me without an image, without a thali, without a temple — through tears alone — that is the purest form of worship. You sat in the Jyeshtha sun, nothing stopped you — not hunger, not the heat. This is bhakti."*

Ganesha assumed the form of **Kriti** — the one who creates, the one who shapes through love. He touched the broken pieces of clay — and a beautiful image came into being.

And Ganesha gave his word — *"Ballal, I will always be present at this place. One of my names will be joined to your name — **Ballaleshvara**."*

The temple of Ballaleshvara in Pali (near Pune) exists to this day — one of the eight Ashtavinayaka pilgrimage sites of Maharashtra. Its origin is this very katha.

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said that by hearing this katha of Kriti Ganesha on Jyeshtha Sankashti Chaturthi and completing the fast at moonrise, the obstacles of those devotees are removed who call upon Ganesha in inner pain. The devotee who observes this vrata with faith receives the blessing of Kriti Ganesha.

## Significance

**Sankashti** means — the one who removes obstacles. This vrata is observed on the Krishna Chaturthi of every month, and each month a particular form of Ganesha is worshipped.

**Kriti** — this is that form of Ganesha which is the symbol of the power of creation and love. Kriti means — the one who acts, the one who creates. In the katha of Ballal, Ganesha shaped the broken image anew — this is the Kriti-power: to recreate with love what has been shattered within.

**Ballaleshvara and the Ashtavinayaka:** Among the Ashtavinayaka of Maharashtra — the eight svayambhu Ganesha pilgrimage sites — Ballaleshvara (Pali) is a foremost tirtha. It is the only Ashtavinayaka named after a devotee. This is the glory of this katha.

**The context of Jyeshtha:** The blazing heat of Jyeshtha and Nirjala Ekadashi — the tapas of this month is deep. Ballal's fast was not by any rule, but from the anguish of love — and that is why it was the most extraordinary of all. Jyeshtha Sankashti teaches: even when outer means are absent, the door of bhakti is not closed.

**The moonrise-parana rule:** The fast of Sankashti Chaturthi is broken only after sighting the moon. In Ballal's katha too, Ganesha appeared on the night of the moonrise of that Chaturthi — this is no coincidence.

## Vidhi

- **Materials:** Modak or laddus (Ganesha's beloved naivedya); durva grass (21 durva); red flowers; sindura; ghee lamp.
- **Morning puja:** Bathe and install the image or picture of Kriti Ganesha; perform panchopachara puja — water, fragrance, flowers, incense, lamp.
- **Offerings:** Offer 21 or 3 durva grass to Ganesha; offer modak or laddus as bhoga.
- **Katha:** Hear the katha of Ballal.
- **Fasting:** Observe a fast from sunrise to moonrise; abstain from grains; fruit-based food (fruits, milk, sabudana) may be taken once.
- **Evening — moonrise:** Check the moonrise time for that tithi on DrikPanchang; await moonrise and upon sighting the moon offer arghya with water and Ganesha's name.
- **Breaking the fast:** No parana before sighting the moon; after the moon arghya, take food.
- **2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Jyeshtha Krishna Chaturthi).
