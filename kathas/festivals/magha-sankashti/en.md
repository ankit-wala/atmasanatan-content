## Katha

It was the month of Magha.

King Somakanta had been observing the Sankashti Chaturthi vrata every month for twelve years — without a single lapse. Each month on the Krishna Chaturthi: waterless fast from sunrise to moonrise, Ganesha puja, sighting of the moon, and parana. Over twelve years, one by one, each link in the chain had been added.

Now his twelfth year was about to be completed.

The twelfth Sankashti arrived — and it was the one in Magha.

But on that very day the king fell ill with such a serious disease that he could not even rise from his bed. Neither a fast was possible, nor puja, nor sighting the moon. He lay helpless on his bed. His mind was in agony — "Twelve years of vrata, and this final link — and it has broken."

He called for his queen.

He said — "Queen, today is Magha Sankashti. I am unable to keep the vrata. Can you perform it on my behalf?"

The queen accepted immediately.

She began her fast from sunrise. In the evening she performed Ganesha puja — offered modak, durva, flowers. At night, after sighting the moon, she observed parana.

---

That night Ganesha appeared in the queen's dream.

His form was extraordinary — neither Ekadanta, nor Trinetra, nor in any one specific form — he was in the Maha Sankashti form of Magha, which is the summation of all forms.

He said —

*"Queen, give this message to the king — all eleven Sankashti vratas that he performed with love were moving toward this one. Magha Sankashti is the link that joins them all. Just as Kashi is the most exalted of all pilgrimage sites, just as the Ganga is the most sacred of all rivers — so too is this Magha Sankashti the crown of all the monthly Sankashti vratas. You performed this vrata on the king's behalf — and in this way the two of you together completed the cycle that had been woven across twelve months. By morning the king will rise."*

The queen opened her eyes.

As dawn came the king sat up in his bed. The illness that had been there through the night — was gone. As though some old knot had been untied.

---

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said — "Magha Krishna Chaturthi is the Maha Sankashti among all Sankashti vratas. The one who performs Ganesha puja and the moonrise vrata with devotion on this day has years-old obstacles and difficulties destroyed at the root."

## Significance

Magha is a special month in the Hindu calendar — Mauni Amavasya, Ratha Saptami, and Vasant Panchami all fall in this month. The sun is at this time on its uttarayana journey — nights grow shorter, days lengthen. Just as light increases in the sky, so too do the dark obstacles of life dissolve through Ganesha's grace on Magha Sankashti.

**The distinction of Maha Sankashti:** Each of the twelve Sankashti vratas of the year involves worship of a particular Ganesha form. But Magha's Sankashti stands above all — it is called 'Maha Sankashti.' It is not that the others are lesser; rather this one is the summit of their accumulated power.

**The destruction of old obstacles:** The very meaning of Sankashti is protection from obstacles. But Maha Sankashti removes those obstacles that have persisted for years — those that are not everyday matters but are seated at the root. This vrata is especially for those who have been struggling with a difficulty for a long time.

**The complete twelve-Sankashti vrata:** If one performs all twelve Sankashti vratas through the year, the full samvatsara vrata is completed at Magha. This is called the purnahuti of the **Samvatsara Sankashti Vrata**. If only one Sankashti is to be performed in the year — it is the one in Magha.

## Vidhi

- **Fasting:** Observed on Magha Krishna Chaturthi; fast runs from sunrise to moonrise — waterless (nirjal) is most excellent; fruit-based diet is also acceptable; grains and salt are prohibited.
- **At dawn:** Rise before sunrise, bathe, and take the sankalpa — "I observe the Sankashti vrata on Magha Krishna Chaturthi; I will break my fast only after sighting the moon."
- **Installing Ganesha:** Spread a red cloth and install the image or picture of Ganesha; place a water-pot nearby.
- **Offerings:** Red flowers, durva (21 durva are especially dear to Ganesha), modak, laddus, betel leaf, sindura, incense, lamp.
- **Puja:** Worship with panchopachara or shodasopachara; chant Ganesha stotra, Ganesha Chalisa, or *OM Gam Ganapataye Namah*; read or hear the katha of Magha Sankashti.
- **Moonrise:** At night when the moon rises, first sight the moon, offer arghya with water, and offer salutations to Ganesha — the vrata is not complete without sighting the moon; if clouds are present, observe parana at the designated moonrise time.
- **Breaking the fast:** After sighting the moon, first take Ganesha's prasad (modak), then drink water and take food.
- **Special:** For devotees who perform all twelve Sankashti vratas through the year, there is a tradition of udyapana at Magha Sankashti — feeding of brahmanas, distribution of modak, and darshana at a Ganesha temple.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.

---

**Vighnashana shloka:**

> वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटिसमप्रभ ।
> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda, vast of form, radiant as a crore of suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.

---

**Opening shloka of the Sankashti-nashana Ganesha Stotra:**

> प्रणम्य शिरसा देवं गौरीपुत्रं विनायकम् ।
> भक्तावासं स्मरेन्नित्यमायुःकामार्थसिद्धये ॥

Praṇamya śirasā devaṃ gaurīputraṃ vināyakam |
bhaktāvāsaṃ smarennityam āyuḥkāmārthasiddhaye ||

Meaning: Bowing the head to Gauri's son Vinayaka, the devotees who remember him daily attain fulfilment of longevity, desire, and material wellbeing — all three.
