## Katha

It was the month of Phalgun. The lanes of Nandgaon were still stained with gulal. The day of Holi had passed, yet the celebration in Braj had not ended — because Krishna's color was so deep that a single day could not hold it.

That year, on the day of Holi, Shri Krishna and his friends had traveled from Nandgaon to Barsana. There Radharani and her companions welcomed them with sticks — and this play was so full of joy that both villages wrapped themselves around each other in laughter. There was color, there was laughter, there was song — and above it all, the golden winter-end light of Phalgun.

But how could it end in a single day?

On the second day the play moved to Nandgaon. On the third, in the forest groves of Vrindavan, the Gopis came together to celebrate with color. On the fourth day, thousands of devotees drowned in color on the banks of the Yamuna in the courtyard of the Dwarkadhish temple in Mathura. And on the fifth day — Phalgun Krishna Panchami — near the Rangji temple in Vrindavan, the moment everyone had awaited finally arrived.

Shri Krishna himself stood on an elevated place. In his hands was golden gulal — not the kind found in any market, but gulal made of love. He raised that fistful of gulal and released it into the air — and in an instant, all of Vrindavan was bathed in a golden-red radiance.

The companions of Radharani say that on that day even the flowers were colored, even the waters of the Yamuna turned rose-pink, and even the sky was dyed in the color of Radharani's love.

This was Rang Panchami. In Braj it is believed that on this day Krishna's color was not merely gulal, but the color of the five rasas of devotion — shanta, dasya, sakhya, vatsalya, and madhurya. Each devotee received the color belonging to the rasa in which they were absorbed. One who was a jnani received the color of shanta peace; one who was a servant received the color of dasya surrender; one who was a friend received the color of sakhya companionship; one who worshipped in the feeling of a mother received the color of vatsalya love — and one who was submerged in love like Radha received the color of madhurya, the color that never fades.

The tradition of Braj holds that any devotee who takes part in this divine festival of color on Rang Panchami with a pure heart receives the blessing of all five rasas of the Lord's love — and their life is forever adorned in those five colors.

## Significance

Rang Panchami is an expression of that folk-consciousness of Braj which believes that the Lord's celebration cannot be contained in a single day. Where Holi is the festival of burning the old — the burning of ego in the Holika Dahan — Rang Panchami is the festival of coloring the new. This five-day celebration is a tradition of worshipping the five elements — earth, water, fire, air, and sky — through color. When color rises in Vrindavan, it feels as though the earth itself is rejoicing, the Yamuna is dancing, and the gods are showering gulal from the sky.

The second significance of Rang Panchami is that it reminds us of the five rasas of devotion. In the Bhagavata tradition, these are the five levels of bhakti — shanta (the serene mind), dasya (the spirit of service), sakhya (the love of friendship), vatsalya (a mother's affection), and madhurya (the love of the beloved). On the day of Rang Panchami, when we offer color to another, we unknowingly live these very rasas — vatsalya with a child, sakhya with a friend, madhurya with a beloved. Krishna transformed color from a mere celebration into a form of yoga.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Rise early, bathe, and put on clean clothes.
- **Morning puja:** Before the image of Shri Krishna and Radharani, offer a lamp, flowers, and gulal; perform an abhishek with panchamrita; perform the aarti with invocations "Radhe-Radhe" and "Jai Shri Krishna."
- **Colors:** Use only natural colors made from Tesu, rose, and marigold flowers — avoid chemical dyes; offer gulal to Radha-Krishna first, then play with color among family and loved ones.
- **Spirit of the day:** Hold no ill will toward anyone — any distance or misunderstanding from the five days of celebration should be washed away with this final color.
- **Bhajan-Kirtan:** Sing phag-songs and Holi verses of Braj — "Holi Khele Raghuvira Avadh Mein," "Barse Bhige Chunarwali," or "Aaj Biraj Mein Holi Re Rasiya"; in the evening, sit with family and sing Krishna's lila-bhajans.
- **Special:** If possible, take darshan at the Rangji temple and the Banke Bihari temple in Vrindavan — both hold a special celebration on this day.

## Mantras

**1. Vasudeva Mantra**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय ॥

Om namo bhagavate vasudevaya

Meaning: O Lord Vasudeva (Shri Krishna), I bow to you.

**2. Radha-Krishna Rasa Stuti**

> गोपीभिर्निजकान्तसंगरसिकैर्व्यक्तान्वितं ते पदं
> मायामानुषलीलया वितरतां मोदं परं भक्तिमान् ।
> त्वद्गोविन्द मनोहरं सुखकरं राधासमेतं हरे
> नित्यं नौमि वृन्दावने रसरतं राधेश्वरं केशवम् ॥

Gopibhir nijakanta sangrasikair vyaktanvitam te padam,
mayamanushalilaya vitaratam modam param bhaktiman.
tvad govinda manoharam sukhakaram radhasametam hare,
nityam naumi vrindavane rasaratam radheshvaram keshavam

Meaning: Absorbed in rasa with the Gopis, granting supreme bliss to devotees through his maya-human lila, ever immersed in the rasa of love in Vrindavan together with Radha — the enchanting, joy-bestowing Shri Krishna — I bow to him always.
