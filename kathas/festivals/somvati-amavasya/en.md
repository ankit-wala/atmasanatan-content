## Katha

There is an episode in the Mahabharata that is also cited in the Skanda Purana.

Yudhishthira once asked Bhishma Pitamah — *"Pitamah, the sanctity of the Amavasya tithi is known to all. But is there an Amavasya that is more meritorious than the rest?"*

Bhishma replied — *"Yes, Yudhishthira. When Amavasya falls on a Monday, it is called Somvati Amavasya. Soma — the Moon — sustains the realm of the ancestors. The Vedas declare: Somo rājā pitṝṇām — Soma is the king of the ancestors. When the essence of the Amavasya tithi and the lunar energy of Monday come together, the gateway to the ancestral realm stands especially open. Tarpan offered on that day reaches the ancestors many times more than on ordinary days."*

Bhishma continued — *"On this day, the worship of the peepal tree holds special importance. The peepal — the ashvattha — is the foremost among all trees. Brahma dwells in its roots, Vishnu in its trunk, and Shiva in its branches. The ancestors too dwell in this tree — especially those souls whose sons and daughters offer them tarpan. Circumambulating the peepal, offering water, and tying a thread — all of these bring peace to the ancestors."*

Along with this teaching, a story of a woman is also widely told.

In a certain city there lived a woman named Sumitra. She was married, her household was prosperous, yet she had no children. Husband and wife both observed vrats and performed puja for the blessing of a child, but nothing came of it.

One day a sadhu arrived. He told Sumitra — *"There is a pitru-rin in your household — a debt to your ancestors. Among your ancestors and those of your husband, certain souls remain unsatisfied; their tarpan is not offered regularly. As long as pitru-rin remains, the blessing of children is obstructed."*

Sumitra asked — *"What is the remedy?"*

The sadhu said — *"Begin from the next Somvati Amavasya. On every Somvati Amavasya, perform pitru-tarpan — for the ancestors of both yourself and your husband. Circumambulate the peepal tree 108 times. Offer water and milk. Tie a thread. And on that day, feed a Brahmin."*

Sumitra observed this vrat on every Somvati Amavasya that came during the year. The ancestors were pleased. They granted a boon. By the end of that year, a son was born in Sumitra's home.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that pitru-tarpan and peepal worship on Somvati Amavasya is the most accessible path to liberation from pitru-rin.

## Significance

Amavasya is the tithi of the ancestors. On this day, the veil between the ancestral realm and the mortal world is at its thinnest. Tarpan offered on this day — with water, sesame, and barley — reaches the ancestors directly.

On a Monday, this is heightened further. Soma — the Moon — is the provider of nourishment for the ancestors. *"Annādbhavanti bhūtāni parjanyādannasambhavaḥ"* — this cycle begins with Soma. Soma nourishes the plants, the plants nourish living beings, and those who are ancestors are likewise sustained by this lunar energy. When Amavasya and Monday coincide, the conjunction is extraordinarily meritorious.

The peepal tree holds a singular place. In the Bhagavad Gita, Sri Krishna declares — *"Aśvatthaḥ sarvavṛkṣāṇām"* — among trees, I am the peepal. Devas, ancestors, and rishis dwell in this tree. Circumambulating it and offering water brings peace to the ancestors of the entire family.

Somvati Amavasya comes only three or four times a year — its rarity makes it all the more meritorious.

## Vidhi

- **Fasting:** Fast or single meal on this day; grains are avoided; fruits are appropriate.
- **At dawn:** Bathe in a sacred river, tank, or at home; gather materials for pitru-tarpan — water, black sesame, barley, kusha grass.
- **Pitru-tarpan:** Face south; mix black sesame, barley, and kusha into the water; offer jalanjali with the right hand pouring from the thumb side (pitru-tirtha); recall each ancestor by name and offer water three times — father, mother, paternal grandfather, paternal grandmother, maternal grandfather, maternal grandmother; offer one collective jalanjali for unknown ancestors. Note: tarpan without black sesame is considered incomplete — sesame is most dear to the ancestors.
- **Peepal puja:** Offer water and milk at the root of the peepal tree; offer yellow flowers, turmeric, and sandalwood; light a lamp; tie a red or yellow thread around the trunk with a prayer for ancestral peace; perform 108 circumambulations (minimum 11 or 21).
- **For women:** Married women circumambulate the peepal for their husband's long life, in the spirit of Vat Savitri.
- **Brahmin bhoj:** Feed a Brahmin on this day — it is the most direct means of satisfying the ancestors.
- **Breaking the fast:** The following morning after bath.
- **Note:** Auspicious rites (weddings, graha-pravesh) do not take place on Amavasya — this day belongs to the ancestors.

## Mantras

**Pitru-tarpan mantra:**

> ॐ पितृभ्यः स्वधायिभ्यः स्वधा नमः ।
> मातामहेभ्यः स्वधायिभ्यः स्वधा नमः ॥

oṃ pitṛbhyaḥ svadhāyibhyaḥ svadhā namaḥ | mātāmahebhyaḥ svadhāyibhyaḥ svadhā namaḥ ||

Meaning: O ancestors, who are rightful recipients of svadha (tarpan) — to you, svadha and salutation. O maternal ancestors, who are rightful recipients of svadha — to you, svadha and salutation.

**Peepal vandana:**

> अश्वत्थ सर्ववृक्षाणां देवानां त्वं प्रियो मतः ।
> मूले ब्रह्मा त्वयि विष्णुः शाखासु हरिरीश्वरः ॥

aśvattha sarvavṛkṣāṇāṃ devānāṃ tvaṃ priyo mataḥ | mūle brahmā tvayi viṣṇuḥ śākhāsu harirīśvaraḥ ||

Meaning: O Ashvattha (peepal), you are the most beloved among all trees and among the devas. Brahma dwells in your roots, Vishnu in your trunk, and Hari-Ishvara in your branches.
