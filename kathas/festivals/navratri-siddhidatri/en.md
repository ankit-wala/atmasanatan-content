## Katha

It is the ninth day of the Navratri journey — and here this journey reaches its completion.

Nine days ago we met Shailaputri — the mountain's daughter, the power of rebirth. Then the tapasya of Brahmacharini, the radiance of Chandraghanta, the creation of Kushmanda, the motherly love of Skandamata, the valour of Katyayani, the darkness of Kalaratri — and the purity of Mahagauri. That was the journey through nine forms.

And now we have arrived at her — **Maa Siddhidatri**. She who grants all siddhis.

---

But this story is from long before Navratri. This story is from before the very beginning of creation.

Before creation — there was no sun, no earth, no gods, no demons. There was only an infinite void. And in that void dwelt — **Adi Shakti**. Formless, unmanifest, all-pervading.

She willed — *let there be creation.*

And from her resolve appeared — Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva.

Brahma received the burden of creation. Vishnu, of sustenance. And Shiva, of dissolution.

But Shiva thought — *only dissolution? This is not enough.*

He wished to know the supreme mystery of creation — that transcendent knowledge through which all things become possible.

---

Shiva performed tapasya — intense, prolonged, extraordinary.

He knew that this knowledge is not asked for — it is attained. And the only path to attainment is — worship.

In the tradition of the Devi Bhagavata Purana, it is said that Shiva worshipped Adi Shakti. He became absorbed in blazing sadhana — no food, no water, no rest.

Then Adi Shakti — Maa Siddhidatri — appeared.

---

She said to Shiva — *"Ask."*

Shiva said — *"Mother, give me all that will make me complete."*

The Mother smiled.

And then she bestowed upon Shiva the **Ashta Siddhis**: Anima (the power to become infinitely small), Mahima (the power to become immeasurably large), Garima (the power to become extremely heavy), Laghima (the power to become extremely light), Prapti (the power to reach anywhere instantly), Prakamya (the power to obtain any desired object), Ishitva (supreme sovereignty), and Vashitva (the power to bring all beings under one's will).

Along with these, she also bestowed upon him the **Nav Nidhis** — the nine divine treasures.

---

But even this was not enough.

The Mother gave something more.

She established herself in Shiva's vama anga — his left side.

Shiva and Shakti became one.

What had been two became one — **Ardhanarishvara**. Half Shiva, half the Goddess. One body, two beings.

And from that moment it is said — *"Without Shakti, Shiva is a corpse."* Until the Mother's touch is present, even Shiva is incomplete. It is only by the grace of Maa Siddhidatri that Shiva became wholly Shiva.

---

Navami of Navratri — the nine-day journey reaches its fullness here.

We had set out from Shailaputri — from the root of the mountain. And now we have arrived at Maa Siddhidatri — at that summit from which there is nothing further to seek.

The form of Maa Siddhidatri: enthroned upon a lotus, four arms — the right hands holding her Sudarshan Chakra (the spinning disc of light) and Kaumodaki (the mace), the left hands holding the Panchajanya (the conch) and a lotus. Around her, the siddhas, gandharvas, yakshas, nagas, and even the asuras bow in her worship.

In the tradition of the Devi Bhagavata Purana, it is said that a devotee who worships Maa Siddhidatri with devotion on Navami attains all siddhis from Anima to Vashitva, and their Navratri journey is fulfilled.

## Significance

**The Ashta Siddhis — in brief:**
Maa Siddhidatri is the mistress of eight siddhis —
- **Anima** — the power to assume an infinitely subtle form
- **Mahima** — the power to assume a boundlessly vast form
- **Garima** — the power to become extremely heavy
- **Laghima** — the power to become lighter than air
- **Prapti** — the power to reach any desired place instantaneously
- **Prakamya** — the power to obtain any desired object
- **Ishitva** — supreme sovereignty over creation
- **Vashitva** — the power to bring all beings under one's will

**Ardhanarishvara — the unity of Shiva and Shakti:**
Ardhanarishvara is not merely an image — it is a philosophy. The union of Shiva (consciousness) and Shakti (energy) is the very foundation of creation. Without Shakti, consciousness is inert; without consciousness, Shakti is uncontrolled. It is by the grace of Maa Siddhidatri that this balance becomes possible.

**Navami — the culminating point of Navratri:**
Navami is the final and supreme day of Navratri. Shailaputri laid the foundation of the journey — Siddhidatri gave it its completion. She is the beginning and the end — she was before creation, and will remain even after liberation.

**Kanya pujan — the special rite of Navami:**
Kanya pujan is considered mandatory on Navami. Nine girls are honoured as the nine forms of the nine Goddesses — worshipped, fed, and given dakshina. This is the crowning act of the Navratri vrat.

**Colour: light blue or purple** — symbol of spiritual height, peace, and siddhi.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe in Brahma Muhurta and wear light blue or purple clothing — the colour of Navami; puja is best performed early morning and kanya pujan completed before midday.
- **Puja setup:** Install an image or idol of Maa Siddhidatri; meditate upon her lotus-seated, four-armed form; light a lamp of pure ghee and offer incense.
- **Offerings:** White and purple flowers (lotus is especially dear); halwa, puri, and black chana as the traditional Navratri prasad.
- **Recitation:** Chant the dhyana shloka and Ya Devi Stotra (see Mantras); recite "Om Siddhidatryai Namah" 108 times.
- **Kanya pujan (mandatory):** Invite nine girls under ten years of age; honour them as the nine Goddess-forms — wash their feet, apply tilak, offer halwa-puri-chana, and give dakshina; Navratri is not complete without this rite.
- **Breaking the fast:** After kanya pujan, receive prasad to perform parana; this completes the udyapan of the nine-day vrat.
- **Note:** Vijayadashami (Dussehra) falls the next day (Dashami) — Navami night is for the Mother's worship; the following day celebrates victory of dharma.
- **2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Ashwin Shukla Navami — ~19 October 2026).

## Mantras

**Siddhidatri Stuti Shloka:**

> सिद्धगन्धर्वयक्षाद्यैरसुरैरमरैरपि ।
> सेव्यमाना सदा भूयात् सिद्धिदा सिद्धिदायिनी ॥

Siddhagandharva yakṣādyair asurair amarair api |
sevyamānā sadā bhūyāt siddhidā siddhidāyinī ||

Meaning: "She who is ever served by the siddhas, gandharvas, yakshas, asuras, and the gods — may that Siddhidayini, Maa Siddhidatri, forever grant us siddhi."

---

**Ya Devi Stotra — salutation to Maa Siddhidatri:**

> या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ सिद्धिदात्री रूपेण संस्थिता ।
> नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः ॥

Yā devī sarvabhūteṣu māṃ siddhidātrī rūpeṇa saṃsthitā |
namastasyai namastasyai namastasyai namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: "To the Goddess who abides in all beings in the form of Maa Siddhidatri — salutations, salutations, salutations."
