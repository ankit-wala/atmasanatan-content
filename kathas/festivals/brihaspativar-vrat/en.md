## Katha

In the folk tradition of Brihaspativar Vrat, it is said that there was once a king — wealthy, renowned, a protector of his subjects. His kingdom had abundance and happiness. He performed worship daily. He was proud of his devotion.

One Thursday morning, as the king sat in his prayer room, an elderly sage came to his door.

The sage said — *"O King, give alms."*

The king sent word through his doorkeeper — *"The worship of the deities is underway. This is not the time for alms."*

The sage departed.

No one knew that the sage was Brihaspati Dev himself — the Guru of the gods, the lord of knowledge and dharma.

After that day, calamity began to fall upon the king's life, one after another. The storehouses began to empty. The queen fell ill. There was no rain in the kingdom; the crops withered. The king's decisions went wrong — the merchants who had stood with him began to leave.

The king could not understand — I perform worship daily, so why this misfortune?

One day the queen went to the market in disguise. There she met an elderly woman who was learned in vrat-kathas. The queen told her of her sorrow.

The elderly woman said — *"Devi, your husband has insulted the Guru. Brihaspati Dev himself came to the door and was turned away. Brihaspati is the lord of the guru-principle — disrespect for the guru closes the path to knowledge, and where there is no knowledge, wealth too cannot remain. The remedy is this — observe the vrat for twenty-one Thursdays. Wear yellow garments. Worship the banana tree. Offer chana dal and jaggery. Listen to the Brihaspativar katha. Abandon pride and take refuge in the Guru."*

The queen came home and told the king everything.

The king understood his mistake. He began the vrat the following Thursday. He wore yellow garments. He offered water to the banana tree, worshipped it with turmeric and sandalwood paste, and distributed chana and jaggery as prasad. The queen also observed the vrat alongside him. Both, with devotion and humility, performed the sadhana one Thursday after another.

On the twenty-first Thursday, the same elderly sage came again to the door.

This time the king himself came to the door. He touched the sage's feet and said — *"Swami, I made a mistake. I turned you away that day — that was my transgression. Now whatever I have, all of it is yours."*

The sage — who was Brihaspati Dev — lifted him up and said — *"O King, the wealth and renown that come through the Guru's grace are permanent. What is earned through pride is fleeting. Your dharma has returned. Prosperity will come again to the kingdom."*

That year the rains came. The crops flourished. The queen recovered. Happiness spread through the kingdom once more.

In the folk tradition of Brihaspativar Vrat, it is said that whoever observes the Thursday vrat with devotion and humility — knowledge, children, and prosperity abide in their home.

## Significance

Brihaspati is the Guru of the gods — Devaguru. He is the lord of knowledge, dharma, discernment, and grace. Where Budha is intellect — sharp and practical — Brihaspati is wisdom — deep, dharma-aligned, and guiding.

This is why the Thursday vrat is not merely the worship of a planet — it is the worship of the guru-principle. Whoever wishes to receive the blessings of a guru, parents, or God in life — for them, Brihaspativar Vrat is especially fruitful.

Vishnu also has a special connection with this day. In some traditions, Thursday is primarily the day of Vishnu's worship — because Brihaspati is regarded as a devotee of Vishnu, and Vishnu is the upholder of dharma. The simultaneous worship of both on this day is sanctioned by the shastras.

The banana tree holds a special place in this vrat. The banana is considered Vishnu's beloved tree. Its worship brings sons, prosperity, and auspiciousness in sacred rites to the household.

Yellow is Brihaspati's colour — the light of knowledge, the richness of gold. In the vrat, everything is yellow — garments, flowers, food, chana and jaggery.

## Vidhi

- **Fasting:** Complete fast or eating once; in some traditions banana and water suffice; abstaining from salt is the practice in several traditions.
- **Sankalpa:** On the first Thursday, take the sankalpa — state the purpose and the number of Thursdays (twenty-one is most common; sixteen in some traditions; or until the wish is fulfilled).
- **At dawn:** Rise before sunrise, bathe, and wear yellow garments.
- **Banana tree worship:** Offer water, yellow flowers, turmeric, and sandalwood paste to the banana tree in the courtyard or at the temple.
- **Puja:** Light incense and a ghee lamp before the images of Brihaspati Dev and Vishnu; offer yellow flowers and tulsi leaves; offer chana dal and jaggery as bhog.
- **Mantra:** Chant *Om Brim Brihaspataye Namah* at least 108 times; listen to or read the Brihaspativar Vrat katha together with family — this is considered an essential part of the vrat.
- **Prasad and charity:** Distribute chana dal, jaggery, and banana as prasad; donate bananas to a brahmin, at the temple, or to someone in need — considered highly meritorious; yellow foods (turmeric-tinted rice, besan halwa) hold special importance.
- **What to avoid:** Do not wash or cut hair on this day.
- **Breaking the fast:** Paran on Friday morning after sunrise, with gratitude to Brihaspati Dev and Vishnu.
- **2026 dates:** Confirm on DrikPanchang.

## Mantras

**Brihaspati Stuti:**

> देवानां च ऋषीणां च गुरुं काञ्चनसन्निभम् ।
> बुद्धिभूतं त्रिलोकेशं तं नमामि बृहस्पतिम् ॥

devānāṃ ca ṛṣīṇāṃ ca guruṃ kāñcanasannibham | buddhibhūtaṃ trilokeśaṃ taṃ namāmi bṛhaspatim ||

Meaning: I bow to Brihaspati Dev — the Guru of gods and sages, radiant as gold, the very embodiment of wisdom, the lord of the three worlds.

---

**Vishnu Mantra — Thursday worship of Vishnu:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya

Meaning: Salutations to Bhagavan Vasudeva (Vishnu). This twelve-syllable mantra is the most celebrated mantra for the worship of Vishnu.

---

**Brihaspati Bija Mantra:**

> ॐ बृं बृहस्पतये नमः

oṃ bṛṃ bṛhaspataye namaḥ

Meaning: Salutations to Brihaspati Dev. This bija mantra awakens the energy of the planet Brihaspati and opens the path to knowledge, the Guru's grace, and prosperity.
