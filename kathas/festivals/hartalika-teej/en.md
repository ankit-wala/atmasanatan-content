## Katha

Parvati, daughter of the Himalayas, had decided from childhood that Shiva was her husband.

Even as a young girl she had one unwavering vow — "Shiva. No one else." She would carve Shiva's name on stones, gather bel leaves from the forest to place on a Shivalinga, and sit in meditation for hours. Her mother Maina and father Himalaya would watch her with quiet worry.

One day, the divine sage Narada arrived at Himalaya's palace.

Narada said — "O Lord of Mountains, I have studied Parvati's birth chart. There is a most worthy match for her — Lord Vishnu himself. He is all-sufficient, the protector of all creation. What greater fortune could there be?"

Himalaya was delighted. He agreed at once.

When Parvati learned of this, her heart grew heavy. She went to her dear sakhis — Jaya and Vijaya — and shared her grief. She said — "Friend, I have chosen Shiva as my husband in my heart. If this other marriage goes forward, living will be impossible for me."

Her sakhis saw her pain. They said — "Do not worry. We will leave under cover of night."

That night, when the entire palace lay sleeping — the sakhis lifted Parvati up and carried her deep into the forest. They stopped at a riverbank where the trees grew thick and no one would come.

It is from this act that the festival gets its name — **Hartalika.** "Harat" means to carry away, to take by force. "Aalika" means female friend, sakhi. Together: the friend who carried Parvati away.

---

That night was **Bhadrapada Shukla Tritiya** — under the Hasta Nakshatra.

At the riverbank, Parvati shaped a Shivalinga with her own hands from the damp river sand. She gathered forest flowers and placed them on it. She offered leaves. She lit a lamp.

Then she took her vow — "No water, no food — not until Shiva appears before me."

The vigil lasted through the whole night. Bhajans rose in the darkness. Parvati remained absorbed in meditation — one name on her lips alone.

This was no ordinary night. What happened here was the culmination of years of fierce tapas. Parvati had been practicing austerities for ages to win Shiva — sitting amidst five fires in the blazing summer heat, standing under the open sky through the monsoon rains, immersing herself in cold water through winter. She had even given up eating leaves. For this she came to be called **Aparna** — she who does not eat even a leaf.

And now this night was the peak of it all.

Before dawn, in the depths of that dark forest — Lord Shiva appeared before her.

He said — "Devi, your tapas has drawn me here. Your love and your resolve are both extraordinary. I accept you as my wife. You will be my other half."

Parvati's eyes filled with tears. Her sakhis overflowed with joy.

---

At dawn, Himalaya, sick with worry, had searched through the forest until he found his daughter — the sand Shivalinga before her, her eyes radiant from the night's austerity, her face utterly at peace.

His heart filled.

Himalaya joined his hands — "Daughter, you asked for something and you received it. Now it is my wish too that Lord Shiva become our son-in-law." He withdrew the proposal to Vishnu and accepted Shiva as Parvati's husband.

In the Bhavishyottar Purana, Hargauri Samvade, Shiva tells Parvati — "Whatever woman observes this vrat on Bhadrapada Shukla Tritiya with true faith — keeping a waterless fast, staying awake through the night, and worshipping a hand-shaped sand Shivalinga — she receives my blessing. She gains unbroken good fortune, long life for her husband, and the sins of many lifetimes are washed away."

## Significance

"Hartalika" — haran and aalika. This festival commemorates the loyalty of a sakhi who risked everything to save her friend from an unwanted fate.

This vrat is **nirjal** — even water is forbidden. Among the three Teejs, this is the most austere. Hariyali Teej is a festival of celebration — swings, songs, mehndi. Hartalika Teej is a festival of tapas — resolve, night vigil, waterless fast.

**Unmarried women** observe this vrat — "May I be blessed with a husband like Shiva."
**Married women** observe this vrat — "May my husband have a long life; may my good fortune remain unbroken."

One distinctive feature: the Shivalinga used in this puja is not brought from a temple. It is shaped by hand from sand or earth — exactly as Parvati shaped one at the riverbank. This act of creation is at the heart of the ritual.

This vrat is observed especially in Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, and Nepal.

## Vidhi

1. **The day before Tritiya** — gather puja materials: sand or river clay, bel leaves, datura flowers, shami leaves, white flowers, a lamp, incense. Take the sankalpa (vow) of fasting.
2. **Tritiya morning** — bathe and wear clean clothes. The fast begins at sunrise.
3. **Ganesh puja first** — every puja begins with Ganesha.
4. **Shape the Shiva-Parvati forms** — with your own hands, from sand or clay, make a Shivalinga and a small image of Parvati. This is handmade, not bought.
5. **Shodashopachara puja** — worship with the sixteen offerings: water, sandalwood paste, flowers, incense, lamp, food offering, dakshina, and so on.
6. Offer **bel leaves, datura, shami leaves** to Shiva; offer **red and white flowers and shringar items** to Parvati.
7. **Listen to the Hartalika Teej katha.**
8. **All-night vigil** — sing Shiva-Parvati bhajans, do naam-jap, read or listen to the katha.
9. **Nirjal fast** — no food, no water — from sunrise on Tritiya until paran the next morning.
10. **Chaturthi morning** — complete the puja. Immerse the sand/clay image in a river or water source. Then break the fast (paran).

## Observance

This vrat is **nirjal** — its defining characteristic is that even water is forbidden. The fast runs from sunrise on Tritiya until the puja is complete on Chaturthi morning.

**Night vigil is essential** — one must not sleep. The entire night should be spent in bhajans, katha-shravan, and naam-jap.

**Paran:** After sunrise on Chaturthi, once the morning puja is complete.

**2026 date:** Bhadrapada Shukla Tritiya — 14 September 2026 (verified on DrikPanchang).

## Mantras

**Shiva Panchakshara Mantra:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय

*Oṃ Namaḥ Śivāya*

Meaning: "Salutation to Lord Shiva." This five-syllable mantra was the one Parvati chanted through her tapas. It is chanted during the Hartalika Teej puja of the sand Shivalinga.

---

**Parvati Mantra:**

> ॐ उमायै नमः

*Oṃ Umāyai Namaḥ*

Meaning: "Salutation to Uma (Parvati)." Uma was the name Parvati's mother gave her.

---

**Sarva-Mangala Stuti (recited in Parvati's aarti):**

> सर्वमङ्गलमाङ्गल्ये शिवे सर्वार्थसाधिके ।
> शरण्ये त्र्यम्बके गौरि नारायणि नमोऽस्तु ते ॥

*Sarva-maṅgala-māṅgalye śive sarvārtha-sādhike |
Śaraṇye tryambake gauri Nārāyaṇi namo'stu te ||*

Meaning: "O auspicious one who is the source of all auspiciousness, who fulfills all purposes, who gives shelter — O three-eyed Gauri, Narayani, salutation to you."
