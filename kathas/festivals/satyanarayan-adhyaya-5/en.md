## Katha

At Naimisharanya, the great yajna was nearing its close.

Sutha Goswami had completed the four adhyayas of the katha — the story of the impoverished brahmin, of the woodcutter, of the king and the merchant, and of Sadhukara's son-in-law. In each story, Lord Satyanarayan had appeared in one form or another to protect the devotee.

Now a deep stillness had settled over the assembly.

Shaunaka Rishi rose and folded his hands. Behind him, the other sages straightened attentively in their seats. Shaunaka ji spoke:

"Sutha ji, you have told us four profound and beautiful kathas. In every one, the Lord's compassion shone forth. Now we wish to know — what is the fruit of hearing, reading, and performing the worship of this Satyanarayan Katha? Who should observe this vrat? And how is this puja to be completed?"

Sutha Goswami closed his eyes for a moment and inwardly called to mind Lord Vishnu. Then he smiled.

He said:

"Maharshi Shaunaka, you have asked exactly what ought to be asked. The tradition of the Skanda Purana holds that the katha of Lord Satyanarayan proclaims its own fruits — and this proclamation is what is called the phalashruti (the declaration of fruits/blessings)."

---

"A pregnant woman who listens to this katha with faith and reverence will be blessed with a healthy, radiant, and dharmic son.

A person who is in bondage — in prison, in debt, or caught in the chains of any adversity — is freed through the mere act of hearing this katha.

One who is far from home — on a long journey, living in a distant land — will return safely.

One who has lost wealth — through loss in trade, through calamity — will recover that wealth.

Whoever performs this puja regularly on every Purnima will never know want in their home.

And if this katha is held in a home at the time of a person's passing — that soul is given a place in the abode of Lord Satyanarayan."

---

Shaunaka Rishi then asked: "And the method of worship, Sutha ji?"

Sutha Goswami replied:

"The method is simple. On any auspicious tithi — especially Purnima — all members of the family should gather together. Arrange the puja offerings on banana leaves: panchamrit, tulsi, flowers, a lamp, and fruit. Invite a brahmin or an elder to read all five adhyayas aloud.

After the katha, prepare suji halwa — roasted semolina with ghee, sugar, and banana — as prasad, and distribute it to everyone present.

Never defer the prasad, never forget it. As we saw in the kathas of the third and fourth adhyayas — the neglect of prasad was the very root of every misfortune."

---

At the very end, Sutha Goswami said one thing more:

"O sages, Lord Satyanarayan is the union of Satya and Narayan. 'Satya' means that which always was, always is, and always will be. 'Narayan' means the ultimate refuge of all living beings.

Wherever his katha is told — he himself is present there. This is not merely poetry; it is lived experience. Those devotees who perform this puja not as mere ritual, but with sincere bhav and shraddha — in their homes, the Lord truly dwells."

---

Hearing this, Shaunaka Rishi and all the sages and seers seated in that great assembly were filled with joy. They all contemplated Lord Satyanarayan and offered their pranams.

The tradition of the Skanda Purana holds that — without this fifth adhyaya, the phalashruti, the Satyanarayan puja is considered incomplete. The five adhyayas complete a circle: beginning with a question, expanding through kathas, and coming to its fullness with the phalashruti.

## Significance

The fifth adhyaya is a singular chapter — it contains no new story.

This adhyaya is the phalashruti — the declaration of the fruits of the katha. In the tradition of Indian scriptures, every important katha, stotra, or text concludes with a phalashruti. It gives the listener the opportunity to ask: "What does what I have heard mean for my life?"

The phalashruti of the Satyanarayan Katha is especially wide in its reach — from a pregnant woman to a man imprisoned, from a traveller abroad to a soul on their deathbed — this katha offers hope to all.

The second message of this adhyaya is subtle: the Lord's katha is not merely a religious rite — it is an invitation to his presence. Where his katha is heard with devotion, he comes.

And the third message concerns prasad — the central failing of the two preceding adhyayas is here plainly corrected: the distribution of prasad is an inseparable part of this puja, not an optional act.

The fifth adhyaya is the key that closes all five doors of the Satyanarayan Katha together.

## Vidhi

- **When:** Any auspicious tithi; Purnima is traditional and brings special fruit — observing on every Purnima ensures the home never knows want (phalashruti).
- **Sankalpa:** After bathing, call Lord Satyanarayan to mind and resolve inwardly: "I am performing the puja and hearing the katha of Lord Satyanarayan."
- **Setup:** Clean the place of worship; spread banana leaves or yellow cloth; place an image or murti of Lord Satyanarayan (Vishnu) on a consecrated seat; offer yellow flowers and tulsi leaves; perform panchamrit abhisheka (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar); offer a lamp, incense, akshata, and naivedhya (fruit).
- **Katha path:** Invite a brahmin or elder to read all five adhyayas aloud; family and all present should listen together — do not leave or break the katha midway; a single sitting is required.
- **Bhav first:** Perform the puja with genuine shraddha, not merely as ritual — the Lord comes where there is sincere bhav; this is the central teaching of the fifth adhyaya.
- **Purnahuti and aarti:** When all five adhyayas are complete, offer pranams; perform the aarti of "Om Jai Jagadish Hare."
- **Prasad:** Suji halwa is the traditional prasad — roast semolina in ghee until golden, add sugar syrup, stir in pieces of banana; distribute to every person present without exception; do not defer or hesitate; neglect of prasad was the root of calamity in the third and fourth adhyayas.
- **Special:** Once the puja begins, do not leave the home until after prasad has been distributed — the lesson of Kalavati's story in the fourth adhyaya.
- **Paran:** Receive the prasad and conclude the vrat.

## Mantras

**Satyanarayan mool mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya

Meaning: O Lord Vasudeva (Krishna/Vishnu), I bow to you. This twelve-syllable mantra is the foundational mantra of Vishnu and is chanted at every Satyanarayan puja.

---

**Satyanarayan Aarti (closing):**

> ॐ जय जगदीश हरे, स्वामी जय जगदीश हरे।
> भक्त जनों के संकट, दास जनों के संकट,
> क्षण में दूर करे। ॐ जय जगदीश हरे।

oṃ jaya jagadīśa hare, svāmī jaya jagadīśa hare।
bhakta janoṃ ke saṃkaṭa, dāsa janoṃ ke saṃkaṭa,
kṣaṇa meṃ dūra kare। oṃ jaya jagadīśa hare।

Meaning: O Jagadish (Lord of the world), O Hari — glory to you. The sorrows and trials of your devotees and your servants, you dispel in an instant. This aarti is traditionally sung at the conclusion of the Satyanarayan Katha and brings the entire puja circle to its completion.
