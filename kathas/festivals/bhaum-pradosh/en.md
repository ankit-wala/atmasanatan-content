## Katha

The root katha of Pradosh Vrat — the churning of the ocean, the halahal, and the gods' worship of Shiva at the twilight hour — that is given in full in the chapter on Pradosh Vrat.

The special context of Bhaum-Pradosh is linked to a different event.

Once, a question arose among the gods — who would make the first circuit of this creation? Whoever circumambulated all of creation first would receive primacy.

Kartikeya mounted his vehicle the peacock and set out — to circumambulate the three worlds. His speed was extraordinary, his resolve firm.

Ganesha waited. He thought, and then rose and circumambulated Mother Parvati and Father Shiva. He said — "Whoever circumambulates the parents has circumambulated all of creation."

Shiva declared Ganesha first.

Kartikeya returned — weary, but when he learned of what had happened, his heart held neither resentment nor anger. He bowed to his father.

Shiva took Kartikeya's hand and said — *"Son, what you did was equally great. You circumambulated the entire cosmos — this radiance of victory, this valor, shall be yours in this creation forever. Whenever Pradosh falls on a Tuesday — Bhaum-Pradosh — on that day your radiance of victory shall rain down upon the world in a special way."*

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that the devotee who observes Bhaum-Pradosh — obstacles, enemies, and the burden of debt are lifted from their life, and a radiance of victory comes into their work.

## Significance

Bhaum means Mangal — the planet Mars, born from the womb of the earth, whose day is Tuesday. Tuesday is also the day of Kartikeya (Shiva's son, commander of the divine army) — both are indicators of strength, courage, and valor.

When Pradosh falls on a Tuesday, Shiva's grace and Kartikeya's radiance of victory are combined. This is why Bhaum-Pradosh is especially auspicious for those who wish to be free from some obstacle, enemy, or debt in their life.

The adverse influence of Mangal in the birth chart — Manglik dosha — is also said to be pacified through the sadhana of this Pradosh.

## Vidhi

The puja-vidhi of Bhaum-Pradosh is the same as the main Pradosh Vrat. For the detailed procedure, see the chapter on Pradosh Vrat.

**Special additions:**
1. After the Shiva puja, also worship Kartikeya — offer red flowers, red sandalwood paste, and fruit.
2. Donating red lentils or red garments is considered auspicious for relief from Mangal affliction.
3. Along with *Om Namah Shivaya*, chant *Om Shanmukhaya Namah* (the Kartikeya mantra).
4. Use sesame oil in the lamp — this is beloved by the planet Mangal.

## Observance

**Fasting:** Complete fast or ekahara on Trayodashi (Tuesday) — abstaining from salt is recommended.

**Paran:** Wednesday morning after sunrise, with remembrance of Shiva.

**Sankalpa:** If the vrat is for freedom from debt or removal of enemies, take a sankalpa for a fixed number of observances.

**2026 dates:** Confirm on DrikPanchang.

## Mantras

**Panchakshari Mantra — the essential chant of Pradosh:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय

oṃ namaḥ śivāya

Meaning: Salutations to Shiva. This five-syllable mantra is the symbol of Shiva's five actions — creation, sustenance, dissolution, concealment, and grace.

**Karpoorgauram Shloka — the twilight shloka of Pradosh puja:**

> कर्पूरगौरं करुणावतारं संसारसारं भुजगेन्द्रहारम् ।
> सदावसन्तं हृदयारविन्दे भवं भवानीसहितं नमामि ॥

karpūragauraṃ karuṇāvatāraṃ saṃsārasāraṃ bhujagendrahāram | sadāvasantaṃ hṛdayāravinde bhavaṃ bhavānīsahitaṃ namāmi ||

Meaning: White as camphor, the very incarnation of compassion, the essence of the world, who wears the king of serpents as a garland — who dwells forever in the lotus of the heart — I bow to that Bhava (Shiva) along with Bhavani (Parvati).
