## Katha

**Yama and Yamuna — children of the Sun:**

Suryadev had two children — a son and a daughter. They were twins.

The son was Yamaraj — the god of death and justice. His work was to account for the deeds of all living beings and, after death, to grant each one their rightful passage. His seat in Yamaloka was never left empty.

The daughter was Yamuna — who flowed across the earth as a sacred river. The land of Mathura and Vrindavan was nourished by her waters.

Between the two ran a deep bond of love — yet they rarely met. Yamaraj bore the weight of the entire world's death upon his shoulders. He longed to visit his sister, but the work never ceased.

---

**Yamuna's wait:**

Yamuna missed her brother constantly. Again and again she would send him an invitation —

"Brother, please come. Come home just once. Have a meal made by my hands."

Yamaraj would send word — "Sister, I will come. But not yet. There is work."

Yamuna would wait.

---

**That one day:**

One year, on Kartik Shukla Dvitiya, Yamaraj made a resolve — today he must go to his sister.

Yamaraj left Yamaloka. He arrived at Yamuna's door.

When Yamuna saw her brother, her heart overflowed with joy. She welcomed him — performed his aarti, washed his feet, applied a tilak on his forehead. The home had been adorned with flowers and lamps. Many dishes had been prepared.

Yamaraj sat down. Yamuna served him with her own hands — with care, with love.

Yamaraj ate.

After years of waiting — at his sister's home, a meal made by his sister's hands.

---

**The boon:**

As a brother, Yamaraj's heart filled to the brim. He said to Yamuna —

"Sister, what you have given me today is the greatest wealth in the world. The love between a brother and a sister. I wish to repay this debt. Ask — whatever you wish."

Yamuna thought for a moment. Then she said —

"Brother, grant me this boon — that any brother who goes to his sister's home on Kartik Shukla Dvitiya, dines there, and receives a tilak from her hands — may he have no fear of Yamaloka. May he be free from your torment."

Yamaraj smiled and said —

"So be it."

---

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana it is said — the brother who goes to his sister's home on this day, dines on food made by her hands, and receives a tilak from her — upon him Yamaraj bestows special grace. The fear of untimely death does not remain. A sister's prayer guards her brother.

This is why this tithi is called Yama Dvitiya — the day when Yama went to his sister's home and granted her a boon.

## Significance

**Yama Dvitiya — the meaning of the name:**
Yama (Yamaraj) + Dvitiya (the second day of Kartik Shukla Paksha) = Yama Dvitiya. This is Bhai Dooj. It is the final day of the five-day Diwali festival — Dhanteras, Narak Chaturdashi, Diwali, Govardhan Puja, and then Bhai Dooj.

**A sister's prayer as armour:**
The soul of this festival is this — a sister's love and blessing protects her brother. The tilak is a sign: "This is my brother — he has my blessing upon him." Yamaraj's boon gives this faith a divine foundation.

**The Yamuna river and this festival:**
In Mathura and Vrindavan there is a tradition of bathing in the Yamuna on this day. Bathing in the Yamuna brings merit to both brother and sister and removes the fear of Yamaloka.

**An echo of Rakshabandhan:**
On Rakshabandhan, a sister ties a rakhi on her brother — the brother pledges to protect her. On Bhai Dooj the roles are reversed — the brother goes to his sister's home, and the sister protects him by applying a tilak. The two festivals are complements of each other.

## Vidhi

1. **Go to your sister's home.** On this day eating at one's own home is considered inauspicious — the brother should go to his sister's home for the meal.
2. **Tilak:** The sister applies a tilak of roli, rice, and saffron on her brother's forehead. A tilak on the right hand is also applied.
3. **Aarti:** The sister performs aarti for her brother — with prayers for his long life and well-being.
4. **The meal:** The brother eats food cooked by his sister's hands. This is the most important moment of the festival.
5. **Gift:** The brother offers his sister a gift according to his means — clothes, jewellery, or whatever she wishes.
6. **Yamuna worship:** If one is near the Yamuna river — bathe and give in charity there.
7. **Yama tarpan (optional):** In some traditions, Yamaraj is remembered and tarpan is offered.

## Observance

**For the brother:** On this day do not eat at your own home — go to your sister's home. If the sister lives far away, face in her direction while eating, holding her in remembrance.

**For the sister:** Observe a fast before the brother arrives. After he comes, perform the tilak and aarti first, and only then eat.

**Yamuna bath:** In Mathura, Vrindavan, and places along the Yamuna, bathing in the river on this day holds special significance. Those who are not on the banks of the Yamuna may bathe at home using water from the Yamuna.

**Close of the Diwali festival:** This day is the final day of the five-day Diwali festival — the formal conclusion of the celebrations.

## Mantras

**Prayer to Yamaraj:**

> यमाय धर्मराजाय मृत्यवे चान्तकाय च ।
> वैवस्वताय कालाय सर्वभूतक्षयाय च ॥

Yamāya dharmarājāya mṛtyave cāntakāya ca |
vaivasvatāya kālāya sarvabhūtakṣayāya ca ||

Meaning: "Salutations to Yamaraj in all his forms — as Yama, Dharmaraja, Mrityu, Antaka, Vaivasvata, and Kala."

---

**Bhai Dooj tilak mantra:**

> भ्रातृभद्रं च भगिनी दीर्घायुषि शुभाशिषः ।
> यमद्वितीयायां तिलकं भ्रातुः कल्याणवर्धनम् ॥

Bhrātṛbhadraṃ ca bhaginī dīrghāyuṣi śubhāśiṣaḥ |
yamadvitīyāyāṃ tilakaṃ bhrātuḥ kalyāṇavardhanam ||

Meaning: "May the sister bring blessings (bhadra) to her brother — may this tilak of Yama Dvitiya increase the brother's well-being."
