## Katha

**Birth — Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya, the first tithi of the Treta Yuga:**

In the Bhrigu lineage, the hermitage of Maharshi Jamadagni was a centre of tapas, agnihotra, and Vedic recitation. His wife Renuka was unrivalled in pativrata-dharma — it was through the power of her sadhana that the hermitage held its divine atmosphere.

On Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya, Renuka gave birth to her fifth son.

This was no ordinary birth. The tithi itself was the commencement of the Treta Yuga — the moment when the wheel of time was entering a new age. And with that turning of the wheel, Bhagavan Vishnu descended onto this earth as the sixth avatar.

The infant held a parashu — a divine axe — in his hands from the very moment of birth. This was the weapon of Vishnu himself, which had descended with this avatar. The child was named Rama. But because he bore the parashu, he came to be known as Parashurama — Rama of the axe, the son of Jamadagni, Bhargava Rama.

---

**Kartavirya Arjuna — the adharma of Sahasrabahu:**

In the hermitage there was a divine Kamadhenu — **Surabhi** — who could grant whatever was asked of her. Through her grace, Maharshi Jamadagni was able to satisfy every guest who came to his ashrama.

In those days, the city of Mahishmati was ruled by **Kartavirya Arjuna** of the Haihaya dynasty — also called **Sahasrabahu**, for by the grace of Bhagavan Dattatreya he had received the boon of a thousand arms. He was mighty — yet with time, arrogance had also awakened within him.

One day he arrived at Jamadagni's hermitage with a great army. The sage received them as guests — through Surabhi's power, even that vast army was fed and satisfied.

But Sahasrabahu's eyes fell upon that divine cow — and greed arose.

He said — "Maharshi, give this cow to me."

Jamadagni replied calmly — "O king, this is the Kamadhenu of the hermitage. It is not possible to give her away."

Without a moment's pause, Sahasrabahu gave the order — take Surabhi by force. The trees of the ashrama were uprooted, the vines torn down.

Parashurama was not in the hermitage at that time.

---

**The dharmic wrath of Parashurama:**

When Parashurama returned, he saw — the hermitage in ruins, the ground where his father had practised tapas looking desolate, and Surabhi gone.

He learned everything from his father Jamadagni.

What arose within Parashurama was no ordinary anger. The Bhagavata Purana calls this dharma-krodha — that blazing intensity which arises when an ascetic's sacred ground is violated.

He took up his parashu and set out toward Mahishmati.

He met Sahasrabahu's army in battle. Sahasrabahu himself came forward — hurling weapons with his thousand arms. Parashurama fought as only an avatar of Vishnu could — with his parashu, he severed all of Sahasrabahu's arms. Sahasrabahu was slain. Surabhi returned to the hermitage.

---

**The murder of his father and the twenty-one vows:**

The enemies of dharma do not stop even after one defeat.

Kartavirya's sons took revenge. One day, while Parashurama was away from the hermitage, they entered and slew Maharshi Jamadagni — defenceless, deep in meditation.

Mother Renuka, calling out to her son, struck her chest twenty-one times in lamentation — her cries of grief piercing the four directions.

Parashurama returned. His father's body. His mother's lamentation.

He sat at his father's feet. And counting the twenty-one blows his mother had struck, he made a vow:

*"I shall circumambulate this earth twenty-one times and remove those adharmic, arrogant kings who crush the people and dishonour ascetics."*

Parashurama fulfilled that vow. He circumambulated the earth twenty-one times. Then he performed a yajna and gave the entire earth as a gift to Maharshi Kashyapa.

In the tradition of the Bhagavata Purana, it is said that one who worships Parashurama on Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya, beholds his parashu, and gives charity to brahmanas — the fruit of whatever is done on this akshaya tithi never diminishes.

## Significance

**The confluence of two glories:**

Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya is supremely sacred for two reasons simultaneously — it is the tithi of the beginning of the Treta Yuga, and it is the birth-date of Parashurama. On a single day, the radiance of the yugadi and the arrival of the avatar — this coincidence makes this tithi extraordinary.

**Akshaya Tritiya — the self-proven tithi:**

Any auspicious act performed on this day is fruitful without any need of a muhurta. Bathing, charity, japa, pitru-tarpana — the fruit of all of these is akshaya (imperishable). Akshaya means that which never diminishes. Parashurama himself is chiranjeevi — one of the seven immortals — and so his birth too fell on that akshaya tithi which knows no diminishment.

**The avatar of dharmic wrath:**

Among Bhagavan Vishnu's Dashavataras, Parashurama is the sixth avatar. He appeared in an age when the ruling class had forgotten its bounds. Parashurama's avatara teaches that even a brahmana must take up arms for the protection of dharma — and the anger that arises for the sake of dharma is not sin, it is tapas.

**The immortal Guru:**

Parashurama is one of the seven chiranjivis — he still resides on Mount Mahendra. He gave the knowledge of arms to Bhishma, Dronacharya, and Karna. He will become the guru of the Kalki avatar.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe and wear clean garments — yellow or white.
- **Puja setup:** Install an image of Bhagavan Parashurama; if possible, place a small parashu (axe) in the puja as a symbol of his divine weapon.
- **Abhisheka and offerings:** Perform panchamrita abhisheka (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar); offer yellow flowers, tulasi, sandalwood, incense, and a lamp.
- **Recitation:** Chant **Om Jamadagnyaya Namah** 108 times; read or listen to the Parashurama-charitra from the Bhagavata Purana.
- **Fasting:** Tradition of fasting on this day — take only fruit; break the fast after the evening puja and aarati.
- **Akshaya Tritiya acts:** This tithi is self-proven — begin new undertakings, purchase gold and silver, perform pitru-tarpana; no separate muhurta is needed for any auspicious work.
- **Charity:** Give gifts of food, scripture, a water-pot, sandalwood to brahmanas and scholars — charity given on this akshaya tithi yields imperishable fruit.
- **Special:** Sadhus, soldiers, or those who follow kshatradharma should perform puja of their weapons on this day.

## Mantras

**Parashurama Janma-stuti:**

> नमो भगवते परशुरामाय विष्णवे नमः ।
> क्षत्रान्तकाय महाबाहो भार्गवाय नमो नमः ॥

namo bhagavate paraśurāmāya viṣṇave namaḥ |
kṣatrāntakāya mahābāho bhārgavāya namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: To Bhagavan Parashurama — who is an avatar of Vishnu, the destroyer of kshatriya arrogance, the great-armed Bhargava — I bow to you.

---

**Akshaya Tritiya shloka:**

> वैशाखे शुक्लपक्षे च तृतीयायां युगादिके ।
> यदि स्नानं ददाद्दानं तदक्षय्यं भवेद्ध्रुवम् ॥

vaiśākhe śuklapakṣe ca tṛtīyāyāṃ yugādike |
yadi snānaṃ dadāddānaṃ tadakṣayyaṃ bhaveddhruvam ||

Meaning: On Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya — which is the primal tithi of the yugas — the fruit of bathing and giving charity on that day is akshaya (imperishable), this is certain.
