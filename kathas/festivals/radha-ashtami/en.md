## Katha

In the village of Raval, near Mathura, there lived a great devotee named Vrishabhanu — a devotee of the Yamuna, simple of heart.

One morning at dawn, he went to the Yamuna for his bath and meditation.

Then he saw it — floating on the surface of the water, a golden lotus was approaching. A thousand-petalled lotus, radiant as though the sun itself had descended onto the river.

Vrishabhanu moved toward it. He looked within the lotus — a newborn girl. Luminous, divine, of incomparable beauty.

He lifted her in his arms and brought her home.

His wife Kirtida saw the child — and her heart overflowed with a mother's love. She drew the girl to her chest.

But there was a concern.

The child's eyes were closed. Days passed, weeks passed, months passed — the eyes did not open.

The family's elders were called. They said: "This child does not see with worldly eyes. Her eyes will open only when she sees the one for whom she has appeared."

They waited.

---

In those days, in Gokul, Krishna was born at the home of Nanda Maharaj.

Vrishabhanu took his family — and that newborn girl — and went to Nanda Maharaj's home to offer felicitations.

There was great celebration in Nanda's home. Mother Yashoda sat holding Krishna in her arms.

Vrishabhanu's daughter was also there — still in someone's arms, eyes still closed.

Then the infant Krishna came near the girl — and leaning in, looked at her face.

In that moment — the girl's eyes opened.

For the first time. For the very first time since birth.

And what she saw — was Krishna.

Their eyes met — and in that single moment, that eternal truth was revealed which had existed even before creation.

---

These are Radha ji — daughter of Vrishabhanu, daughter of Kirtida. She appeared at Raval, and grew up at Vrindavan.

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana, Prakriti Khanda, it is said that Radha ji is the supreme shakti of Krishna — Mulaprakriti. From her flows all the creative power of this creation. One who wishes to reach Krishna must first have Radha's grace.

That is why — **Radhe-Krishna**. Radha's name comes first.

## Significance

**The glory of the name Radha:**

The Brahma Vaivarta Purana (2.49.60) says:

> अदौ राधां समुच्चार्य पश्चात्कृष्णम् उदाहरेत्

Adau Rādhāṃ samucchārya paścāt kṛṣṇam udāharet |

"The wise one should first utter the name of Radha, and then Krishna."

Radha = Rā + Dhā. "Rā" = the one who gives; "Dhā" = the one who holds. She who holds Krishna within herself — that is Radha.

**Radha ji's divine status:**

According to the Brahma Vaivarta Purana:
- **Mulaprakriti** — from whom all creative power originates.
- **Hladini Shakti** — Krishna's supreme bliss-power. Krishna cannot experience bliss without her.
- **Ekatma Dvideh** — "One soul, two bodies." Radha and Krishna are incomplete without each other.

**Radha Ashtami and Janmashtami:**

Janmashtami falls on Bhadrapada Krishna Ashtami. Radha Ashtami is Bhadrapada Shukla Ashtami — exactly fifteen days later. Just as the moon moves from the full moon toward the new moon, in these fifteen days the energy of Krishna's birth celebration reaches its fullness in Radha ji's appearance.

**Where Radha ji appeared:** Raval village (near Mathura, close to Gokul). She remained there briefly, then Vrishabhanu moved to Barsana. Barsana — where Radha ji grew up.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe and meditate on Radha ji.
- **Fasting:** Some devotees observe a fast — taking only fruit on this day.
- **Adornment:** Adorn Radha ji's image or portrait with lotus flowers, roses, and yellow flowers.
- **Abhishek:** Perform panchamrit abhisheka.
- **Radha-Krishna together:** Worship of Radha ji is not complete alone — it is fulfilled only alongside Krishna; offer tulasi, bel-patra, makhan-mishri (butter and sugar), and pedas.
- **Kirtan:** Chanting and kirtan of "Radhe Radhe" is the central devotion; recite the Radha Sahasranama or Radha-stuti; sing Radha padavalis of Vrindavan.
- **Special:** Radha Ashtami is observed with special grandeur in Barsana — darshan, magnificent puja, and raas-leela.

## Mantras

**Radhe naam — root mantra:**

> ॐ राधायै नमः

Oṃ Rādhāyai Namaḥ

Meaning: "Salutation to Shri Radha ji." — This is the simple, heartfelt mantra of Radha ji.

---

**Radha-Krishna yugal mantra:**

> ॐ श्रीराधाकृष्णाभ्यां नमः

Oṃ Śrī Rādhākṛṣṇābhyāṃ Namaḥ

Meaning: "Salutation to Shri Radha and Shri Krishna — both together."

---

**The Brahma Vaivarta Purana's declaration of precedence:**

> अदौ राधां समुच्चार्य पश्चात्कृष्णम् उदाहरेत् ।

Adau Rādhāṃ samucchārya paścāt kṛṣṇam udāharet |

Meaning: "Speak Radha's name first, then Krishna's." — Brahma Vaivarta Purana, 2.49.60
