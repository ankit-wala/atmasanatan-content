## Katha

Naimisharanya — that great forest where eighty-eight thousand sages once gathered.

Shaunaka Rishi asked Suta Goswami — "Sutaji, in this age of Kali, what is a simple remedy for liberation from the cycle of birth and death, from poverty, from suffering? Tell us a remedy that is within reach of all."

Suta Goswami replied — "Maharshi, once Narada Muni himself went to Lord Vishnu with this very question."

---

Narada Muni was wandering through the mortal world. He saw — people in distress. Someone ill, someone destitute, someone who had lost their loved ones.

Narada Muni's heart was moved. He went straight to Vaikuntha.

Lord Vishnu was seated on his throne. Narada Muni offered his pranam and asked — "Prabhu, the beings of the mortal world are suffering greatly. Is there a simple vrat by which their sorrows are removed and swift fruit is obtained?"

Lord Vishnu smiled. He said —

"Narada, you have asked an excellent question. There is a vrat — the 'Satyanarayan Vrat.' Whoever performs it — on Shravan Purnima, or any Purnima, or any auspicious occasion — all their sorrows are removed. The vrat requires three things: fasting, my worship, and listening to the katha. And the prasad must be distributed. That is all.

There is no obstacle of wealth, of caste, of learning. Whoever performs it with devotion — they receive the fruit."

---

Narada Muni heard this vrat-vidhan and returned to earth with joy.

At that time, in a certain town, there lived a poor brahmin. He went out every day to beg — but could barely fill his stomach.

One day he sat on the road, hungry, tired, and with a troubled heart.

Narada Muni was passing that way. He saw the brahmin and asked — "Vipravara, what troubles you?"

The brahmin said — "Maharshi, there is great poverty. The family is hungry. No remedy is in sight."

Narada Muni said — "There is a remedy. Perform the Satyanarayan vrat. On Purnima — fast, worship, katha. Distribute the prasad. The Lord's grace will surely come."

The brahmin listened. A resolve arose in his heart.

On the next Purnima he kept the vrat. He fasted. He performed the worship of Lord Satyanarayan — with bananas, panchamrita, flowers. He heard the katha. From what little he had he prepared prasad and distributed it.

That night, Lord Satyanarayan — in the form of an aged brahmin — appeared in his dream and said: "Vipra, your devotion is accepted."

The next day the brahmin went out to beg. Wherever he went — he found generous hands. When he came home, his wife told him — "Today, from somewhere, grain has come into the house."

Gradually the poverty receded. The brahmin continued the vrat every month — and his life was transformed.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, Reva Khanda, it is said that by hearing the Satyanarayan Vrat Katha, all desires are fulfilled, sins are destroyed, and in the end the attainment of Vishnu-loka is obtained.

## Significance

The Satyanarayan Vrat is one of the most widely observed and most accessible vrats in India.

No caste, no season, no minimum wealth required. Whoever wishes to perform it — can.

"Satyanarayan" — the union of 'Satya' (truth) and 'Narayan' (Vishnu). The form of the Lord who is the refuge of truth. The devotee who worships with truth and devotion — is heard.

The greatest feature of this vrat is the katha-shravan — this five-chapter katha has been recited in homes across generations. The family gathers, the katha is told, the prasad is shared — this is the most natural form of dharma.

## Vidhi

- **When:** Any Purnima or auspicious occasion; Shravan Purnima is the most traditional day.
- **At dawn:** Bathe and take the vow — "I am observing the Satyanarayan Vrat and puja today."
- **Fasting:** Keep the fast before the katha begins; break it only after the katha and prasad.
- **Setup:** Install an image or picture of Lord Satyanarayan (Vishnu) on a clean seat; perform abhishek with panchamrita (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar); offer flowers, tulsi, yellow cloth, and a lamp.
- **Katha shravan:** Recite or hear all five chapters of the Satyanarayan Katha — the family and those present should listen together; a single sitting is ideal.
- **Aarti:** Perform the aarti after the katha.
- **Prasad:** Prepare panjiri or sheera (wheat flour, ghee, sugar, banana); distribute to every person present — this is not optional, no one should leave without prasad.
- **Breaking the fast:** Receive the prasad and break the fast.
- **2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (~August 28, 2026, Shravan Purnima).

## Mantras

**Satyanarayan dhyana mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

*Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya*

Meaning: "Salutations to Lord Vasudeva (Vishnu)." — This is Vishnu's twelve-syllable mantra (dvadashakshari).

**Satyanarayan Aarti:**

> जय लक्ष्मीरमणा, स्वामी जय लक्ष्मीरमणा ।
> सत्यनारायण स्वामी, जन-पातक-हरणा ॥

*Jaya Lakṣmī-ramaṇā, svāmī jaya Lakṣmī-ramaṇā |
Satya-nārāyaṇa svāmī, jana-pātaka-haraṇā ||*

Meaning: "Victory to the consort of Lakshmi! O Satyanarayan, remover of devotees' sins — victory to you!"
