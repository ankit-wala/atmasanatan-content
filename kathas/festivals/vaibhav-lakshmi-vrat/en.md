## Katha

This katha is told in the folk tradition of Vaibhav Lakshmi Vrat.

In a town lived a woman named Sheela — simple, devout, fully absorbed in home and family. Her husband Ramesh did small odd jobs — sometimes something came in, sometimes nothing. The household carried debt. One child fell ill with fever again and again. Another's studies were being disrupted. Ramesh left home every day and returned in the evening exhausted — with nothing in hand.

In Sheela's heart there was neither bitterness nor reproach — only a deep weariness.

One Friday, her neighbour Sumati called Sheela over. There was a puja at her home — a picture of Lakshmi seated on a lotus cushion nearby, red flowers, white rice, a coconut, a lit lamp.

Sheela asked — *"What is this, Sumati behen?"*

Sumati said — *"Vaibhav Lakshmi Vrat. Eleven Fridays — fast, katha, puja. Place red flowers before Mother Lakshmi, offer rice, present a coconut, light one lamp. That is all — nothing more is needed. I did it — a stability came to the home that was not there before."*

Sheela returned home that day and took the sankalpa.

The following Friday Sheela bathed. She brought red hibiscus flowers from the market. In a corner of the house she placed a small picture of Lakshmi. White rice, a coconut, and a ghee lamp — she offered them all. Sitting before the Mother, she said — *"Ma, I do not want gold. Only give this much — that Ramesh finds work, that the children remain well, that there be peace in the home."*

---

The first Friday — nothing changed.

The second Friday — Ramesh received a small contract. For the first time in many months, he came home and something was in his expression.

By the fifth Friday, part of the debt had been paid. The elder child's health improved.

By the eleventh Friday, the home had changed. There was no miracle — no sudden rain of gold. Only a steady current had come: Ramesh had work, the children were well, there was an easy contentment in the home.

That day Sheela performed the udyapan. She invited eleven women — neighbours, relatives. She fed them sweet rice as bhog. She gifted each of them a piece of blouse cloth — one piece to each.

That night Sheela had a dream.

A radiant goddess — golden garments, seated on a lotus — stood before Sheela.

She said — *"Sheela, you did not ask me for gold. You asked for 'enough.' And 'enough' is the most sacred form of vaibhava — one who lives in contentment is nearest to me. You who distributed eleven blouse-pieces among those eleven women — you understood that I do not dwell in only one home; I spread when I am given."*

In the folk tradition of Vaibhav Lakshmi Vrat, it is said that a woman who observes this vrata for eleven consecutive Fridays with sincerity — with true heart, without greed, and with the spirit of giving to others in the udyapan — will find that prosperity in her home becomes lasting. This is traditional belief — the fruit depends on God's will, the devotion is in the sadhaka's hands.

## Significance

Vaibhav Lakshmi Vrat is not a vrata propounded in any ancient Purana — it is a weekly vrata that emerged from the folk tradition of the twentieth century, with deep roots in Gujarat and Maharashtra, and has spread throughout the rest of India as well. Its power lies in its simplicity.

**The connection between Friday and Lakshmi:** The planet Shukra is the karaka of wealth, beauty, and domestic happiness. The energy of Lakshmi and the energy of Shukra are two expressions of the same stream — this is why Friday is considered the finest day for Lakshmi-upasana.

**The meaning of vaibhava:** In Sanskrit, "vaibhava" means sovereignty, opulence, splendour. But in the folk tradition of this vrata, the "vaibhava" is not the accumulation of gold — it is the stability of the home, the health of the children, the peace of the marriage, the laughter in the family. This is the most expansive form of Mother Lakshmi.

**The meaning of udyapan:** Distributing eleven blouse-pieces is not merely a rite. It is this understanding: Lakshmi becomes lasting only when she moves beyond one household. For one who acts only to receive, Lakshmi is a guest. For one who acts with the spirit of giving, she becomes the Grihalakshmi.

## Vidhi

**Taking the sankalpa:**
On the first Friday, take the sankalpa — "By the grace of Mother Lakshmi, I shall observe this vrata for eleven (or twenty-one) consecutive Fridays." Once the sankalpa is taken, the sequence must not be broken.

**The procedure for each Friday:**
1. Bathe in the morning and wear clean clothes (red or yellow).
2. At the place of worship, install Mother Lakshmi's picture or idol on a red cloth.
3. Offer red flowers (hibiscus) — this is the special worship-flower of this vrata.
4. Offer white rice, a coconut, a red chunari.
5. Light a ghee lamp. Burn incense and agarbatti.
6. Offer sweet rice (kheer) or jaggery-coconut as bhog.
7. Read or listen to the katha of Vaibhav Lakshmi Vrat.
8. Perform Lakshmi aarati.
9. Fast throughout the day — take fruit or food after the evening puja.

**Udyapan (the eleventh Friday):**
Invite eleven women (neighbours, relatives, those in need). Feed them sweet rice. Gift each one a piece of blouse cloth, turmeric-kumkum, and prasad. This udyapan is an essential part of the vrata — without it the vrata is considered incomplete.

## Observance

**Duration of the vrata:** Eleven consecutive Fridays (twenty-one in some traditions). According to one's sankalpa.

**Fasting:** Nirahoma or fruit throughout the day. Avoid salty and tamasic food.

**Colour of the puja:** Red — flowers, chunari, rice with the colour red — this is the colour of Lakshmi's energy and auspicious mangalya.

**Purity of the home:** Keep the home clean on Fridays. Mother Lakshmi does not stay where there is impurity and laziness. Draw a swastika or footprints at the doorstep.

**The spirit:** The foremost requirement of this vrata is a true heart. Not greed, but devotion. Not gold, but stability. And the spirit of giving to others in the udyapan — this is the soul of this vrata.

**2026 dates:** This is a weekly vrata — it may be begun on any Friday. Beginning on the Friday of the Shukla Paksha is considered especially auspicious.

## Mantras

**1. Lakshmi Bija Mantra**

> ॐ श्रीं ह्रीं श्रीं महालक्ष्म्यै नमः ॥

oṃ śrīṃ hrīṃ śrīṃ mahālakṣmyai namaḥ

Meaning: O Shrim (the goddess of beauty and prosperity), O Hrim (the maya-shakti), O Mahalakshmi — I bow to you. Recite this bija mantra a minimum of 108 times during puja on each Friday.

**2. Lakshmi-prarthana**

> नमस्ते सर्वदेवानां वरदासि हरिप्रिये ।
> या गतिस्त्वत्प्रपन्नानां सा मे भूयात्त्वदर्चनात् ॥

namaste sarvadevānāṃ varadāsi haripriye |
yā gatistvatprapannānāṃ sā me bhūyāttvadarcanāt ||

Meaning: O beloved of Hari, giver of boons to all the devas — I bow to you. May the grace (welfare) that comes to those who take refuge in you — may that same grace come to me through your worship.
