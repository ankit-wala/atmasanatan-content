## Katha

Parvati was the daughter of Himavan — king of the Himalayas. Beautiful, luminous, serene. The light of her mother Mena's eyes. But since the day Narada Muni came to Himavan's court and said — *"Your daughter's destiny is extraordinary, and her life-partner will be extraordinary too — the one who is beyond all worldly attachment, he is hers"* — Himavan had been troubled.

He understood at once. Narada meant Shiva. That Shiva who sat deep in meditation on Kailash, who had no home, no household, who wore ash and roamed cremation grounds.

When Parvati heard this, a quiet smile came to her face. She told her mother — *"I love him. And until he accepts me, I will do tapasya."*

Mena protested. Himavan reasoned with her. But Parvati's mind was decided.

She went into the forest.

First she lived on leaves — parnaahar. Then on water alone. Then she gave up water too — no food, no water, no shelter. Only tapasya. This is why one of her names is Aparna: the one who gave up even leaves.

On Kailash, Shiva's meditation shifted. He felt the heat of Parvati's austerity. He sent the seven great sages — the Saptarishis — to her. They came to Parvati and said:

*"Devi, why do you torment yourself? What kind of husband is Shiva — he has no house, no order of life. He wanders cremation grounds, he wears serpents, he lives on alms. You deserve someone like Indra or Vishnu."*

Parvati answered them calmly:

*"Rishivara, my mind is made up. He is mine. The things you call defects — I find them more dear to me than anything. Please go."*

The seven sages left. Then Shiva himself came to her — disguised as a young brahmin. He spoke disparagingly of Shiva: his strange appearance, his rough ways, his yogi life untethered to any household.

Parvati grew angry — *"Enough, Brahmin. The one I worship will not be spoken ill of in my presence. You must leave."*

In that instant the young brahmin vanished — and standing there was Mahadeva himself. Trident, the crescent moon, the Ganga, Nandi — all present.

He said: *"Parvati, your test is complete. Your love, your resolve, your tapasya — all held firm. I accept you."*

The wedding was celebrated in the Himalayas. The gods rained down flowers. Brahma conducted the sacred rites. Mena's eyes filled with tears — her daughter had received what she had asked for.

The Skanda Purana tradition holds the merit of this five-day vrat: an unmarried girl who observes it with sincerity will find a husband like Shiva — patient, powerful, supremely devoted. A married woman who observes it will have her husband's long life and good health protected. And those for whom marriage has been obstructed will find their path cleared.

## Significance

The Jaya Parvati Vrat is not merely a love story — it is a story of resolve that does not waver under any test.

Parvati did not receive the Lord as a gift. She won him through tapasya. The sages tried to stop her — she did not stop. Shiva himself tested her — she did not break.

In Gujarat, this is considered the most important vrat for unmarried girls. Five days, five prayers — and the faith that what Parvati received, we too may receive.

## Vidhi

- **For whom:** observed by unmarried girls seeking a devoted husband and by married women for their husband's long life and wellbeing; a five-day vrat from Ashadha Shukla Trayodashi through Shravan Krishna Tritiya.
- **Daily — morning:** rise before sunrise, bathe, and set up an image of Shiva and Parvati together.
- **Daily — puja:** offer white flowers (jasmine, mogra), bilva leaves, unbroken rice (akshat), and a ghee diya; the primary puja time is pradosh (evening twilight); hear or read the Jaya Parvati Vrat katha; chant *Oṃ Umā-Maheśvarābhyāṃ Namaḥ*.
- **No salt:** the most distinctive rule — consuming salt diminishes the vrat's merit; this applies to all five days without exception.
- **Eating:** once a day, phalahar only (fruits, milk, dry fruits) — or observe a full fast; sleep on the ground.
- **Breaking the fast:** on Shravan Krishna Tritiya after sunrise, after completing the day's puja and offering dakshina.
- **Udyapan:** on the fifth day, feed unmarried girls and offer them dakshina — this is the formal completion of the vrat.
- **2026 dates:** Vrat begins Jul 27 (Ashadha Shukla Trayodashi); parana Aug 1 after sunrise

## Mantras

**Puja mantra — offered to Shiva and Parvati together:**

> ॐ उमामहेश्वराभ्यां नमः

*Oṃ Umā-Maheśvarābhyāṃ Namaḥ*

Meaning: "I bow to Uma (Parvati) and Maheshvara (Shiva) — to both together."
