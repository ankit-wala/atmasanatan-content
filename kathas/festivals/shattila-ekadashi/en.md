## Katha

This sacred story was narrated by the sage Pulastya to the sage Dalbhya in ages long past.

In a certain city there lived a Brahmin woman. She was deeply righteous, devoted to God, and generous of heart. No one who came to her door ever turned away empty-handed. Food, clothing, money — whatever was asked, she gave. Her devotion was without guile, and her life was filled with spiritual discipline.

When her final moment came and she departed from this world, the merit of her devotion and righteous deeds earned her a place in the divine abode of Lord Vishnu. But when she arrived at her celestial palace, she found that despite everything being present, something essential was missing — the palace was beautiful, yet hollow within. There was no food, no water, no sense of fulfilment.

At that very time, the divine sage Narada was wandering through that realm and arrived at her door. The Brahmin woman bowed to him and asked in distress, "O Devarshi! I devoted my whole life to devotion, gave in charity, observed fasts — then why is this palace empty? Why am I not at peace?"

Narada Ji answered with compassion, "Devi, you gave food, you gave wealth, you gave clothing — but in all your life you never once offered sesame in daan. Til is most dear to Lord Vishnu. The scriptures say that til was born from the Lord's own body, and is therefore the most sacred of all offerings. The merit of til-daan in the month of Magha burns away the sins of countless lifetimes. This one act you never performed — that is why your palace stands empty."

The Brahmin woman asked, "Then what is the remedy now?"

Narada Ji said, "You will need to take birth on the earth once more. There, observe the fast of Magha Krishna Ekadashi — Shattila Ekadashi. On this Ekadashi, six acts are performed with til: bathing in til-water (til-bath), applying til as ubtana, performing til-homa, giving til in daan, eating til (til-bhojan), and worshipping the Lord with til (til-puja). These six acts together form 'Shattila.' Through the merit of this vrat, your palace will become complete."

With the permission of Lord Vishnu, the Brahmin woman took birth on the earth once more. On Magha Krishna Ekadashi she observed the Shattila vrat — she bathed with til-water, applied til as ubtana, performed a homa with til, gave til in daan to Brahmins, ate til herself, and worshipped Lord Vishnu with til. On Dwadashi she broke her fast and offered til in daan to a Brahmin, feeding him a meal.

Through the power of this vrat, all her sins were destroyed. When she passed from the world again and returned to Vishnu Dhama, her palace overflowed with food, joy, and divine fulfilment.

The tradition of the Padma Purana declares that whoever performs these six acts with til on the day of Shattila Ekadashi has their sins destroyed, gains long life and good health, and attains in the end the supreme abode of Lord Vishnu.

## Significance

Til holds a special place in the month of Magha. The scriptures say that sesame was born from the very body of Lord Vishnu, which is why it is most dear to Him. In the cold of Magha, til provides the body with warmth, nourishment, and purification — making this vrat excellent from both a religious and a practical standpoint.

"Shat" means six and "tila" means sesame — thus "Shattila" means the six meritorious acts performed with til. Each act individually has the power to destroy specific sins; but when all six are performed together, this Ekadashi carries the devotee toward complete liberation.

The merit of til-daan lightens the burden of karmas accumulated across many lifetimes. Especially for those who spent much of their life absorbed in worldly pleasures, this Ekadashi offers an opportunity for deep spiritual purification.

## Vidhi

- **Dashami prep:** Take only one meal; avoid urad dal; observe brahmacharya; sleep while meditating on Lord Vishnu.
- **Fasting:** Observe a waterless (nirjal) fast or fruit-only fast on Ekadashi according to one's capacity; those unable to fast fully may take one meal (ekahara), but the six til-acts are essential regardless.
- **Avoid:** Urad dal, meat, intoxicants, and all tamasic foods throughout the vrat.
- **At dawn:** Rise early; bathe in water mixed with til (til-bath); apply til as ubtana on the body.
- **Six til-acts (Shattila):** Perform all six — til-bath, ubtana, til-homa before Lord Vishnu or a Shaligram, til-daan to a Brahmin, til-bhojan (til laddus or til-jaggery), and worship of Lord Vishnu with til; performing all six together yields the complete fruit of the vrat.
- **Puja:** Chant *Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya*; recite Bhagavata Katha or Vishnu Sahasranama.
- **Breaking the fast (Dwadashi):** Break the fast within the paran-kala after sunrise; offer til in daan to a Brahmin and feed him a meal first before eating oneself.
- **Special:** In the cold of Magha, bathing and applying ubtana with til also provide bodily warmth — this vrat is perfectly in harmony with the season.

**2. Til-Shatkarma Shloka**

> तिलस्य दानं च तिलं च होमः तिलोदकस्नानमथो तिलाशः।
> तिलेन देवार्चनमेव षट्भिः षट्तिला एषा कुरुते प्रतिष्ठाम्॥

tilasya dānaṃ ca tilaṃ ca homaḥ tilodakasnānam atho tilāśaḥ।
tilena devārcanam eva ṣaṭbhiḥ ṣaṭtilā eṣā kurute pratiṣṭhām॥

Meaning: The gift of til, the homa with til, bathing in til-water, applying til as ubtana, eating til, and worshipping the deity with til — through these six acts Shattila Ekadashi acquires its greatness, and the devotee receives the complete fruit of the vrat.
