## Katha

In the Treta Yuga there was a demon — Tarakasura.

He performed fierce austerities to propitiate Brahma and asked for a strange boon: "Let my death come only at the hands of Shiva's son."

Brahma granted the boon.

Tarakasura thought — this is immortality itself. Shiva is a renunciant, an ascetic, a great yogi. He has been absorbed in meditation for years in the grief of Sati's parting. How could he ever have a son?

Tarakasura spread chaos across the three worlds. The gods were defeated. Indra's throne was shaken. Sages could not perform their yajnas. Dharma began to break apart.

---

The gods went to Brahma. Brahma said: "There is only one way. The son of Shiva."

But Shiva was deep in meditation — in the caverns of the Himalayas. Who had the power to awaken him?

Then Himalaya's daughter, Parvati, took a vow.

She sat in tapasya — for years, unmoved. In summer, surrounded by the five fires; in winter, immersed in icy water. The mountains trembled at the force of her austerities; the celestial realm was lit with her radiance.

Shiva was pleased. He accepted Parvati as his consort.

The two were united in a divine bond of marriage — the greatest union in all the cosmos.

---

And then — from Shiva's radiance a divine seed appeared.

That radiance was so fierce that no realm in the universe could contain it. Agni accepted it but was himself set ablaze. Ganga took it and bore it, and from that seed a child was born in the reeds of the Sarakanda forest.

That child had six faces. The Krittikas raised him, so he came to be called 'Kartikeya.'

But Parvati was his mother — and she lifted that child into her arms.

In that moment, the form that appeared — that is the form of **Skandamata**.

Seated on a lion, four-armed — a lotus in each of her upper hands, one hand raised in the boon-giving gesture, and in one arm she holds her son Skanda. Her complexion is golden, her eyes holding both tenderness and fierce brilliance at once.

---

The infant Kartikeya — who came to be called Skanda — was appointed commander of the gods' armies.

Skanda fought a great war against Tarakasura. And slew him.

Dharma was restored to the three worlds. The gods breathed again.

But what stayed in memory — was that form of Maa Skandamata, when she first lifted her newborn son into her arms. That gentleness, that motherly love — and the power behind it — the might of the lion-rider.

A mother's love is the greatest protective force of all.

On the fifth day of Navratri we bow to that compassionate power — who gives birth to a son so that the world may be protected, and who holds that son in her arms.

In the tradition of the Devi Bhagavata Purana and the Markandeya Purana it is said — the devotee who worships Skandamata on the fifth day with a pure heart receives all cherished wishes, the joy of children, and the welfare of the family.

## Significance

**A mother's love — the greatest protective force:**
Skandamata's form carries a profound message — a mother is not only tenderness; she is also the greatest protective power in the world. The same mother who holds her infant in her arms will, when need arises, mount a lion and fight demons. Compassion and valour — both reside in the one form.

**Skanda — commander of the gods:**
Kartikeya (Skanda) is the commander of the divine armies. As his mother, Skandamata is 'the one who gave birth to the source of protection.' She embodies the power that stands behind every protector.

**Vishuddha Chakra — the fifth day:**
On the fifth day of Navratri the centre of spiritual practice is the Vishuddha Chakra (located at the throat). This chakra is the seat of speech, expression, and the power of truth. Worship of Maa Skandamata awakens this chakra.

**Colour — green:**
The colour of the fifth day is green — a symbol of nature, life, and new beginnings. Just as Maa Skandamata brought a new life (Skanda) into the world, green is the symbol of birth and nourishment.

## Vidhi

1. Bathe in the morning and wear green garments — green is the colour of the fifth day.
2. Install an image or idol of Maa Skandamata on a clean seat.
3. Offer a garland of white flowers — jasmine or kunda flowers are especially preferred.
4. Place bananas, green offerings (raw coconut, green grapes), and mishri as bhog.
5. Light incense and a lamp; apply yellow sandalwood.
6. Chant the mantra of Maa Skandamata 108 times.
7. Recite the fifth chapter of the Durga Saptashati.
8. Conclude with aarti and distribute prasad.

**Ashwin Shukla Panchami 2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (~October 15, 2026).

## Observance

**Navratri vrat — fifth day:**
Devotees observing the nine-day Navratri fast should remain without food or on a fruit diet on the fifth day as well. A single sattvik meal — kuttu, sabudana, or fruit — may be taken.

**The colour of the fifth day is green:**
Wear green garments on this day — it is a sign of gratitude towards nature and new life.

**Special devotion:**
Worship of Maa Skandamata is considered especially fruitful for the blessing of children and the welfare of the family. On this day, meditate while beholding the image of the Goddess with the infant Skanda in her arms.

## Mantras

**Skandamata Dhyana Shloka:**

> सिंहासनगता नित्यं पद्माश्रितकरद्वया ।
> शुभदास्तु सदा देवी स्कन्दमाता यशस्विनी ॥

Siṃhāsanagatā nityaṃ padmāśritakaradvayā |
śubhadāstu sadā devī skandamātā yaśasvinī ||

Meaning: "Ever enthroned on her lion-seat, holding lotuses in both her lotus hands — may the glorious Maa Skandamata always grant us auspiciousness."

---

**Ya Devi — Skandamata form:**

> या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ स्कन्दमाता रूपेण संस्थिता ।
> नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः ॥

Yā devī sarvabhūteṣu māṃ skandamātā rūpeṇa saṃsthitā |
namastasyai namastasyai namastasyai namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: "To that Goddess who dwells in all beings in the form of Maa Skandamata — salutations again and again."
