## Katha

In the Reva Khanda of the Skanda Purana, Bhagavan Vishnu explained to Narada Muni the glory of the Satyanarayan Vrat. He said —

*"O Narada, one who performs this vrat once and does not keep it regularly — their fruit shall diminish just as a tree withers without water. One who performs it regularly every month on Purnima, or on any auspicious tithi — my grace shall remain with their household always."*

The memory of this tradition lives on in the story of a merchant.

In a certain town there was a prosperous merchant. His trade was incurring losses; there was unrest at home. Someone suggested — perform the Satyanarayan puja. He did — with a sincere heart, together with his family, with panchamrita and tulsi leaves. That very month his trade flourished. The boat returned laden with goods. His daughter was married; all was auspicious.

But when happiness came, he thought — what need now? He forgot.

The following month Purnima came — no puja. It came again — still none. The boat that brought goods from the sea was caught in a storm. Losses began in trade. Trouble arose in his daughter's in-laws' household.

The merchant found himself in difficulty again. He asked his wife — *"What is the reason?"*

His wife said — *"We abandoned the Satyanarayan puja. The vrat we took in times of hardship — in times of happiness we forgot it."*

He performed the puja again — this time with a sankalpa that he would observe it regularly on every Purnima henceforth. The boat returned safely. Peace came back to the home.

This katha is the folk-memory of the Kalavati-Katha (the fifth chapter) of the complete Satyanarayan Katha. For the complete katha of all five chapters, see `satyanarayan-adhyaya-1` through `satyanarayan-adhyaya-5`.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that one who observes the Satyanarayan Vrat every month — Vishnu's grace remains unbroken in their home.

## Significance

Satyanarayan — the Narayana of truth. The one who dwells in truth, who upholds truth — this is the name of that form of Vishnu.

That is why Satyanarayan Puja is performed on Purnima — Purnima is the symbol of completeness, the fullness of the moon's sixteen kalas. On that day, the invocation of truth is most fitting.

The specialness of the monthly puja is that it is simple. No grand arrangement is needed — no pandal, no crowds. Only at home, with the family — make panchamrita, place tulsi, light a lamp, prepare prasad (wheat halwa or banana and sugar as naivedya) — and perform the puja.

The importance lies in it happening every month — regularly. Performing a puja once in hardship and then forgetting it is not devotion to Satyanarayan — that is bargaining. Satyanarayan means the current of truth — and truth is continuous.

## Vidhi

- **Fasting:** Complete fasting is not required; eating lightly before the puja — ekahara or fruit — is sufficient; the fast is broken with the prasad after the puja.
- **Preparations:** Bathe and wear yellow or clean garments; gather panchamrita (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar), tulsi leaves, yellow flowers, sandalwood paste, incense, and lamp; prepare prasad — wheat halwa (sheera), or banana, coconut, sweets.
- **Sankalpa & worship:** Light a lamp before the image of Vishnu or the shalagrama; take sankalpa — *"Today on Purnima, I perform the puja of Satyanarayan for Vishnu-bhakti and the welfare of the family"*; perform abhishek with panchamrita while chanting *Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya*; offer tulsi leaves, yellow flowers, and sandalwood paste.
- **Katha & aarti:** Offer the prasad as naivedya; read an abridged version of the Panchadhyayi Katha or narrate a summary; perform aarti — *Om Jai Jagadish Hare*; distribute prasad among family and neighbours.
- **Prasad rule:** Receiving the prasad is an integral part of this vrat — there is a traditional prohibition against refusing it; if someone declines, request them gently.
- **Tithi flexibility:** This puja may be performed on any auspicious day — Purnima, Ekadashi, or the first day of the month; Purnima is the most widely observed.
- **Regularity:** Beginning with a sankalpa to observe the vrat on every Purnima for a year is the ideal approach; performing it once in hardship and then forgetting is not true observance.
- **Sankalpa fulfillment:** When a wish is fulfilled through a puja performed for a specific purpose, perform the udyapana (concluding puja) and feed brahmins.

## Mantras

**Vishnu Dvadasakshari Mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya

Meaning: Salutations to Bhagavan Vasudeva (Vishnu). This twelve-syllable mantra is the foremost mantra for the worship of Vishnu.

---

**Satyanarayan Dhyana Mantra:**

> श्रीं ह्रीं क्लीं ग्लौं नमः श्रीसत्यनारायणाय नमः

śrīṃ hrīṃ klīṃ glauṃ namaḥ śrīsatyanārāyaṇāya namaḥ

Meaning: Salutations to Sri Satyanarayan, together with these four bija mantras. Shrim is the bija of Lakshmi-shakti, Hrim of the dissolution of maya, Klim of attraction, and Glaum of the Ganesha-principle.
