## Katha

In the folk tradition of North India, this story of Santoshi Mata is told.

In a certain town lived a woman named Satyavati — gentle, pious, and hardworking. Her husband had gone far away in search of work. In the house were her father-in-law, mother-in-law, and three sisters-in-law. No news of the husband ever came. No letter, no money.

Satyavati's condition in the household was pitiable. The in-laws taunted her and made her do hard labour. The sisters-in-law would taunt her — *"Your husband has forgotten you."*

Satyavati wept — but somewhere in her heart there was hope.

One Friday morning Satyavati met an elderly woman — calm of face, tender of gaze.

She said — *"Child, why are you sad? Observe the vrat of Santoshi Mata. Sixteen Fridays — that is all. Each Friday, keep the fast, offer jaggery and roasted chickpeas as bhog, and listen to the katha. Remember one rule — do not eat anything sour on Friday. Not tamarind, not lemon, not curd, not dry mango powder."*

Satyavati touched the feet of the elderly woman. She took the sankalpa.

The first Friday arrived. Satyavati bathed, cleaned the place of worship, placed a picture of Santoshi Mata before her. She offered jaggery and roasted chickpeas as bhog. She listened to the katha. She kept the fast.

The second, third, fourth — Friday by Friday passed. Satyavati's vrat held firm.

Far away, her husband Biru's mind suddenly began to be drawn homeward. Wealth began to accumulate for him. His trade prospered.

But Satyavati's sisters-in-law did not take well to her vrat.

The fifteenth Friday arrived. The final day of the vrat was drawing near. The sisters-in-law devised a scheme.

They said to Satyavati — *"Tonight we will all eat together — come, make a tamarind chutney."*

Satyavati said — *"Today is Friday. I will not touch anything sour."*

The sisters-in-law said — *"You will make it — you do not have to eat it."*

Satyavati thought — if I do not eat it, the rule will not be broken.

She squeezed the tamarind water with her hands.

That night the sisters-in-law gave her that water to drink — in secret.

The vrat was broken.

The very next week, word came from afar — the husband had met with some trouble, he was stuck. The money was lost.

When Satyavati understood what had happened, she wept — *"Mata, my vrat was broken. But my heart did not waver. Forgive me."*

She took the sankalpa once more.

This time sixteen Fridays — with even greater care. She did not fall for the sisters-in-law's words. She did not allow even a particle of sour to touch her. From morning to evening on Fridays she fasted, offered jaggery and chickpeas as bhog, and listened to the Mata's katha.

The sixteenth Friday was completed.

That very night there was a knock at the door.

Biru returned — smiling, well, with a bag in his hand.

*"I don't know why, but suddenly everything fell into place. The trade yielded profit. My heart said — go home."*

Satyavati's eyes filled with tears. She silently bowed to Santoshi Mata within her heart.

Now it was time for the udyapan — the closing ceremony of the sixteen-Friday vrat.

Satyavati invited the women of her neighbourhood. She prepared a bhog of halwa-puri — without any sour ingredient. She fed sixteen married women. She told the katha of Santoshi Mata.

Through the grace of the Mata, happiness, prosperity, and contentment came to dwell in her home.

In the folk tradition of the Friday Santoshi Mata vrat, it is said that a woman who observes sixteen Fridays sincerely, without sour, with an offering of jaggery and chickpeas to Santoshi Mata, and who completes the udyapan — will have the Mata's grace abide in her home and full contentment dwell in her life.

## Significance

Santoshi Mata's very name is her form — "Santoshi" means one who grants contentment.

This Devi, known as the daughter of Ganesha, was born of the tenderness of Riddhi and Siddhi. She is the goddess of simple wishes — not vast kingdoms, but small joys. A husband's love, peace in the home, the nourishment of family.

The distinctiveness of this vrat is that it rests not on the rites prescribed in the Puranas but on the devotion alive in people's hearts. Countless women of North India learned this vrat from their mothers and grandmothers — it is a folk devotion passed down generation after generation.

The 1975 film "Jai Santoshi Maa" gave this tradition wide popularity — but the roots of this vrat are much older.

The avoidance of sour is the central discipline of this vrat — it is not merely a dietary rule, but a test of the devotee's steadfastness and single-pointed focus.

## Vidhi

1. On Friday morning, bathe and take the sankalpa for the vrat.
2. Place a picture or image of Santoshi Mata at the place of worship.
3. Offer yellow or orange flowers, roli, and akshata.
4. Offer **jaggery and roasted chickpeas** as bhog — this is the special naivedya of this vrat.
5. Listen to or read the katha of Santoshi Mata.
6. Perform the Mata's aarti.
7. Keep the fast through the day — fruit may be taken.
8. **Udyapan:** After the sixteenth Friday, perform udyapan — feed sixteen married women a meal of halwa-puri.

## Observance

**Duration of the vrat:** Sixteen consecutive Fridays. The vrat must not be interrupted in between.

**The most important rule — sour is forbidden:** Nothing sour in any form on Fridays. No tamarind, no lemon, no curd, no dry mango powder. Even contact with something made from sour ingredients is said to break the vrat — the katha of Satyavati teaches precisely this.

**Fasting:** No food all day, or fruit only. Breaking the fast after the evening puja.

**Bhog:** Jaggery and roasted chickpeas — distribute these to other members of the household as prasad.

**Udyapan:** After sixteen Fridays are complete, udyapan is obligatory. Feed sixteen married women a meal (halwa-puri, without sour ingredients) and offer them a dakshina. According to the household's means.

**2026 date:** This is a weekly vrat — it may be begun on any Friday.

## Mantras

**Santoshi Mata Salutation Mantra:**

> ॐ संतोषी मातायै नमः

oṃ saṃtoṣī mātāyai namaḥ

Meaning: Salutation to Santoshi Mata.

---

**Santoshi Mata Aarti:**

> जय संतोषी माता जय जय माता ।
> अपने भक्तों को सुख-संपत्ति देती माता ॥

jaya saṃtoṣī mātā jaya jaya mātā |
apane bhaktoṃ ko sukha-sampatī detī mātā ||

Meaning: Victory to Santoshi Mata, victory, victory to the Mata — she grants her devotees happiness and prosperity.
