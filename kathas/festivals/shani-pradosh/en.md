## Katha

The root katha of Pradosh Vrat — the churning of the ocean, the halahal, and the gods' worship of Shiva on the Trayodashi evening — that is given in full in the chapter on Pradosh Vrat.

The special context of Shani-Pradosh comes from a deep tradition.

Shani Dev is the deity of justice. His work is to deliver the fruit of deeds. He is neither friend nor foe to anyone. As one acts, so one reaps. That is why, when his gaze falls — whether on gods or on humans — all undergo hardship.

Yet Shani Dev knows how much suffering his justice causes to beings. He himself witnesses that pain.

Once, Shani Dev came to Shiva — during Pradosh Kala, on that sacred Trayodashi evening. He folded his hands and said — *"Mahadeva, I am the judge of karma. My dharma is — give each being the fruit of their actions as they are. But those beings who take your shelter, who are in your refuge — protect them. Let your grace be with them, even when my gaze falls upon them."*

Shiva was pleased. He said — *"Shani, you have spoken the truth. Those devotees who observe Shani-Pradosh, perform abhishek of the Shivalinga with sesame, and worship with devotion — for them your pain shall remain stilled. Your justice shall be their companion, not their enemy."*

From that time comes the tradition of Shani-Pradosh — in Shiva's puja, sesame, sesame oil, and blue flowers — the very materials beloved to Shani Dev — are offered. This pleases both Shani and Shiva together.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that the devotee who observes Shani-Pradosh — the suffering of their Sade-Sati, Dhaiya, and Shani's Mahadasha is pacified, and stability and patience come into their life.

## Significance

Shani-Pradosh is rare — it comes only a few times in a year. That is why when it comes, it should not be missed.

Saturday is the day of Shani Dev — the symbol of karma, justice, discipline, and patience. When Pradosh falls on this day, Shiva's devotion and Shani's power of justice are combined. Shiva transforms Shani's justice into grace.

For those going through Shani's Sade-Sati or Dhaiya, those in the Mahadasha of Shani in their birth chart, or those facing sudden difficulties in life — Shani-Pradosh is said to be especially fruitful.

## Vidhi

The puja-vidhi of Shani-Pradosh is the same as the main Pradosh Vrat. For the detailed procedure, see the chapter on Pradosh Vrat.

**Special additions:**
1. Add sesame (black sesame) to the Shiva-abhishek — this is the very material that pleases both Shani and Shiva together.
2. Light a lamp with sesame oil.
3. Offer blue flowers (if available) — Shani's colour is blue.
4. Along with *Om Namah Shivaya*, chant *Om Sham Shanaishcharaya Namah*.
5. Offering water to a peepal tree and remembering Shani Dev is also auspicious.

## Observance

**Fasting:** Complete fast or ekahara on Trayodashi (Saturday) — the use of sesame and jaggery is appropriate.

**Paran:** Sunday morning after sunrise, with remembrance of Shiva.

**Donation:** Black sesame, black garments, mustard oil — donating materials associated with Shani on this day is especially meritorious.

**2026 dates:** Confirm on DrikPanchang.

## Mantras

**Panchakshari Mantra — the essential chant of Pradosh:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय

oṃ namaḥ śivāya

Meaning: Salutations to Shiva. This five-syllable mantra is the symbol of Shiva's five actions — creation, sustenance, dissolution, concealment, and grace.

**Karpoorgauram Shloka — the twilight shloka of Pradosh puja:**

> कर्पूरगौरं करुणावतारं संसारसारं भुजगेन्द्रहारम् ।
> सदावसन्तं हृदयारविन्दे भवं भवानीसहितं नमामि ॥

karpūragauraṃ karuṇāvatāraṃ saṃsārasāraṃ bhujagendrahāram | sadāvasantaṃ hṛdayāravinde bhavaṃ bhavānīsahitaṃ namāmi ||

Meaning: White as camphor, the very incarnation of compassion, the essence of the world, who wears the king of serpents as a garland — who dwells forever in the lotus of the heart — I bow to that Bhava (Shiva) along with Bhavani (Parvati).
