## Katha

There was once a great sage.

He was an ascetic, a scholar — students came to his ashrama. But one day he passed near another sage's ashrama. He saw — the number of students there was greater, the renown was greater.

A momentary contraction arose in his mind — *"Why do they have more? Have I done less?"*

That feeling lasted only an instant. But from that single moment a force was born.

Its name was — **Matsarasura**.

---

Matsarasura's power was singular.

He did not conquer anyone in battle. His weapon was — comparison. Wherever he went, he filled people's minds with this feeling: *"Look at what he has — and what you do not have."*

The same thing happened in devaloka. Indra saw Vishnu's popularity and began to seethe. Brahma saw the number of Shiva's devotees and his mind grew clouded. Discord began to rise among the gods — but no one could understand the cause.

What was not war — was discord. What was not an enemy — was a poison within.

---

The gods offered their prayers to Ganesha.

Ganesha assumed the form of **Vakratunda** — that form in which his trunk is vakra, curved. The curved path — the one that does not strike head-on, but proceeds by a particular route.

Matsarasura's power was that he could answer a direct attack — envy with envy, competition with competition. But the one who takes no straight path, he could not block.

Vakratunda Ganesha went to him and did something extraordinary.

He became joyful. He took delight in the successes of the gods. Looking at Indra's throne he said — "How beautiful!" Looking at Brahma's creation he said — "Wondrous!" Seeing Shiva's devotees he danced with them.

For Matsarasura's power to function, it needed a shelter — the mind that compares. But where there is joy in another's success, envy finds no home to build.

Matsarasura had no shelter left.

He dissolved.

---

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said — *"The one who worships Vakratunda on Chaitra Krishna Chaturthi has matsarya removed from the mind — the feeling of envy and comparison. This is the first Sankashti of the Hindu New Year — one that begins the new year with the spirit of generosity and joy."*

## Significance

The Hindu New Year begins with Chaitra Shukla Pratipada. In that same Chaitra — on the Krishna paksha Chaturthi — falls the first Sankashti of the new year.

**The meaning of Vakratunda:** Vakra = curved, tunda = trunk. This form is considered Ganesha's first form — in the Ganesha Purana among the eight forms this is the foremost. The quality of the curved path is that it does not collide head-on, but creates its own route. In life too, sometimes the straight path is blocked — then it is the curved path that serves.

**The poison of matsara:** Matsara means envy, malice, burning at another's success. This is the mental poison that forces us to spend our energy on comparison with others. The prayer for liberation from this feeling at the beginning of the new year — this is the very essence of Chaitra Sankashti.

**The consciousness of abundance:** The message of Vakratunda's worship is — learn to see your own happiness in the happiness of others. This is not merely good conduct — it is a sadhana. One who learns to take joy in the success of others finds abundance also entering their own life.

**The proximity of Chaitra Navaratri:** Ram Navami and Chaitra Navaratri fall in this same month. Hanuman Jayanti also comes in Chaitra. Amidst all of these, Chaitra Sankashti is an occasion for quiet, personal sadhana.

## Vidhi

- **Morning sankalpa:** After bathing, take the sankalpa — "I observe the Sankashti vrata with worship of Vakratunda Ganesha on Chaitra Krishna Chaturthi."
- **Fasting:** Waterless fasting from sunrise to moonrise is best; fruit-based diet is acceptable — grains and salt are prohibited.
- **Puja:** Install the image of Vakratunda Ganesha on a red cloth (any Ganesha image may be used while remembering the name "Vakratunda"); offer red flowers, 21 durva, modak, laddus, sindura, incense, and a lamp; perform panchopachara or shodasopachara; chant "OM Gam Ganapataye Namah."
- **Matsara-mukti practice:** After puja, bring to mind someone whose success has caused comparison or contraction in your mind — send them sincere good wishes from the heart; throughout the day, whenever a comparative thought arises chant "OM Gam Ganapataye Namah" and kindle goodwill. This is the living form of Vakratunda's worship.
- **Katha:** Read the Chaitra Sankashti katha.
- **Moonrise:** The vrata is complete only after sighting the moon; offer arghya to the moon and then observe parana.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.

---

**Vighnashana shloka:**

> वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटिसमप्रभ ।
> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda, vast of form, radiant as a crore of suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.

---

**Matsara-nashana prayer shloka:**

> सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः सर्वे सन्तु निरामयाः ।
> सर्वे भद्राणि पश्यन्तु मा कश्चिद् दुःखभाग् भवेत् ॥

Sarve bhavantu sukhinaḥ sarve santu nirāmayāḥ |
sarve bhadrāṇi paśyantu mā kaścid duḥkhabhāg bhavet ||

Meaning: May all be happy, may all be free from disease, may all see what is auspicious, may no one be a sharer in sorrow.

*(There is a tradition of reciting this shloka after Vakratunda's puja — to awaken the feeling of universal wellbeing opposite to matsara.)*
