## Katha

It was the dawn of Phalgun Shukla Dvitiya. Spring in Vrindavan was at its fullest bloom. Kadamba, champa, parijata, and countless forest flowers had opened wide. There was a sweetness in the air, fragrance in every direction, and the hum of bees along the banks of the Yamuna.

That morning Shri Krishna went to the flower garden of Vrindavan. He stopped suddenly — before him, golden champa flowers were drifting down from a kadamba tree, as though the tree itself were welcoming him. Krishna filled both hands with flowers and began to play.

When the gopas and gopis arrived there, Krishna began to shower flowers upon them. Laughter and delight echoed through the garden. Someone picked up rose petals, someone picked up marigolds — and in moments the Holi of flowers had begun.

Just then Radha arrived. She came with her companions. The moment Krishna saw her, he lifted a fistful of golden champa flowers and showered them upon Radha. Radha laughed and scooped up rose petals and poured them over Krishna. The companions broke into peals of joy. In that moment, Vrindavan became more beautiful than even the heavens.

At that time Devarshi Narada was passing by. He witnessed this otherworldly scene — Radha and Krishna at play with flowers, a rain of blossoms all around, fragrance and laughter filling the air. He was transfixed. Joining his palms, he said —

"What I have witnessed today cannot be obtained through any yajna, any tapas, or any pilgrimage. To be a witness to this Pushpa-Lila of Radha-Madhava on this Dvitiya — this alone is the highest merit. Among all the tithis of Phalgun, this Dvitiya is the most blessed."

The tradition of Braj holds that from that day onward, the festival of Phulera Dooj began — when Krishna, before colors, played Holi with flowers and thus gave the festival of colors its sacred opening. In the rasa-lila of the Bhagavata, this is the very principle: that Krishna's pushpa-lila in Vrindavan is the most tender and pure expression of madhurya bhakti — where between devotee and God there is only love, no separation at all.

## Significance

Phulera Dooj is celebrated on Phalgun Shukla Dvitiya — exactly two days before Holika Dahan. In the Braj tradition, this tithi is considered a svayamsiddha muhurta, meaning that on this day marriages, griha-pravesh, the commencement of a new business, or any auspicious work can be undertaken without consulting any panchang for an auspicious time. The entire day is auspicious. In Jyotisha Shastra, such tithis are exceedingly rare.

Flowers here are not merely decoration — they are the symbol of pure devotion. When Krishna showered champa upon Radha, it was no instrument; it was the most tender language of love. In the tenth canto of the Bhagavata Purana, Krishna's lilas are the supreme expression of the madhurya bhava. Phulera Dooj is the celebration of that very madhurya — where the devotee offers flowers to the Lord and the Lord showers flowers upon the devotee. This bridge of love is the very essence of Braj-bhakti.

## Vidhi

- **Morning bath and sankalpa:** Bathe at dawn; take the intention to observe Phulera Dooj with pushpa-puja to Shri Radha-Krishna.
- **Flower decoration:** Adorn the Radha-Krishna image or deity with fresh blossoms — especially champa, rose, marigold, and parijata; tulsi leaves and yellow flowers are particularly dear to Krishna.
- **Puja:** Offer pushpanjali; light incense and a ghee lamp; chant "Om namo bhagavate vasudevaya."
- **Svayamsiddha muhurta:** No auspicious time need be consulted — the entire tithi from sunrise to sunset is a svayamsiddha muhurta; marriages, engagements, griha-pravesh, and new beginnings may all be commenced freely on this day.
- **Temple visit:** Visit a Radha-Krishna temple if possible and offer flowers there.
- **Evening bhajan-kirtan:** Sing Braj bhajans such as "Hori Khelat Nandlal" and "Phulan ki Hori"; play Holi with flowers before the Radha-Krishna deity together with family.
