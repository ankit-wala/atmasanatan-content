## Katha

Hiranyakashipu's patience had snapped.

He stood before his son Prahlad — trembling with rage, eyes burning like embers. He demanded: "Where is this Vishnu of yours? Where is your protector?"

Prahlad replied, with perfect stillness — "He is everywhere, Father. He is in this stambha (pillar) too."

Hiranyakashipu roared — "In this pillar?" — and raised his mace and struck that column of the palace.

The Bhagavata Purana describes what followed — as though it were the final great thunder of creation.

---

The stambha cracked open. And what came out of it — no one had ever seen, no one had ever heard of, no one had imagined even in a dream.

Not fully human. Not fully lion.

The upper body of a man — not two arms but countless arms, filling the cosmos. The face of a lion — a mane like a storm-cloud, eyes like molten gold, teeth like lightning, claws like thunderbolts. His roar made all three worlds tremble.

This was Narasimha — a form of Lord Vishnu he had never revealed before.

Brahma's boon had every condition: "not man, not beast." Narasimha was both, and therefore neither. "Not day, not night." This was the sandhyakala (twilight hour) of Vaishakh Shukla Chaturdashi — neither day nor night. "Not inside, not outside." Narasimha placed Hiranyakashipu on the threshold — the doorstep itself. "Not on earth, not in the sky." He lifted him onto his own thighs. "Not by any weapon fashioned, nor unfashioned." His claws — nails — were neither forged weapons nor unformed ones.

Every single condition of the boon was honoured. And the devotee was protected.

Hiranyakashipu was slain.

---

But Narasimha's fury did not subside.

He remained there — seated on the palace throne. In his ugra rupa (fierce form), terrible to behold. His eyes still blazing. Blood on his hands. Mane standing upright. Tongue lashing. The gods wished to approach — they stopped. Brahma tried to step forward — he could not draw near. Mahadeva Shiva sent a hymn of praise — Narasimha did not hear it. Mother Lakshmi herself came — she too turned back. No one could stand before that fierce form.

The entire cosmos was shuddering.

Then Brahma brought Prahlad forward.

That boy — for whose sake all of this had happened — stepped slowly ahead. His palms were joined. Tears were in his eyes. On his face there was no fear, no hesitation — only love: the love that had taken root even in the womb.

Prahlad offered full prostration and began his praise.

His words were simple — "Lord, you are terrifying to the asuras and supremely dear to your devotees. This ugra rupa is yours, this tender form is also yours. I am only yours."

And when that boy stepped forward and reached to touch Narasimha's feet —

the Lord's rage melted.

What Brahma could not do, what Shiva could not do, what Lakshmi could not do — a child's guileless love accomplished.

Narasimha placed his hand on the boy's head.

In his eyes — which until a moment ago had burned like live coals — tears appeared.

He said: "Vatsa, ask for a boon."

Prahlad said: "Lord, I want nothing for myself. If you must give something — give me this: that I may never have any craving."

In the tradition of the Bhagavata Purana it is said that at that moment Narasimha drew Prahlad to his heart — and he grew calm. That day was Vaishakh Shukla Chaturdashi — sandhyakala. This is why that tithi is Narasimha Jayanti.

## Significance

**Why this avatar is singular:**
Narasimha is the form Lord Vishnu has never shown in any other lila — not in Rama, not in Krishna, not in Vamana. This is the form that manifested for the complete protection of a devotee when no other way was possible. Narasimha is proof that the Lord's devotee-love is greater than any of his own rules, any of his own forms.

**The mystery of the ugra rupa:**
Narasimha's failure to calm down even after the slaying was not mere anger — in the Bhagavata's reading, it was the pinnacle of love. The power that awakened to protect his beloved does not return so easily. This is why this form is called "ugra-kalyana" — both terrifying and supremely auspicious.

**The significance of chaturdashi and sandhyakala:**
The convergence of Vaishakh Shukla Chaturdashi and the sandhyakala (twilight hour) was not accidental — according to the Bhagavata, it was the precision of the Lord's lila. Neither day nor night — this was the moment that fulfilled every condition of Brahma's boon. This is why the worship of Narasimha is performed especially at twilight.

**Prahlad — the victory of bhakti:**
Narasimha Jayanti is not only the victory of a deity but the victory of a child devotee. The boy who was not afraid of fire, poison, elephants, or the ocean was not afraid before that fierce form either — because he had never known the Lord as "terrifying," only as "his own." Prahlad teaches that it is not fear of God but love for God that is true bhakti.

## Vidhi

1. On the morning of Narasimha Jayanti, bathe and take the sankalp of the vrat.
2. Establish an image or picture of Lord Narasimha — both the ugra Narasimha and the shanta Narasimha forms are worthy of worship.
3. Perform panchamrita abhishek — milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar.
4. Offer yellow clothing and yellow flowers. Tulsi leaves must be offered.
5. **Special puja at sandhyakala** — this is the very hour when Narasimha manifested. Light a lamp.
6. Read or listen to the katha of the Narasimha avatar from Bhagavata Purana Skandha 7.
7. Recite the **Narasimha Kavach (protective prayer)** — this is the complete-body-protection stotra of Lord Narasimha.
8. Night vigil or kirtan — with the chant of "Jai Narasimha, Jai Narasimha."
9. Fast — one meal during the day or no meal at all; after the evening puja, fruits may be taken.

## Observance

**Fast:** Keep a fast throughout Vaishakh Shukla Chaturdashi. Water, fruits, and milk may be taken. Abstain from grain. If a complete fast is not possible, observe ekahara (one meal).

**Evening puja:** The puja at sandhyakala is more fruitful than daytime puja — because Narasimha's manifestation occurred at twilight. Near the time of sunset, light a lamp, offer incense and flowers, and chant the Narasimha mantra.

**Narasimha Kavach:** In the Bhagavata Purana, Narada Muni gave the Narasimha Kavach to Prahlad's mother Kayadhu. Recite this kavach at sandhyakala — it is considered the foremost stotra for complete-body protection and freedom from fear.

## Mantras

**1. Narasimha Mantra**

> ॐ उग्रं वीरं महाविष्णुं ज्वलन्तं सर्वतोमुखम् ।
> नृसिंहं भीषणं भद्रं मृत्युमृत्युं नमाम्यहम् ॥

oṃ ugraṃ vīraṃ mahāviṣṇuṃ jvalantaṃ sarvatomukham |
nṛsiṃhaṃ bhīṣaṇaṃ bhadraṃ mṛtyumṛtyuṃ namāmyaham ||

Meaning: I bow to Mahavishnu Narasimha, who is fierce, heroic, blazing in all directions, facing all ways, terrifying, supremely auspicious, and the death of death itself.

---

**2. Prahlad-Narasimha Stuti**

> नमस्ते नरसिंहाय प्रह्लादाह्लाद-दायिने ।
> हिरण्यकशिपोर्वक्षः शिलाटङ्क-नखालये ॥

namaste narasiṃhāya prahlādāhlāda-dāyine |
hiraṇyakaśiporvakṣaḥ śilāṭaṅka-nakhālaye ||

Meaning: Salutations to Narasimha — who gives delight to Prahlad and whose claws were like a chisel splitting open the stone-like chest of Hiranyakashipu.

---

**3. Narasimha Gayatri**

> ॐ वज्रनखाय विद्महे तीक्ष्णदंष्ट्राय धीमहि ।
> तन्नो नृसिंहः प्रचोदयात् ॥

oṃ vajranakhāya vidmahe tīkṣṇadaṃṣṭrāya dhīmahi |
tanno nṛsiṃhaḥ pracodayāt ||

Meaning: We know him of the thunderbolt claws; we meditate on him of the sharp fangs — may that Narasimha inspire and illuminate us.
