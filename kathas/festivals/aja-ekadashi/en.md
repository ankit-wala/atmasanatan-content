## Katha

Dharmaraja Yudhishthira asked Lord Krishna: "Madhava, what is the name of Bhadrapada Krishna Ekadashi? Tell me its greatness."

The Lord replied: "O King, this Ekadashi's name is **Aja** — that which is never born, that which has no beginning and no end — this is Vishnu's supreme name. On this Ekadashi, he is worshipped in that very form. Listen — I shall tell you an ancient story."

---

In the Solar dynasty, in the city of Ayodhya, King Harishchandra reigned. He was a king in the lineage of the ancestors of Rama — and famous throughout the three worlds for his truth.

"Harishchandra does not lie" — this was his identity.

One day the sage Vishwamitra came to him and said: "O King, in a dream you gave me your kingdom as a gift. Fulfil that promise."

Harishchandra did not remember the dream. But he did not hesitate for a moment. To protect truth, he gave away his entire kingdom — throne, treasury, land — all of it to Vishwamitra.

More dakshina was still owed.

Harishchandra had nothing left. He sold his wife Taramati as a servant to a brahmin's household. He gave his son Rohitashva to that same brahmin. And finally he sold himself — to a Dom who ran a cremation ground at Kashi.

King Harishchandra's task was to collect the cremation fee from whoever came with a body to burn. For a king, this was the most humiliating work — yet he accepted it.

---

Days passed. Harishchandra's clothes wore to rags, his body weakened — but the thread of truth did not break.

One day Rohitashva was plucking flowers for puja — and a snake bit him. Rohitashva fell there.

Taramati lifted her son's body. Torn clothes, eyes filled with tears — she arrived at the cremation ground.

In the dark, a woman in rags stood at the gate, holding a dead child.

Harishchandra asked: "Have you brought the fee?"

Taramati lifted her face. Their eyes met.

One moment — when husband and wife recognized each other. And in that one moment, all the pain surged at once.

Taramati had nothing. She tore the edge of her sari and handed it over.

Harishchandra's hands were trembling. But there was duty. There was truth.

---

At that very moment the sage Gautama arrived. He looked at the king — bones visible, gatekeeper of the cremation ground. His heart filled with grief.

He said: "O King, what you have endured, even the gods could not. There is a way. **Bhadrapada Krishna Ekadashi — Aja Ekadashi.** Fast on this day, keep vigil through the night, take Vishnu's name. The power of this fast will remove all your accumulated suffering."

Harishchandra observed that Ekadashi fast. A full day's fast, an entire night's vigil — "Hari… Hari… Hari…"

The Ekadashi night ended.

Drums sounded in the sky. Flowers rained down.

Brahma, Lord Vishnu, Lord Shankara, and Indra — all appeared before him.

Rohitashva opened his eyes — alive.

Taramati stood before him in royal clothes.

The kingdom returned. Vishwamitra acknowledged: "O King, your truth is unconquerable."

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana, Krishna Janma Khanda, it is said — merely hearing this story is equal to the merit of the Ashwamedha yajna. Whoever fasts on this Ekadashi, all the sins of their life are destroyed and in the end they attain Lord Vishnu.

## Significance

"Aja" — A + ja. The one who is not born, the one who is eternal. This name of Lord Vishnu signifies his beginninglessness. The Ekadashi takes its name from this very name.

The presiding deity of this Ekadashi is **Hrishikesha** — the lord of the senses. For the devotee who takes refuge in Hrishikesha, the senses do not lead them astray.

This Ekadashi is also called **Annada Ekadashi** — she who gives food, she who nourishes.

Harishchandra's story is the pinnacle of satya-dharma. He did not know it was a test. He simply acted out of his commitment to truth. That was his greatness.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe on Ekadashi and take the vow to fast.
- **Puja setup:** Install an idol or image of Lord Vishnu (Hrishikesha); perform Panchamrita abhisheka — milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar.
- **Offerings:** Tulsi, yellow flowers, yellow garments, and a lamp.
- **Fasting:** Nirjal (waterless) or on fruits — observed through the full Ekadashi day.
- **Recitation:** Vishnu Sahasranama or "Hari-smarana" throughout the day.
- **Night vigil:** All-night "Hari-Hari" kirtan and Vishnu-nama smarana — the hallmark of this Ekadashi.
- **Breaking the fast:** On Dwadashi morning, after sunrise, between the Rahu period and Dwadashi end (paran).

## Mantras

**Hrishikesha dhyana mantra — the presiding deity of Aja Ekadashi:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते हृषीकेशाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Hṛṣīkeśāya

Meaning: "O Hrishikesha (lord of the senses), Lord, salutation to you." — This is the name of Vishnu that is especially associated with this Ekadashi.

---

**For Ekadashi night vigil:**

> ॐ नमो नारायणाय

Oṃ Namo Nārāyaṇāya

Meaning: "Salutation to Narayana." — The eight-syllable mantra of Vishnu.
