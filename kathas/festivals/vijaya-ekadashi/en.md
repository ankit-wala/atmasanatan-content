## Katha

In the Dvapara Yuga, before the battle of Mahabharata, Dharmaraja Yudhishthira asked Bhagavan Shri Krishna — "O Madhava, what is the name of the Ekadashi of Phalguna Krishna Paksha? What fruit does its fast bestow, and what is its katha?"

Bhagavan Vasudeva replied with a smile — "O King, the name of this Ekadashi is Vijaya Ekadashi. Its katha is rooted in the Treta Yuga itself — in the story of Shri Rama, who is Maryada Purushottama incarnate."

---

When Ravana abducted Mother Sita, Bhagavan Shri Rama arrived at the shore of the ocean with his army of vanaras. Before them lay the vast ocean — boundless, unfathomable, and seemingly unconquerable. The army was full of spirit, yet no path presented itself. Shri Rama prayed to the ocean — for three days. The ocean gave no answer.

At that time, the ascetic sage Bakadalbhya was residing in his hermitage in that very region. He was a great knower of wisdom, well versed in the Vedas and scriptures, and a devoted bhakta of Bhagavan Vishnu. Shri Rama went to him. He offered the sage his pranams and placed his difficulty before him.

Sage Bakadalbhya listened to Shri Rama with full attention. Then he spoke — "O Prabhu, You are the Supreme yourself, yet You have come here for the teaching of the world — this too is Your lila. Listen: tomorrow is Phalguna Krishna Ekadashi — Vijaya Ekadashi. One who observes the Ekadashi fast properly on this day, worships Bhagavan Vishnu, and keeps a night vigil, attains victory everywhere. This fast makes one unconquerable across all three worlds. O Rama, observe this fast together with your entire army."

Shri Rama received the sage's words upon his head. Gratitude shone in his eyes. He gathered Lakshmana, Sugriva, Hanuman ji, Vibhishana — the entire vanara army — and under the guidance of sage Bakadalbhya, all of them together took up the fast of Vijaya Ekadashi.

That night the army kept vigil. The name of Bhagavan Vishnu resounded. The chant of "Jai Siya Ram" began to ring out along the ocean shore. The waves of the sea seemed to be listening to the praise.

The next day — on Dvadashi — when Shri Rama performed parana, the Lord's grace became manifest. Nala and Nila began the construction of the setu. The vanara army started lifting mountains and boulders. The very ocean that had not spoken a word for three days now yielded a path. The setu was built, and Shri Rama's army set out toward Lanka.

What happened in Lanka is known to all — Ravana's arrogance was ground to dust, Mother Sita was freed, and Shri Rama was victorious.

Bhagavan Shri Krishna said to Yudhishthira — "O King, this is why this Ekadashi is called Vijaya Ekadashi. One who observes this fast with devotion sees all his endeavours succeed, his enemies are vanquished, and victory comes in life. This fast was observed by Shri Rama himself — what greater proof could there be?"

## Significance

Vijaya Ekadashi holds extraordinary importance because it is directly connected to Bhagavan Shri Rama's conquest of Lanka. This is not merely a day of fasting — it is a remembrance of the triumph of dharma. When the greatest battle stands before us, when the path is blocked, when there is strength but no direction — it is surrender to the Lord and taking refuge in fast and devotion that opens the door to victory. This is precisely what Shri Rama did, and this Ekadashi made his army victorious.

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana, it is said that the fruit of observing this Ekadashi surpasses even the Ashvamedha Yajna. One who meditates upon Shri Vishnu and keeps the fast on this day is released from the sins of birth after birth, and after death attains Vishnuloka. This fast is not merely a symbol of material victory — it is also a symbol of spiritual victory: victory over ego, over inner impurities, and over delusion.

This Ekadashi falls in the Krishna Paksha of the Phalguna month, when spring is at its fullness. This transitional juncture of the season is conducive to the purification of body and mind. Fasting and keeping night vigil purify the body, and remembrance of the Lord's name purifies the mind. It is for this reason that there is a special injunction to observe this Ekadashi before the commencement of any important undertaking in life.

## Vidhi

- **Evening of Dashami:** Take only sattvic food; abstain from onion, garlic, and non-vegetarian food.
- **Fasting:** On Ekadashi, keep a nirjala (waterless) or fruit-only fast; grains, lentils, and grain-based foods are forbidden. Those who cannot keep waterless may consume fruits, milk, and water.
- **Morning of Ekadashi:** Rise at Brahma Muhurta; bathe and take the sankalp of the fast.
- **Puja:** Bathe the image of Bhagavan Vishnu or the Shaligram with panchamrita; offer flowers, tulasi leaves, and sandalwood; perform aarti with incense and lamp. Repeat worship in the evening.
- **Forbidden:** Rice, wheat, lentils, onion, garlic, meat, alcohol, and falsehood; avoid anger and quarrelling throughout the day.
- **Night vigil:** Keep night vigil with bhajans, kirtans, recitation of the Ramayana, or the Vishnu Sahasranama — as Shri Rama did with his army. Chant "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya" throughout.
- **Breaking the fast:** On Dvadashi, after sunrise and before Dvadashi tithi ends, drink water with tulasi leaves and then take parana; offer charity and dakshina to a brahmin. Confirm parana time on DrikPanchang.
