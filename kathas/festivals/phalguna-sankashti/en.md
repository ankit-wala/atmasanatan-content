## Katha

The month of Phalguna — the last month of the Hindu samvat.

In that town there lived an astrologer named Varadaraja. He was learned, and his prophecies were considered infallible. One day he examined the horoscope of an honourable family — and his face became grave.

He said to the head of the family — "There are signs that a great calamity will befall your family in the coming year. I cannot say what it will be — but the planets and stars are giving warning."

Fear settled in the family's hearts.

---

In their home there was an elderly grandmother — she had been observing the Sankashti vrata for many years. She calmed the children and said — "Do not be afraid. Phalguna Sankashti is approaching — the Sankashti of Trinetra Ganesha. He can see — what has happened, what is, and what will be. Ask of him — that he look upon us with all three eyes."

On the day of Phalguna Krishna Chaturthi, the entire family fasted together. They worshipped together. And their prayer was extraordinary — they were saying:

*"O Trinetra, look with your right eye — see the punya of our ancestors. Look with your left eye — see our devotion here today. And look with your third eye — what is coming, only you know. We are not asking that the calamity not come — we are asking that we be able to bear it, and emerge from it greater."*

After moonrise at night they observed parana.

---

That year passed.

The calamity that Varadaraja had predicted — came. The family's large trading venture suffered a loss — money was lost. But the family did not break. They stood together. What was meant to go, went — but the home remained, health remained, love remained.

When they met Varadaraja the following year, they said — "Astrologer ji, you spoke the truth. But what happened was not as terrible as I had feared. As though someone had made it smaller."

Varadaraja said — "This is the grace of Trinetra Ganesha. He does not erase what is coming — but he changes its scale."

---

In the tradition of the Ganesha Purana, it is said — *"The one who worships Trinetra Ganesha on Phalguna Krishna Chaturthi has the obstacles of their rashi loosened. The accumulated obstacles of the old year are destroyed by this vrata and entry into the new year happens without impediment."*

## Significance

Phalguna is the last month of the Hindu samvat. Mahashivaratri and Holi fall within it — both festivals that move by burning the old toward the new. The Sankashti of this month involves worship of Trinetra Ganesha.

**The mystery of Trinetra:** Ganesha's three eyes are the gift of his father Shiva. The puranic account is — once, in play, Mata Parvati covered Shiva's eyes. In that instant darkness fell over all of creation. Then a third eye appeared from Shiva's forehead — it blazed like fire and illuminated the world. Ganesha is Shiva's son — he too received that Trinetra power.

**The meaning of the three eyes:**
- **Right eye** — the sun — the outer world, active vision, what has already passed
- **Left eye** — the moon — the inner world, receptive vision, what is now
- **Third eye** — beyond time — the witness that is not subject to destruction

**The vrata at year's end:** This is the final Sankashti of the year — before Chaitra Shukla Pratipada (the Hindu New Year). On this day there is a tradition of placing before Trinetra Ganesha all that remained stuck in the old year — old wishes, unfinished work, accumulated obstacles — and praying that he see and show the way with his three eyes. Just as Holi burns the old wood — Trinetra Sankashti burns the old obstacles.

## Vidhi

This vrata is observed on Phalguna Krishna Chaturthi.

1. **Morning sankalpa:** After bathing, take the sankalpa — "I observe the Sankashti vrata with worship of Trinetra Ganesha on Phalguna Krishna Chaturthi."

2. **Fast:** Waterless or fruit-based fast from sunrise to moonrise.

3. **Installing Ganesha:** Install the image or picture of Trinetra Ganesha on a red cloth. Apply three dots with sindura as tilaka — as the symbol of Trinetra.

4. **Puja materials:** Red flowers, durva, modak, laddus, tulasi, incense, ghee lamp.

5. **Puja procedure:** Shodasopachara or panchopachara. Chanting "OM Gam Ganapataye Namah" 108 times is especially fruitful.

6. **Offering the obstacles of the old year:** After puja, write on a piece of paper the matters that remained stuck in the past year — and placing it before Ganesha, pray that he look with his three eyes and show the way.

7. **Hearing the katha:** Read the katha of Phalguna Sankashti.

8. **Sighting the moon and parana:** After moonrise, offer arghya to the moon and observe parana.

## Observance

**Rule of fasting:** From sunrise to moonrise. Waterless fasting is best; fruit-based diet is acceptable — grains and salt are prohibited.

**The special feature of Trinetra puja:** On this day there is a tradition of offering a garland of three durva to Ganesha — as a symbol of the three eyes.

**Year-end offering:** The spirit of offering to Ganesha the unfulfilled wishes and obstacles of the old year on Phalguna Sankashti makes this vrata distinctive.

**The rule of moonrise:** The Sankashti vrata is not complete without sighting the moon. If clouds are present, parana may be done at the designated moonrise time.

## Mantras

**Ganesha mula mantra:**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

oṃ gaṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.

---

**Vighnashana shloka:**

> वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटिसमप्रभ ।
> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda, vast of form, radiant as a crore of suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.

---

**Trinetra Ganesha dhyana shloka:**

> त्र्यम्बकं यजामहे सुगन्धिं पुष्टिवर्धनम् ।
> उर्वारुकमिव बन्धनान् मृत्योर्मुक्षीय माऽमृतात् ॥

tryambakaṃ yajāmahe sugandhiṃ puṣṭivardhanam |
urvārukamiva bandhanān mṛtyormukṣīya mā'mṛtāt ||

Meaning: We worship that Tryambaka (the three-eyed one) who is fragrant and the nourisher of all. As a ripened fruit falls free from the stalk — may we be freed from the bondage of death, not from immortality.

*(This Mahamrityunjaya mantra is also recited in the worship of Trinetra Ganesha as a symbol of Trinetra.)*
