## Katha

**Narada's question — the glory of Kartika:**

Once Devarshi Narada asked Bhagavan Vishnu — "Prabhu, you have said that of all months, Kartika is your most beloved. What is it about this month? And what is the significance of bathing every morning at dawn during this month?"

Bhagavan Vishnu said — "Narada, in Kartika I am near to the earth. In that month the veil between the divine and the human grows thin. In this month, a single lamp lit in my name equals ten thousand ordinary lamps. Offering a single tulasi-dal is equal to a full yajna. And one who rises in brahma-muhurta while the stars are still in the sky and bathes in cold water — that person, in one act, receives the fruit of bathing at all tirthas."

Then Narada asked — "But Prabhu, what is the evidence? Is there a katha?"

---

**The katha of Tulasidevi:**

Bhagavan narrated a katha.

Near Mathura, in a small village, there lived a woman — **Tulasidevi**. She was poor, but her devotion to Bhagavan Vishnu was unshakeable. Each day of Kartika month she would rise in brahma-muhurta, go to the Yamuna to bathe, and light a lamp at the feet of Vishnu in the temple.

One year, on a particular Kartika dawn — the stars were still in the sky, the waters of the Yamuna bitterly cold.

Tulasidevi arrived at the ghat. The cold was fierce. She stood at the water's edge — and hesitated for a moment.

Just then, an old woman came and stood beside her — plain garments, a bent frame.

The old woman smiled and said — "Child, why have you stopped?"

Tulasidevi said — "The cold is very great."

The old woman said — "Come, let us bathe together."

The two bathed in those cold waters of the Yamuna. A strange peace descended into Tulasidevi's heart — the peace that always came after bathing was today deeper than before.

When they came out, Tulasidevi turned to look — the old woman was gone. Where she had stood, a golden lamp was floating on the waves of the Yamuna.

Tulasidevi returned home — and fell asleep that night.

---

**The Lord's darshana in a dream:**

That night, Bhagavan Vishnu gave Tulasidevi darshana in her dream. He appeared in his **Damodara** form — that form in which he had accepted the love of Mother Yashoda and was bound to the mortar.

Bhagavan said — "Tulasi, the old woman who bathed with you in the Yamuna today — she was Lakshmi. Every Kartika dawn, Lakshmi comes to this Yamuna — and bathes with the women devotees who come for Kartika Snan. Today you received her company."

Tulasidevi's eyes filled with tears.

Bhagavan continued — "Kartika Snan is not merely the purification of the body. It is to join that divine assembly which gathers every Kartika dawn — Lakshmi, Ganga, Yamuna, and their presiding deities. A devotee who rises in that dawn and steps into the cold water becomes a member of that assembly."

In the tradition of the Padma Purana, it is said that one who bathes every morning in brahma-muhurta during Kartika month, offers a lamp to Bhagavan Damodara, and serves Tulasi — all their sins are destroyed and the door to moksha opens.

## Significance

**Kartika — Vishnu's beloved month:**

According to the Padma Purana, the month of Kartika is most dear to Bhagavan Vishnu. In this month he is pleased by tulasi-dal, lamps, and morning baths. On Kartika Shukla Ekadashi (Devuthani Ekadashi) he awakens from his chaturmas sleep — and so this month is also the month of his awakening.

**Damodara — the special form of Kartika:**

During Kartika, Bhagavan Vishnu is worshipped especially in the Damodara form — that form when Mother Yashoda bound him with a rope (dama) to the mortar (udara = Damodara). This form symbolises the Lord's accessibility and his affectionate tenderness toward devotion.

**The glory of dipadan:**

Dipadan in Kartika — in temples, near the tulasi plant, in the river — yields extraordinary fruit. Every lamp lit in this month reaches the devas.

**The month of Tulasi Vivaha:**

On Kartika Shukla Ekadashi or Dvadashi, Tulasi Vivaha takes place — the symbolic marriage of Bhagavan Vishnu and Tulasi (Vrinda). With this celebration, the season of auspicious marriage muhurtas also begins.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn (daily throughout Kartika):** Rise in brahma-muhurta — approximately one and a half hours before sunrise; bathe in a nearby river, or at home with water mixed with Gangajal; before bathing, take the sankalpa — *"Kārtike māsi prātaḥsnānaṃ karomi."*
- **Damodara puja:** After bathing, meditate on Bhagavan Damodara; offer tulasi-dal, yellow flowers, and sandalwood.
- **Dipadan:** Light lamps near the tulasi plant, in the temple, and if possible in the river; recite the Damodara-ashtakam — the special stotra of Kartika month.
- **Tulasi-seva:** Circumambulate the tulasi plant daily and offer water; during Kartika, plucking tulasi leaves is forbidden — use only leaves that have already fallen.
- **Diet:** Sattvic food throughout — dal-rice, fruits, milk; meat, alcohol, brinjal, and urad dal are considered prohibited.
- **Month-long continuity:** This is a month-long vrata — if one day is missed, take the sankalpa again and resume without discouragement.
- **Devuthani Ekadashi:** On Kartika Shukla Ekadashi, Bhagavan Vishnu awakens from chaturmas sleep; the fast and night-vigil on that day complete the Kartika Snan vrata.
- **Conclusion:** The vrata concludes on Kartika Purnima (Dev-Diwali) — by dipadan in the river and hosting a feast for brahmanas.

## Mantras

**Kartika Snan Sankalpa — Damodara Stuti:**

> दामोदर हृषीकेश कार्तिकप्रिय केशव ।
> गोपाल गोविन्द विष्णो कार्तिकेऽर्चितपादप ॥

dāmodara hṛṣīkeśa kārtikapriya keśava |
gopāla govinda viṣṇo kārtike'rcitapādapa ||

Meaning: O Damodara, O Hrishikesha, O Kartika-beloved Keshava — O Gopala, Govinda, Vishnu — whose feet are worshipped in Kartika — I bow to you.

---

**Tulasi-stuti (recited during Tulasi puja in Kartika):**

> तुलसि श्रीसखि शुभे पापहारिणि पुण्यदे ।
> नमस्ते नारदनुते नारायणमनःप्रिये ॥

tulasi śrīsakhi śubhe pāpahāriṇi puṇyade |
namaste nāradanute nārāyaṇamanaḥpriye ||

Meaning: O Tulasi — companion of Lakshmi, auspicious one, destroyer of sin, giver of merit — praised by Narada and beloved to the heart of Narayana — I bow to you.
