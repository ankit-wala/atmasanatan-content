## Katha

The Ekadashi of the bright fortnight of the month of Vaishakh is called Mohini Ekadashi. The katha of this Ekadashi was told by Lord Sri Krishna himself to King Yudhishthira in the Brahma Vaivarta Purana.

One day Yudhishthira asked Lord Sri Krishna with curiosity, "O Madhav, what is the name of the Vaishakh Shukla Ekadashi and what is its greatness?" Sri Krishna replied, "O King, this Ekadashi is called 'Mohini.' Just as at the time of the churning of the ocean the Lord assumed the Mohini (enchanting form of Vishnu) and secured the nectar for the gods — so too does this Ekadashi enchant all the sins of a devotee and takes them away. Hear its katha."

In ancient times there was a beautiful and prosperous city called Bhadravati. There lived a respected Vaishya merchant named Dhanpal. He had five sons. The first four were humble, industrious, and devoted to dharma, but the nature of the youngest son, Dhrishtabuddhi, was altogether the opposite.

As Dhrishtabuddhi grew older, he sank deep into vice. Drinking, gambling, ill company — these three surrounded him entirely. The household wealth began flowing out. The family's honour was ground into the dust. Dhanpal tried to counsel him; his brothers tried — but nothing touched Dhrishtabuddhi. At last Dhanpal, with a heavy heart, made his decision: he cast Dhrishtabuddhi out of the house.

Dhrishtabuddhi became homeless. The friends he had once known had left along with the money. No shelter for food, no place to spend the night. When hunger became unbearable, he descended to theft. He dragged out his life through petty stealing. Sin piled upon sin, and he sank deeper still.

One day — hungry, exhausted, carrying the weight of his own deeds — Dhrishtabuddhi wandered until he reached the ashram of Maharshi Kaundilya. The sage, with his yogic sight, knew everything. He looked upon that weary young man — on whose face was the shadow of shame, and in whose eyes a tender shoot of pashchatap (remorse/repentance) had just broken through. The sage did not call to him with harshness, but with compassion.

Dhrishtabuddhi fell at the Maharshi's feet and told him everything — the whole account from childhood until that day. He concealed nothing. Tears flowed from his eyes.

Maharshi Kaundilya raised him and said, "Son, you know what you have done, and today you feel shame for it — this very pashchatap is your beginning. Listen: there is a way. The Ekadashi of the bright fortnight of Vaishakh is called Mohini Ekadashi. This Ekadashi burns sins the way the sun burns darkness — without looking at how old the darkness is. Observe this vrat with devotion. Remember Lord Vishnu day and night. Keep the fast, remain awake through the night, repeat the name of Hari."

Those words entered Dhrishtabuddhi's heart. With complete sincerity he observed the vrat of Mohini Ekadashi. He worshipped Lord Vishnu, offered tulsi leaves, and stayed awake through the night, chanting the name of Hari. His mind, which had been clouded for years, seemed to grow clear through that one night of the vrat.

By the grace of Lord Vishnu, all of Dhrishtabuddhi's sins dissolved. His life returned to an auspicious path. In his next birth he was born as a virtuous, prosperous, and dharma-abiding man, and in the end he attained the supreme abode of the Lord.

## Significance

The word "Mohini" carries a deep meaning. When the gods and demons were churning the ocean together and the nectar emerged, the demons seized it. Lord Vishnu assumed the divine form of Mohini — otherworldly beauty, irresistible allure. That form enchanted the demons, and the nectar was delivered to the gods.

This Mohini Ekadashi works in precisely the same way. It "enchants" sin — draws it toward itself and then takes it away. Sins accumulated over years, karma that has settled deep — the grace of this Ekadashi dissolves them the way nectar was wrested from the demons who held it and given to those who deserved it.

The most important message in the katha of Dhrishtabuddhi is this: there is no minimum condition for Vishnu's compassion. Dhrishtabuddhi had reached the very bottom — vice, theft, abandonment by his family. And yet when he observed this vrat with pashchatap, the Lord accepted him. This is why this Ekadashi is so particular — it is for those who believe they have fallen too far, that it is too late. In the Lord's sight, it is never too late.

The month of Vaishakh is itself considered sacred — in this month, service to the tulsi plant, bathing in the Ganga, and the worship of Vishnu carry special merit. The Ekadashi of that month is the fullest expression of that sanctity.

## Vidhi

- **Dashami preparation:** Take a single sattvic, light meal in the evening; abstain from urad dal, meat, garlic, onion, and all tamasic items; observe brahmacharya and keep the mind calm.
- **Fasting:** Complete nirjala fast is the highest observance; those who cannot keep nirjala may take fruits, milk, and water; grain, urad dal, masoor dal, brinjal, garlic, onion, and meat are all prohibited.
- **At dawn (Ekadashi):** Rise during Brahma Muhurta, bathe, and wear clean yellow or white clothing; light a ghee lamp before an image of Lord Vishnu or a shaligram.
- **Puja:** Offer tulsi leaves, yellow flowers, sandalwood paste, and incense while repeating *Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya*; read or listen to the katha of Mohini Ekadashi; perform puja again in the evening and recite Vishnu Sahasranama or sing Hari kirtan.
- **Night vigil:** Stay awake through the night repeating Hari's name, singing bhajans and kirtan, or reciting the Ekadashi mahatmya — night vigil is an especially vital part of this vrat.
- **Conduct:** Avoid speaking falsehood, anger, and speaking ill of others throughout the vrat.
- **Parana (Dwadashi):** After sunrise, within the Dwadashi tithi, break the fast while remembering Hari; take tulsi leaves at parana; offer a meal and dakshina to a Brahmin to complete the sankalpa.

## Mantras

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya

Meaning: O Lord Vasudeva, I bow before you.

---

> त्वमेव माता च पिता त्वमेव
> त्वमेव बन्धुश्च सखा त्वमेव।
> त्वमेव विद्या द्रविणं त्वमेव
> त्वमेव सर्वं मम देव देव॥

tvam eva mātā ca pitā tvam eva
tvam eva bandhuś ca sakhā tvam eva |
tvam eva vidyā draviṇaṃ tvam eva
tvam eva sarvaṃ mama deva deva ||

Meaning: O God of gods, you alone are my mother, you alone are my father, you alone are my kinsman and my friend. You alone are knowledge, you alone are wealth — you alone are my everything.

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana it is said that the devotee who observes the vrat of Mohini Ekadashi receives merit equal to an Ashvamedha yajna, that all their sins — however deep they may be — are destroyed, and that they ultimately attain the supreme abode of Lord Vishnu.
