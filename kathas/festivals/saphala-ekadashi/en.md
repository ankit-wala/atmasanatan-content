## Katha

The Ekadashi of the Krishna Paksha of the month of Pausha is called Saphala Ekadashi. The katha of this Ekadashi was narrated by Lord Sri Krishna to King Yudhishthira in the Brahma Vaivarta Purana.

In ancient times, a righteous king named Mahijassamudra ruled his kingdom with virtue. His son was named Lumpak. Lumpak's character was the complete opposite of his father's. He turned away from the worship of the gods, showed disrespect to his parents, squandered wealth in bad company, and conducted himself with cruelty. The Brahmins and subjects of the kingdom were distressed by his misconduct.

Deeply grieved by his son's misdeeds, King Mahijassamudra one day banished Lumpak from the kingdom. Lumpak began wandering in the forest. There he had neither food nor shelter. He sustained himself on whatever he could find — fruits, roots, or whatever came his way. At night, fear of wild animals kept him on edge. His life was filled with hardship.

One night — the night of Pausha Krishna Dashami — Lumpak was utterly exhausted. He went and sat beneath a great pipal tree. That winter night was bitterly cold. Out of fear, weariness, and the cold, he could neither sleep properly nor eat anything. He spent the entire night awake beneath that pipal tree.

The next morning — the day of Ekadashi — he found no food either. He remained hungry throughout the day. He did not know it was Ekadashi; nor did he hold any intention of keeping a fast. Yet behold the design of Providence — the pipal tree is most dear to Lord Vishnu. The night vigil beneath that sacred tree, and the fast kept without knowing it — this itself became the fruit of Saphala Ekadashi.

In that very moment, Lumpak experienced something divine. The merciful gaze of Lord Vishnu fell upon him. The sins that had accumulated within him over years of bad company and wrongful conduct were destroyed in an instant. A divine chariot appeared. The light of wisdom descended within him. Lumpak's mind was transformed. He returned to his kingdom, ruled it righteously, and in the end attained the abode of Lord Vishnu.

The tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana declares: if an Ekadashi whose fruit was this great even for an unknowing, vowless sinner — then for the devotee who observes it with faith and intention, the merit earned is beyond counting.

## Significance

"Saphala" means fruitful, successful. This Ekadashi is called Saphala precisely because its fast never goes without fruit. The katha of Lumpak teaches that there is no limit to Lord Vishnu's compassion — He showers His mercy even upon the one who serves Him without knowing it.

The pipal tree is beloved of Vishnu, and its worship on the day of Ekadashi is considered especially fruitful. The scriptures declare that Vishnu Himself dwells in the pipal. To keep a night vigil beneath that tree on Ekadashi is an act of great merit.

However deep the sins may be, the fast of Ekadashi can sever them — this is the central message of this Ekadashi.

## Vidhi

**Dashami (the day before Ekadashi):**
- Take food only once in the evening.
- Avoid urad dal, masoor lentils, brinjal, and all tamasic foods.
- Observe brahmacharya.

**On the day of Ekadashi:**
1. Bathe at dawn and wear clean clothes.
2. Offer a lamp, tulsi, flowers, and incense before Lord Vishnu.
3. Worship the pipal tree — offer water, wrap sacred thread around it, and bow before it.
4. Observe a fast throughout the day (complete nirjala, or fruit-only).
5. In the evening, perform Vishnu kirtan and keep a night vigil.

**On Dwadashi:**
- Break the fast (paran) after sunrise.
- Conclude the vrat by offering food and dakshina to a Brahmin.

## Observance

- **Fast:** A complete fast (nirjala) is ideal; those unable may take fruit, milk, and water.
- **Pipal worship:** Worship of the pipal tree holds special importance on this Ekadashi — in Lumpak's katha, that very spot is the center of the mahatmya.
- **Night vigil:** Stay awake through the night chanting or singing in the name of Vishnu — this is an essential part of the vrat.
- **Time of paran:** On Dwadashi, break the fast after sunrise while remembering Hari. Be sure to take tulsi leaves during paran.
- **The night of Dashami:** The true beginning of Ekadashi is from the evening of Dashami itself — from that evening onward, keep a restrained diet and a pure mind.
