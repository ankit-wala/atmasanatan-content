## Katha

In the folk tradition of North and West India, this story of the Friday Lakshmi Vrat is told.

In a certain town lived a woman named Sushila — simple in nature, devoted in faith, skilled in the running of a household.

Her husband Ramesh was a merchant — hardworking, honest. But success in trade would not come. Whatever he put in would be lost. The home was pinched. There was weariness in the heart.

One Friday, Sushila was returning from the market. She passed by the house of her neighbour Kamla. At Kamla's door there were red flowers, sindoor, tulsi — and a small image of Lakshmi.

Sushila stopped and asked — *"Kamla, what is today?"*

Kamla said with a smile — *"The Friday Lakshmi Vrat. Worship Mata Lakshmi for seven Fridays — red flowers, sindoor, belpatra, fruit. Alakshmi cannot stay in the home."*

Sushila asked — *"Alakshmi?"*

Kamla replied — *"Poverty, quarrel, the breaking apart of a home — this is the abode of Alakshmi. Where there is true devotion to Lakshmi, Alakshmi cannot linger."*

Something stirred inside Sushila. She took the sankalpa for the vrat that very Friday.

---

On the first Friday, Sushila bathed in the morning. She adorned a small picture of Lakshmi at the place of worship. She brought red hibiscus flowers from the market. Sindoor, tulsi, betel leaves, a red cloth, a lamp — she arranged them all with care.

As bhog, rice and jaggery — sweet, peaceful, sattvic.

Sitting before the Mata, she said — *"Mother, I do not want wealth. Only let there be peace in this home, let my husband succeed, let the home not fall apart."*

---

The second Friday, the third — the vrat continued.

After the fourth Friday, a new direction opened in Ramesh's trade. An old partner made contact. An old account that had been considered lost — half of it came back.

By the fifth Friday there was laughter in the house for the first time in a long while.

---

On the night of the sixth Friday, Sushila had a dream.

A radiant Devi — golden-hued garments, seated on a lotus, a golden vessel in her hand — stood before her.

She said — *"Sushila, your devotion is true. Alakshmi has left your home. The lack you suffered — it was my absence, not your fate. I have come now."*

Sushila's eyes opened. Her heart was full — there was weeping and there was joy.

---

The seventh Friday arrived.

Sushila performed the puja with especial joy. For the udyapan, she invited seven married women from the neighbourhood and from among her relations. She fed them yellow sweet rice and halwa as bhog. She offered red chunari and sindoor as prasad.

That month Ramesh's trade yielded a great profit.

In the folk tradition of the Friday Lakshmi Vrat, it is said that a woman who worships Mata Lakshmi sincerely for seven consecutive Fridays with red flowers and sindoor and who completes the udyapan — Alakshmi leaves her home, sweetness comes into the marriage, and Lakshmi takes up permanent residence there.

## Significance

Shukra — this word carries two meanings. One is the planet Shukra (Venus) — considered the significator of wealth, beauty, and pleasure. The other is brightness, brilliance, radiance.

Friday is dedicated to Mata Lakshmi for this very reason — the energy of Shukra and the grace of Lakshmi flow from the same source — prosperity, gentleness, and the happiness of the householder.

Lakshmi's elder sister is Alakshmi — the embodiment of poverty and strife. Folk belief holds that where there is sloth, impurity, and lack of devotion — Alakshmi makes her abode. Through sincere worship of Lakshmi, Alakshmi withdraws of her own accord.

In Maharashtra, this vrat is enormously popular under the name "Vaibhav Lakshmi Vrat." In Gujarat too, the tradition of Friday Lakshmi puja is especially alive. In South India, Friday is considered the primary day for Devi worship.

This vrat is also a celebration of the Grihalakshmi — the woman of the house. The woman who sustains the home is herself the living form of Lakshmi.

## Vidhi

- **Duration:** Observe for seven consecutive Fridays; beginning on a Friday in the bright fortnight (shukla paksha) is considered especially auspicious.
- **At dawn:** Bathe, take the sankalpa for the vrat, and keep the house clean; draw a swastika or Lakshmi's footprint at the door with ochre as a welcoming symbol.
- **Puja setup:** Establish a murti, picture, or yantra of Lakshmi on a red cloth; offer red hibiscus flowers (the special offering of this vrat), sindoor, roli, haldi, tulsi, betel leaves, and fruit.
- **Lamp and bhog:** Light a ghee lamp; offer a sweet bhog — sweet rice, kheer, or halwa.
- **Katha and aarti:** Listen to the Lakshmi katha and perform the Mata's aarti.
- **Fasting:** No food all day, or fruit only; avoid salty and tamasic food; break the fast after the evening puja.
- **Colour of worship:** Red throughout — flowers, chunari, sindoor — the colour of Lakshmi's energy.
- **Udyapan:** On the seventh Friday, feed seven married women a meal; offer red chunari, sindoor, and haldi-kumkum as prasad.

## Mantras

**Mahalakshmi Bija Mantra:**

> ॐ श्रीं ह्रीं श्रीं कमले कमलालये प्रसीद प्रसीद ।
> ॐ श्रीं ह्रीं श्रीं महालक्ष्म्यै नमः ॥

oṃ śrīṃ hrīṃ śrīṃ kamale kamalālaye prasīda prasīda |
oṃ śrīṃ hrīṃ śrīṃ mahālakṣmyai namaḥ ||

Meaning: O Kamala Lakshmi, who abides upon the lotus — be pleased, be pleased. O Mahalakshmi, salutation to you.

---

**Lakshmi Gayatri:**

> ॐ महालक्ष्म्यै च विद्महे विष्णुपत्न्यै च धीमहि ।
> तन्नो लक्ष्मीः प्रचोदयात् ॥

oṃ mahālakṣmyai ca vidmahe viṣṇupatnyai ca dhīmahi |
tanno lakṣmīḥ pracodayāt ||

Meaning: May we come to know Mahalakshmi, may we meditate on the consort of Vishnu — may that Lakshmi inspire and guide us.
