## Katha

The sixth day arrived in Navratri's journey — and with it came the story that has been told age after age. It is the story of a demon's ambition, the suffering of the three worlds — and the divine power that manifests when no other path remains.

This is the story of **Maa Katyayani**.

---

Mahishasura — lord of the buffalo — performed fierce austerities to propitiate Brahma. He persevered for years until Brahma was pleased.

He asked for a boon: *"Let no man be the cause of my death."*

He did not count women at all. *What can a woman do?* — that was his arrogance.

Brahma granted the boon.

---

The moment the boon was given, Mahishasura resolved to conquer all three worlds. He attacked the heavens. Indra and all the gods took to battle — but none could stand against Mahishasura. By the power of his boon he was invincible.

Heaven had to be abandoned. The gods wandered the earth.

---

All the gods gathered — Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Indra, and all the divine hosts. Their pain, their fury, their radiance — all combined into a single flame.

Radiance poured from Brahma's face. Vishnu's brilliance fed the blaze further. Shiva's wrath filled it with even greater force. Indra, Agni, Vayu, Surya, Varuna — the radiance of each poured into that flame.

And from that combined radiance a divine form appeared — *a woman's form*, blazing with incomparable light.

---

Each god gave her their finest weapon.

Shiva gave the trident. Vishnu gave the Sudarshana Chakra. Indra gave the Vajra (the thunderbolt). Agni gave the shakti. Vayu gave the bow and arrows. Surya gave armour. The ocean gave lotuses and ornaments. The Himalayas gave a lion as her mount.

The Goddess was adorned. Four-armed, golden of form, seated upon a lion.

---

She first appeared at the hermitage of Rishi **Katyayana**.

Rishi Katyayana had performed tapasya for many years, holding in his heart the longing to behold the Goddess. She appeared at his hermitage, and he worshipped her for three days.

*Because her first worship took place at Katyayana's hermitage — she came to be called 'Katyayani.'*

---

Then the Goddess departed — towards Mahishasura.

The battle was fierce. Mahishasura's army surrounded the Goddess. She alone annihilated that vast army — the gods watched in wonder as she wielded her weapons.

Finally Mahishasura came forward himself. He kept changing his form — now buffalo, now lion, now man.

At the very moment he was shifting from one form to another — the Goddess seized him. And slew him with her trident.

A cry of victory rang across the three worlds.

---

In the tradition of the Markandeya Purana it is said that devotees who worship Maa Katyayani on the sixth day are freed from fear, enemies, and obstacles. The hurdles on the path of dharma are removed by Katyayani's grace.

The form of Maa Katyayani: mounted on a lion, four-armed — a sword in one hand, a lotus in another; the right hands in the abhaya and vara mudras; her body golden, her face a union of valour and compassion.

## Significance

**The combined power of all the gods:**
Maa Katyayani is not the power of any single god — she is the convergence of the radiance of all the gods. Whenever adharma grows so great that no single power suffices, combined radiance creates a new power. This is the message of the sixth day.

**Mahishasura Mardini — the destruction of arrogance:**
Mahishasura's boon was born of his arrogance — *leave women aside, what can they do.* Maa Katyayani is the answer to that very arrogance. The one who underestimates power is destroyed by power itself.

**Ajna Chakra:**
On the sixth day of Navratri the practice centres on the Ajna Chakra — the point between the two eyebrows. This is the chakra of discernment, far-sightedness, and the power of decision. Worship of Maa Katyayani awakens this chakra.

**Colour: red** — a symbol of valour, power, and victory. Wearing red garments on the sixth day is considered auspicious.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe and wear red garments — the colour of the sixth day; performing puja in the Brahma Muhurta is considered best.
- **Puja:** Install an image or idol of Maa Katyayani; meditate on her lion-mount and four-armed form; light a lamp of cow's ghee; perform aarti with incense and lamp; aarti and japa may also be done at dusk.
- **Offerings:** Red flowers (hibiscus, red roses, or any red flower); **honey as bhog** — offering honey on the sixth day is considered especially fruitful.
- **Recitation:** Recite the dhyana shloka and the Ya Devi stotra; chant "Om Katyainyai Namah" 108 times; distribute prasad.
- **Fasting:** The Navratri fast continues — a single sattvik meal (fruit, milk, sabudana) or complete fasting according to the devotee's strength and resolve.
- **Special sankalpa:** On this day resolve in the heart — may Maa Katyayani destroy whatever 'Mahishasura' lives within (fear, arrogance, adharma).
- **2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Ashwin Shukla Shashthi — ~October 16, 2026).

## Mantras

**Dhyana Shloka — meditation on the form of Maa Katyayani:**

> चन्द्रहासोज्ज्वलकरा शार्दूलवरवाहना ।
> कात्यायनी शुभं दद्यात् देवी दानवघातिनी ॥

Candrahāsojjvalakarā śārdūlavaravāhanā |
kātyāyanī śubhaṃ dadyāt devī dānavaghātinī ||

Meaning: "She whose hands blaze with the Chandrahasa sword, who rides upon the finest lion, and who destroys demons — may Maa Katyayani grant us auspiciousness."

---

**Ya Devi Stotra — salutation to Maa Katyayani:**

> या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ कात्यायनी रूपेण संस्थिता ।
> नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः ॥

Yā devī sarvabhūteṣu māṃ kātyāyanī rūpeṇa saṃsthitā |
namastasyai namastasyai namastasyai namo namaḥ ||

Meaning: "To that Goddess who dwells in all beings in the form of Maa Katyayani — salutations, salutations, salutations."
