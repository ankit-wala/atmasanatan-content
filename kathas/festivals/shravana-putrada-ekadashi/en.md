## Katha

In the city of Mahishmati, King Mahijit ruled. Everything was his — a vast kingdom, the love of his subjects, a full treasury, an army. But there was one absence that burned inside him night and day. He had no son.

Years passed. The people of Mahishmati were grieved along with their king. One day the ministers and citizens gathered and resolved: we will seek the refuge of sages.

They went together into the forest. There they found the great Sage Lomasha — deep in austerity, one who knew all three times: past, present, and future.

Sage Lomasha looked at them and asked: *"What is it, good people? Why do your faces carry such worry?"*

They said: *"Maharshi, our king Mahijit has no son. This sorrow is his — and all of ours. Please show us a way."*

Sage Lomasha was quiet for a moment. With his divine sight he looked into the king's previous birth. Then he spoke:

*"Listen. In his previous life, your king was a prosperous merchant. One day, in fierce summer heat, he was returning from trade. On the road there was a pond. A cow had come there to drink, with her calf alongside — both thirsty, both exhausted. That merchant pushed them aside and drank first himself. That one moment of unkindness planted a seed — and in this life it has become childlessness."*

The citizens were stunned. A sin no one remembered. A lapse that seemed small — but whose weight had traveled from one life into the next.

*"Then is there no remedy?"* someone asked.

Sage Lomasha said: *"There is. Shravana Shukla Ekadashi — Putrada Ekadashi. Let the king and queen observe this fast together. Fast through the day, keep vigil through the night. On Dvadashi, make a pavitraka — a sacred cord of gold, silver, silk, or lotus — and give it to brahmins. The merit of this vrat will wash away that sin from a former life."*

King Mahijit and his queen observed the vrat with full devotion together. Through the night of that Ekadashi they kept vigil, holding Vishnu in their hearts. On Dvadashi they broke their fast.

Time passed — and the queen gave birth to a son. Mahijit's joy had no limit. The son for whom he had been longing for years — he had come as the gift of Putrada Ekadashi.

The Padma Purana records the words of Sage Lomasha: *"He who observes this Ekadashi will receive a son. Even one who does not seek a son will have other desires fulfilled. And even one who merely hears this katha with faith — they too receive a share of this merit."*

## Significance

The very name Putrada Ekadashi is a promise — the one that gives a son. In the Indian tradition, a son is the carrier of the lineage, the one who performs the rituals that accompany a parent beyond death.

But the deeper teaching of this katha is this: King Mahijit's grief was not his alone — his people suffered with him. And his sin was not even in his memory — it was a moment of unconsciousness.

The scripture says this: the actions we commit, we forget. But the actions do not forget.

Ekadashi takes up that weight.

## Vidhi

1. Take sattvic food the night of Dashami.
2. On Ekadashi morning, bathe and take the sankalpa (vow).
3. Worship Vishnu with tulsi, flowers, unbroken rice (akshat), and a diya.
4. Chant *Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya*.
5. Keep vigil through the night — this is required.
6. On Dvadashi, make a pavitraka — a sacred cord of gold, silver, silk, or lotus — and offer it to brahmins.
7. Break the fast within the Dvadashi tithi.

**For those seeking children:** Husband and wife should observe this fast together. State the desire clearly before the Lord in prayer.

## Observance

**Parana:** On Dvadashi, break the fast after sunrise, before the Dvadashi tithi ends.

**Pavitraka:** This is the distinctive tradition of this Ekadashi. On Dvadashi, make a pavitraka from gold, silver, silk, or lotus and offer it to brahmins as a ritual gift.

**Couple's vrat:** Husband and wife observing together is considered the most complete form of this vrat.

## Mantras

**Puja mantra for Putrada Ekadashi:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय नमः पुत्रं देहि ।

*Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya namaḥ putraṃ dehi |*

Meaning: "O Lord Vasudeva — grant me a son."

**This name from Vishnu Sahasranama — for the prayer of children:**

> ॐ पुत्रदाय नमः

*Oṃ Putradāya Namaḥ*

Meaning: "Salutation to him who gives sons." (One of the thousand names of Vishnu.)
