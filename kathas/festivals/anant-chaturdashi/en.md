## Katha

**The question in the forest:**

King Yudhishthira once asked Lord Sri Krishna — "Madhava, we followed dharma faithfully, yet our kingdom was lost and we had to wander in the forest. Why?"

The Lord said — "O King, there is a remedy. There is an ancient vrat — Anant Chaturdashi. Hear its story."

---

**The story of Kaundinya:**

In the land of Vidarbha, there lived a learned brahmin named Kaundinya. His wife was Sushila — pious and devoted to vrats.

Their life was happy. Then one Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturdashi, Sushila went to a riverbank where some women were performing a puja.

She asked — "What puja is this?"

They explained — "The Anant Vrat. Worship of Lord Ananta. Tie a fourteen-knotted thread and keep it for fourteen years. Lord Ananta's grace comes upon you."

Sushila also performed that vrat. After the proper puja, she tied the silk thread on her left wrist.

When Kaundinya returned home and saw the thread on her wrist, he asked — "What is this?"

Sushila explained — "It is the Anant vrat sutra. It is Lord Ananta's protection."

Kaundinya laughed — "What will this thread do? It is merely a piece of string." And he pulled the thread off and threw it into the fire.

---

**Everything was lost:**

From that day forward — slowly, steadily — everything began to go.

The cow died. The crops failed. The trade collapsed. The house fell to ruin. Kaundinya sank into debt.

Whatever they had — was gone.

Kaundinya's heart became tormented. He understood — this was of his own doing. He had insulted the sutra of Lord Ananta.

He asked Sushila's forgiveness.

And he set out into the forest — to find Lord Ananta.

---

**Finding the Lord in the forest:**

Wandering in the forest, Kaundinya met an old man.

He asked — "Baba, have you seen Lord Ananta?"

The old man smiled and said — "Why are you seeking him?"

"I insulted the sutra of his vrat. I lost his grace. Now I must make amends."

The old man said — "I am Ananta."

Kaundinya fell at his feet.

"Lord, forgive me. I did not know."

Lord Ananta said — "Go. Every year on Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturdashi, observe the Anant Vrat. Fourteen years. With complete faith. Tie the fourteen-knotted sutra. Offer fourteen types of grain, fourteen types of fruit, fourteen lamps."

Kaundinya observed that vrat — for fourteen years.

At the end of the fourteenth year — everything returned to him. More than before.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that one who observes this vrat receives, through the grace of Lord Ananta, prosperity in life, protection of the family, and ultimately liberation.

## Significance

**The meaning of Ananta:**
"Ananta" = that which has no end. Lord Vishnu's infinite form resting on the Shesha serpent. Shesha is also called Ananta — for he has infinite hoods and bears the weight of creation upon himself.

**The Anant Sutra — fourteen knots:**
Fourteen knots = a symbol of the fourteen worlds (lokas). Or a symbol of the fourteen-year vrat. The thread, of silk or cotton, is tied with fourteen knots. Men tie it on the right arm; women on the left.

**Ganesh Visarjan:**
Anant Chaturdashi is the concluding day of the ten-day Ganesh festival that begins on Ganesh Chaturthi. In Maharashtra, Gujarat, and Karnataka, the day sees the grand procession of **Ganesh Visarjan** — the immersion of Ganesha's image in water. Two great festivals on one tithi — the Anant vrat and the farewell to Ganesha.

**Connection to the Pandavas:**
In the tradition of the Bhavishya Purana, it is said that Lord Sri Krishna taught this vrat to Yudhishthira during his exile — and through the power of the Anant Vrat, the Pandavas regained their kingdom.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Bathe and take the vrat sankalpa; install an image of Lord Ananta (Shesha-Shayi Vishnu).
- **Anant Sutra:** Prepare a red or yellow silk thread dyed with turmeric, tied with fourteen knots; men tie it on the right arm, women on the left.
- **Puja:** Panchamrit abhisheka; offer fourteen types of fruit, grain, and lamps; hear the Anant Chaturdashi vrat katha.
- **Fasting:** Complete fast or one meal (ekabhukta) — hearing the katha is mandatory.
- **Vrat commitment:** Once tied, the Anant Sutra vrat is observed annually for fourteen years; in the fourteenth year, the final sutra is immersed in water.
- **Ganesh Visarjan:** On this final day of Ganapati Utsava, the Ganesha image is immersed with a grand procession in the evening.

## Mantras

**Ananta Bhagavan mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते अनन्ताय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Anantāya

Meaning: "Salutation to Lord Ananta." — The root mantra of the Anant Vrat.

**Ananta Shayana dhyana shloka:**

> शान्ताकारं भुजगशयनं पद्मनाभं सुरेशं
> विश्वाधारं गगनसदृशं मेघवर्णं शुभाङ्गम् ।
> लक्ष्मीकान्तं कमलनयनं योगिभिर्ध्यानगम्यं
> वन्दे विष्णुं भवभयहरं सर्वलोकैकनाथम् ॥

Śāntākāraṃ bhujagaśayanaṃ padmanābhaṃ sureśaṃ
viśvādhāraṃ gaganasadṛśaṃ meghavarṇaṃ śubhāṅgam |
Lakṣmīkāntaṃ kamalanayanaṃ yogibhir dhyānagamyaṃ
vande viṣṇuṃ bhavabhayaharaṃ sarvalokaikānātham ||

Meaning: "The one of peaceful form, who rests on the serpent, lotus-naveled, Lord of the gods, the support of the universe, vast as the sky, dark as a cloud, of auspicious form — beloved of Lakshmi, lotus-eyed, attainable in meditation by yogis — I salute that Vishnu, who removes the fear of this world, the one Lord of all realms."
