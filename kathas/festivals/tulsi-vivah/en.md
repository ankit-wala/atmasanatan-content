## Katha

### The Rise of Jalandhara

This katha is described in the Uttara Khanda of the Padma Purana.

Long ago, from an invincible power born at the time of the churning of the ocean, an asura named Jalandhara came into being. He was immensely powerful — his radiance and valor were such that even the gods lived in fear of him. Jalandhara attacked the heavenly realms and defeated the gods.

Yet the secret of Jalandhara's invincibility lay not in his own strength — it lay in his wife, **Vrinda**.

Vrinda was a supremely devoted and chaste wife. Her virtue was so steadfast and pure that her power formed an impenetrable armour around Jalandhara. As long as Vrinda's vow remained unbroken — whether gods, demons, or Shiva himself — no one could defeat Jalandhara. On the battlefield stood the armies of the gods and Shiva; and on the other side, within her home, sat Vrinda absorbed in single-minded worship.

### Vishnu's Deception

The gods went to Vishnu and prayed: "O Madhava, there seems to be no way to defeat Jalandhara. Only you can do something. This war cannot be won without breaking Vrinda's vow of devoted chastity."

Vishnu saw the suffering of the gods. He had to protect dharma, to stop the advancing empire of adharma. He assumed the form of Jalandhara and set out toward Vrinda's ashrama.

Vrinda was absorbed in worship. When she saw that her husband had left the battlefield and returned, she was filled with joy. She did not recognise him — the veil of maya was dense. Thinking him her husband, Vrinda touched him.

In that very moment — Vrinda's unbroken vow of devoted chastity was shattered.

On the battlefield, the real Jalandhara stood. His armour suddenly broke. His strength fell away. Shiva slew Jalandhara in that same instant.

### Vrinda's Curse

When the truth dawned on Vrinda — when the veil of maya lifted and she recognised that it was Vishnu before her, not her husband — her heart shattered.

Her virtue, her love, her years of devoted worship — all had been destroyed by deception. There were no tears in Vrinda's eyes — only a deep grief, and from that grief, an unwavering resolve.

Vrinda looked at Vishnu and said:

"You deceived me. You, who are Shri Hari himself, by cunning broke the vow of a devoted and chaste wife. Let one whose heart is stone become stone itself — **you shall become a rock.**"

Vishnu became stone — **Shaligrama**, the sacred black stone. On the banks of the Gandaki river stands that black stone — which to this day is revered as the living form of Vishnu himself.

### Vishnu's Boon

Yet Vishnu accepted Vrinda's curse with joy. He knew — an injustice had been done in this story. A devoted wife had been made to suffer for the protection of dharma. There must be atonement for this.

He said to Vrinda:

"Vrinda, the curse you have given — it is just. I accept it. But I also grant you a boon.

Your devotion, your steadfast virtue as a devoted and chaste wife, your years of worship — none of this will go to waste. Leaving this body, you will be born as **Tulsi** — the most sacred plant on earth. If a single leaf of yours is offered in my worship, that worship will be complete. Wherever Tulsi grows, there I shall dwell.

And every year — on **Kartik Shukla Dwadashi** — you will be wed to my Shaligrama form. The world will know you as my beloved — **the eternal sacred marriage of Tulsi and Vishnu**."

Vrinda left her body. Where her remains fell, a Tulsi plant grew.

---

This is why — in every act of worship, a Tulsi leaf is offered to Vishnu. Shaligrama and Tulsi are worshipped side by side. And every year, on the day after Dev Uthani Ekadashi — Kartik Shukla Dwadashi — **Tulsi Vivah** takes place. The Tulsi plant is wed to Vishnu in his Shaligrama form — and from that sacred marriage begins the auspicious season for Hindu weddings.

The Padma Purana declares — the devotee who performs Tulsi Vivah receives merit equal to **kanyadan**, the gift of a daughter in marriage.

## Significance

- **The beginning of the wedding season:** With Tulsi Vivah, the Chaturmas period ends and the auspicious season for Hindu weddings begins. It falls on the day after Dev Uthani Ekadashi — when Vishnu awakens from his yogic sleep.

- **Shaligrama — Vishnu's stone form:** Shaligramas are black stones found in the Gandaki river (Nepal). They are naturally spiral-shaped and are revered as the living form of Vishnu. This form came into being through Vrinda's curse — here lies the philosophical depth of this katha.

- **The supremacy of devotion:** Vrinda's devotion was so great that Vishnu joyfully accepted her curse. This katha teaches — a devotee's grief touches even the Lord.

- **Tulsi — Vishnu's beloved:** The Tulsi plant is present in every Hindu home. She is not merely a medicinal plant — she is the living form of Vrinda's devotion. Vishnu's own word is clear: worship without a Tulsi leaf is incomplete.

- **The balance of dharma and compassion:** This katha also says — sometimes harsh decisions must be taken to protect dharma, but the innocent who suffers because of them must be honoured.

## Vidhi

- **Day & time:** Kartik Shukla Dwadashi (the day after Dev Uthani Ekadashi); ceremony performed in the evening after sunset, by the light of lamps.
- **Fasting:** In some families, women observe a fast on this day and break it only after Tulsi Vivah is performed.
- **Adorning the Tulsi:** Bathe the Tulsi plant; dress her in a red chunari and yellow sari; apply sindoor in the parting of her branches; adorn her as a bride with turmeric, henna, and flowers.
- **Installing the Shaligrama:** Bathe the Shaligrama stone in clean water; clothe him in yellow garments; light incense and a lamp; place him beside the Tulsi — this is Vishnu as the bridegroom.
- **Wedding canopy:** Construct a mandap of sugarcane around the Tulsi (sugarcane is auspicious in Kartik); hang banana-leaf and mango-leaf torana.
- **Puja:** Perform panchopachara puja — incense, lamp, naivedya (amla, jujube, sweets), flowers, and water; offer Tulsi leaves to both Tulsi and Shaligrama.
- **Kanyadan & Saptapadi:** Take the sankalpa of "Tulsi kanyadan"; tie a sacred thread around Tulsi and Shaligrama and perform seven rounds, reciting Vishnu's names throughout; place a mangalsutra on Tulsi and fill her parting with sindoor.
- **Charity:** According to one's means, feed brahmins; give in charity — a cow, clothing, and grain; distribute prasad to neighbours and the needy.
- **Special:** From this day Chaturmas concludes — sadhus who observed the vow may resume travel; auspicious wedding dates begin falling in Margashirsha, Pausha, and Magha.
- **Note:** Do not pluck Tulsi leaves on this day; collect leaves for puja the day before (Tulsi leaves are also never plucked on Sundays).

## Mantras

**Tulsi Vivah Mantra:**

> तुलसीं विवाहयामि विष्णुना सह शोभने ।
> मया कृतं पुण्यकर्म सर्वदोषं व्यपोहतु ॥

Tulasīṃ vivāhayāmi viṣṇunā saha śobhane | mayā kṛtaṃ puṇyakarma sarvadoṣaṃ vyapohatu ||

Meaning: "I perform the sacred marriage of the auspicious Tulsi with Vishnu — may this virtuous act of mine remove all faults."

---

**Tulsi Stuti Mantra:**

> तुलस्यमृतजन्मासि सदा त्वं विष्णुवल्लभा ।
> नमस्तुभ्यं देवदेवि पापं हर हरिप्रिये ॥

Tulasyamṛtajanmāsi sadā tvaṃ viṣṇuvallabhā | namastubhyaṃ devadevi pāpaṃ hara haripriye ||

Meaning: "O Tulsi, you are born of amrita, you are ever Vishnu's beloved — O goddess of goddesses, beloved of Hari, take away our sins."
