## Katha

In the folk tradition of the Wednesday fast, it is said that the birth story of Budh Dev is different from that of the other planets — it holds a question, a grief, and in the end, an attainment.

Chandra had become enamoured of Tara, the wife of Devaguru Brihaspati. He took Tara away with him. The devas asked for her return, Brihaspati demanded it — but Chandra would not relent. Finally through Brahma's intervention and the insistence of the divine assembly, Tara returned.

But when Tara came back, she was with child.

From that womb a child was born — wise, radiant, of incomparable beauty. The devas asked — whose son is this? Brihaspati's, or Chandra's? Tara remained silent for a long time. At Brahma's insistence she said — this is Chandra's son.

Thus Budh was born.

Chandra accepted him — but a question always lived in Budh's heart. He knew that his birth had happened in controversy. Among the devas, there was an ambiguity about his standing. There was no place for him in Brihaspati's home. He was in Chandra's home, but the glory that came to Brihaspati's twenty-six children was not his.

Budh undertook tapas.

He went into solitude. He worshipped Lord Vishnu — through his intellect, through his sadhana.

In the folk tradition, it is said that Vishnu appeared before him.

Vishnu said — *"Budh, the question of your origin troubles you — but hear me. In this creation, no one is great or small by birth. One is great who knows, who understands, who can make others understand. Your intellect is extraordinary — your mastery of speech is such as is rare even among the devas. I grant you that place in the planetary assembly which belongs to intellect and discernment. Wednesday — the day of the week that falls between Brihaspati (the knowledge of the guru) and Shukra (the grace of art) — that shall be your day. Trade, speech, writing, skill — the lordship of all these shall be yours."*

Budh Dev bowed before Vishnu.

From that day, Wednesday became the sacred day of Budh Dev — lord of intellect and speech.

---

There is another traditional story — from practical life.

There was a merchant named Somdatt. His trade had once flourished greatly — but over several years everything fell apart. Decisions began to go wrong, he would falter in conversations, opportunities would slip from his hands.

An elderly saint told him — *"Observe the Wednesday fast. Budh Dev is the deity of speech and discernment. Merchants, students, writers, lawyers — all those who live by words and intellect — Budh Dev is their refuge."*

Somdatt began the Wednesday fast. He fasted, worshipped Budh Dev, wore green garments. Gradually a clarity came to his thinking. He could say the right thing at the right time. His grip on his trade returned.

In the folk tradition of the Wednesday fast, it is said that one who worships Budh Dev with devotion receives mastery of speech, clarity of intellect, and success in business.

## Significance

Budh is one of the nine planets of the graha-mandala — and his position is distinctive. He is the planet nearest the Sun. In astrology, Budh is the significator of intellect, speech, calculation, trade, writing, and travel.

Brihaspati is knowledge — deep, philosophical, the knowledge of dharma. Budh is intellect — sharp, practical, skilled in communication. The two are different. Brihaspati is the deity of Thursday; Budh of Wednesday. The two are complementary to each other.

Wednesday is the day of the week when the note of intellect rings clearest. Any undertaking begun on this day that requires intellect, communication, or trade is considered auspicious.

Budh's colour is green. Green is the symbol of freshness, balance, and intellect. On this day, green garments, green bangles, a green bhog — all hold special significance.

## Vidhi

**Morning:**
1. Rise before sunrise and bathe.
2. Wear green garments.
3. First perform worship of Ganesha — Wednesday is also associated with Ganesha.
4. Before Budh Dev's murti or picture, offer green flowers, durva, incense, and a lamp.
5. Chant *Om Bum Budhaya Namah* — at least 108 times.
6. Recite the Budh stuti.

**Prasad:**
Green moong dal, green vegetables, or spinach as prasad. Use green cardamom in sweets.

**Special:**
Students may place their textbooks at the place of worship on this day and pray to Budh Dev for intellect and memory.

## Observance

**Fasting:** Abstaining from grains, one meal, or fruit only — according to one's capacity and tradition. In some traditions, salt is forbidden.

**Duration of the vrat:** Begin on any Wednesday. Traditions of twenty-one or twenty-seven Wednesdays exist. According to the sankalpa.

**Breaking the fast:** Thursday morning after sunrise, offering thanks to Budh Dev.

**Colour:** Green — in garments, flowers, bhog, throughout.

**What not to do:** Speaking falsehood, speaking ill of others, or engaging in verbal disputes is considered forbidden on this day — for it is the day of the deity of speech.

## Mantras

**Budh Stuti:**

> प्रियंगुकलिकाश्यामं रूपेणाप्रतिमं बुधम् ।
> सौम्यं सौम्यगुणोपेतं तं बुधं प्रणमाम्यहम् ॥

priyaṃgukalikaśyāmaṃ rūpeṇāpratimam budham | saumyaṃ saumyaguṇopetaṃ taṃ budhaṃ praṇamāmyaham ||

Meaning: Dark as the buds of the priyangu plant, of incomparable beauty, gentle and endowed with noble qualities — I bow to that Budh Dev.

---

**Budh Bija Mantra:**

> ॐ बुं बुधाय नमः

oṃ buṃ budhāya namaḥ

Meaning: Salutation to Budh Dev. This bija mantra awakens the energy of the Budh graha and aids in intellect, speech, and livelihood.
