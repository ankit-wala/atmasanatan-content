## Katha

**Birth — Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya:**

In the lineage of Bhrigu, Maharshi Jamadagni and his wife Renuka had four sons. But the fifth was unlike all the others.

On Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya, Renuka gave birth to a child. From the moment of his birth, a parashu — a divine axe — rested in his hand. This was no ordinary weapon; it was the very arms of Lord Vishnu himself, descended to earth with this avatar.

That child was named Rama. But because he bore the parashu, he came to be called Parashurama.

Jamadagni's ashrama stood on the banks of the Godavari — a centre of tapas, agnihotra, and Vedic recitation. The ashrama kept a divine Kamadhenu cow — **Surabhi** — who could grant whatever was asked of her. By her grace, Maharshi Jamadagni was able to feed and satisfy every guest who came to the ashrama.

---

**Kartavirya Arjuna — the adharma of Sahasrarjuna:**

In those days, the kingdom of Mahishmati was ruled by **Kartavirya Arjuna** of the Haihaya dynasty — also known as **Sahasrarjuna**, for by the grace of Lord Dattatreya he had been granted the boon of a thousand arms. He was a warrior of immense might — yet over time, arrogance had also taken root within him.

One day Sahasrarjuna arrived at Jamadagni's ashrama with his vast army during a journey.

The Maharshi received him with honour. Through the divine power of the Surabhi cow, he fed and satisfied that entire great army.

But Sahasrarjuna's gaze fell upon the divine cow — and greed awoke.

He said, "Maharshi, give me this cow. She will serve the purposes of the kingdom."

Jamadagni replied with calm composure, "O King, this is Kamadhenu — she is the heritage of the ashrama. It is not possible to part with her."

Sahasrarjuna did not pause even for a moment to consider. He ordered his soldiers to take Surabhi by force. He had the trees of the ashrama uprooted and its vines torn down.

At that time, Parashurama was not present in the ashrama.

---

**Parashurama's dharmic wrath:**

When Parashurama returned, he found the ashrama laid waste — his father's ground of austerity reduced to ruin, and Surabhi gone.

He learned everything from his father.

What arose within Parashurama was not ordinary anger. It was **dharma-krodha** — that blazing intensity which is kindled when a hermit's ashrama is desecrated and the rights of the defenceless are trampled underfoot. The Bhagavata calls it dharmamaya kopa — wrath that is entirely of dharma.

He took up his parashu and set out toward Mahishmati.

He met the army of Sahasrarjuna in battle. Sahasrarjuna himself entered the field — wielding weapons with all his thousand arms.

Parashurama fought as only a son of a brahmarshi and an avatar of Vishnu could. One by one, with his parashu, he cut off all thousand arms of Sahasrarjuna.

Sahasrarjuna was slain.

Surabhi returned to the ashrama.

---

**The slaying of his father and the twenty-one vows:**

What Parashurama had done was dharma — but the enemies of dharma do not stop even after one defeat.

The sons of Kartavirya plotted revenge. One day when Parashurama had gone from the ashrama, they entered and, striking the unarmed and meditation-absorbed Maharshi Jamadagni, put him to death.

Mother Renuka struck her own chest twenty-one times, one blow for each lament, calling out her son's name — her cries of grief piercing the very quarters of the sky.

Parashurama returned. His father's body. His mother's lamentation.

He sat at his father's feet.

Then he made his vow — counting the memory of his mother's twenty-one blows: *"I will sweep from this earth, twenty-one times over, those kshatriya kings who, having abandoned dharma, crush their subjects, who dishonour those in tapas, who in their arrogance have come to mistake adharma itself for the duty of a ruler."*

He kept that vow.

Twenty-one times he traversed the earth — and wherever there was an unrighteous, arrogant king who oppressed the people, he went there. This was not destruction born of vengeance — it was the correction of an imbalance that arises when the ruling class forgets its own limits.

In the tradition of the Bhagavata Purana it is said that the fruit of Parashurama's campaign was that once again the balance between brahmanical radiance and kshatriya dharma was established on earth, and he gave all the land to Maharshi Kashyapa as a gift.

## Significance

**The sixth avatar — avatar of dharmic fury:**

Among the ten avatars of Lord Vishnu, Parashurama is the sixth. He appeared in an age when the class of kings had become tyrannical and arrogant. The avatar of Parashurama establishes that to protect dharma, even a brahmin must sometimes take up arms — and that wrath which arises for dharma is not sin but tapas.

**Chiranjeevi:**

Parashurama is one of the seven Chiranjeevi — the immortals. He is still present in this creation, dwelling on Mount Mahendra. His sadhana is unending. In the next kalpa he will become the guru of the Kalki avatar.

**Guru of three great warriors:**

Parashurama imparted the science of arms to Bhishma, Dronacharya, and Karna. The martial mastery of three of the greatest warriors in the Mahabharata traces back to a single source — Parashurama. He is therefore the **param guru** — who taught his disciples not merely warfare but the sadhana of the Brahmastra.

**The confluence with Akshaya Tritiya:**

Parashurama Jayanti and Akshaya Tritiya fall on the same tithi — Vaishakha Shukla Tritiya. This tithi is self-auspicious — any auspicious work done on this day requires no separate muhurta. That Parashurama was born on this akshaya tithi is a symbol of his Chiranjeevi, ever-undimmed radiance.

## Vidhi

- **At dawn:** Rise early, bathe, and put on clean clothes.
- **Fasting:** Fast on this day — take only fruit; break the fast after sunset.
- **Puja:** Install an image or portrait of Lord Parashurama; use yellow cloth and yellow flowers; perform Panchamrita abhisheka with milk, curd, ghee, honey, and sugar; offer tulasi, sandalwood, incense, and a lamp; worship Parashurama as the sixth avatar form of Vishnu.
- **Parashu-puja:** If possible, place a small parashu (axe) as a symbol in the worship — it is his divine weapon. Shastra-puja holds special significance on this day; those who follow the path of the sadhaka, soldier, or kshatriya-dharma should worship their weapons.
- **Japa:** Chant **Om Jāmadagnyāya Namaḥ** 108 times; read or listen to the Parashurama-charitra from the Bhagavata Purana.
- **Giving:** Give to brahmins according to your means — anna-dana and shastra-dana are especially meritorious.
- **Special (Akshaya Tritiya):** This tithi is also Akshaya Tritiya — dana, japa, and puja performed on this day bring akshaya (imperishable) fruit; purchasing gold, beginning new undertakings, and pitru-tarpana are also among the special observances.
- **Evening puja:** Perform aarti again at dusk.

## Mantras

**Parashurama bija mantra:**

> ॐ जामदग्न्याय नमः

Oṃ Jāmadagnyāya Namaḥ

Meaning: "Salutation to Parashurama, son of Jamadagni." — The root mantra of Parashurama worship; chanting 108 times increases dharmic courage and strength of resolve.

> जमदग्नि-सुतं वीरं षष्ठावतारमच्युतम्॥

Namāmi Bhārgavaṃ Rāmaṃ paraśuṃ dharmarakṣaṇe |

Meaning: "I bow to Bhargava Rama — bearer of the parashu, guardian of dharma, son of Jamadagni, the valiant, the sixth avatar of the imperishable Vishnu."
