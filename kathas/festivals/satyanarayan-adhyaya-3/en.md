## Katha

In the city of Kashipur there lived a prosperous merchant named Sadhukara.

He was blessed with wealth and abundance — yet one longing remained in his heart. He had no child.

One day he observed the Satyanarayan Vrat. With a full heart he prayed — *"Lord, let this home ring with the laughter of a child."*

The Lord heard him.

In time, a beautiful daughter was born in his home. Sadhukara named her — **Kalavati**.

---

Kalavati grew up. In skill, in temperament, in beauty — she was exceptional in every way.

When she came of age to be married, Sadhukara arranged her wedding to a worthy young merchant.

The household filled with celebration. Yet the memory of the vow was left behind — Sadhukara had not properly completed the concluding rites of that old manauti.

---

After the wedding, Sadhukara and his son-in-law boarded a ship for trade. The journey was long — by sea, all the way to the kingdom of **Ratnapur**.

In Ratnapur, their trade went well. Wealth came. The heart was glad.

---

One day the son-in-law was strolling along the riverbank.

There, some townspeople were conducting a **Satyanarayan puja**. The words of the katha filled the air, lamps were burning, and prasad had been prepared.

The puja concluded. Those gathered received prasad.

The priest extended prasad toward the son-in-law as well.

The son-in-law drew his hands back.

The thought came to him — *"I am a merchant. This is a puja for ordinary people. What need do I have of this prasad?"*

He turned away without accepting it.

---

He returned to the ship — and it had stopped.

The waves were there, the wind was there — yet the ship did not move an inch. The sailors were baffled. Sadhukara grew anxious.

At that very moment, back in Kashipur, trouble descended upon Kalavati's home. Ornaments began to go missing, the household fell into disorder, and an unease settled over the mind.

---

That night, Sadhukara had a dream.

Lord Satyanarayan appeared before him. He spoke —

*"Sadhukara, you took the vow to worship Me — but you never completed the concluding rites. And your son-in-law, out of pride, refused My prasad. This is why the ship stands still and your home is in distress."*

Sadhukara's eyes opened.

Right there on the ship, he performed the Satyanarayan puja with full devotion. He completed the concluding rites of the vow. Prasad was prepared and distributed to all.

Prasad was sent to the son-in-law as well.

This time, he extended both hands — and received the prasad with reverence.

---

At that very moment, the ship stirred.

The waves rose. The sails filled. The ship began to move.

And in Kashipur — at that very moment — Kalavati's restlessness lifted. Peace returned to the home.

Sadhukara and his son-in-law arrived home safely. Kalavati welcomed them at the door.

Once again, a Satyanarayan puja was held in the home. Prasad was shared. This time, not a single person left without prasad.

---

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, Reva Khanda, it is said — devotees who listen to this third chapter with faith and attentiveness, who receive prasad with joined palms, are always held in the grace of Lord Satyanarayan. Their wealth, family, and journeys are protected.

## Significance

The katha of the third chapter is a lesson in **the danger of spiritual pride**.

The son-in-law had wealth, trade, and lineage. It was this very sense of self that said to him — *this puja is for ordinary people, not for me.* That thought itself is pride — and pride is the very door through which trouble enters.

"Prasad" means **grace** — it is the symbol of the Lord's joy and favour. When one accepts prasad, one is truly accepting the Lord's grace. And when one refuses prasad — one returns that grace.

This katha also teaches that the concluding rites of a vow are as important as the vow itself. Sadhukara had made the manauti — yet he delayed in completing it. To receive the Lord's grace and then acknowledge it with gratitude — this too is a form of devotion.

## Vidhi

- **When:** Any Purnima, Ekadashi, or auspicious occasion.
- **Sankalpa:** Bathe in the morning and take the resolve — "I am performing the vrat and puja of Lord Satyanarayan."
- **Fasting:** Keep a fast before the katha — fruit may be taken; break the fast only after the katha and prasad.
- **Setup:** Place an image or idol of Lord Satyanarayan (Vishnu) on a consecrated seat; perform panchamrit abhishek (milk, yoghurt, ghee, honey, sugar); offer tulsi leaves, flowers, yellow cloth, and a lamp.
- **Katha shravan:** Recite or have recited all five chapters — family and those gathered should listen together in a single sitting.
- **Arati:** Perform the arati of the Lord after the katha.
- **Prasad:** Prepare panjiri or sheera (wheat flour, ghee, sugar, banana); offer prasad to every person present without exception — to skip, refuse, or receive prasad carelessly is inauspicious (the direct teaching of this third chapter).
- **Completing a manauti:** If a manauti was made to Lord Satyanarayan, perform the vrat and puja in good time once the boon is received — delay is not appropriate.
- **Paran:** Break the fast after the katha and prasad.

## Mantras

**Satyanarayan Mula Mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते सत्यनारायणाय

oṃ namo bhagavate satyanārāyaṇāya

Meaning: O Lord Satyanarayan, I bow to You.

**Brahma-Satya Shruti Vachan:**

> सत्यं ज्ञानमनन्तं ब्रह्म।

satyaṃ jñānam anantaṃ brahma

Meaning: Truth, knowledge, and the infinite — this is Brahman. (Taittiriya Upanishad 2.1.1)
