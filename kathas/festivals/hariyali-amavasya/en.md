## Katha

The rainy season was in its full glory. The forest of Naimiṣāraṇya was blanketed in green — the leaves gleamed, the earth was moist, and the air carried the sweet fragrance of wet soil.

Ṛṣi Śaunaka sat in the midst of his disciples. A question arose in his mind.

He came before Brahmā and said — "Lord, among all months Śrāvaṇa is the most beloved of Śiva. Among all tithis, amāvasyā is regarded as the supreme tithi for the ancestors. When these two come together — the month of Śrāvaṇa and the tithi of amāvasyā — what is the significance of that conjunction?"

Brahmā smiled.

He said — "Śaunaka, you have asked well. This meeting of Śrāvaṇa and amāvasyā is no ordinary coincidence — it is a trivenī-saṅgama of three streams of dharma. Devotion to Śiva, gratitude toward the ancestors, and care for the earth — this is the true nature of Hariyali Amavasya."

---

Brahmā continued — "See how the earth transforms the moment Śrāvaṇa arrives. The same ground that had cracked and dried in Vaiśākha and Jyeṣṭha now wraps itself in a mantle of green. Rice is sown in the rains, rivers run full, new leaves emerge on the trees. This greenness — this verdure — is Śiva's blessing made visible upon the earth.

Śiva is also called Vanaspatipati — the lord of all plants. He dwells in every plant, in every tree. When you plant a pīpal, a neem, a vaṭa — you are building a temple to Śiva that will stand for a hundred years. The birds that nest in that tree, the shade it gives, the air it purifies — all of this is offered to Śiva."

Śaunaka said — "And what of the ancestors? Amāvasyā belongs to them."

Brahmā replied — "The ancestors await Śrāvaṇa's amāvasyā with special longing. This is the season of rain — their thirst grows more intense in this season. When descendants offer them water, black sesame, and flowers on this day — that offering reaches seven generations. The ancestors are satisfied and they bless their lineage.

And in tree-planting on this day too there is a fulfilment of the debt to the ancestors. A pīpal tree that gives shade for a hundred years, at whose roots thousands of creatures dwell — planting such a tree bestows upon the ancestors the same merit as performing śrāddha at a tīrtha."

---

Śaunaka asked again — "Then why is this tithi named 'Hariyali'?"

Brahmā said — "Because this tithi is a celebration of the earth's greenness. In Śrāvaṇa there is green in every direction — and that greenness is a sign of Śiva's presence. By planting sacred trees on this day, by preserving that greenness of the earth — a person worships Śiva, satisfies the ancestors, and repays the debt to the earth. These three streams together make 'Hariyali Amavasya.'"

In the tradition of the Padma Purāṇa, it is said that whoever plants one sacred tree on this day, offers tarpaṇa to the ancestors, and remembers Śiva — attains merit in all three worlds. Their ancestors are satisfied, Śiva's grace abides in their home, and the tree they planted continues to perform dharma on their behalf even after their own lifetime.

## Significance

Hariyali Amavasya is a confluence of three great traditions.

**Śrāvaṇa — Śiva's Month:** The Purāṇas declare Śrāvaṇa to be the month most dear to Śiva. In this month, Śiva-devotees observe special fasts, perform jalābhiṣeka, and visit temples. The Mondays of Śrāvaṇa are especially auspicious — and the amāvasyā of this month too is filled with that same sanctity. Śiva is called Vanaspatipati — he dwells in plants and trees. That is why tree-planting on this day is regarded as the supreme form of Śiva-bhakti.

**Amāvasyā — The Day of the Ancestors:** Every amāvasyā is the tithi of the ancestors. Offering tarpaṇa on this day satisfies them and they bless the lineage. When this amāvasyā falls in Śrāvaṇa, its merit is further multiplied — for in Śrāvaṇa the ancestors watch especially for their descendants.

**Hariyali — Care for the Earth:** The word 'hariyali' (greenness) is the heart of this festival. In the rainy season the earth turns green — crops grow, forests bloom, water is stored. On this day, planting sacred and useful trees such as pīpal, neem, vaṭa, āmlā, and tulasī is considered a religious duty. A tree that gives shade for fifty years is a yajña performed every single day.

This festival reminds humanity that dharma is not confined to the temple — maintaining the earth's greenness, remembering one's ancestors, and honouring the natural cycle of life — all of these together constitute true dharma.

## Vidhi

On Hariyali Amavasya, three rites are performed — tree-planting, pitṛ-tarpaṇa, and Śiva-pūjana:

**Tree-Planting Procedure:**
1. Bathe in the morning and put on clean clothes.
2. Choose a sacred tree — pīpal, neem, vaṭa (banyan), tulasī, āmlā, or aśoka. These trees are considered worthy of veneration in the scriptures.
3. Perform bhūmi-pūjana — bow to Dharaṇī Mātā, sprinkle a little water on the spot where the tree will be planted, and say "Oṃ Bhūmyai Namaḥ."
4. Plant the tree and water its roots.
5. Take the saṅkalpa — "This tree is offered to Lord Śiva and for the merit of my ancestors."

**Pitṛ-Tarpaṇa Procedure:**
1. Take water, black sesame seeds, and white flowers for the tarpaṇa.
2. Sit facing the south direction.
3. With both hands, pour water mixed with sesame seeds three times.
4. Holding the thought — "May my ancestors receive this tarpaṇa, may they be satisfied, may they be liberated."
5. At the end, offer a brāhmaṇa whatever dākṣiṇā and food you are able to give.

**Śiva-Pūjana Procedure:**
1. Perform abhiṣeka of the Śivalinga with water and milk.
2. Offer belpatra, bhāṅg, dhatūra, and white flowers.
3. Chant "Oṃ Namaḥ Śivāya" — 108 times in Śrāvaṇa.
4. Recite a Śiva-stotra or the Mahāmṛtyuñjaya Mantra.
5. Offer pañcāmṛta and fruit as prasāda.

## Observance

**Tree-Planting:** The most important rite of this day. Plant at least one sacred tree — pīpal, neem, vaṭa, āmlā, or tulasī. If there is no space in your garden, plant one in a temple courtyard or a public place. The scriptures call tree-donation a mahādāna.

**Pitṛ-Tarpaṇa:** In the morning, facing south, offer tarpaṇa to the ancestors with black sesame and water. Families who cannot wait for the Pitṛ-pakṣa may perform their ancestral rites on this amāvasyā — according to the Padma Purāṇa, tarpaṇa on Śrāvaṇa amāvasyā is especially fruitful.

**Śiva-Pūjana:** On this day in Śrāvaṇa month, be sure to perform jalābhiṣeka at a Śiva temple. If there is no Śiva temple nearby, perform abhiṣeka at home on a Śivalinga with water and milk.

**Fasting:** Fasting on Hariyali Amavasya is optional. Some devotees observe a nirjalā or fruit-only fast. Abstaining from meat, alcohol, and tāmasika food on this day is especially appropriate.

## Mantras

**Pitṛ-Tarpaṇa Verse**

> ॐ पितृभ्यः स्वधायिभ्यः स्वधा नमः ।
> पितामहेभ्यः स्वधायिभ्यः स्वधा नमः ।
> प्रपितामहेभ्यः स्वधायिभ्यः स्वधा नमः ॥

Om pitṛbhyaḥ svadhāyibhyaḥ svadhā namaḥ | pitāmahebhyaḥ svadhāyibhyaḥ svadhā namaḥ | prapitāmahebhyaḥ svadhāyibhyaḥ svadhā namaḥ ||

Meaning: To our fathers, to their fathers, and to their fathers' fathers — we offer svadhā and salutation. May this tarpaṇa reach three generations and may they attain satisfaction.

> द्वे द्वे तथा दाडिमरम्भलम्बे एकं च निम्बं वपते नरो यः ॥
> न यास्यते नरकं वासुदेवप्रियः सः ॥

Aśvattham ekaṃ picumardamekaṃ nyagrodham ekaṃ daśa puṣpajātīḥ | dve dve tathā dāḍima-rambha-lambe ekaṃ ca nimbaṃ vapate naro yaḥ || na yāsyate narakaṃ vāsudeva-priyaḥ saḥ ||

Meaning: The person who plants one pīpal, one pārijāta, one vaṭa, ten flowering trees, two each of pomegranate and banana, and one neem — that person does not go to hell and becomes dear to Vāsudeva.
