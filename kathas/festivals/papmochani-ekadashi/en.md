## Katha

Dharmaraja Yudhishthira asked Lord Shri Krishna — "Madhava, what is the name of the Ekadashi that falls in the Krishna paksha of Chaitra month? Tell me of its greatness."

Lord Shri Krishna replied — "O King, this Ekadashi is called **Papmochani** — papa + mochani. It is the Ekadashi that liberates even the gravest of sins. Listen, I shall tell you an ancient katha."

---

In Indra's Chaitratha vana — that wondrous forest, the most beautiful of heaven's gardens — Medhavi, the son of the great sage Chyavana, had taken his seat for tapas. He had been practising austerities for many years. His tapas was so fierce that its radiance spread in all directions. Through the accumulated merit of a brahmin and the power of tapas, he astonished even the gods.

There, in that same forest, the apsara Manjughosha arrived, sent by Kamadeva. The notes of her veena dissolved into the air; at her dance, the leaves themselves began to sway. She came close to Medhavi and began to sing and play — soft, sweet, enchanting.

Medhavi's eyes lifted. And what he saw, he could not stop seeing.

---

One who has remained in tapas for years can drown in a single moment of attachment. That is what happened to Medhavi.

He stayed in that forest in Manjughosha's company. Days passed, months passed, years passed. For Manjughosha the flow of time was different — what were a few years for her became fifty-seven years of Medhavi's life. The merit of years of accumulated tapas burned away in that enchantment.

One day Manjughosha said — "My lord, give me leave to go now."

Those words fell on Medhavi's mind like a splash of cold water. He awoke.

And in awakening, he saw everything — the years that had passed, the merit that had turned to ash, the tapas that had dissolved to dust.

Anger rose — and its target was Manjughosha.

Medhavi said — "You have destroyed my sadhana. You have stolen my years. Therefore you shall wander this world as a **pishachi** — in suffering and dishonour."

Tears filled Manjughosha's eyes. She fell at his feet — "My lord, I erred. I did this at Kamadeva's command; there was no malice in my heart toward you. Have mercy. Show me the path out of this curse."

Medhavi's anger softened a little. Compassion stirred within him.

He said — "Chaitra Krishna Ekadashi — **Papmochani Ekadashi**. It liberates even the greatest of sinners. Observe this Ekadashi vrat. Through it alone will your curse be broken."

---

Manjughosha departed. And Medhavi made his way to the ashram of his father, the great sage Chyavana.

He told him everything.

Chyavana listened. He drew a long breath. Then he said — "Son, you did well to speak the truth. But know this — you too have sinned. A brahmin's own dharma — knowledge, tapas, brahmacharya — to lose it in delusion is itself a sin. Your lost merit will return to you only when you yourself observe this Ekadashi vrat."

Medhavi accepted his father's words and took them to heart.

Both — Manjughosha and Medhavi — observed the Papmochani Ekadashi vrat. Fasting on Ekadashi, keeping vigil through the night, holding Lord Vishnu in their hearts.

And what was meant to happen, happened.

Manjughosha's pishachi form broke. She manifested again in her divine aspect and set out for the world of the gods.

The merit that Medhavi had built through decades of tapas returned to him through the power of the vrat. His sadhana was lit again.

The tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana holds that the fruit of this Ekadashi vrat is so vast that merely listening to this katha bestows merit equal to a thousand Vajapeya yagnas. One who observes this Ekadashi with proper rites has all the sins of their life — whether committed knowingly or unknowingly — released and dissolved.

## Significance

"Papmochani" — this name alone is the proclamation of this Ekadashi's power. Papa + mochani — the one who releases sins. Chaitra Krishna Ekadashi holds a special place in the sequence of Ekadashis through the year — it falls just before Chaitra Shukla, when the Hindu new year rises. The dissolution of sins before the beginning of the new year is the soul of this Ekadashi.

The katha of Medhavi carries a deep truth — sin does not arise only from evil intent. Attachment, enchantment, turning away from dharma — these too are sin. And one who punishes another for sin becomes himself a participant in that event. This is precisely why the sage Chyavana's words are so important — "You too have sinned, son." No one is without fault; yet Papmochani Ekadashi is for all.

The presiding deity of this Ekadashi is Lord Vishnu — the boundless ocean of compassion. This Ekadashi is especially dear to him because he himself is Patitapavana — the one who purifies the fallen. For one who has been broken by their own sins, who thinks their redemption is no longer possible — Papmochani Ekadashi carries this message: the door of the Lord is always open.

## Vidhi

1. On the evening of Dashami, eat a sattvic meal and keep the mind calm and pure.
2. On the morning of Ekadashi, bathe and take the vrat sankalpa — "For the grace of Lord Vishnu and for the release of my sins, I observe this vrat."
3. Establish an image or portrait of Lord Vishnu. Perform panchamrita abhisheka — with milk, curd, ghee, honey, and sugar.
4. Offer tulsi, yellow flowers, akshata, incense, and a lamp.
5. Read or listen to the Papmochani Ekadashi katha.
6. Chant the Vishnu Sahasranama or the mantra "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya."
7. **Keeping vigil through the night is especially recommended** — spend the night in bhajan, kirtan, and the name of Vishnu.
8. On Dwadashi, after sunrise, break the fast at the parana time.

## Observance

**Upavasa:** On Ekadashi, observe a complete nirjala fast or a phalahar fast. Those unable to fast completely may sustain themselves on fruit, milk, and water.

**Prohibited:** Rice, urad dal, and tamasic food are forbidden on Ekadashi. Keep the mind free from anger, untruth, and sensory indulgence as well.

**Parana:** On Dwadashi morning, at the prescribed parana muhurta, break the fast. Before parana, offer bhog to the Lord and receive tulsi-water.

**Night vigil:** The night vigil holds special importance for this Ekadashi — in the katha, both Medhavi and Manjughosha kept vigil through the night. Spend the night in bhajan, remembrance of the Name, or reading of the Ekadashi mahatmya.

## Mantras

**Vasudeva Dvadasakshari Mantra — the root mantra of Papmochani Ekadashi:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya

Meaning: O Lord Vasudeva, I bow to you. — This twelve-syllable mantra is the supreme mantra for the worship of Vishnu on all Ekadashis.

---

**Papa-mochana shloka — in praise of Patitapavana Vishnu:**

> त्वमेव सर्वपापानां क्षयकर्ता जनार्दन।
> तस्माद् भक्तिप्रणम्यास्मि प्रसीद परमेश्वर॥

tvameva sarvapāpānāṃ kṣayakartā janārdana
tasmād bhaktipraṇamyāsmi prasīda parameśvara

Meaning: O Janardhana, you alone are the destroyer of all sins. Therefore I bow to you in devotion — O Parameshvara, be gracious.
