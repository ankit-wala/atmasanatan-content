## Katha

Long ago, in the Satya Yuga, there lived an extraordinarily powerful demon — **Mura**. He had spread terror across all three worlds. The gods, tormented by him, had abandoned heaven. Indra's throne stood empty. All the gods together sought refuge at the feet of Lord Vishnu.

Lord Vishnu said — "I myself will confront the demon Mura."

And so the battle began.

---

A fierce war broke out between Lord Vishnu and Mura. The two fought for a thousand years. Lord Vishnu unleashed every weapon and missile at his command, but Mura's maya was impenetrable — he revived again and again.

At last Lord Vishnu thought — "In this war I must rest for a while."

He went to a divine cave called **Hemavati**, situated at Badarikashrama. It was secluded there, and still. The Lord entered that cave and lay down in yogic slumber.

---

Mura came following close behind.

He had learned that Vishnu was there. He thought — "He is asleep right now. This is the moment. I will slay him at once."

He entered the cave. Lord Vishnu lay motionless, his eyes closed.

Mura raised his weapon.

At that very instant — from the sleeping body of Lord Vishnu, from the union of his senses and his yogic power, a supremely radiant divine woman appeared. Her form blazed like a thousand suns. Weapons gleamed in her hands. Fire burned in her eyes.

She was Ekadashi Devi.

---

Mura saw her. For a moment even he was startled.

Then he spoke — "Who are you? How did you dare to stand before me?"

Ekadashi Devi said — "I am born of this Lord's power. And I have come to put an end to you."

Battle was joined.

Ekadashi Devi displayed such brilliance and valor as no one had witnessed before. The radiance that blazed from her weapon shattered Mura's maya apart. For all his strength, Mura could not hold his ground.

And at last — Mura the demon was slain at the hands of Ekadashi Devi.

---

Lord Vishnu's yogic slumber broke. He looked — Mura lay fallen on the ground, and before him stood a divine, radiant woman.

The Lord was filled with joy. He asked — "Devi, who are you? Was it you who did this?"

Ekadashi Devi said humbly — "Lord, I was born from your own body. I manifested in order to protect you, while you slept, from the demon."

The Lord said — "Devi, you have made the impossible possible. I am exceedingly pleased with you. Ask — ask of me whatever boon you wish."

Ekadashi Devi said — "Lord, if you are pleased, then grant me one boon. May whoever fasts on this tithi — on the Ekadashi of the dark fortnight of Margashirsha — and worships you with devotion, have all their sins destroyed and attain your divine abode."

The Lord said — "So be it."

Then he gave that goddess her name — **Ekadashi** — she who was born on the Ekadashi tithi.

---

In the tradition of the Padma Purana it is said that this Margashirsha Krishna Ekadashi is the day of origin of all Ekadashis. From this day the twenty-four Ekadashis of the year received their power and glory. Whoever hears or reads the katha of Utpanna Ekadashi receives the fruit of all Ekadashis combined — and, freed from all sins, attains Vishnudhama.

## Significance

**Utpanna** — meaning she who was born. This is the day of Ekadashi Devi's manifestation.

This Ekadashi is the **mother** of all Ekadashis. The twenty-four Ekadashis of the year — whether Devshayani, Nirjala, or any other — the root of their power lies in this very Ekadashi. Therefore one who observes the fast of Utpanna Ekadashi receives the combined fruit of all Ekadashis.

This Ekadashi is considered especially potent for destroying **accumulated karma**. Sins gathered over many births, karma that does not dissolve by ordinary means — for these, the fast of this Ekadashi is exceedingly fruitful.

The katha of Ekadashi Devi also reveals that the Lord's power already dwells within him — when the need arose, it manifested of its own accord. The devotee who observes the Ekadashi fast worships this very divine power.

## Vidhi

1. **Evening of Dashami:** Take one meal. Urad dal and brinjal (eggplant) are prohibited.
2. **Morning of Ekadashi:** Bathe and take the vow of the fast. "I observe the fast of Utpanna Ekadashi for the pleasure of Lord Vishnu."
3. Establish an image or picture of Lord Vishnu.
4. Worship with tulasi, yellow flowers, incense, a lamp, and panchamrita.
5. Recite the **Vishnu Sahasranama**.
6. Fast — either completely without water or on fruit alone.
7. **Overnight vigil** — chanting the name of Lord Vishnu, bhajans, and kirtan.
8. **Break the fast on Dwadashi after sunrise** — open the fast with simple, sattvic food.

## Observance

**Fasting:** Without water or on fruit alone. Urad dal is prohibited from Dashami onward.

**Overnight vigil:** Night-long waking holds special importance on this Ekadashi — spend the night chanting "Hari Hari" in kirtan and repeating Vishnu's name.

**Parana:** Break the fast on Dwadashi morning, after sunrise and before the Dwadashi tithi ends.

## Mantras

**The root mantra of Utpanna Ekadashi:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya

Meaning: O Lord Vasudeva, I bow to you.

---

**For the Ekadashi vigil:**

> एकादश्यां निराहारो यो जागर्ति जनार्दनम्।

ekādaśyāṃ nirāhāro yo jāgarti janārdanam

Meaning: One who fasts and keeps vigil for Janardana (Vishnu) on Ekadashi — attains liberation.
