## Katha

Heaven stood empty.

King Bali, the asura lord, had seized all three worlds through his austerities and might. The gods were expelled from heaven. Indra wandered homeless. The mother of the gods, Aditi, was heartbroken.

Maharishi Kashyapa told her a way — "Devi, Lord Vishnu alone is the refuge. Observe the Payovrata for twelve days — surviving only on cow's milk, meditating on Him with complete devotion."

Aditi observed that vrat.

Twelve days — fasting, meditation, tears, prayer.

On the twelfth day — Lord Vishnu appeared before her.

He said — "Devi, I have seen your devotion. Ask what you wish."

Aditi said — "Lord, restore my sons to their rightful place. Return heaven to the gods."

The Lord said — "I shall be born as your son from your own womb. What the gods could not do — I shall do it as a brahmin boy."

---

**Bhadrapada Shukla Dwadashi — under the Shravana Nakshatra.**

From Aditi's womb, as the son of Maharishi Kashyapa — Lord Vishnu took the **Vamana** form.

The form of a small brahmin boy. Dark eyes, luminous face, with an umbrella and a kamandal in hand. Small in stature — but radiant with such light that Brahma, great sages, and gandharvas all came to witness the birth of this child. Brahma performed the naming ceremony, the Saptarishis sang praises, and Maharishi Vishvamitra performed the sacred thread ceremony.

This was the birth celebration of Vamana.

---

What followed — the three strides, Trivikrama, Bali in Sutala — that katha is told in full in the Parivartini Ekadashi chapter of this book.

But here, the essence of Vamana Jayanti is simply this —

**Today is the day a mother's devotion was answered.**

The pain in Aditi's eyes — for her sons, for the gods — was heard by the Lord. And He said: "I shall come Myself."

In the tradition of the Bhagavata Purana, it is said that the devotee who observes the Vamana Jayanti vrat, hears the katha of Vamana's birth, and meditates on Vamana-Trivikrama receives the special grace of Lord Vishnu.

## Significance

**Vamana Jayanti and Parivartini Ekadashi:**
Parivartini Ekadashi (Bhadrapada Shukla Ekadashi) is the day when the Vamana-Bali katha is heard. Vamana Jayanti falls exactly one day later — on Dwadashi. The fast kept on Parivartini Ekadashi is completed with the paran on Vamana Jayanti. These two days together form one complete celebration.

**Onam — Kerala's tradition:**
In Kerala and South India, this day is celebrated as the **Onam** festival. In the regional tradition of South India, it is believed that King Bali comes from Sutala on this day to see his people — and Onam is the festival welcoming him. This tradition expresses a profound reverence and love for Bali.

**Vamana — the glory of smallness:**
Vamana's form was small — but within that small form, sovereignty over the entire universe was hidden. This reminds us that the power of the divine is not recognized from outward appearance, but from within.

**The fruit of devotion:**
Aditi's Payovrata is proof of this truth: when a mother meditates on the divine with complete surrender — for the sake of her children — the divine Himself answers.

## Vidhi

1. At dawn, bathe and take the vrat sankalpa.
2. Install an image or depiction of Lord **Vamana**.
3. Panchamrit abhisheka — milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar.
4. Offer yellow clothes, yellow flowers, tulasi, and a lamp.
5. Read or hear the Vamana birth katha (Bhagavata Purana, Skandha 8).
6. Observe the fast — the Parivartini Ekadashi fast is completed by paran on this Dwadashi.
7. Night kirtan — chanting of Lord Vamana's names.
8. Paran on Dwadashi morning at the appropriate time.

**2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Bhadrapada Shukla Dwadashi — ~September 23, 2026).

## Observance

**Paran:** Those who fasted on Parivartini Ekadashi complete the fast with paran on this Dwadashi morning at the correct time.

**Janmotsava:** Vamana Jayanti is a birth celebration — katha recitation, bhajan-kirtan, Bhagavata Purana reading.

**Onam in Kerala:** In Kerala, the ten-day Onam Mahotsava reaches its climax on this day.

## Mantras

**Vamana Bhagavan mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वामनाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāmanāya

Meaning: "Salutation to Lord Vamana." — The root mantra of the Vamana avatar.

**Trivikrama stuti:**

> विक्रमस्य त्रयः पादाः त्रयो लोकाः सनातनाः ।
> त्रिविक्रमं नमस्यामि त्रिभुवनेश्वरं हरिम् ॥

Vikramasya trayaḥ pādāḥ trayo lokāḥ sanātanāḥ |
Trivikramaṃ namasyāmi tribhuvaneśvaraṃ harim ||

Meaning: "He whose three strides encompass the three eternal worlds — to that Trivikrama Hari, Lord of the three realms, I offer my salutation."
