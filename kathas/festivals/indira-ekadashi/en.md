## Katha

King Yudhishthira asked Lord Sri Krishna — "Madhava, what is the name of the Ashwin Krishna Ekadashi? What is its special significance?"

The Lord said — "O King, this Ekadashi is called **Indira Ekadashi**. It falls within Pitru Paksha — and so its special virtue is that its merit benefits not only the observer but also their ancestors. Listen — here is its story."

---

In ancient times there lived a righteous king named **Indrasena**. He nurtured his subjects like a father. His own father too was a devout and revered man.

One day, the great sage Narada came flying through the sky and arrived at King Indrasena's palace.

The king received him with respect, offering water and fruit.

Narada said — "O King, I am coming from Yamaloka. There I saw your father. He is in the realm of Yama — because of one unknown sin."

The words struck Indrasena like a thunderbolt. He said — "Sage, my father was a righteous man. What remedy is there?"

Narada said — "Do not grieve, O King. Observe the Ashwin Krishna Ekadashi vrat. This Indira Ekadashi has the great power to liberate ancestors from Yamaloka."

---

That very year, King Indrasena observed the Ashwin Krishna Ekadashi with complete devotion.

On Dashami — a single sattvic meal. On Ekadashi — full fast. Meditation and kirtana of Lord Vishnu, night-long vigil. On Dwadashi — parana.

By the merit of this vrat, his father was freed from Yamaloka and departed toward Vishnuloka — Vaikuntha.

As he departed, he appeared before Indrasena — in divine clothes, a radiant face.

He said — "Son, by the power of this Ekadashi you have freed me from the torment of Yama. Your sense of filial duty is exemplary."

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana it is said that one who observes Indira Ekadashi is freed from the sins of seven births, and their ancestors are released from the bonds of Yamaloka.

## Significance

**Ekadashi within Pitru Paksha:**
Indira Ekadashi occupies a special place because it falls in the middle of Pitru Paksha. Other Ekadashis are primarily for personal liberation — Indira Ekadashi is also for ancestors who may be bound in their states.

**Named after Indrasena:**
This Ekadashi is named after King Indrasena — whose devotion and filial duty established it as the "Pitru-mokshadayini" (liberator of ancestors) Ekadashi.

**Repaying pitru-rin:**
Alongside shraddha and tarpan, observing this Ekadashi is another path to repaying one's ancestral debt. Donating in the name of one's ancestors on this day is considered especially meritorious.

## Vidhi

- **On Dashami:** take one sattvic meal; avoid tamasic food.
- **Fasting:** nirjala (waterless) or fruit-only fast observed from Dashami through Dwadashi parana.
- **At dawn on Ekadashi:** bathe and take the vrat sankalpa; install Lord Vishnu's image or shaligram.
- **Puja:** panchamrit abhisheka, tulsi, yellow flowers, lamp; hear the Indira Ekadashi katha.
- **Donation for ancestors:** feed brahmins and donate sesame-lamp in the name of one's ancestors — especially meritorious on this day.
- **Night vigil:** remain awake performing hari-kirtana through the night.
- **Breaking the fast:** parana on Dwadashi morning, before Rahu Kaal.
- **2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang (Ashwin Krishna Ekadashi — ~October 6, 2026)
