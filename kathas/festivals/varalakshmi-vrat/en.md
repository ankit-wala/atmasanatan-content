## Katha

In the city of Kundinyapura in the kingdom of Vidarbha, there lived a devoted woman named Charumati.

Her life was simple. Rising early, morning worship, the work of the home, service to her husband. No extraordinary event, no hunger for fame. Just a quiet, steady life — through which ran a fine thread of devotion, invisible but real.

And Goddess Lakshmi knew her.

The month of Shravan came. It was a Thursday, the night before the Friday preceding the full moon. Charumati was sleeping deeply.

Then a divine light descended into her dream.

Goddess Lakshmi — conch, lotus, golden garments, seated on a lotus — appeared before her. On her face was that tenderness a mother carries when she looks at her child.

She said: *"Charumati, your devotion has called me here. Tomorrow is Friday — that Friday in Shravan which precedes the full moon, the day of the Varalakshmi Vrat. I am 'Varalakshmi' — the giver of boons. Observe this vrat. Call your neighbors too. I shall grant boons to all of you."*

Charumati's eyes opened.

It was a dream — but so vivid, so alive, so true.

She rose immediately and bathed. She decorated a mandapa — mango leaves, coconut, flowers, lamps. She filled a kalash with water and placed a coconut on top — this is Varalakshmi's very form: the goddess installed in the sacred pot.

She called her neighborhood women. Together they performed the shodashopachara puja — sixteen offerings, naivedyam, aarti.

And Maa Lakshmi's grace poured down.

What was in the heart — it was given. What was lacking in the home — it was filled. She who longed for a child — received a child. She who needed health — received well-being.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana it is said: *"The woman who observes this vrat with proper rites receives the merit of worshipping all eight Ashta Lakshmis."*

## Significance

Varalakshmi Vrat is observed primarily in Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana — but its spirit is universal.

"Vara" means boon, wish, desire. Varalakshmi is the goddess who fulfills the deepest wish of the heart.

This vrat carries a beautiful teaching — the goddess comes seeking you. Charumati did not have to search or ask — Maa herself walked over and said: *"I am here. Do this."*

This is Lakshmi's nature — she seeks out her devotees herself.

## Vidhi

This vrat is observed on the Friday before the full moon of Shravan month.

1. Rise early and bathe; wear new clothes.
2. Fill a kalash with water, place a coconut on top with mango leaves — this is the ghata-svarupa (pot-form) of Varalakshmi.
3. Perform the shodashopachara puja with kumkum, turmeric, flowers, fruits, and naivedyam.
4. In the South Indian regional tradition, tie a nine-thread sacred cord (thoranam/kankana) on the right wrist.
5. Listen to or read the Varalakshmi Vrat katha.
6. Distribute prasad among family and neighbors.

## Observance

**Fast:** Full day's fast — one sattvic meal or fruits allowed.

**Breaking the fast:** After the evening puja, or the following morning.

**Communal worship:** This vrat has a tradition of gathering neighborhood women for the puja together — just as Charumati did.

**2026 date:** Confirm on DrikPanchang — the Friday before Shravan Purnima (~August 21, 2026).

## Mantras

**Lakshmi Bija Mantra:**

> ॐ श्रीं ह्रीं श्रीं कमले कमलालये प्रसीद प्रसीद ।
> ॐ श्रीं ह्रीं श्रीं महालक्ष्म्यै नमः ॥

*Oṃ Śrīṃ Hrīṃ Śrīṃ Kamale Kamalālaye Prasīda Prasīda |
Oṃ Śrīṃ Hrīṃ Śrīṃ Mahālakṣmyai Namaḥ ||*

Meaning: "O Lotus-dweller Lakshmi, be pleased, be pleased. Salutations to Mahalakshmi."

---

**Lakshmi Gayatri — for japa during worship:**

> ॐ महालक्ष्म्यै च विद्महे विष्णुपत्न्यै च धीमहि ।
> तन्नो लक्ष्मीः प्रचोदयात् ॥

*Oṃ Mahālakṣmyai ca vidmahe Viṣṇu-patnyai ca dhīmahi |
Tanno Lakṣmīḥ pracodayāt ||*

Meaning: "We meditate on Mahalakshmi, we know Vishnu's consort — may that Lakshmi guide and inspire us."
