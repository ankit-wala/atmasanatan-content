## Katha

High in the Himalayas, in those peaks where ordinary men cannot go — where clouds brush the mountaintops and healing herbs flower in the cracks of stone — lay the city of Alaka, the abode of Kubera, the Lord of Wealth. It was a city of yakshas and gandharvas, brilliant with Kubera's inexhaustible treasury.

In Kubera's court lived a yaksha named Hemamali. His work was simple but sacred: every morning, he would go out and gather fresh flowers so that Kubera could worship Lord Shiva. A daily act of service — small, but pure.

Hemamali loved his wife deeply. One day, he sat beside her and lost track of time. He forgot to go. The flowers never came. Kubera's worship of Shiva went undone.

When Kubera learned of it, his anger blazed up. He summoned Hemamali before him and pronounced the curse: *"You who disrupted the worship of the gods — be separated from your wife. May eighteen kinds of leprosy break out upon your body. Leave Alaka and carry this sin wandering through the world."*

In that moment, Hemamali's radiant body was covered with disease. He was cast out from Alaka. Separated from his wife, filled with sores and suffering, he wandered through forests and through mountains.

After years of this misery, he reached the high Himalayas — and came upon the ashrama of the great sage Markandeya. Markandeya was one whom time itself could not touch, for Shiva's grace had placed him beyond death. He saw this sick, broken yaksha before him and asked with compassion: *"Who are you? How has this come upon you?"*

Hemamali bowed his head and told the whole story. The sage listened and then said: *"Your mistake was made in ignorance — and there is a way through it. The Ekadashi of the dark half of Ashadha is approaching — it is called Yogini Ekadashi. Observe this fast with full devotion and take the shelter of Lord Vishnu. As the sun destroys darkness, this vow burns away sin."*

Hemamali did exactly that — with the kind of surrender that a drowning man gives to a rope. On the day of Ashadha Krishna Ekadashi, he fasted. Through the night, he kept vigil in prayer. At dawn, he offered his prayers.

When he opened his eyes, every one of the eighteen kinds of leprosy was gone. He was as he had been in Alaka — radiant, whole, at peace. His wife was returned to him. His home was restored.

The Padma Purana says of this vow: the merit of observing this one Ekadashi equals the merit of feeding eighty-eight thousand Brahmins. Whoever hears or reads this story with a devoted heart is freed from all accumulated sin.

## Significance

Yogini Ekadashi is the Ekadashi of Ashadha Krishna paksha — the last Ekadashi before Devshayani, before Chaturmas begins.

Its name, "Yogini," points to a power that unties the knots inside. This is the Ekadashi of atonement. Hemamali's story does not say that those who commit great sins can escape lightly — it says that the burden of what we have carried unknowingly, what accumulated without our full awareness, can be set down at the Lord's feet.

Coming two weeks before Devshayani, this Ekadashi is a kind of preparation — an interior cleaning before the four months of Chaturmas begin. The mind that lays down its old burden here is the mind ready for genuine devotion through Shravan and beyond.

## Vidhi

- **Evening of Dashami:** Take only sattvic food; abstain from onion, garlic, and non-vegetarian food.
- **Fasting:** Full fast on Ekadashi — no grain, rice, wheat, dals, or legumes; onion, garlic, and eggplant are also traditionally avoided. Permitted: fruit, milk, sabudana, singhare ki roti, and sendha namak.
- **Morning of Ekadashi:** Wake before sunrise; bathe with clean water; set up the puja space with an image or idol of Vishnu.
- **Puja:** Perform abhishek with panchamrita or clean water; offer tulsi leaves, yellow flowers, and a ghee diya — tulsi is especially dear to Vishnu.
- **Evening:** Recite the Vishnu Sahasranama or the Ekadashi Mahatmya.
- **Night vigil:** Stay awake in prayer if possible, singing bhajans through the night.
- **Breaking the fast:** On Dwadashi, after sunrise but before the Dwadashi tithi ends — check DrikPanchang for the exact parana window for your location.
- **Special note for 2026 parana:** Ekadashi tithi ends at 5:22 AM on July 11 — before sunrise — so the Dwadashi tithi is already running at sunrise; the parana window may be unusually narrow. Confirm on DrikPanchang.

## Mantras

**Mool mantra for Ekadashi japa:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

*Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya*

Meaning: "I bow to the Lord Vasudeva." This is the twelve-syllable mantra — its recitation on Ekadashi is considered especially fruitful.

*Maṅgalaṃ Bhagavān Viṣṇur Maṅgalaṃ Garuḍadhvajaḥ |
