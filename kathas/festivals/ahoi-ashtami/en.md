## Katha

In a village there lived a mother.

She had seven sons — her home was full, her courtyard rang with their voices. She was a hardworking, loving woman, and she cherished all seven of her boys more dearly than life itself.

One year, a few days before Kartik Krishna Ashtami, preparations for Diwali were underway. The house needed plastering, the courtyard needed sweeping. She needed clay.

She went into the forest — where the earth was soft and moist.

She picked up her spade and began to dig.

---

Beneath that very ground lay the cubs of an ahoi — a small forest creature.

They were hidden from her eyes. She did not know.

The spade struck hard — and the cubs were badly hurt. Hearing their cries, the mother's heart trembled. She looked down and saw the tiny creatures lying wounded.

She was shaken, distressed. But what could she do — what had happened could not be undone. She took the clay and returned home.

---

That same year her eldest son fell gravely ill — and passed away.

The next year, her second son.

Then the third. Then the fourth.

One by one, year after year, all seven sons were taken from her.

That mother was broken. The threshold of her home stood desolate, her courtyard held no laughter. She could not understand — what sin had she committed that God would give her such a punishment.

---

One day, a wise old woman of the village came to her.

She listened to that mother's anguish. Then she asked gently — "Did you ever, even unknowingly, cause harm to the cubs of a forest creature?"

The mother went numb.

The memory of that incident from years past came rushing back — the forest, the blow of the spade, those crying cubs.

The elder woman said — "Child, even a sin committed unknowingly bears its fruit. But go and seek the refuge of Ahoi Mata. Repent with a true heart. Ahoi Mata is full of compassion — she understands a mother's pain."

---

That woman took the vow to fast on Kartik Krishna Ashtami.

She rose before sunrise. She bathed. Through the whole day she remained without water or food.

In the evening she drew a picture of Ahoi Mata on the wall of her home — Ahoi Mata with her seven cubs. She performed puja with roli, akshat, and a lamp. She offered chori, halwa, and puri as bhog.

Then she folded her hands and spoke —

"Ahoi Mata, unknowingly I committed a sin. I hurt your cubs. I am at fault. Forgive me, Ma. Please give my sons back to me."

Her eyes were flowing with tears, but in her heart there was faith.

At night the stars came out. She offered arghya to the stars and broke her fast.

---

Ahoi Mata's grace descended.

One by one, all seven of her sons returned — with health, long life, and vitality.

The courtyard of her home rang out again.

According to the folk tradition, it is said that a mother who observes the waterless fast on Kartik Krishna Ashtami, draws the image of Ahoi Mata and performs puja with proper rites, hears the katha, and breaks her fast only after sighting the stars — her sons receive the blessing of long life, good health, and protection.

## Significance

Ahoi Ashtami is **a mother's vrat** — for the long life and protection of her son.

Other vrats like Karva Chauth are for a husband's long life; Ahoi Ashtami is devoted entirely to the bond between a mother and her son. This vrat is a mother's faith that says — "Protect my child from every hardship in this world."

**Ahoi Mata** is also called "Andheri Mata" — the one who protects even in darkness. She is the forest goddess, the mother of all living creatures. This is why the katha centres on the harming of a forest creature's cubs — the sin of taking a life, whether knowingly or unknowingly, must be accounted for.

**The image with the cubs:** What makes Ahoi Mata distinctly recognizable is that her image is always drawn alongside seven cubs. This picture is made in folk-art style — on the wall in ochre or red. This tradition of drawing sets this vrat apart from all others and gives it its unique character.

**Exactly eight days before Diwali** this vrat arrives. In this dark fortnight of Kartik — when the nights are deep and unlit — mothers light lamps before Ahoi Mata for their sons.

This vrat is observed primarily in **North India** — in Uttar Pradesh, Haryana, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, and Rajasthan.

## Vidhi

- **For whom:** Observed by mothers — for the long life and wellbeing of their sons. A mother without sons may also observe this vrat with the prayer for a son in the future.
- **Waterless fast:** On Kartik Krishna Ashtami, rise before sunrise, bathe, and take the vow — "I observe the vrat of Ahoi Mata for the long life of my son." Through the entire day, neither water nor food (nirjal — this is the core rule).
- **Drawing Ahoi Mata:** Before the evening puja, draw the image of Ahoi Mata on the wall of the home (or on paper) — Ahoi Mata with her seven cubs. Use ochre, turmeric, or red colour.
- **Offerings:** Roli, akshat, a lamp, sindoor, and a water-filled kalash. For bhog — chori (a smaller form of puri), halwa, and puri.
- **Hearing the katha:** Sit with the women of the household and listen to the Ahoi Ashtami katha — this is an essential part of the vrat.
- **Sighting the stars:** In the evening when the stars appear, sight them through a sieve (in some regions the moon is also awaited, as the moonrise on this tithi is late into the night).
- **Breaking the fast:** Offer water as arghya to the stars; pray to Ahoi Mata for the long life, health, and protection of your son; then drink water and take food.
- **The spirit of repentance:** Accepting a mistake made knowingly or unknowingly, and seeking forgiveness before Ahoi Mata with a true heart — this is the very soul of this vrat.

## Mantras

**Prayer to Ahoi Mata:**

> अहोई माता अहोई माता, तेरे हाथों में सात छाता ।
> जितनी जल्दी संध्या आए, उतनी जल्दी मेरा पुत्र लम्बी उम्र पाए ॥

Ahoi mātā ahoi mātā, tere hāthoṃ meṃ sāt chātā |
jitnī jaldī sandhyā āe, utnī jaldī merā putra lambī umra pāe ||

Meaning: O Ahoi Mata, in your hands rests the shelter of seven cubs. Just as the evening comes at its appointed hour, may my son receive the gift of long life.

---

**Salutation mantra to Ahoi Mata:**

> ॐ ह्रीं अहोई माताये नमः

Oṃ hrīṃ ahoi mātāye namaḥ

Meaning: Salutations to Ahoi Mata.

Recite this mantra at the beginning and at the close of the puja.
