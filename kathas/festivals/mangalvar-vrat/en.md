## Katha

In a certain town lived a woman named Subhadra. Her husband, Somdatt, was a merchant — skilled, industrious, the sole support of their home. One day he set out on a journey to a distant land for trade. As he left, he said — "I will return soon."

But the months passed.

No news came. No message, no letter.

The people of the town began to say — "Subhadra, your husband has not returned. Perhaps he is no longer in this world." Friends who had once been close slowly began to turn away.

It was a very difficult time for Subhadra. No certain word, no support.

One day she went to see an elderly woman of the town — one who was known as a wise woman, deeply versed in vrat and puja. Subhadra told her everything of her suffering.

The woman listened. Then she said — "Child, do not be afraid. Observe the Tuesday fast — twenty-one consecutive Tuesdays. Worship Mangal Dev, offer red flowers and red sindoor. Give up salt. Do it with feeling, do it with faith."

Subhadra took the sankalpa.

The first Tuesday. The second. The third.

Every Tuesday — morning bath, worship of Mangal Dev, red flowers, red sindoor, one meal without salt, a prayer in her heart for her husband's well-being.

The seventh. The tenth. The fifteenth.

Subhadra did not waver. Whatever people said on the outside, the faith within her held firm.

The twentieth Tuesday came. The twenty-first Tuesday — the final day.

That morning Subhadra completed her puja. And that very afternoon —

There was a sound at the door.

Somdatt had returned.

Tired, but safe. He told her — on the journey, a king of a border region had taken him prisoner. For months he had lived in that dungeon — no means of escape. But on that twenty-first Tuesday — for no clear reason — a feeling of compassion arose in the king's heart and he released him.

In the folk tradition of the Tuesday fast, it is said that a woman who observes this vrat with true devotion will have her good fortune protected, her husband's life and well-being sustained, and the malefic effects of Mangal Dosha in her life diminished.

## Significance

Tuesday is the day of two deities — Mangal Dev and Hanuman.

**Mangal Dev:** Mangal is one of the nine navagrahas — born from the womb of the earth, red in hue, with shakti in hand. In astrology, Mangal is the significator of courage, energy, and protective strength. When there is Mangal Dosha in the horoscope — especially causing obstacles in marriage or conflict in marital life — the Tuesday fast is prescribed as a remedy.

**Hanuman (North Indian tradition):** In North India, Tuesday is also the day of Hanuman. Hanuman is Bajrangbali — like Mangal, he too is of red hue and a symbol of indomitable strength. It is said that the malefic influence of the Mangal graha is pacified through the worship of Hanuman. That is why many devotees recite the Hanuman Chalisa and the Sundarkanda on Tuesdays.

**Kartikeya (South Indian tradition):** In South India, Tuesday is also considered the day of Kartikeya (Murugan), for he too is a god of war and a symbol of energy akin to Mangal.

## Vidhi

**Tuesday puja procedure:**
1. Bathe in the morning and wear red clothing if possible.
2. Perform worship of Hanuman and Mangal Dev.
3. **For Mangal Dev:** Offer red flowers (rose, marigold), red sindoor, and red sandalwood.
4. **For Hanuman:** Offer sindoor, jasmine oil, and red flowers.
5. Light a lamp — one of sesame oil.
6. Chant *Om Ham Hanumate Namah*.
7. Recite the Mangal stuti.

**Sankalpa for twenty-one Tuesdays:**
Devotees who observe this vrat for the removal of Mangal Dosha or for the protection of good fortune should take a sankalpa for twenty-one consecutive Tuesdays. On the twenty-first Tuesday, perform udyapan — prepare prasad and distribute it.

## Observance

**Fasting:** No food at all or one meal — giving up salt is essential. Items made with jaggery, fruit, and milk may be taken.

**Breaking the fast:** Wednesday morning, after sunrise.

**Take note:** If a sankalpa for twenty-one Tuesdays has been taken and one Tuesday is missed, some traditions require the vrat to be begun again — according to the terms of the sankalpa.

## Mantras

**Hanuman Bija Mantra — the primary chant of the Tuesday fast:**

> ॐ हं हनुमते नमः

oṃ haṃ hanumate namaḥ

Meaning: Salutation to Hanuman.

---

**Mangal Stuti — worship of Mangal Dev:**

> धरणीगर्भसम्भूतं विद्युत्कान्तिसमप्रभम् ।
> कुमारं शक्तिहस्तं च मंगलं प्रणमाम्यहम् ॥

dharaṇīgarbhasambhūtaṃ vidyutkāntisamaprabham | kumāraṃ śaktihastam ca maṃgalaṃ praṇamāmyaham ||

Meaning: Born from the womb of the earth, radiant as a flash of lightning, bearing shakti in hand — I bow to that Mangal Dev.
