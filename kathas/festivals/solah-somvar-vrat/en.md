## Katha

Amravati — that city of the heavens where Indra holds sway and the gods roam. In that city stood a Shiva temple, and that temple's priest was a Brahmin — calm, devoted, a Shiva-bhakta.

One day Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati came to that temple. There they sat down to play a game of dice — that game which has been played among the gods since ancient times.

Parvati called the Brahmin priest forward and asked: *"Brahmin, tell me — who will win this game?"*

The priest thought for a moment. He felt: Lord Shiva is here, I should say Shiva will win. He said: *"Lord Shiva will win."*

But Parvati won the game.

She did not smile. She said: *"Brahmin, you did not speak the truth. What was in your heart, you did not say — you said what seemed favorable. This untruth does not befit you."*

And Parvati cursed him: *"You shall fall ill. Until the atonement for this untruth is made, suffering will remain with you."*

The priest, stricken with illness, departed from the temple. But where to go — this was his service, this was his life. He sat on the outer steps of the temple; he had no right to enter within.

Much later, one day some apsaras came to that temple — descended from the heavens, on pilgrimage. They saw the sick Brahmin sitting on the steps and asked: *"Who are you? Why this state?"*

The priest told them everything.

The apsaras said: *"There is a way. The vrat of sixteen Mondays — that sadhana of Shiva which can melt even the hardest curse. Sixteen consecutive Mondays, fasting, night vigil, Shiva worship. On the seventeenth Monday, perform a special puja — prepare prasad from wheat flour, offer it to Shiva, and distribute it among family members. Lord Shiva will be pleased."*

The priest observed sixteen Mondays with complete devotion. One by one — fast, puja, Shiva's remembrance. With feeling, with faith, without lapse.

On the seventeenth Monday, when he performed Shiva's puja — something lifted. The disease went. The curse was lifted.

And then Shiva's voice was heard: *"Your sadhana is complete. Return to Amravati."*

In the tradition of the Shiva Purana, this is said of the Solah Somvar Vrat: one who observes it finds diseases gone, obstacles removed, wishes fulfilled — and in the end, attains Shivaloka.

## Significance

Solah Somvar is no ordinary fast — it is a vow.

Sixteen weeks, without missing once, with a single unwavering intent. This is the practice that steadies the mind and gives life the direction of Shiva.

Beginning in Shravan is especially auspicious because Shravan is itself the month of Shiva — to plant the seed of sadhana in the midst of that energy causes it to take deeper root.

In tradition, this vrat is observed by unmarried women for an ideal husband; by men for relief from business troubles, illness, or enemies; by married women for the long life and welfare of their husbands.

## Vidhi

- **When:** Sixteen consecutive Mondays, ideally beginning from the first Monday of Shravan.
- **At dawn:** Bathe early; visit a Shiva temple or worship at a Shivalinga at home.
- **Offerings:** Water, milk, bilva leaves, white flowers, and sandalwood on the Shivalinga; chant *Oṃ Namaḥ Śivāya*.
- **Fasting:** Full fast — no rice or grains; fruits, milk, and sabudana may be taken once. Some traditions observe a complete waterless fast — according to one's strength.
- **At night:** Night vigil and Shiva's remembrance.
- **Breaking the fast:** Break on the following Tuesday after sunrise; offer water to Shiva and remember him before breaking.
- **Seventeenth Monday — Udyapan:** Perform a special puja; prepare prasad from wheat flour (halwa or churma); offer it to Shiva; distribute among family, friends, and Brahmins; then break the fast. The vrat is complete after this udyapan. If a Monday is missed, some traditions require beginning the vrat again — according to the sankalpa.
- **For whom:** Unmarried women (for an ideal husband); married women (for husband's long life and welfare); men (for relief from illness, business troubles, or enemies).

## Mantras

**Shiva Stuti — for the Solah Somvar puja:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय नमः सोमाय नमश्चन्द्रशेखराय ।
> नमः प्रमथनाथाय नमस्ते विश्वभावन ॥

*Oṃ namaḥ śivāya namaḥ somāya namaś-candra-śekharāya |
Namaḥ pramatha-nāthāya namas te viśva-bhāvana ||*

Meaning: "Salutations to Shiva, to Soma, to Chandrashekara — salutations to the lord of the Pramathas and to the sustainer of the world."
