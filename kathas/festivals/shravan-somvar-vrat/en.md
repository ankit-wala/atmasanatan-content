## Katha

Gandharvasena was the daughter of Ghanavahana, king of the Gandharvas. Matchless in beauty, excellent in qualities — but one day a thought arose in her mind that should not have.

She declared: *"In all three worlds, none has beauty like mine."*

Shiva's attendants heard this. Shikhandī — a gana of Shiva, distinct from the Shikhandī of the Mahabharata — stood before her and said: *"Devi, this pride does not become you. Beauty is a gift from the Lord, not your own achievement."*

Gandharvasena ignored his words.

Shikhandī said: *"Very well. Then this form you take such pride in — you will have to see it with different eyes."*

In that very moment, the marks of leprosy appeared upon Gandharvasena's body.

There was panic in the family. Ghanavahana summoned physicians, sent her to holy sites — but no remedy came. This ailment was no ordinary disease; it was a curse.

Then someone brought her before Maharshi Goshringa. The sage observed everything, understood, and said:

*"The pride of this girl has brought her to this state. But where there is a curse, there is also a remedy. Go to the Prabhasa Kshetra — the sacred land where Lord Someshvara (Mahadeva) resides. There, observe the Somavar Vrat — fasting, night-long vigil, worship of Someshvara. By the grace of Bholenath, this disease will depart."*

Gandharvasena reached Prabhasa Kshetra. There, at the temple of Someshvara, she began the Somavar Vrat. Each Monday — fasting, worship, vigil. Chanting the name of Lord Shiva. Offering bilva leaves, datura, and water in abhisheka.

And Shiva relented.

Through the power of the vrat, gradually the disease began to leave Gandharvasena's body. The purification that could not come from outside came from within. Pride departed, disease departed, and the beauty that remained was more serene than before.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, Prabhasa Khanda, it is said of the Somavar Vrat: *"One who observes it receives the merit of bathing at Pushkar, drinking the waters of Kedara, and beholding the Jyotirlinga. All bodily ailments are destroyed, all wishes are fulfilled."*

And when this Somavar Vrat falls in the month of Shravan — its fruit becomes many times greater. For Shravan is the month of Shiva.

## Significance

Shravan and Monday — both are beloved by Shiva.

Shravan is the month of Shiva because in this month, water reigns over the earth — and upon Shiva's own head, Ganga herself resides. The rain, the floods, this surging sky — all carry a glimpse of Shiva's very nature.

Monday is Shiva's day because "Soma" means the moon — and the moon is the jewel on Shiva's crown.

When the confluence of Shravan and Monday occurs — this is no longer ordinary worship. It is the very gateway to reaching Shiva directly.

## Vidhi

- **For whom:** Observe on all four Mondays of Shravan; all four are equal — none lesser or greater; observing all four is ideal, even one or two is auspicious.
- **Fasting:** Fast from morning through the day — with fruits only or without any food; break the fast the following morning (Tuesday) after sunrise.
- **At dawn:** After the morning bath, visit a Shiva temple or worship at a Shivalinga at home.
- **Abhisheka:** Perform abhisheka with water, milk, curd, honey, ghee, and Ganga water.
- **Offerings:** Offer bilva leaves, datura, bhang, white arka flowers, and sandalwood.
- **Puja:** Listen to or read the Shravan Somvar Vrat katha; chant *Oṃ Namaḥ Śivāya*.
- **Evening:** Perform worship again at pradosha time (dusk).
- **Special in Shravan:** This month holds a special tradition of jalabhisheka at Shiva temples; the "Bol Bam" pilgrimages also take place during this month.

## Mantras

**Mahamrityunjaya Mantra — the special mantra of Shravan Mondays:**

> ॐ त्र्यम्बकं यजामहे सुगन्धिं पुष्टिवर्धनम् ।
> उर्वारुकमिव बन्धनान् मृत्योर्मुक्षीय माऽमृतात् ॥

*Oṃ tryambakaṃ yajāmahe sugandhiṃ puṣṭi-vardhanam |
Urvārukam iva bandhanān mṛtyor mukṣīya mā'mṛtāt ||*

Meaning: "We worship the three-eyed one (Shiva), fragrant and nourishing. As a cucumber is freed from its vine, liberate us from the bonds of death — not from immortality."

---

**Panchakshara Mantra — for continuous japa:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय

*Oṃ Namaḥ Śivāya*

Meaning: "Salutations to Shiva." — The simplest and most powerful mantra of Shiva.
