## Katha

The Pandavas were in exile. Kingdom, wealth, honour — all of it was gone. The days passed in the solitude of the forest, yet there were times when they could not even arrange food for the guests and Brahmins who came to them. Draupadi carried a heavy sorrow in her heart — watching her husbands suffer was breaking her from within.

One day the family preceptor, Rishi Dhaumya, came to the Pandavas. He understood everything from one look at Draupadi's grief-stricken face. He drew her aside and said —

"Devi, the root cause of the Pandavas' misfortune is the withdrawal of Suryadev's grace. Poverty cannot take hold in a household where the Sun is worshipped. On Kartik Shukla Shashthi, the 'Surya Shashthi vrat' is observed — what people also call 'Chhath'. If you perform this vrat with faith and steadfast devotion — standing in the river without food or water, offering arghya to the setting and then the rising Sun — Suryadev will surely be pleased."

Draupadi touched Rishi Dhaumya's feet and took the vow.

The month of Kartik arrived. Draupadi began this sacred four-day journey with complete devotion.

**First day — Nahay-Khay (ritual bath and one pure meal):** She bathed, purifying both body and mind. That day she ate only sattvic food — gourd vegetable, chana dal, and rice. Her resolve was firm.

**Second day — Kharna (fast ending with a sacred evening offering):** She observed a strict fast from sunrise to sunset without water. When the Sun began to dip toward the horizon in the evening, Draupadi cooked kheer and roti on a clay stove — in complete purity, untouched by anyone else. She first offered this naivedya to Suryadev, then distributed it as prasad to the Pandavas and the Brahmins present. After this, the nirjal (waterless) fast began — no water, no food.

**Third day — Shashthi, Sandhya Arghya (the evening offering):** This was the main day. Draupadi arranged thekua, fruits, coconut, sugarcane, lemon, and betel nut in a bamboo winnowing basket and went to the riverbank. The Sun was moving toward the west. She stepped into the water — standing waist-deep in the current. Facing the setting Sun — the Sun at dusk — she raised the basket and offered arghya. This offering was singular, because the world worships the rising Sun; in Chhath, the setting Sun receives equal reverence — a recognition that God is to be worshipped at every stage of life.

**Fourth day — Saptami, Usha Arghya (the dawn offering and breaking of the fast):** Before dawn, Draupadi returned to the riverbank. She had kept vigil through the night; her body was exhausted, but her devotion was unshaken. When the eastern sky turned crimson and the first ray of Suryadev fell upon the water, she stood in the river and offered arghya to the rising Sun. The vrat was complete.

Suryadev was pleased. Before long, the Pandavas received the **Akshaypatra** in the forest — the divine inexhaustible vessel given by Surya, which would never become empty. As long as Draupadi herself had not eaten, food would keep flowing from it. The Pandavas' poverty ended; their radiance and strength returned.

This is the foundational katha of the Chhath vrat. The Skanda Purana tradition says — those men and women who observe this vrat with devotion will find their children blessed with long life and good health, their homes filled with peace and prosperity, and the grace of Suryadev resting upon them all their lives.

## Significance

Chhath Puja is among the rare festivals of India where **the setting Sun is worshipped**. The world bows to the rising Sun — symbol of success, light, and new beginnings. But in Chhath, the setting Sun receives equal honour — conveying that at every stage of life, in rising as well as in decline, worship of the Lord must never be abandoned.

In this festival **there is no idol, no temple** — only the river, the Sun, and the open sky. Direct worship of nature. This is one of India's most ancient worship traditions, one that regards the primal elements of creation — Sun, water, earth — as the living Divine.

Chhathi Maiya is Shashthi Devi, the mind-born daughter of Brahma. She is the protector of newborn children. This is why the vrat is observed especially for the long life and health of one's children. Suryadev is the deity of health — his worship grants healing and vitality.

This festival is the very identity of Bihar, Jharkhand, and eastern Uttar Pradesh — a communal festival in which the entire community gathers at the riverbank. Today it is celebrated in every corner of India and, through the Indian diaspora, across the world.

## Vidhi

- **Day 1 — Nahay-Khay (Kartik Shukla Chaturthi):** Bathe in the morning; clean the home; eat only sattvic food — gourd vegetable, chana dal, and parboiled (arwa) rice; garlic and onion are prohibited; this day purifies body and mind.
- **Day 2 — Kharna (Kartik Shukla Panchami):** Observe a waterless (nirjal) fast from sunrise to sunset; at sunset prepare kheer with sugarcane juice or jaggery and ghee-spread roti on a clay stove in complete purity; offer naivedya to Suryadev first, then distribute as prasad; after this the nirjal fast of 36 hours begins — not even a drop of water is taken.
- **Day 3 — Sandhya Arghya / Shashthi Puja (Kartik Shukla Shashthi):** Fill a bamboo winnowing basket with thekua (jaggery-wheat), fruits (banana, coconut, lemon, water chestnut), sugarcane, betel nut, ginger, and raw turmeric; reach the riverbank before sunset; stand waist-deep in the water; face the setting Sun and offer arghya three times with a mixture of milk and water; sing the aarti of Chhathi Maiya.
- **Day 4 — Usha Arghya (Kartik Shukla Saptami):** Rise at brahma muhurta and return to the riverbank; stand waist-deep in the water at sunrise; offer arghya to the rising Sun with the basket in the same manner; conclude the vrat; break the fast with thekua and fruit — do not eat a heavy meal immediately.
- **Fasting:** The main fast runs approximately 36 hours (Kharna evening to Usha Arghya) without water; standing waist-deep in water at both arghya timings is the most essential requirement.
- **For whom:** Women primarily observe this vrat but men may also do so; it is observed especially for the long life and health of one's children; once begun it must be observed every year — a prescribed ritual exists for those who need to discontinue it.
- **Purity:** Maintain purity throughout; wear new or freshly washed clothes; the offerings in the bamboo basket must not be touched by anyone else.

## Mantras

**Surya Namaskar Mantra:**

> ॐ सूर्याय नमः । ॐ आदित्याय नमः । ॐ नमः सवित्रे ॥

Oṃ Sūryāya namaḥ | Oṃ Ādityāya namaḥ | Oṃ namaḥ savitre ||

Meaning: "Salutation to Suryadev, salutation to Aditya, salutation to Savitridev."

---

**Chhathi Maiya Prayer:**

> ॐ जय छठी मैया, छठी मैया जय हो ।
> सूर्य षष्ठी व्रत तेरा, संतान सुखी सदा हो ॥

Oṃ jaya chaṭhī maiyā, chaṭhī maiyā jaya ho |
sūrya ṣaṣṭhī vrata terā, santāna sukhī sadā ho ||

Meaning: "Victory to Chhathi Maiya, glory to Chhathi Maiya. Through this Surya Shashthi vrat of yours, may one's children always live in joy."
