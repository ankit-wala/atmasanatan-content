## Katha

Vaishakh Shukla Tritiya — the tithi known as svayamsiddha muhurta, one that requires no consultation of the panchang. Whatever is begun on this day, whatever is offered — it becomes akshaya, it never diminishes. The glory of this tithi is most deeply illuminated by one katha: the katha of Sri Krishna and his childhood friend Sudama.

Sudama and Sri Krishna had studied together in the same gurukul — at the ashram of Sandipani Muni. In childhood they ate from the same plate, gathered wood from the same forest, and were companions in each other's joys and sorrows. Then life set them on two entirely different paths. Sri Krishna became the lord of Dwaraka; and Sudama? Sudama remained a poor Brahmin — struggling even for two meals a day, living in worn garments, sustaining himself in austerity and devotion.

One day Sudama's wife could bear it no longer. She said, "Your friend is the lord of Dwaraka. Go to him once, meet him." Sudama felt inwardly reluctant. To go to a friend and ask — this his soul could not accept. Yet his wife's appeal was persistent. He thought to himself, "I will go to meet my friend. I will ask for nothing."

There was nothing in the house to bring as an offering. His wife borrowed some rice from the neighbours and made a small amount of chivda — a handful of poha. She tied it in a piece of old cloth, and Sudama set off toward Dwaraka.

At the sight of Dwaraka's magnificence, Sudama's heart shrank still further. Golden palaces, gem-studded gateways, thousands of attendants — and there he was, in tattered garments, barefoot. He stopped at the gateway and said, "Sudama, the childhood friend of Sri Krishna, has come."

Inside, when Sri Krishna heard that name — he rose from his seat at once. He had been sitting with Maharani Rukmini; leaving everything, he came running. The moment he saw the lowly Brahmin standing at the door, his eyes filled with tears. He embraced Sudama — as though the thirst of years was quenched in a single moment. Rukmini herself brought water and washed Sudama's feet. Sri Krishna seated him on his own throne and had him fanned with a chamara.

Sudama kept concealing the little bundle — where in this palace will I offer this chivda? But Sri Krishna's eyes knew everything. He said, "Sakhe, what have you brought for me?" and pulled open the bundle himself. A handful of dry, plain chivda — which Sudama had been ashamed to offer. But Sri Krishna? His eyes lit up. He took a handful and ate it with great love, saying, "Aha! What a taste! Lakshmi ji, do taste this — there is the very flavour of Lakshmi herself in this." He was about to take a second handful when Rukmini gently caught his hand: "My Lord, two handfuls have already given the riches of the three worlds; if a third is taken, what will remain even in this world?"

Sudama spent the day there and then departed. He had asked for nothing — and Sri Krishna had said nothing. On the way home, Sudama's mind was heavy: "How will I face my wife, what will I tell her, going back empty-handed?" But as he drew closer to his village, the road began to look unfamiliar. Where the broken-down hut had stood, a beautiful mansion now rose. His wife, adorned in fine garments, came forward: "My lord, how did all this come to be?" Sudama understood. Sri Krishna, without being asked, without a word — had given him everything.

It is stated in the tradition of the Bhagavata Purana that this meeting took place on Vaishakh Shukla Tritiya — and this is why whatever is given with love on this tithi bears akshaya fruit. What a handful of chivda accomplished cannot be accomplished by the gifting of crores of gold — for within that chivda was selfless love, nothing more.

## Significance

Akshaya Tritiya is counted among the "sade-teen muhurtas" — a tithi that is svayamsiddha, needing no auspicious-time calculation. On Vaishakh Shukla Tritiya, both the sun and the moon are in their exalted signs — a conjunction that is exceedingly rare.

The second great katha of this day comes from the Mahabharata. When the Pandavas were serving their exile in the forest, sages and guests continued to arrive at their dwelling. Draupadi found it increasingly difficult to feed each person. Seeing Yudhishthira's distress, Sage Dhaumya counselled him to worship the Sun. On Akshaya Tritiya, Suryadeva was pleased and bestowed upon Yudhishthira the Akshaya Patra — a vessel that would never be empty so long as Draupadi herself had not eaten from it. This patra fed all guests throughout the twelve years of forest exile.

The word akshaya means that which never diminishes. Whatever dana, punya, or new beginning is made on this tithi bears akshaya fruit. For this reason:
- The purchase of gold on this day is considered auspicious — for gold is itself an akshaya metal, and gold bought on this day brings unbroken prosperity to the home.
- A new business, new construction, a marriage ceremony — all are excellent on this tithi without consulting the panchang.
- The fruit of dana is akshaya — the gifting of grain, cloth, and water is especially fruitful on this day.

## Vidhi

**Bathing and sankalpa:** Rise at Brahma Muhurta and bathe. Sit facing east or north.

**Worship of Lord Vishnu:** Wear yellow garments. Offer Lord Vishnu yellow flowers, tulasi leaves, yellow sandal paste, and panchamrita. Perform shodashopachara worship. Lakshmi ji is pleased by lotus flowers and kumkum.

**Gold worship:** If gold or silver is purchased on this day, bring it home and first place it before Vishnu-Lakshmi at the puja space, and show dhoop and deepa. It is the belief that gold acquired on this day establishes Lakshmi's permanent presence in the home.

**Dana:** The dana of this day bears akshaya fruit. The giving of water (filled in an earthen pot), grain-dana, cloth-dana, the gifting of an umbrella, the gifting of footwear — all are meritorious. Feed Brahmins or sattvic persons.

**Parashurama Jayanti:** Akshaya Tritiya is also the birth-tithi of Lord Parashurama. Worshipping him and chanting his name on this day is especially fruitful.

**New beginnings:** A new business, a new account, new construction, a grahapravesh — if begun on this tithi, there is no need to consult the panchang.

## Observance

**Dana:**
The giving of grain, water, cloth, a chhatra (umbrella), and paduka (footwear) — the dana of this day bears fruit multiplied to infinity. In particular, the giving of water (offering water to the thirsty, placing earthen pots of water) is supremely meritorious.

**Gold worship:**
The purchase of gold, silver, or new vessels is auspicious on this day. Place the gold purchased before Vishnu-Lakshmi and seek their blessing for prosperity.

**New beginnings:**
Marriage, grahapravesh, starting a new business, opening a new account — all of these have this tithi as their svayamsiddha muhurta.

**Fasting:**
This is principally a festival tithi; fasting is not obligatory. Those who wish to fast may take a fruit-meal. In the evening, recite Vishnu Sahasranama or the Sudama Katha, and then take food.

## Mantras

**Vishnu Mantra:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

oṃ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya

Meaning: Salutations to you, O Lord Vasudeva. (This dvadashakshara mantra is chanted for the grace of Vishnu and for liberation.)

**Akshaya Tritiya shloka — invocation of Lakshmi and Kubera:**

> ॐ श्रीं ह्रीं श्रीं कमले कमलालये प्रसीद प्रसीद
> ॐ श्रीं ह्रीं श्रीं महालक्ष्म्यै नमः

oṃ śrīṃ hrīṃ śrīṃ kamale kamalālaye prasīda prasīda
oṃ śrīṃ hrīṃ śrīṃ mahālakṣmyai namaḥ

Meaning: O Mother Lakshmi, enthroned on the lotus, dwelling in the lotus — be gracious, be gracious. Salutations to Mahalakshmi.

(Chanting this mantra 108 times on this tithi is especially fruitful for the grace of Lakshmi and akshaya prosperity.)
