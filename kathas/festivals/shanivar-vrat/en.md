## Katha

In Ujjayini, Vikramaditya ruled.

His justice was renowned, his valour unmatched. His fame had spread through all three worlds. Among all the kings on earth, Vikramaditya's name stood highest.

Once the navagrahas — Surya, Chandra, Mangal, Budh, Guru, Shukra, Shani, Rahu, and Ketu — gathered in assembly. The subject was: who among the nine is supreme?

---

One by one each graha declared its glory.

Surya said — "Without me the world would be plunged in darkness."

Chandra said — "The mind, health, and the tides — all flow from me."

Mangal said — "I am the master of strength and courage."

Finally Shani Dev spoke — "I am supreme. My Sade Sati brings even the greatest to the dust. Even the devas fear me. In my time, kings become paupers, the mighty bow low."

Silence fell over the assembly.

---

Vikramaditya was in that assembly.

He could not hold back. He said — "Shani Dev, I have no fear of you. My fate rests on my karma and my deity. I will not abandon my dharma out of fear of any graha."

Shani Dev said with a calm smile — "Very well, O King. Your Sade Sati will come. Then we shall see."

Vikramaditya remained unmoved.

---

Time passed. Shani's Sade Sati came upon Vikramaditya's sign.

In the first month, his horses and chariot were lost.

Then the royal treasury began to dry up.

Then one day a foreign horseman-merchant arrived in Ujjayini. He was selling a fine horse. Vikramaditya looked at the horse, mounted it — and the horse carried him off to some unfamiliar forest. There the horse vanished.

Vikramaditya stood alone, on foot, on a strange land.

---

There was a town there. Vikramaditya considered how to make a living.

He approached a wealthy merchant and asked for work. The merchant gave him the task of grazing horses and tending the stables. The one who had once spoken in the assembly of the navagrahas was now sweeping the stables.

But Vikramaditya did not complain, nor did he reveal his identity. He knew — this is Shani's time. Patience is the only dharma.

---

One day a rare gem had been kept in the merchant's stable. It disappeared in the night.

The accusation fell on Vikramaditya.

He was seized. He was produced before the king's court.

No witness, no defence. Only suspicion.

The king ordered — cut off this thief's hands.

And both of Vikramaditya's hands were cut off.

He lay mutilated, blood-soaked, in a stranger's city, without anyone's help. An oil-presser took pity and gave him shelter — the work of tending the bullocks who turned the oil-press, which he could manage only with his feet and his voice.

Seven years passed in this condition.

---

At the end of the seventh year, it was the final moment of Shani's Sade Sati.

The daughter of the king of that city was seated at the window of her palace. It was night. She looked in the direction of the stable — and she saw a wondrous sight.

The horse at the oil-presser's place suddenly began to weep. Then it spoke — in a human voice — "O King! O King! Tonight there is a plot against the royal palace. Your commander has made a pact with the enemy."

The princess heard this. She ran and woke her father.

The king was vigilant — and the plot was foiled. The city was saved.

---

The king asked — "Whose horse is this? Who is this person who has saved my kingdom?"

The oil-presser told the truth. Vikramaditya was brought.

All were stunned to see his condition. The princess looked at Vikramaditya and said — "I will marry this man."

The king gave his consent.

And that very night — the moment Sade Sati's last instant was over — Vikramaditya's severed hands miraculously grew back.

Vikramaditya told them everything. He was the king of Ujjayini.

The marriage was celebrated. With honour, with splendour, he returned to his city.

---

Back in Ujjayini, Vikramaditya bowed his head before Shani Dev.

He said — "Shani Dev, you have proved the truth. You are the most mighty. Your Sade Sati brought me to the dust — but it did not break my dharma. Today I bow before you."

Shani Dev was pleased.

He said — "Vikrama, you did not abandon your dharma even in suffering. This is what I love most. Whatever person keeps the Saturday fast, lights an oil lamp, offers sesame and urad, and listens to this katha — I will make his Sade Sati gentle. Hardships will come, but he will not be broken."

In the tradition of the Saturday fast, it is said that from Shani Dev's word, those devotees who observe this vrat with sincere heart receive Shani's grace and find support in the difficult seasons of their lives.

## Significance

Shani Dev is the **deity of justice** — he is no one's enemy; he is the dispenser of the fruits of karma.

In astrology, Shani's **Sade Sati** (a transit of seven and a half years) and **Dhaiya** (a transit of two and a half years) are counted among the most difficult periods of a life. But the shastras say — Shani only makes one carry the weight of one's karmic debts; one who has accumulated merit cannot be drowned even by Shani.

**Shani's nature:** He moves slowly — which is why he is called "Shanaishchara" (one who moves slowly). His colour is krishna (deep blue-black). He is the son of Surya, but his relationship with his father is complex — it is from this tension that his power arises.

**Shani and Hanuman:** There is a widespread folk tradition in North India — Hanuman is also worshipped on Saturdays. It is believed that Hanuman protects devotees from the afflictions of Shani Dev. When Shani Dev himself took refuge with Hanuman at the time of the burning of Lanka — on account of that story, the worship of Hanuman on Saturdays holds special significance.

**The significance of sesame and oil:** Shani Dev is extremely fond of black sesame, sesame oil, and urad dal. All of these are things associated with Shani's element. Giving them in donation pleases Shani.

## Vidhi

1. **Vrat from sunrise:** On Saturday, rise before sunrise. Bathe. You may wear black or dark blue clothing (Shani's colour). Take the sankalpa — "I am observing this vrat for Shani Dev's grace and for the pacification of my Sade Sati / Dhaiya."

2. **Worship of Shani Dev:** Before Shani Dev's murti or picture — offer black sesame, sesame oil, urad dal, dark blue or black flowers, and black cloth. Light a lamp with black sesame oil.

3. **Water to the Peepal tree:** The tradition holds that Shani Dev resides in the Peepal tree. On Saturday, offer water to the Peepal tree and light a sesame-oil lamp beneath it.

4. **Katha listening:** Listen to or read the Saturday vrat katha (the story of Vikramaditya). This is considered an indispensable part of the vrat.

5. **Dana:** Dana holds special significance on Saturdays — give black sesame, urad, oil, iron objects, or black cloth to someone in need. Given as "Shani-dana," this is considered highly beneficial.

6. **Hanuman worship:** On Saturday, recite the Hanuman Chalisa. Perform abhisheka of Hanuman with sindoor and oil. This practice is especially held in tradition for protection from Shani's afflictions.

7. **Breaking the fast:** Take one meal after sunset. The meal may include black sesame, urad, or sesame sweets (sesame laddoos).

## Observance

**Fasting:** The Saturday fast prescribes ekahara — one meal a day, after sunset. In strict fasting, no food at all or fruit only is observed. Those who are in Sade Sati or Dhaiya observe a particularly strict fast.

**What not to do:** Do not oil your body on Saturday (oil is offered to Shani, not used for oneself); do not cut your hair; do not buy or sell iron objects; do not behave harshly with anyone.

**For whom especially:** This vrat is said to be especially beneficial for those who are in Sade Sati, Dhaiya, or Shani's Mahadasha / Antardasha. Also for those who regard Shani as their Ishta, or for natives of Makara and Kumbha rashis (whose signs Shani rules).

**Regular vrat:** The Saturday fast may be kept regularly week by week, or with a special sankalpa for consecutive three, five, seven, or twenty-one Saturdays during a particular period of Shani.

## Mantras

**Shani Stuti:**

> ॐ नीलांजनसमाभासं रविपुत्रं यमाग्रजम् ।
> छायामार्तण्डसंभूतं तं नमामि शनैश्चरम् ॥

oṃ nīlāñjanasamābhāsaṃ raviputraṃ yamāgrajam |
chāyāmārtaṇḍasambhūtaṃ taṃ namāmi śanaiścaram ||

Meaning: Of radiance like blue collyrium, son of Surya, elder brother of Yama, born of Chhaya and Martanda (the Sun) — I bow to that Shanaishchara Dev.

---

**Shani Bija Mantra:**

> ॐ शं शनैश्चराय नमः ॥

oṃ śaṃ śanaiścarāya namaḥ ||

Meaning: Salutation to Shani Dev. (This bija mantra is for japa — 108 times on the rosary.)

---

**Invocation of Hanuman (for the removal of Shani's affliction):**

> ॐ हनुमते नमः शनि-पीड़ा-हराय च

oṃ hanumate namaḥ śani-pīḍā-harāya ca

Meaning: Salutation to Hanuman — who removes the afflictions of Shani. (Hanuman worship on Saturday for the pacification of Shani is traditional in North India.)
