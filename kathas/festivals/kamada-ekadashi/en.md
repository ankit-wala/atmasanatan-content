## Katha

The Ekadashi of the bright fortnight of the month of Chaitra is called Kamada Ekadashi. The story of this Ekadashi was told by Lord Krishna himself to King Yudhishthira in the Brahma Vaivarta Purana.

Once Yudhishthira asked Lord Krishna, "O Madhava, what is the name of the Ekadashi of Chaitra Shukla Paksha, and what is its glory?" Krishna smiled and said, "O King, this Ekadashi is 'Kamada' — the one who fulfills the deepest desire of a devotee's heart. Listen to her story."

In ancient times there was a beautiful city called Ramyapura, ruled by King Pundarika. Many Gandharvas, Kinnaras, and Apsaras lived in that city. Among them was a Gandharva couple — Lalit and his wife Lalita. They loved each other beyond measure. Lalit's art of singing was unmatched, and Lalita's affection was unbreakable.

One day, a great yajnaotsava was held in King Pundarika's court. All the Gandharvas were required to present their art. Lalit was called upon to sing. But at that moment, the image of Lalita lived in his mind — his thoughts were lost in the memory of his beloved. He was present in the court, but his soul was elsewhere. His singing faltered, his notes went astray, and his performance remained incomplete.

There was a serpent named Karkotaka in the king's court, who was deeply cunning. He witnessed all of this and whispered into the king's ear. King Pundarika was filled with great anger. He said, "A Gandharva so lost in his wife that he forgets his duty in the royal court — he is not worthy of the Gandharva race." And in his fury the king pronounced a curse upon Lalit: "Be thou a Rakshasa — terrible, fearsome, wandering in darkness!"

The moment the curse fell, Lalit's beautiful Gandharva form was extinguished. In his place stood a frightful Rakshasa — enormous in body, hideous of face, emanating foulness from every pore. Lalita stood stunned at the sight. But she did not surrender. Her love did not break at fear, nor did it break at the change of his form.

Lalita began to wander from forest to forest. She walked behind her demon-formed husband, protecting him, tending to him, caring for him. Tears were in her eyes, but her resolve in her heart was unshaken — "The love that caused this curse was no sin. My husband will surely be freed."

One day, wandering thus, they reached the hermitage of the sage Shringi. Lalita told him everything. Maharishi Shringi heard her story full of compassion and said, "Daughter, a terrible curse lies upon your husband, but there is a remedy. The Ekadashi of Chaitra Shukla Paksha is called 'Kamada Ekadashi' — she who fulfills all desires and destroys even the most terrible sins and curses. Observe this vrat with devotion and offer its merit to your husband."

Lalita did just that. With complete steadfastness she kept the Kamada Ekadashi vrat — a full day of nirjala fasting, worship of Lord Vishnu, offering of tulasi, and night vigil. On the morning of Dvadashi, after completing the parana, she folded her hands before the Lord and said, "O Lord, whatever merit I have earned from this vrat — let all that fruit go to my husband Lalit. May his curse be erased."

At that very moment a wondrous thing occurred. A wave of light swept across that terrible Rakshasa body. The fearsome form dissolved. Lalit appeared again in his divine Gandharva form — radiant, beautiful, free. He took Lalita's hand, and both their eyes filled with tears.

In the tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana, it is said that a devotee who keeps the Kamada Ekadashi vrat receives merit equal to an Ashvamedha yajna, all his sins are destroyed, and in the end he attains the supreme abode of Lord Vishnu.

## Significance

"Kamada" means — she who fulfills desires. This Ekadashi is called Kamada because she fulfills the deepest wish in a devotee's heart — whether that wish is for a son, for wealth, for health, or for the liberation of a loved one. Lalita's single desire was the freedom of her husband — and the Lord fulfilled it.

Kamada Ekadashi falls in the month of Chaitra in the Hindu New Year. This is the season of spring — when nature is reborn, new shoots emerge, and new light arrives. Observing this Ekadashi vrat is considered an auspicious beginning to the new year. Just as spring sheds old leaves and brings new life, so this Ekadashi cuts old sins and curses and gives a new beginning.

A deep message lies hidden in Lalita's story — that love and duty are not opposed. Lalit's love was no sin, but a lapse of duty did occur in the court. The consequence of that lapse was received. Yet Lalita's surrender, her selfless devotion, reversed that consequence. Kamada Ekadashi teaches this — the merit of bhakti does not stop with the devotee alone; it can reach one's beloved as well.

## Vidhi

- **Dashami (eve of Ekadashi):** Take one sattvic meal in the evening; abstain from urad dal, meat, garlic, onion, and all tamasic items; observe brahmacharya and keep the mind calm.
- **At brahma muhurta:** Rise before dawn, bathe, and wear clean yellow clothes.
- **Puja:** Light a ghee lamp before the idol of Lord Vishnu or a Shaligrama; offer tulasi leaves, yellow flowers, sandalwood, and incense; chant "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya"; read or listen to the Kamada Ekadashi katha.
- **Fasting:** A complete nirjala vrat is the highest form; those unable to keep nirjala may take fruit, milk, and water; grain food is strictly forbidden on this day.
- **Prohibited:** Grain, urad dal, masoor lentils, brinjal, garlic and onion, meat, speaking falsehoods, anger, and speaking ill of others — all forbidden during the Ekadashi vrat.
- **Evening puja:** Worship again; recite Vishnu Sahasranama or sing Hari kirtan.
- **Night vigil:** Remain awake through the night in bhajan, kirtan, naam-japa, or recitation of the Ekadashi mahatmya — the vigil multiplies the merit of the vrat manifold.
- **Breaking the fast (Dvadashi):** Perform parana after sunrise, within the Dvadashi tithi; take tulasi leaves at parana; if keeping the vrat for a particular wish, offer that wish to the Lord at the time of parana; feed a Brahmin and offer dakshina to complete the vrat sankalpa.
