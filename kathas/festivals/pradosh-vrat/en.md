## Katha

The gods and the demons joined together to churn the ocean of milk. The purpose was amrita — whoever obtained the nectar would become ageless and immortal.

Mount Mandara was made the churning rod, Vasuki the serpent was the rope. On one side the gods, on the other the demons — both pulled together. The ocean heaved, the mountain began to sink, Vishnu took the form of a tortoise and held it steady.

The churning began. One by one the treasures emerged — Kamadhenu, Uchchaihshrava, Airavata, the Kalpavriksha, Lakshmi, Dhanvantari — fourteen gems in all.

But the first thing to emerge was not amrita.

The first to emerge was **halahal**.

That poison was so ferocious that its very smell overwhelmed both gods and demons. A single drop of it could destroy the three worlds. The water began to burn, smoke rose in the sky, the earth trembled.

The gods fled. They went to Brahma — "Handle this." Brahma said — "This is beyond my power." The gods went to Vishnu — "Save us." Vishnu said — "The only one with the strength to endure this poison is Shiva."

All the gods went together to Kailash. Brahma walked ahead. He prayed to Shiva — *"Mahadeva, the three worlds seek your shelter. No one can drink this poison — but without you the world will not survive."*

Shiva was unmoved. He looked at Parvati. Parvati took his hand.

Shiva lifted the halahal and swallowed it in one breath.

Parvati held his throat with her hands — she did not let the poison descend. The poison was held in Shiva's throat. The throat turned blue.

From that time Shiva came to be called **Neelkanth**.

Yet the heat of the poison remained — even though it had not descended, the burning in his throat persisted. From Shiva's heat, the surrounding creation also began to smoulder.

Then, on the Trayodashi evening — in **Pradosh Kala** — the gods worshipped Shiva. They bathed him with water, offered bilva leaves, sang his praises. Chandra cooled Shiva's blue throat with his gentle rays. The navagrahas also performed puja. Indra showered flowers.

Shiva's heat was stilled.

From that very moment — that evening of Trayodashi — came to be known as **Pradosh**. The word "Pradosh" means the onset of night, that sacred hour between day and night.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that the devotee who worships Shiva during Pradosh Kala — from one and a half hours before sunset to one and a half hours after — receives the same share of merit that all the gods received on that day.

## Significance

The significance of Pradosh Vrat lies in its timing.

That hour and a half to two hours around sunset — when the day is departing and the night is arriving — is the sandhikala, the twilight juncture. Worship of Shiva during sandhikala is held to bear special fruit, because it was at this very time that the gods performed the first Pradosh puja.

Trayodashi — the thirteenth lunar day — is Shiva's day. It comes twice a month: in Shukla Paksha and in Krishna Paksha. Both Pradoshas are equally sacred.

This vrat is observed for the welfare of the family, for freedom from illness, and for the removal of obstacles in life. When Pradosh falls on a particular weekday — Som-Pradosh, Bhaum-Pradosh, Shani-Pradosh — its fruit is even more pronounced.

## Vidhi

- **Morning:** On Trayodashi, bathe at dawn and take the sankalpa for the vrat.
- **Fasting:** Keep the fast through the day — complete fast or ekahara (fruit, milk, and water only).
- **Timing:** Pradosh puja must begin before sunset and be completed within Pradosh Kala (one and a half hours before to one and a half hours after sunset); it is the puja of twilight, not of darkness.
- **Pre-puja bath:** Bathe again one hour before sunset; then go to a Shiva temple or set up puja at a Shivalinga at home.
- **Abhishek:** Pour pure water and raw milk over the Shivalinga slowly while reciting Om Namah Shivaya.
- **Offerings:** Offer bilva leaves (three-leafed); white flowers (datura, aak, jasmine); sandalwood paste; akshata; light a ghee lamp and incense.
- **Mantra and aarti:** Chant Om Namah Shivaya at least 108 times; recite the Karpoorgauram shloka; perform Shiva's aarti.
- **Breaking the fast (Paran):** On Chaturdashi — the following morning after sunrise — remember Shiva and break the fast.
- **Regularity:** Observing both Trayodashis of every month yields the full fruit; if both are not possible, keep whichever is within reach consistently.

## Mantras

**Panchakshari Mantra — the essential chant of Pradosh:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय

oṃ namaḥ śivāya

Meaning: Salutations to Shiva. This five-syllable mantra is the symbol of Shiva's five actions — creation, sustenance, dissolution, concealment, and grace.

---

**Karpoorgauram Shloka — the twilight shloka of Pradosh puja:**

> कर्पूरगौरं करुणावतारं संसारसारं भुजगेन्द्रहारम् ।
> सदावसन्तं हृदयारविन्दे भवं भवानीसहितं नमामि ॥

karpūragauraṃ karuṇāvatāraṃ saṃsārasāraṃ bhujagendrahāram | sadāvasantaṃ hṛdayāravinde bhavaṃ bhavānīsahitaṃ namāmi ||

Meaning: White as camphor, the very incarnation of compassion, the essence of the world, who wears the king of serpents as a garland — who dwells forever in the lotus of the heart — I bow to that Bhava (Shiva) along with Bhavani (Parvati).
