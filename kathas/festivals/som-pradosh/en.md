## Katha

The katha of Pradosh Vrat is connected to the churning of the ocean — when the gods worshipped Shiva on the Trayodashi evening and saved the world from the burning of the halahal. For that full story, see the chapter on Pradosh Vrat.

The special context of Som-Pradosh is linked to Chandra's own story.

Chandra was the son-in-law of Prajapati Daksha — husband to the twenty-seven nakshatra daughters. But his heart was fixed on Rohini; the other twenty-six were neglected. Daksha counselled him many times. Chandra did not listen. Finally Daksha gave the curse — "You shall be afflicted by decay."

Chandra began to wane. His radiance diminished. On earth, crops withered and medicinal herbs wilted.

Brahma pointed the way — "Go to Prabhas Tirtha and worship Shiva."

Chandra came to Prabhas Tirtha. There, on a Monday — and that Monday when the conjunction of Trayodashi was present — he worshipped Shiva during Pradosh Kala. Water, bilva leaves, devotion — and Shiva was pleased.

Shiva said — *"Daksha's curse I cannot remove entirely. But I will moderate it. You will wax for fifteen days and wane for fifteen days — and this shall be your nature."*

Chandra's radiance returned. That tirtha stands to this day — **Somnath** — the first among the twelve Jyotirlingas.

And when the heat of the halahal came upon Shiva during the churning of the ocean, it was on that very evening that Chandra cooled Shiva's blue throat with his gentle rays. Monday, the Pradosh hour — Chandra's worship and Shiva's grace come together at once.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said that the devotee who observes Som-Pradosh — their mind's restlessness is stilled, sweetness comes into married life, and they are protected from the adverse influence of Chandra.

## Significance

Monday is the day of Chandra and also of Shiva. Shiva is Chandrashekhara — on whose head Chandra resides. When Pradosh falls on a Monday, both energies come together at once.

Chandra is the karaka of the mind — of mental peace, emotional steadiness, and marital happiness. When Chandra's day and Shiva's Pradosh meet, the fruit of this vrat is especially pronounced in these very areas.

For those whose Chandra is afflicted in the birth chart, or who have an unsettled mind, or who face obstacles in marriage — for them, Som-Pradosh is said to be especially fruitful.

## Vidhi

The puja-vidhi of Som-Pradosh is the same as the main Pradosh Vrat. For the detailed procedure, see the chapter on Pradosh Vrat.

**Special additions:**
1. On this day milk holds special importance in the Shiva-abhishek — Chandra is connected to the ocean of milk; kshira (milk) is his symbol.
2. White flowers — jasmine, bela — are symbols of Chandra's coolness; offer these specially.
3. Along with the chant of *Om Namah Shivaya*, recite the Mahamrityunjaya Mantra.
4. If possible, perform the worship while remembering the Somnath Jyotirlinga.

## Observance

**Fasting:** Complete fast or ekahara on Trayodashi (Monday) — fruit and milk.

**Paran:** On Chaturdashi — that is, Tuesday — after sunrise, with remembrance of Shiva.

**Special:** On Monday evening during puja, be sure to light a lamp — as the symbol of Chandra's light.

**2026 dates:** Confirm on DrikPanchang.

## Mantras

**Panchakshari Mantra — the essential chant of Pradosh:**

> ॐ नमः शिवाय

oṃ namaḥ śivāya

Meaning: Salutations to Shiva. This five-syllable mantra is the symbol of Shiva's five actions — creation, sustenance, dissolution, concealment, and grace.

**Karpoorgauram Shloka — the twilight shloka of Pradosh puja:**

> कर्पूरगौरं करुणावतारं संसारसारं भुजगेन्द्रहारम् ।
> सदावसन्तं हृदयारविन्दे भवं भवानीसहितं नमामि ॥

karpūragauraṃ karuṇāvatāraṃ saṃsārasāraṃ bhujagendrahāram | sadāvasantaṃ hṛdayāravinde bhavaṃ bhavānīsahitaṃ namāmi ||

Meaning: White as camphor, the very incarnation of compassion, the essence of the world, who wears the king of serpents as a garland — who dwells forever in the lotus of the heart — I bow to that Bhava (Shiva) along with Bhavani (Parvati).
