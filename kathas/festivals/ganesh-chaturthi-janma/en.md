## Katha

Parvati was about to go and bathe.

There was no one at Kailash she could fully trust as a guardian. Shiva's ganas were there, but they belonged to Shiva — they would not bar anyone at Parvati's command. And if Shiva himself came, who would stop him?

Parvati thought of a way.

Before her bath, she gathered the ubtan — the paste of turmeric, sandalwood, and fragrance — that she had applied to her body. She took that paste in her hands and shaped it into a boy — beautiful, well-formed, radiant. Then she breathed life into him with her own power.

The boy came to life.

Parvati drew him close. She gave him weapons and said — "You are my son. Stand at the door. Without my permission, no one is to enter — no one at all."

The boy took his place at the door.

---

A little while later, Shiva returned with his ganas.

He walked toward his own home — and at the door stood an unknown boy.

The boy blocked his way.

Shiva said — "I live here. Let me through."

The boy said — "Mother has said — no one enters without her permission."

Shiva's ganas laughed. This was their own home — and this small boy was keeping them out? The ganas tried to force their way through. The boy held them back. One by one the ganas were turned away. Nandi, Kartikeya — all were pushed back. The boy was alone, but he carried Parvati's own power in him.

Shiva explained again. The boy did not yield.

The battle grew. Shiva's anger rose. He lifted his trishul — and severed the boy's head from his body.

---

Parvati came out from within.

She saw her son lying on the ground. A moment of silence — then she broke.

Her grief turned to fury in an instant. She was no longer Parvati — she became Shakti. She took on a fearsome form. Her forces rose and fell upon the gods from every direction. The cosmos shook. The gods were terrified.

Brahma and Vishnu came forward. They pleaded with Parvati.

Parvati put two conditions before them — "Restore my son to life. And declare him Agrapujya — in all three worlds, in every puja, in every yajna, he is to be worshipped first."

---

Shiva's ganas set out toward the north.

North — the direction that is auspicious, the direction of good fortune and grace. There they found an elephant — single-tusked, lying with its head facing north. This was the elephant fit for this purpose.

They brought the elephant's head back with them.

Shiva placed that head upon the boy's body. He breathed life into him.

The boy opened his eyes.

Parvati ran to him. She held her son close.

---

Then Shiva stepped forward.

He placed his hand on the boy's elephant head and, speaking so all three worlds could hear, said —

"From this day, this one is Ganapati — lord of all the ganas. In all three worlds, in every puja, in every yajna, at the beginning of every good undertaking — his worship shall come first. **Whoever begins any work without first worshipping him — that work shall not be fulfilled.**"

Brahma, Vishnu, and all the gods bowed before Ganesha. That day was Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi.

And from that day, this tithi is Ganesh Chaturthi — the festival of his birth and the declaration of his primacy in worship.

The Shiva Purana, Rudra Samhita, Kumara Khanda tells us — the devotee who worships Ganesha on this Chaturthi with a pure heart, offering flowers, durva grass, and modak, will find all obstacles removed from their path and all their endeavours brought to completion.

## Significance

**Ganapati = lord of the ganas.** When Shiva declared him Agrapujya, it was not merely about one puja — it was a declaration that no good undertaking in the cosmos would be completed without Ganesha's blessing.

**Why an elephant's head?** Because it was the head found in the north — the auspicious direction. The elephant is a symbol of vast memory, intelligence, and quiet strength. Ganesha, the elephant-headed, is the deity of precisely this wisdom and power. For those who worship him, obstacles fall away; for those who do not, difficulties may arise.

In Maharashtra, Ganesh Chaturthi is a ten-day festival. **Lokmanya Bal Gangadhar Tilak** gave it its public form in 1893 so that people would come together as one. Today the festival is celebrated across the country. On Chaturthi, an image of Ganesha is installed and worshipped for ten days, then immersed in water on Anant Chaturdashi.

In some traditions — according to the Brahma Vaivarta Purana — Vishnu went to the Pushpabhadra river to bring the elephant's head. The primary account is from the Shiva Purana.

## Vidhi

The traditional observance of Ganesh Chaturthi puja:

1. **Morning bath and sankalp.** Bathe in the morning and take the sankalp (vow) of Ganesha's birth celebration.
2. **Pratima sthapana.** Place an image of Ganesha in clay on a clean red or yellow cloth. Set before him flowers, fruit, modak, durva, incense, and a lamp.
3. **Panchamrit abhishek.** Bathe Ganesha with the five nectars — milk, curd, ghee, honey, and sugar.
4. **Durva offering.** Offer twenty-one blades of durva grass (dub) to Ganesha — in groups of three. Durva is especially dear to him.
5. **Modak offering.** Offer modak made from coconut or sesame and jaggery — his most beloved food.
6. **Red flowers.** Red roses, marigolds, or hibiscus are particularly favoured by Ganesha.
7. **Shodashopachara puja.** Worship with the sixteen traditional services — fragrance, flowers, incense, lamp, food offering, aarti, and the rest.
8. **Katha shravan.** After the puja, hear or recite the Ganesha birth katha — this is the heart of the festival.
9. **Aarti.** Perform the aarti with "Jai Ganesh Jai Ganesh Deva." Distribute prasad (modak and fruit).
10. **Visarjan.** For home installations, immersion may be on the 1.5th day, the 5th, 7th, or 10th day. For public pandals, immersion takes place on Anant Chaturdashi.

**Note:** Do not look at the moon on this night. If you accidentally see it, hear the Syamantaka Mani katha — that entry is separate.

## Mantras

**Vakratunda Shloka**

> वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटिसमप्रभ ।
> निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा ॥

Vakratuṇḍa mahākāya sūryakoṭisamaprabha |
Nirvighnaṃ kuru me deva sarvakāryeṣu sarvadā ||

Meaning: O Vakratunda (curved-trunk), vast of form, radiant as ten million suns — O Lord, remove all obstacles from my every endeavour, always.

---

**Ganesha Mool Mantra**

> ॐ गं गणपतये नमः

Oṃ Gaṃ Gaṇapataye Namaḥ

Meaning: O Ganapati, I bow to you.
