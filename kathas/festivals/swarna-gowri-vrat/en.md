## Katha

It was the month of Bhadrapada.

In that town lived a woman named Sumati. Her home was small — there was poverty, but the devotion in her heart toward the Goddess was unbroken.

Her husband had been afflicted by a serious illness. He had not been able to rise from his bed for months. The physician had given up. Grain was scarce in the house. Every day Sumati would petition Mother Devi — yet within her burned a hope that refused to go out.

The day of Bhadrapada Shukla Tritiya arrived.

Sumati rose early and bathed. She had no golden idol, no new garments, no costly offerings. But she had turmeric.

With her own hands she kneaded the turmeric and shaped a small image — of Mother Gauri. Turmeric is golden, like the flame of a lamp. This was her Swarna Gauri.

She worshipped with the sixteen offerings — whatever she had, she offered. Water, tulasi, kumkum, flowers, a lamp, naivedya. In her heart there was only one prayer —

*"O Mother Gauri, restore my husband to health. Bring peace to my home. There is no one but you."*

That night Sumati fell into a deep sleep.

Then a divine radiance descended into her dream.

Mother Gauri — fair-complexioned, draped in golden cloth, a crescent moon on her brow — stood before her. On her face was that tenderness a mother carries when she looks upon her daughter.

She said —

*"Sumati, your devotion has reached me. Your husband will rise. Your home will become 'golden' — but that gold is not of metal. It is the gold of health, of joy, of a child's laughter. This is the real gold."*

Sumati's eyes opened.

And she saw — her husband was sitting up in bed. The fever that had not broken for months had lifted overnight.

Tears streamed from Sumati's eyes.

She took the small turmeric image of Gauri into her hands — still golden, like the light of dawn. And she knew — this was her Swarna Gauri.

Slowly, that household became green and full again. And in all the years that followed, whenever Bhadrapada Tritiya came, Sumati performed that vrat again — with gratitude, with love, with that same turmeric Gauri.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana, it is said — *"The woman who on this Tritiya worships Swarna Gauri with faith and devotion shall have prosperity, good health, and the blessing of children abide in her home."*

## Significance

Swarna Gowri Vrat is observed on Bhadrapada Shukla Tritiya — exactly one day before Ganesh Chaturthi. The day carries a deep familial meaning.

Just as a daughter comes to her parents' home before the birth of her child — so too does Mother Parvati, one day before Ganesha's birth (Chaturthi), arrive at her devotees' homes as a daughter. She is both mother and daughter — this feeling makes the vrat uniquely tender.

'Swarna' means gold — but in this vrat, gold is not merely a metal. The image of Gauri made from turmeric is the gold — because turmeric is golden in colour, and Mother Gauri's blessing too makes life golden.

This vrat is observed especially by married women — for the health and long life of their husbands, for children, and for prosperity in the home. In Karnataka it is known as **Gowri Habba** and is one of the major festivals there.

At the time of Ganesh Visarjan, the turmeric image of Mother Gauri is also immersed in water — mother and son depart together. This sight is as sacred as it is moving.

## Vidhi

- **Fasting:** Observe a fast through the day; a single sattvic fruit meal is permitted. Begin in the morning with a sankalpa (resolve).
- **At dawn:** Rise early; bathe; put on clean garments.
- **Gauri image:** Shape an image of Mother Gauri from turmeric (raw turmeric or turmeric powder) — this is the Swarna Gauri. A clay or metal idol from the market is also acceptable, but the turmeric tradition is older and more beloved.
- **Setup:** Place the image on a clean asana; establish a kalasha beside it.
- **Shodashopachar puja:** Worship with the sixteen offerings — dhyana, avahana, asana, padya, arghya, achaman, snana, vastra, yajnopavita, gandha, pushpa, dhupa, dipa, naivedya, tambula, pradakshina-namaskar.
- **Offerings:** Kumkum, turmeric, and items of suhaga (bangles, bindi, kajal) — especially offered by married women as a prayer for marital wellbeing and auspiciousness.
- **Katha:** Hear the Gauri Vrat Katha as part of the puja.
- **Visarjan:** After Ganesh Chaturthi celebrations (the following day or that evening), immerse the turmeric Gauri image in water — mother and son depart together.
- **For whom:** Observed especially by married women — for the health and long life of their husbands, for children, and for prosperity in the home.

## Mantras

**Gauri Mantra — for recitation during worship:**

> ॐ गौर्यै नमः ।
> ॐ पार्वत्यै नमः ।
> ॐ जगन्मात्रे नमः ॥

Oṃ Gauryai Namaḥ |
Oṃ Pārvatyai Namaḥ |
Oṃ Jaganmātre Namaḥ ||

Meaning: "Salutations to Gauri, to Parvati, to the Mother of the Universe."

---

**Shiva-Parvati Auspiciousness Shloka — after aarti:**

> गौरी शंकरयोर्भक्त्या यः पूजयति सर्वदा ।
> तस्य सौभाग्यमारोग्यं पुत्रपौत्रादिसम्पदः ॥

Gaurī Śaṅkarayor bhaktyā yaḥ pūjayati sarvadā |
Tasya saubhāgyam ārogyaṃ putrapautrādi sampadaḥ ||

Meaning: "The devotee who always worships Gauri and Shankara with devotion receives the blessings of auspiciousness, good health, and the wealth of sons and grandsons."
