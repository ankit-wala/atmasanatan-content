## Katha

There was a wealthy merchant and his wife — they had everything, but for years one lack hollowed them out from within. No children.

They tried everything — charity, pilgrimage, fasting. But the years passed.

One day an old Brahmin woman came to their door. She saw in the merchant's wife's face that sadness which shows even when the eyes try to hide it.

She asked: *"Child, what is the matter?"*

The woman told her everything.

The old woman said: *"There is a vrat of Mangala Gauri in the month of Shravan. Each Tuesday — worship Maa Gauri with the shodashopachara rite. In the regional tradition, each item of offering is presented in sets of sixteen. Observe this vrat for five Shravans. Maa Gauri's grace will come."*

The wife began the vrat. Each Tuesday — bath, puja, sixteen flowers, sixteen fruits, sixteen lamps, meditation on Maa Gauri. Five Shravans passed.

On one Tuesday of the fifth Shravan — by Maa Gauri's grace — a son was born in their home.

Joy filled the house. The son grew.

When the son came of marriageable age, his birth chart was examined. The astrologer turned leaf after leaf, looked again — and his face grew grave.

He said: *"There is a heavy combination in this son's horoscope. On the seventh day after his wedding, his life will be taken by a serpent's sting."*

Grief fell over the house. The mother wept and prayed to Maa Gauri: *"You gave him — you protect him."*

The wedding took place. The new bride came home. The mother told her — this was no secret; it was a prayer.

The young bride listened and did not panic. She said: *"Mother, the akshat from the Mangala Gauri puja — I will keep it with me."*

On the seventh night, a great serpent came to the threshold of the bedroom. The young bride was awake. She saw it and, with serene composure, scattered the akshat from Maa Gauri's puja in the serpent's direction — and took Maa Gauri's name.

The serpent stopped. Then it turned and left.

Morning came. The son was alive.

In the tradition of the Skanda Purana it is said: *"The woman who observes the Mangala Gauri vrat on the Tuesdays of Shravan — her husband gains long life, her home remains filled with auspiciousness, and Maa Gauri's grace endures through generations."*

## Significance

The Mangala Gauri Vrat means — the auspicious form of Maa Gauri, she who protects a wife's saubhagya.

In the Indian tradition, Gauri is that power who performed tapasya to attain Shiva. She who herself remained so unwavering — why would she not protect the saubhagya of her devoted women?

In this vrat, "Mangala" means mangalakaarini — bringer of auspiciousness. The Tuesdays of Shravan are chosen for this vrat because Shravan is the month of both Shiva and Shakti, and the very word "Mangal" expresses this vrat's spirit — the husband's well-being, the home's well-being, life's well-being.

This vrat is especially meant for newly-wed women — but every married woman may observe it.

## Vidhi

- **When:** Each Tuesday of the month of Shravan; observe for the first five Shravans after marriage — especially auspicious for newly-wed women.
- **At dawn:** Rise early and bathe; wear red or yellow clothes.
- **Puja setup:** Install an image or idol of Maa Gauri; perform the shodashopachara puja — in the regional tradition each item in sets of sixteen: sixteen flowers, sixteen fruits, sixteen areca nuts, sixteen cardamom, sixteen betel leaves, sixteen bindi/tikuli; if sixteen is not possible, follow the standard shodashopachara method.
- **Shringar offerings:** Also offer sindoor, bangles, and bindi.
- **Katha and aarti:** Listen to or read the Mangala Gauri Katha; then perform the aarti.
- **Fasting:** Observe the fast — one sattvic meal; break the fast after the evening puja, or the following morning.

## Mantras

**Gauri Puja Mantra:**

> ॐ गौर्यै नमः

*Oṃ Gauryai Namaḥ*

Meaning: "Salutations to Maa Gauri."

---

**Gauri Stuti — to be recited in worship:**

> सर्वमङ्गलमाङ्गल्ये शिवे सर्वार्थसाधिके ।
> शरण्ये त्र्यम्बके गौरि नारायणि नमोऽस्तु ते ॥

*Sarva-maṅgala-māṅgalye śive sarvārtha-sādhike |
Śaraṇye tryambake gauri Nārāyaṇi namo'stu te ||*

Meaning: "O auspicious one among all that is auspicious, O Shiva, fulfiller of all purposes, giver of refuge, Gauri of three eyes — Narayani, salutations to you."
