## Katha

One day Dharmaraja Yudhishthira asked Lord Krishna — "Madhava, what is the name of the Ekadashi of Jyeshtha Krishna Paksha? Please tell me of its greatness."

The Lord said — "Rajan, this Ekadashi bears the name **Apara** — she who has no shore, whose fruit cannot be measured. The fast of this Ekadashi bestows merit equal to the Ashvamedha yajna. Listen, let me tell you a katha."

---

Long ago, there lived a king named Mahidhvaja who ruled over a prosperous city. He was a righteous man — just, compassionate, a protector of his people. His younger brother was called Vajradhvaja. But where Mahidhvaja carried dharma in his heart, Vajradhvaja carried greed and cunning in his.

One night.

Mahidhvaja was resting alone beneath a peepal tree at the edge of the city. The darkness was deep. Vajradhvaja came there — and seeing his own elder brother alone and unguarded, he did what is unthinkable even to imagine — he treacherously killed his own blood as he lay sleeping.

Mahidhvaja's death was sudden. There was no last rite, no mantra, no proper vidhi. The soul found no passage forward.

And from that moment, Mahidhvaja's soul became bound to that very peepal tree — wandering as a preta. Years passed. The king who had lived by dharma now drifted around that tree — neither in heaven nor in liberation. Only waiting.

---

One day Maharshi Dhaumya was passing through that forest.

He was a siddha sage — his vision was not ordinary. As he walked past that peepal tree, his yogic sight perceived what no common person could see. He saw — a soul, bound within that tree, restless, suffering.

He stopped. He drew near. He said — "Who are you? Why are you bound within this tree? Tell me."

And the preta told his story. Through tears — as though the weight of years poured out all at once.

Sage Dhaumya listened. He knew — this death had been unjust. This bondage was unjust. Yet he also knew that there is no injustice in this world so deep that the grace of God cannot reach beyond it.

He said — "Rajan, hear me. Your death was untimely, it was unjust. But this does not mean your journey has ended. No prana lies beyond the compassion of the Lord."

"The Ekadashi of Jyeshtha Krishna Paksha is called **Apara Ekadashi** — 'Apara,' she who has no shore. Keep this fast — a full day of upavasa, a night of vigil, meditation upon Lord Vishnu and the remembrance of his name. The power of this Ekadashi is without limit. It severs every bondage — the bondage of sin, of untimely death, of incomplete rites. It can free even a soul from preta-yoni."

The preta listened. And for the first time, he felt hope.

---

Mahidhvaja's soul observed that Ekadashi fast. All day in meditation on Vishnu, all night repeating his name — "Narayana… Narayana…"

The night passed. The first ray of light broke in the eastern sky.

And then — a divine radiance descended. That preta-form which had suffered for so many years slowly dissolved away. A divine vimana arrived. Mahidhvaja's soul — now free, resplendent — ascended that vimana and departed toward the abode of Vishnu.

In the shade of that peepal tree where a soul had wandered for years — there was now only peace.

The tradition of the Brahma Vaivarta Purana holds that whoever observes this Ekadashi with faith and devotion receives the combined merit of the Ashvamedha yajna, residence at Kashi, a bath in the Ganga, and the darshan of Kurukshetra. This Ekadashi has the power to sever sins, release souls from preta-yoni, and cut every manner of bondage.

## Significance

"Apara" — a + para. Without a further shore. This is the description of this Ekadashi's merit — so vast it cannot be measured.

This Ekadashi is especially dear to those whose loved ones have died an untimely death, who were not given their funeral rites in the proper manner. The tradition holds that observing this Ekadashi's fast also bears fruit for the peace and onward journey of such souls.

This Ekadashi points toward a profound spiritual truth — that no injustice, no misfortune, no untimely death can permanently halt a soul's journey. The compassion of the Lord has no shore — and that is precisely the name of this Ekadashi.

The katha of Mahidhvaja also tells us that a life lived by dharma is never spent in vain — no matter how unjust its ending may have been.

## Vidhi

- **Dashami preparation:** On the eve of Ekadashi, eat sattvic food; keep the evening meal light.
- **At dawn:** Rise before sunrise; bathe; take the vow — "I observe the fast of Apara Ekadashi for the pleasure of Lord Vishnu."
- **Puja:** Place an image or murti of Lord Vishnu at the place of worship; perform panchamrita abhishek (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar); offer tulasi leaves, yellow flowers, yellow cloth, and a lamp.
- **Fasting:** Nirjal or on fruits — both are accepted; grains, lentils, wheat, rice, onion, garlic, leafy vegetables, and brinjal are prohibited; fruits, milk, sabudana, singhara, and rock salt may be taken; avoid anger, harsh speech, and untruth.
- **Daytime observance:** Recite Vishnu Sahasranama, a chapter of the Bhagavad Gita, or chant "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya."
- **Night vigil:** Spend the night in kirtan — chanting "Narayana… Narayana…" or "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya"; if any member of your family has died an untimely death, offer a prayer for their peace this night.
- **Breaking the fast:** On Dwadashi morning, after sunrise and before the Dwadashi tithi ends; check the exact parana muhurta on DrikPanchang.

## Mantras

**Dvadasakshari Mantra — for japa on this Ekadashi:**

> ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya

Meaning: "O Lord Vasudeva (Krishna/Vishnu), I bow to you." This twelve-syllable dvadasakshari mantra is regarded as the supreme mantra of Vishnu-bhakti. Its japa during the fast of Apara Ekadashi is held to be especially fruitful.

---

**Liberation Prayer — for bound souls:**

> सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः सर्वे सन्तु निरामयाः ।
> सर्वे भद्राणि पश्यन्तु मा कश्चिद् दुःखभाग्भवेत् ॥

Sarve bhavantu sukhinaḥ sarve santu nirāmayāḥ |
Sarve bhadrāṇi paśyantu mā kaścid duḥkhabhāg bhavet ||

Meaning: "May all be happy, may all be free from illness, may all behold what is auspicious, may none be a partaker of sorrow." — On the night of Apara Ekadashi, offer this prayer for all souls who have been held back on their journey for any reason. The compassion of the Lord is apara — without shore — and that is this Ekadashi's name.
